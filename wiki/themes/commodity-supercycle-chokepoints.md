## The governing distinction (held throughout)

A commodity supercycle is a *capex* cycle whose prices flatten then **collapse** when supply expands (1986, 2014). So the durable way to "play" it is to own the **chokepoint the buildout can't avoid** — the picks-and-shovels with pricing power that survive the glut — **not** the commodity price or the producer whose earnings merely track it. For each leg, three claims are kept separate: (1) is the *demand* a supercycle? (2) is the *price* a supercycle, or a supply-cut spike that reverts? (3) *where does the durable value sit*?

## The verdict

**Currie is directionally right that physical constraints are repricing — but the six-leg "supercycle" framing is overstated, and the durable value is NOT in the commodity prices he says to own.** The structural chokepoints sit in **processing / precision-manufacturing concentration** (critical-materials refining, enrichment, grid equipment), not in oil and not in the metal tick. The demand inflection holds for **electricity/electrification and critical materials**, **fails for oil**. Scenario read: **Narrower** (of Base / Narrower / Overstated). Confidence: high on the per-leg structural facts (primary IEA/USGS/WNA); medium on the cross-leg ranking and the Currie-claim cross-check.

## Per-leg verdicts (cross-leg summary; detail on the per-leg pages)

- **Critical materials (#1).** Bottleneck is **midstream, not geology** — China refines ~91% of rare-earth *separation* vs ~60% of *mining* (the 31-pt gap *is* the chokepoint); magnets 94%; top-3 refiner share *rising* (82→86%, projected ~82% to 2035). Rare earths are geologically abundant. **Deepest node = gallium + the chelating-resin extraction tech (Sunresin ~90%, the tech itself export-controlled)** — "deeper than rare earths." **Falsifier fired (flag):** pricing power rests on **export-control policy that toggles** (China escalated Apr/Oct/Dec 2025, then suspended for a year Nov 2025–Nov 2026) and a documented **predatory-pricing flood deterrent**; the Western response ([[MP]]) is **policy-contingent** (DOD $400M + a 10-yr $110/kg NdPr floor *explicitly to shield from Chinese price collapse*). Detail: [[rare-earth-magnet-chokepoint]].
- **Nuclear fuel (#2) — confirms the vault's prior finding.** WNA: "commercial supply of HALEU is only available from Russian company Tenex"; the US Russian-uranium ban is the *trigger*, the ~42-mo cascade lead time is what *sustains* it. Uranium is geologically abundant (~90 yr) → **not a uranium-price supercycle.** Durable value = enrichment, with Centrus the sole NRC-licensed Western producer ($900M DOE task order Jan 2026). Independently confirms the S160 nuclear assessment. Detail: [[HALEU-fuel-chokepoint]].
- **Electrification / grid (#3) — the most on-thesis leg.** IEA: generation spend rose ~70% since 2015 to ~$1T/yr while grid spend rose at *less than half* that pace to ~$400B — "tight markets for transformers and other components," 2,500+ GW in grid queues. The durable play is the **equipment** (sells scarce capacity, survives the metal glut), not the electricity commodity. Open: does the equipment's *pricing power* survive once capacity is added? Detail: [[transformer-supply]].
- **Copper (#4) — genuine geology, but value tracks the metal.** IEA: ~30% supply shortfall by 2035 from grade decline (−40% since 1991), ~17-yr lead times, 14 discoveries in a decade vs 225 prior — "structural, not cyclical." **Not the uranium pattern.** But it's a *modeled* gap (high price spurs exploration/recycling/substitution), and the value is commodity-price-correlated. **No vault page — connection-finder candidate.**
- **Oil & gas (#5) — the leg that FAILS.** IEA: demand +2.5 mb/d to a ~105.5 plateau by 2030 vs capacity **+5.1 mb/d → a ~1.7 mb/d surplus** ("prices would have to drop"). The most recent upstream cut is only **−6% (2025)** — Currie's thread blurs that single-year cut with the multi-year cumulative. *(Honest caveat: a genuine post-2030 lag exists — ~$570B spend vs ~$640B/yr needed by 2030 — but it qualifies, not rescues, a to-2030 surplus.)*
- **Grains/softs (#6) — unassessed.** Zero verified claims; default cyclical pending a dedicated potash/seed-IP/logistics pass.

## Cross-check of Currie's claims

- **Oil fails on primary data** (structural surplus to 2030). His **−35% capex starvation** is a real *decade cumulative* (~$869B 2015 → ~$567B 2025), but the thread inflates severity by blurring it with the single-year −6% cut.
- **The "13–15.5% vs 2.6% FCF-yield gap" and the "$10T forced rotation" could not be primary-verified** — and are *valuation/flow* arguments, not supply facts (so they mean-revert, by construction).
- **Phase-dating (~2026-2037)** is a narrative frame. Currie/Goldman/Carlyle carry an **aligned incentive** to push a get-long supercycle.

## Phase-timing read

Because prices eventually collapse like 1986/2014 when supply expands, the chokepoints that **win late-cycle** are the ones whose pricing power does **NOT** depend on the commodity price — grid-equipment lead times, rare-earth separation chemistry, enrichment scarcity — rather than producers whose earnings merely track the metal/crude. Own the picks-and-shovels with **non-price-linked pricing power**, not the commodity or the producer. (Early phase: enablers re-rate. Mid: the chokepoint picks-and-shovels win. Late: de-risk the cyclical, keep the chokepoint.)

# CEPL — Capstone Energy+, Inc.

## Thesis role

Capstone Energy+ makes **low-emission microturbines** for **behind-the-meter (BTM), on-site distributed power** — small recuperated gas turbines (30 kW–1 MW class) that let a site generate its own electricity instead of waiting on the grid. It is the vault's **fifth behind-the-meter technology base** on [[BTM-grid-bypass-workaround]] — the **microturbine modality**, alongside [[BE]] (solid-oxide fuel cell), [[FCEL]] (molten-carbonate fuel cell), [[GEV]] (utility-scale gas turbine), and [[CAT]] (reciprocating engine + aero-derivative turbine). It relisted on Nasdaq as **CEPL on July 8, 2026** (formerly Capstone Green Energy / Capstone Turbine). Placement is **`layer 4` + `energy_power_tier 4`** — a specialty distributed-generation supplier and BTM participant, **not** a chokepoint owner (microturbines are a competitive product, and its AI-datacenter revenue is unproven). Closest vault analog: [[FCEL]] — a specialist BTM technology base with a real product but no proven hyperscaler traction at ingest.

**Why it's in the vault — and where it is honestly weak.** CEPL connects to the AI-datacenter **power-is-the-bottleneck** thesis (the binding 2026 constraint per `_thesis.md`: ~50% of planned US datacenter builds delayed on power, 4-year transformer lead times) through the BTM grid-bypass workaround — self-generating on-site to skip the interconnection queue. But three honest-verdict tensions govern the page, and the financial one is binding:

1. **A real post-bankruptcy turnaround — but a live refinancing cliff.** FY2026 was the **first full fiscal year of positive net income in the company's ~38-year history** ($2.8M net income, revenue +23.9%, adjusted EBITDA doubled, going-concern doubt removed, all prior material weaknesses remediated). Yet the **$25.3M Exit Notes (Chapter 11 exit financing) mature Dec 7, 2026** — classified a current liability, with the 10-K stating plainly there is **"no assurance"** it can repay or refinance them. That maturity, now ~5 months out, is the binding risk (see Financial-quality read).
2. **The AI/data-center angle is real strategic direction but not yet revenue.** The 800-volt DC microturbine and "Energy Surplus Program" reference design are pilot-stage; management said on the call there is **"nothing signed at this time."** Current revenue is oil & gas rental fleet + combined-heat-and-power (CHP) — not datacenters.
3. **A micro-cap with a thin balance sheet.** ~$106M revenue, negative operating cash flow, a total stockholders' deficit, and single-analyst coverage.

**Honest-verdict framing.** CEPL is included for BTM-thesis participation and datacenter optionality, not thesis centrality. The page leads with the **financial-quality read** (the Exit-Notes cliff) as the honest centerpiece — neither the earnings call's turnaround-forward framing nor the (now-stale) "renewed bankruptcy risk" web narrative captures it: the going-concern doubt was lifted, but a near-term debt maturity remains front-of-Item-1A. **Section 2.11** non-calendar filer: fiscal year ends **March 31** (month+ offset → applies); **FY2026 = year ended Mar 31 2026; Q3 FY2026 = quarter ended Dec 31 2025 ≈ calendar Q4 2025**.

## Financial-quality read — the honest centerpiece

CEPL is a **financial-quality-screen name** (the discipline the vault applies to post-bankruptcy / thin-balance-sheet small caps, e.g. [[FCEL]] / [[AMPX]]). Three things sit together and must be read together:

- **★ The Exit-Notes refinancing cliff (the binding risk).** The Chapter 11 **Exit Notes** — **$25.3M net / $28.1M gross** ($21.1M "Roll Up" + $7.0M "New Money"), **maturing December 7, 2026**, at **SOFR + 7%** (a portion paid-in-kind), **secured on substantially all assets**, with minimum-liquidity and minimum-adjusted-EBITDA covenants tested quarterly — are **classified a current liability** at Mar 31 2026. The 10-K states the company **"has not yet secured additional financing to repay the Exit Notes at their maturity"** and that **"there is no assurance that we will be able to repay or refinance the Exit Notes at or prior to their maturity date."** Cash ($28.9M) barely covers the balance, and operating cash flow is negative — so repayment depends on a refinancing, an uplisting-enabled raise ("LPLUS"), or fresh capital. This is the single most important fact on the page.
- **Going concern — removed, but recently and narrowly.** The FY2026 10-K carries **no going-concern qualification**; the **Q3 FY2026 10-Q (Dec 31 2025) still did** ("substantial doubt about the Company's ability to meet its obligations"). The doubt was lifted between Q3 and year-end, driven by the March 2026 Monarch investment that lifted cash from $15.2M to $28.9M — i.e., the removal is real but fresh, and it does not resolve the December 2026 maturity.
- **Controls — remediated.** The 10-K concludes disclosure controls were **effective** as of Mar 31 2026 and that the **"previously reported material weakness has been fully remediated. There are no remaining material weaknesses"** (the prior weakness was in Factory Protection Plan service-contract accounting + IT controls). A clean audit opinion was issued. (Cross-vault contrast worth noting only in passing: this is the mirror image of a name that *adds* control weaknesses — CEPL is coming *out* of them.)

The net read: a genuine operating turnaround riding on a balance sheet that must be refinanced within the year. The turnaround is real; its durability is contingent on clearing the December 2026 maturity.

## Behind-the-meter positioning — the fifth technology base

CEPL is the **microturbine modality** on [[BTM-grid-bypass-workaround]]. Its microturbines are a **distinct BTM technology base** from the vault's other four: a **recuperated small gas turbine** (a single moving part on air bearings — no oil, water, or coolant), **30 kW / 65 kW / 200 kW** cores scaling to MW-class packages (C200 / C600 / C1000 / C1000 Signature; a larger **C250** engine in development), **multi-fuel** (natural gas, biogas, landfill gas, hydrogen-capable), with **ultra-low emissions** (single-digit-ppm NOx) and combined-heat-and-power / waste-heat recovery. Its structural position matches the BTM thesis: on-site generation that bypasses the grid-interconnection queue and relieves overloaded distribution infrastructure (management pitches it explicitly as relief for field-deployed [[transformer-supply|transformers]] overloaded by EV-charging and datacenter load — a "grid-relief technology, not just a generator").

**Honest placement within the five.** CEPL is the **smallest and weakest-balance-sheet participant** of the five BTM technology bases, and — like [[FCEL]] at its ingest — has **no proven hyperscaler/datacenter traction yet**. Its edge vs the fuel-cell and large-turbine participants is the microturbine's small modular size, multi-fuel flexibility, low emissions, and waterless operation; its disadvantage is scale and financial fragility. It is a real BTM supplier *today* (deployed for oil & gas and CHP), which is why it lands `energy_power_tier 4` rather than `outside`.

## The AI / data-center angle — real direction, unproven revenue

The datacenter opportunity is the reason CEPL is interesting to the thesis, and the reason to be disciplined about it. In the 10-K it appears as an **emerging market and a risk factor** ("our ability to enter into and compete in the emerging data center market"), **not** a revenue line. What management is building (call + 10-K, attributed):
- An **800-volt DC microturbine** meant to interface with next-generation AI-chip power architectures (referencing NVIDIA's 800V DC standard); deployed in a C600 unit through "a full quarter of test protocols," piloting with unnamed "data center infrastructure companies" — **"nothing signed at this time."**
- The **Energy Surplus Program** reference design — a modular package that integrates into a datacenter's medium-voltage ring bus, recovers waste heat to drive an absorption chiller (chilled water at ~1/10th the energy of air-cooled electric chillers), and pairs a waterless dry cooler (no evaporative towers) — pitched at the noise / water / emissions / grid-strain concerns behind datacenter community backlash (the Ashburn, Virginia rate-surge episode).
- Management frames the datacenter pipeline as "doubled" and better-qualified, citing [[GEV]]'s "20% of 100 GW under contract tied to data centers" and Solaris's 600+ MW behind-meter deal as market backdrop (these are cited as evidence the *market* is real, not as CEPL relationships).

Per the honest-verdict discipline: the direction is credible and the product is genuinely BTM-capable, but **no datacenter revenue, no named customer, and nothing signed** — this is optionality, not a proven thesis. A signed datacenter order is the pre-registered trigger to revisit the placement.

## Business & end-markets

**One reportable segment**, with revenue disaggregated by category: **product & accessories** (new microturbines), **parts & service** (maintenance, the Factory Protection Plan lifecycle contracts), and **rental** (an on-site fleet, heavily oil & gas). End-markets: **oil & gas** (rental fleet for compression / flare-gas / remote power — the largest and most cyclical, sensitive to oil-price and rig-count swings), **energy-efficiency CHP** (hotels, hospitals, industrial), **renewables** (landfill/biogas), **ports** (electrification), and **emerging datacenter**. Installed base ~**10,800 units / 1.18 GW across 89 countries**; HQ Van Nuys, California; distribution via a territory/distributor network (the FY2026 **Cal Microturbine** acquisition brought the Western Territory in-house as direct sales, plus a new Global/National Accounts program to follow multi-site clients directly). **Supply-chain / tariff risk:** the 10-K flags tariff exposure on imported components (China / Mexico / Canada), higher inbound freight in FY2026, and recuperator lead-time pressure — offset partly by DFMA cost-out and by adding secondary/tertiary suppliers. (China exposure here is general import-tariff + cyber risk, not a rare-earth / critical-mineral chokepoint dependency — so CEPL is not added to the [[china-exposure]] matrix.)

## Open questions

1. **★ Can it clear the December 7, 2026 Exit-Notes maturity?** The binding risk. Watch for a refinancing, the "LPLUS" uplisting (a Monarch covenant) enabling a capital raise, or a covenant waiver — vs a forced restructuring. (Trigger: any 8-K / 10-Q financing or maturity-extension disclosure.)
2. **Does the AI/data-center pipeline convert to a signed order?** Currently "nothing signed." A first named datacenter order (or an 800V DC / Energy Surplus commercial contract) is the trigger to revisit the `energy_power_tier 4` placement and the thesis fit. (Trigger: an announced datacenter award.)
3. **Oil & gas rental volatility vs diversification.** The rental fleet's utilization swings with oil prices (a FY2026 second-half headwind). Does datacenter / CHP / ports diversification reduce the oil-price sensitivity? (Trigger: rental revenue + utilization by segment.)
4. **Can positive operating cash flow be sustained?** FY2026 operating cash flow was negative $(2.5)M despite the net-income turn (working-capital build). Does it turn positive as the business scales? (Trigger: cash-flow statement each quarter.)
5. **800V DC / Energy Surplus pilot → commercial.** Progress from lab/pilot to a signed proof-of-concept; the 5 ppm combustion liner (targeted end-of-calendar-year) and Enhanced Heat Recovery Module (targeted commercial ~September). (Trigger: product-readiness / customer-pilot disclosures.)

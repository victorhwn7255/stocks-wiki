# Fabrinet (FN)

## Thesis role

FN occupies Layer 6 (scale manufacturing & materials) per `frameworks.md` v7 and Photonics Tier 4 (differentiated components / good indirect exposure). Fabrinet is a pure optical contract manufacturer with no in-house products — Layer 6 is the structurally correct placement; the absence of product design is the feature, not a bug, for customer relationships. Second Layer 6 vault page (after [[AXTI]]); Layer 6 representation now spans substrate manufacturing ([[AXTI]] InP/GaAs/Ge substrates) and contract manufacturing (FN optical assembly) at structurally distinct chokepoint relevance.

This page covers Fabrinet through the lens of AI datacenter photonics manufacturability. Industrial laser, automotive, medical devices, and other non-AI end markets are excluded per scope discipline; references where AI-datacenter-adjacent.

**Manufacturability stress-test framing.** FN's analytical contribution to the vault is structurally distinct from prior thesis-position ingests. FN is a stress-test instrument for AI optical capacity scaling — does the contract-manufacturer side of the supply chain confirm or challenge the laser-supplier scaling thesis backed by [[NVDA]]'s $4B March 2026 equity in [[LITE]] + [[COHR]]? Session 21 primary-source findings materially refine the Session 19 Tier 3 framing of FN as "central [[LITE]] and [[COHR]] partner": FN's actual top customers are NVIDIA (27.6% FY2025) and Cisco (18.2%); LITE was 15.4% in FY2023 only and dropped below the 10% threshold for FY2024-FY2025; COHR is not in FN's customer concentration table. The manufacturability stress-test is real but the customer center is NVDA + Cisco + emerging AWS HPC, not LITE/COHR.

**Kickoff hypothesis falsification — Layer 5 expectation.** The Session 21 kickoff anticipated FN as "first dedicated Layer 5 contract manufacturing standalone coverage." This is a kickoff drafting error: `frameworks.md` v7 explicitly places FN at Layer 6 in the Framework 2 diagram ("FN — optical contract manufacturing" under Scale Manufacturing). Layer 5 in the canonical framework is reserved for "integrated systems" (AAOI, ANET, DELL, HPE) — finished commercial products, not contract manufacturing. Per CLAUDE.md `frameworks.md` ownership convention, frameworks.md is canonical; Layer 6 placement is correct. Documented as kickoff drafting discipline gap; surfaced in Phase 4 reflection.

## Layer placement reasoning (Layer 6)

Layer 6 (scale manufacturing & materials per `frameworks.md` v7) fits cleanly across multiple structural dimensions:

1. **Pure contract manufacturer.** Grady (Q2 FY2026 call): "We don't have any of our own products. That's actually a positive for many of our customers. They don't want us to have our own products." Absence of in-house product design is a structural moat — customers do not perceive FN as a competitive threat. This is the structural reason FN is Layer 6 not Layer 4 (component supplier).

2. **Margin profile — Layer 6 thin-margin scale economics.** FY2025 GAAP gross margin 12.1% ($413.3M on $3,419.4M); Q2 FY2026 non-GAAP gross margin 12.4%; Q2 non-GAAP operating margin 10.9%. Below Layer 4 differentiated component supplier ranges (LITE non-GAAP GM ~42.5%; COHR ~39%; AEHR ~36.5%; ONTO ~50%) and below Layer 5 integrated systems supplier ranges (AAOI non-GAAP GM ~31%). Manufacturing-scale economics rather than IP/process-driven margins.

3. **Multi-competitor landscape.** 10-K Item 1 names competitors: Benchmark Electronics, Celestica Inc., InnoLight Technology (Suzhou) Ltd., Jabil Inc., Sanmina Corporation, Venture Corporation Limited. Customers also have internal manufacturing capabilities. No monopoly position; differentiation via customer relationships, capacity, and execution rather than IP moat.

4. **No Layer 4/5 straddling.** Distinguishes from [[AAOI]] (Layer 4/5 straddling via in-house InP/GaAs MBE+MOCVD laser manufacturing alongside Layer 5 transceiver assembly). FN does no in-house design or material production — pure contract manufacturing.

## Photonics tier reasoning (Tier 4)

Photonics Tier 4 (differentiated components and good indirect exposure) per Framework 5:

- **Indirect AI exposure** via OEM customers: NVDA (Tier 1 platform definer), Cisco (Tier 4), Lumentum (Tier 3), Infinera/Nokia (telecom), AWS via HPC product line (hyperscaler direct).
- **Not chokepoint participant.** Six+ named optical contract manufacturers compete; FN is differentiated but not monopolistic. Customer concentration of 46% top-2 (NVDA + Cisco) is real but multi-customer-distributed, not single-customer-dominant.
- **Thailand-concentration risk** (89% of long-lived assets in Thailand) is a single-jurisdiction supply chain dependency comparable to [[AXTI]]'s 100% China manufacturing — noted in 10-K Risk Factors but does not elevate Tier classification because Thailand operational continuity has not historically been disrupted.

Distinguishes from [[AXTI]] (Layer 6 + photonics_tier 3 via InP substrate chokepoint position) because FN is downstream and substitutable, not chokepoint-positioned.

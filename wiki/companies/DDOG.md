# DDOG — Datadog, Inc.

## Thesis role

Datadog is a cloud **observability + security** platform — infrastructure monitoring, APM, logs, security, and (increasingly) AI/LLM observability — sold on a **usage-based (consumption) billing** model. It is an application-layer *consumer* of AI compute, not a supplier of any datacenter-supply-chain component, so it follows the demand-side placement precedent of [[MSFT]] and [[NOW]]: `layer: outside` + `outside` on all five per-domain tiers. It captures no supply-side chokepoint rent.

**Why it's in the vault — two specific jobs.** (1) **The best consumption-side demand dial in the vault.** Because Datadog bills on actual cloud/AI workload activity, its revenue is a near-real-time thermometer on whether the AI buildout is being *used* — the 2022 precedent is the point: when customers optimized cloud spend, Datadog's growth halved within quarters. That makes it an early-warning sensor for [[AI-demand-durability]], [[what-could-go-wrong]], and [[telecom-bust-analog]]. (2) **The 4th [[software-AI-moat-durability]] scorecard name ingested at primary** (after [[MSFT]] S112, [[NOW]] S113, [[PLTR]] S114) — and the **first pure-consumption archetype**, the cleanest test of the theme's "Durable / amplified" consumption verdict. Per honest-verdict discipline (Section 2.1), its fit to the supply-chain thesis is weak by design; this page is verdict-oriented around those two jobs.

**Placement framing.** Like [[NOW]], Datadog sits off the supply-side value-capture ladder entirely — an application-layer SaaS that *rents* the infrastructure the vault tracks downstream. It carries a genuine interconnection map, though (richer than NOW): a **custom-silicon heterogeneity tailwind** (→ [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]]), a hyperscaler-as-infrastructure/partner/competitor dynamic, and a consolidation contest with [[CSCO]]'s Splunk — see the interconnection section. The **`frameworks.md` application-layer gap** flagged by [[MSFT]] and [[NOW]] — for which DDOG was the fourth instance — is now **RESOLVED (codified S189, 2026-07-19): CLAUDE.md Section 3.21 + frameworks.md Framework 10.1** (app-layer-consumer sub-role), with Vic's looser inclusion bound. `_thesis.md` unchanged.

**Research-goal verdict (the scorecard verdict, validated at primary):** **DURABLE / AMPLIFIED — held and strengthened.** The scorecard's pre-registered single falsifier — *"$100k+ customer adds decelerating with NRR compression"* — did **not** fire; the opposite happened (see Consumption dial). Datadog is the primary-source confirmation of the consumption end of the theme's billed-unit spectrum.

## The consumption dial — the primary-confirmed demand signal (the page's centerpiece)

This is the reason Datadog matters most to the vault. Q1 2026 was an unusually strong, primary-confirmed demand read:

- **The strongest sequential existing-customer usage growth since Q1 2022.** CFO Obstler: the $53M quarter-over-quarter revenue add was a Q1 record and "included the strongest quarter of sequential usage growth from existing customers since the first quarter of 2022." Because Datadog bills on usage, this is direct evidence that deployed AI/cloud infrastructure is being *consumed* — the demand-side validation the vault's supply chain ultimately depends on.
- **★ The base is broadening, not narrowing.** Revenue growth **excluding the AI-native customers** re-accelerated to **mid-20s% (from 23% last quarter, 19% a year ago)** — i.e., the acceleration is not just AI-startup concentration; the core cloud-migration base is speeding up. This is the single most reassuring signal on the page for the "is the buildout being used broadly" question.
- **The falsifier did not fire.** The [[software-AI-moat-durability]] scorecard pre-registered *"$100k+ customer adds decelerating with NRR compression"* as what would break the Durable verdict. At primary the opposite happened: **NRR re-accelerated to low-120s%** (from ~120), $100k+ customers grew **3,610 → 4,550**, $1M+ grew 462 → 603. Verdict held and strengthened.
- **The agentic-consumption thesis, primary-sourced.** Pomel: *"we're usage-based, it doesn't really matter where the usage is coming from... we see both a stratospheric increase of agent usage"* — usage bills whether a human or an AI agent drives it, so agent proliferation is a tailwind to the billed unit itself.

**The honest counterweight (the dial cuts both ways).** The same usage-based model that makes Datadog a clean demand sensor makes its revenue volatile: the **2022 "cloud optimization" precedent** shows growth can halve within quarters when customers throttle spend. The demand dial is only bullish while usage is rising — a pullback would show here first, which is exactly why it is worth tracking.

## AI-native cohort concentration + the feature-or-market test

The counterweight to the bullish tape is concentration, and Datadog's own filing is candid about it.

- **10-K disclosure (verbatim):** *"Larger customers in our AI-native cohort, which cohort includes our largest customer and represented approximately seven percentage points of our year-over-year revenue growth for the quarter ended December 31, 2025, have rapidly increased their usage of our product and then optimized or may in the future optimize their usage or fail to renew their subscriptions."* So the AI-native cohort — **which includes the largest customer** — drove ~7 percentage points of Q4 2025 growth. The cohort is "the leading companies in foundational models, code-gen tools, and vertical-specific AI solutions" and now includes **22 customers spending >$1M and 5 spending >$10M** annually (call). The two largest Q1 land deals were a seven-figure and an eight-figure deal with "the AI research divisions at two of the world's largest technology companies" (disclosed as such; the specific names are **not disclosed** — inferable at best, kept unnamed).
- **Management is sandbagging the concentration.** The guidance applies **"a higher degree of conservatism to our largest customer"** — a direct acknowledgment that the AI-native usage could optimize or not renew, the 2022-style risk applied to a single name.
- **★ The feature-or-market test — disclosure advanced, but the question stays contested.** The [[AI-implementation-deployment-layer]] page pre-registered the trigger *"discloses AI cohort >10% of revenue (would confirm separability)."* Datadog has **advanced** from the Q4-2025 posture of *declining to size the AI cohort* — it now discloses the cohort's **growth contribution (~7pts), that it includes the largest customer, and customer-count granularity** — but it still does **not** disclose a clean AI-cohort revenue *level* as a % of total, and it folds LLM Observability into span-based consumption billing rather than pricing it as a standalone market. So the trigger is **partially, not fully, met**: AI observability's separability as a market remains **contested**.

## Billed-unit lens & the interconnection map

**The billed-unit discriminator (the research-goal contribution).** Across the four scorecard names now ingested at primary, the theme's billed-unit spectrum spans **seat** ([[MSFT]] Copilot) → **seat-converting-to-consumption** ([[NOW]]) → **deal/outcome** ([[PLTR]]) → **pure consumption** (DDOG). Datadog is the cleanest **usage-based "Durable / amplified"** archetype: the billed unit *rises* with AI/agent activity rather than being eroded by it, and the falsifier test (above) passed at primary.

**★ The custom-silicon heterogeneity tailwind (a clean vault interconnection).** As hyperscalers deploy heterogeneous custom silicon — AWS Trainium/Graviton, Google TPUs, Microsoft Maia — monitoring mixed fleets gets harder, and "traditional monitoring tools fail on the custom silicon" (analyst framing on the call). Pomel: *"the heterogeneity of the silicon is definitely a trend that plays in our favor."* Datadog launched **GPU Monitoring** and is now expanding from inference-monitoring into **training** as a market: *"we started landing customers that are actually hyperscalers... using us specifically in their superintelligence labs to help monitor their workloads, accelerate the training runs, monitor the GPUs."* This makes Datadog a picks-and-shovels beneficiary of the same silicon fragmentation the vault tracks at [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] — the more heterogeneous the compute, the more valuable a neutral cross-fleet observability layer.

**Hyperscaler = infrastructure + partner + competitor.** Datadog runs its platform substantially on third-party cloud infrastructure (AWS, implicit) — a hosting-dependence risk flagged in Item 1A — and competes with the hyperscalers' native tools (CloudWatch, Azure Monitor), the standing **in-housing risk**. But a Q1 data point cuts the other way: Datadog is now **winning hyperscaler business** — *"we landed some large parts of hyperscalers... hyperscalers typically have a culture of building everything themselves... yet they come to us to replace some of the things they were using before"* (Pomel). Build-vs-buy is tilting toward buy even at the companies best equipped to build.

**The consolidation moat vs [[CSCO]]'s Splunk + open-source.** Datadog's growth engine is customers consolidating fragmented tool stacks (4 / 6 / 15 / 25 separate tools across business units) onto one platform — replacing legacy log/APM vendors (Splunk, now part of [[CSCO]]), point solutions, and unsustainable open-source stacks. Platform adoption deepens: **56% of customers use 4+ products, 35% use 6+, 20% use 8+** (26 products total). The moat is breadth + unification, not any single product.

## Products, competition & disclosure

Twenty-six products across one reportable segment: infrastructure monitoring, APM, log management (incl. Flex Logs), RUM/synthetics, database/network monitoring, Cloud SIEM + cloud security (CSPM/CWS/ASM), and the AI suite — **LLM Observability, GPU Monitoring, Bits AI SRE / Security Analyst / Assistant, MCP Server**. Named competitors span Dynatrace, New Relic, Grafana, Elastic, Splunk ([[CSCO]]), and the hyperscaler-native tools. Q1 milestones: **FedRAMP High** certification (opens federal-agency sensitive workloads), a planned UK data center, and the DASH user conference (June). International ~29% of revenue (modestly contracting). Dual-class structure keeps voting control with the founders (Pomel).

## Open questions

1. **★ Does Datadog ever disclose a clean AI-cohort revenue % (the separability trigger)?** It advanced to sizing the cohort's *growth contribution* (~7pts) but not its revenue *level* — a clean line would confirm AI observability is a separable market and resolve the [[AI-implementation-deployment-layer]] contest. (Trigger: an AI-cohort revenue % in a filing.)
2. **Does the largest-customer / AI-native concentration optimize?** The demand dial's own downside — a 2022-style usage pullback would show here first, and management's "extra conservatism to our largest customer" flags the risk. (Trigger: sequential usage deceleration; the AI-native cohort's growth contribution turning down.)
3. **Usage-growth durability as the buildout thermometer.** Q1 was the strongest existing-customer usage growth since Q1 2022 — does the broadening (ex-AI-native mid-20s%) hold, validating the consumption leg, or fade? (Trigger: the ex-AI-native growth rate + net-new-usage adds each quarter.)
4. **Hyperscaler in-housing vs Datadog-winning-hyperscalers.** Does the "hyperscalers come to us" dynamic persist, or do CloudWatch/Azure Monitor bundling and hyperscaler-native observability erode it? (Trigger: hyperscaler-customer commentary; competitive win/loss.)
5. **SBC / dual-class dilution trajectory.** GAAP profit stays thin under heavy SBC; does the GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap narrow as the business scales? (Trigger: SBC as % of revenue; diluted share-count growth.)

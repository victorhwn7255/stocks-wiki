# OUST — Ouster, Inc.

## Thesis role

OUST is the third US name off the [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] value-layer map (camera/LiDAR layer, ingest-priority #3 — the "higher-beta optionality with vision-vs-LiDAR route risk"). It is **structurally different from the two `equipment_tier 4` sensor names** [[NOVT]] and [[VPG]]: OUST is a **loss-making but well-capitalized, pure Physical-AI / robotics sensing platform** (LiDAR + the Feb-2026 Stereolabs camera-vision acquisition) with **zero AI-datacenter exposure** — "data center" appears nowhere in the 10-K, and its sensors go into robots, vehicles, drones, and smart infrastructure, not the semiconductor/datacenter tool chain. So OUST lands **outside the AI-datacenter frameworks, theme-anchored to the humanoid value chain** — the [[INOD]] / [[PLTR]] "in the vault for a theme, honestly outside the AI-datacenter supply chain" pattern, not the NOVT/VPG equipment-component pattern.

**What OUST is.** A 3D sensing and perception company built on proprietary digital LiDAR silicon (the custom L4 system-on-chip), now expanded into AI camera-vision via the **Stereolabs acquisition (February 2026)** and repositioned as, in management's words, *"the foundational sensing and perception platform for Physical AI"* (Pacala, OUST Q1 2026 call). Single reportable segment. FY2025 revenue $169.4M (+52%), Q1 2026 $48.6M (+49%) — strong top-line growth off a small base, but the company remains **loss-making** (FY2025 net loss $(60.4)M; Q1 2026 $(17.5)M) while holding **~$174M of cash and investments and no debt**.

**Why it's in the vault — the humanoid/Physical-AI camera/LiDAR layer.** OUST is the US-listed camera/LiDAR name in Goldman's humanoid enabler map. Goldman scores camera/LiDAR at the **bottom of its value-capture ranking (12, "mostly ready"/commoditizing)** — and that is the honest framing here: OUST's sensors are real Physical-AI building blocks, but the layer commoditizes and faces the **vision-vs-LiDAR route risk** (if a Tesla-style vision-only approach wins, the LiDAR TAM shrinks). OUST's own hedge against that risk is to fuse modalities — native-color LiDAR (Rev 8) + Stereolabs stereo cameras + edge AI compute — into one perception stack.

**Placement — outside the AI-datacenter frameworks, theme-anchored humanoid.**
- **`layer: outside` + all five AI `*_tier: outside`.** OUST is not in the AI-datacenter supply chain — no datacenter, semiconductor-equipment, memory, energy, or materials exposure. Its vault relevance is entirely the [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] theme. Per the explicit-assessment convention ([[INOD]] / [[PLTR]] / [[MSFT]] / [[NOW]]), this is recorded as `outside` (assessed and doesn't fit), not omitted. A numeric `equipment_tier` (the [[NOVT]]/[[VPG]] call) was **considered and rejected** — those names supply differentiated components *into AI-datacenter equipment*; OUST has no such slice.
- **`photonics_tier: outside` (not absent).** OUST is a genuine optical/photonics company — custom LiDAR silicon, light engines, native-color sensing fused via Fujifilm color science — but it is **3D-sensing photonics, not datacenter-interconnect photonics** (no transceivers / CPO / optical interconnect). Assessed-and-weak-fit → honest-verdict `outside` (the [[NOVT]] laser precedent).
- **`defense_tier: outside`.** OUST has a real but diffuse government/defense thread — NDAA-compliant manufacturing centers, a U.S. Department of Defense autonomous-earthmoving retrofit project, perimeter/counter-intrusion security (Smart Infrastructure), and **drone-payload LiDAR** (Skydio and Freefly are Rev 8 adopters). Genuine and drone-*adjacent*, but not a focused unmanned-systems play → `outside` (assessed; real exposure; weak focused-fit), the [[VPG]] treatment.
- **No `humanoid_tier`** (held under YAGNI) → theme-anchored. Humanoid is a **named but unsized** "high-growth use case" (10-K) within a broader robotics / automotive / smart-infrastructure platform — the *least* quantified humanoid exposure of the three sensor-layer names, but the most central Physical-AI *positioning*.
- **`foreign_issuer: false`** — Ouster, Inc. is a **Delaware corporation** (it domesticated from the Colonnade Acquisition Corp. Cayman SPAC at the March 2021 merger); HQ San Francisco; 10-K/10-Q; US GAAP; USD. Clean — no edge case.

**Period discipline.** OUST runs a **clean calendar fiscal year** (FY2025 ended December 31, 2025; Q1 2026 ended March 31, 2026) — not a 52/53-week filer, so **Section 2.11 N/A** (joins the calendar-Dec names). **Section 2.1 N/A** — single reportable segment, stable; the Stereolabs acquisition is a business combination *within* the single segment, not a segment-reporting shift (though it affects Q1 comparability — ~7 weeks of Stereolabs are in the +49% revenue).

## The Physical-AI sensing platform (Stereolabs + Rev 8)

The Q1 story was a strategic expansion from "LiDAR maker" to "multi-modal perception platform":
- **Stereolabs acquisition (Feb 4, 2026)** — AI camera-vision + neural-depth + edge AI compute (the ZED camera line; ZED X Nano for wrist-mounted robotic manipulation). Management frames it as completing a "structure + color" sensing stack for Physical AI; integration was ahead of plan, contributing ~7 weeks in Q1.
- **Rev 8** — "the world's first native color LiDAR," on the next-gen in-house L4 silicon (up to 256 channels; native-color via Fujifilm color science). Positioned for functional-safety (auto-grade) deployments and to "generate the petabytes of native-color 3D data to train Physical-AI world models." Integrated across the **NVIDIA Jetson** robotics ecosystem (Isaac Sim, Jetson AGX Orin/Thor).
- **Adopters named (Rev 8):** Google, Volvo Autonomous Solutions, Liebherr, Epiroc, FieldAI, Flyability, Skydio, PlusAI, Constellis, Gecko Robotics, Pratt Miller, Cyngn, Freefly Systems, **ATI Industrial Automation** (the [[NOVT]] subsidiary — a cross-link between two vault sensor-layer names), SwarmFarm Robotics, among others.

Per Section 3.3 rhetorical-vs-factual: the "Physical AI platform / paradigm shift / world's first" language is management framing and is attributed, not adopted. The factual core is real product momentum (13 straight quarters of product-revenue growth; record LiDAR units) against persistent losses.

## End markets and the humanoid use-case

OUST sells across four Physical-AI verticals (FY2025 / Q1 2026):
- **Smart Infrastructure** — largest Q1 contributor; BlueCity 3D-LiDAR traffic management/ITS + perimeter security; 700+ contracted site deployments (Georgia DOT, FIFA World Cup prep); federal/state/local government.
- **Industrial** — second-largest; port automation, autonomous earthmoving (incl. the DoD project), warehouse automation.
- **Robotics** — autonomous delivery, manipulation, precision agriculture; the home of the **humanoid** use-case.
- **Automotive** — ADAS + robotaxi (Motional); Rev 8 auto-grade (ASIL B) for the longer-term opportunity.

**Humanoid** is named in the 10-K as a "new high-growth use case" enabled by the Stereolabs camera-vision addition, and the call cites demand from "companies building foundational AI models and advanced robotics platforms… imitation and reinforcement learning for manipulation." But OUST does **not size** humanoid revenue — it is one robotics application among several, with automotive and smart infrastructure currently far larger. Per the [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] needle-mover screen, OUST is a Physical-AI/robotics platform with humanoid as unsized optionality, not a humanoid pure-play.

## Open questions

1. **Vision-vs-LiDAR route risk.** OUST is the LiDAR bet; the Stereolabs camera + native-color Rev 8 are a hedge toward fusion. Does LiDAR retain a durable role in Physical AI, or does vision-only erode the TAM? Central to the whole camera/LiDAR layer.
2. **Path to profitability.** Management targets profitability "within 2027" on 30–50% growth / 35–40% gross margin. Track the adj-EBITDA trajectory ($(7)M Q1) and whether the Stereolabs opex is absorbed.
3. **Royalty durability.** FY2025 included $22.8M of IP-license royalties (zero-cost, GM-inflating); FY2026 royalties guided to <$5M. Strip royalties when assessing the underlying product business.
4. **Customer concentration.** One customer was ~31% of Q1 2026 revenue — verify diversification as the platform scales.
5. **Humanoid sizing.** Does OUST begin disclosing humanoid/robotics revenue, or does it remain an unsized use-case folded into Robotics? Trigger to revisit theme-anchoring.

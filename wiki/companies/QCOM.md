# QCOM — QUALCOMM Incorporated

## Thesis role

Qualcomm is an **outside-framework counterpoint** whose real vault value is its **interconnection density**, not its thesis fit. The vault pre-scoped this exactly: a QCOM ingest is "a weak-fit counterpoint page (the [[INOD]]/[[PLAB]] pattern), not a strong-thesis one" ([[training-to-inference-shift]]), and Qualcomm is the named, uncovered **edge-inference-silicon coverage gap** ([[physical-AI-value-chain]]). This page closes both — placed `layer: outside` + all five `*_tier: outside`, deliberately verdict-like, with the honest spine below and a cross-vault **interconnection map** as its centerpiece.

Four things carry the page:

1. **Core = handset modems + patent licensing, under the Apple-modem cliff.** FY2025 revenue $44.3B splits into **QCT** (the chip business, $38.4B) and **QTL** (patent royalties, $5.6B at a 72% pre-tax margin — the high-margin engine). Apple is the **largest customer (~21% of revenue)** *and* the structural threat: the 10-K states Apple's shift to its own modem "will have a **significant negative impact on our QCT revenues**, results of operations and cash flows" (QCOM FY2025 10-K). Management now models QCT-Apple revenue at only ~$2B in FY2027, with the QTL royalty held pending renegotiation (Q2 FY2026 call).
2. **Automotive is the proven diversification win.** Auto crossed **$5B annualized for the first time** in Q2 FY2026 (+38% YoY, guided to a ~$6B exit run-rate and ~50% Q3 growth), on the Snapdragon Digital Chassis (Ride ADAS/AD, >1M cars) with BMW / Bosch / Wayve (Q2 FY2026 call). Autonomous-vehicle adjacent.
3. **Real-but-early AI optionality — the interconnection driver.** A datacenter custom-silicon re-entry (for a "leading hyperscaler," Alphawave-enabled, first shipments the Dec-2026 quarter), an agentic-CPU-orchestration thesis (the Oryon CPU), and robotics compute (Figure AI / NEURA; the Dragonwing IQ10 at 700 TOPS). This is what makes QCOM a rich cross-vault node — but it is early optionality, not proven revenue.
4. **Overhangs:** China is **46% of revenue** (Chinese-OEM concentration + a Chinese-competitor / self-sufficiency threat + Huawei already cut off), and the QTL licensing model faces periodic renegotiation risk.

**Outside-Framework placement justification (Section 3.10 four-criterion test).** (1) Qualcomm discloses **no supply-side chokepoint role** — it is a fabless designer that *consumes* leading-edge foundry, memory, and packaging. (2) Its AI exposure appears as broad edge/agentic-device enumeration, not a served chokepoint segment. (3) Its core is off-thesis consumer/handset silicon; the datacenter/edge-AI is broad-cycle and early. (4) Concentration is customer-generic (Apple/Samsung/Xiaomi). Edge-inference silicon — where QCOM competes — is the layer [[physical-AI-value-chain]] ranks *least durable and most contested*. So `outside` is honest-verdict-correct; the datacenter custom-silicon, IF it converts to material revenue, is the pre-registered trigger to revisit (Open questions).

**Section 2.11 period-parity.** Fiscal year ends late September (Sept 28, 2025 for FY2025) — a month+ offset, so Section 2.11 applies. **Q2 FY2026 = quarter ended March 29, 2026 ≈ calendar Q1 2026**, period-parity clean with [[AAPL]] / [[NVDA]] / [[ARM]] calendar-Q1-2026 disclosures.

## Ecosystem & cross-vault interconnections

The reason Qualcomm earns a page despite weak thesis fit: it sits at an unusually dense set of vault intersections — customer, supplier, licensor, litigant, and competitor all at once.

- **★ [[ARM]] — the richest link, triple-stranded.** (1) *Licensor* — Qualcomm builds its custom **Oryon** CPU cores on an Arm architecture license. (2) *Litigant* — the Arm–Nuvia dispute (Arm sued in Aug 2022 alleging Qualcomm breached Nuvia's architecture license after acquiring Nuvia). Per the QCOM FY2025 10-K, a jury found for Qualcomm (Dec 2024) and on **Sept 30, 2025 the court entered a final judgment in Qualcomm's favor** — Oryon-bearing CPUs are licensed under the Qualcomm ALA, and Arm's remaining claims were dismissed — but **Arm filed a notice of appeal to the Third Circuit on Oct 1, 2025**, so the matter is now on appeal. (This updates the [[ARM]] page, which carried it as "still pending.") (3) *New competitor* — Arm is vertically integrating into its own datacenter "AGI CPU" just as Qualcomm re-enters the datacenter CPU market, so the two are now rivals as well.
- **★ [[AAPL]] — customer and existential threat at once.** Apple is Qualcomm's largest customer (~21% of revenue) for modems, and simultaneously the clearest structural risk: Apple is designing Qualcomm out with its own in-house modem, which the 10-K says will "significantly" impair QCT. The bidirectional customer↔competitor dynamic is the single most commercially material QCOM relationship.
- **[[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] — a new (early) entrant.** Qualcomm is re-entering the datacenter as a **custom-silicon provider for a "leading hyperscaler"** (initial shipments the Dec-2026 quarter; a datacenter CPU + AI-inference accelerators), built on its **Alphawave** acquisition (224G SerDes / connectivity IP). This puts Qualcomm alongside [[AVGO]]/[[MRVL]] as a merchant custom-ASIC partner to the hyperscalers — a genuinely new strand, still unproven (Q2 FY2026 call; the hyperscaler is unnamed pending Qualcomm's June-2026 investor day).
- **[[physical-AI-value-chain]] — fills the flagged edge-inference gap + robotics.** Qualcomm was the named, uncovered edge-inference-silicon challenger; this page closes that gap. On the robotics leg it has design wins with **Figure AI** and a multi-year **NEURA** agreement, plus the **Dragonwing IQ10** platform (700 TOPS, 18-core Oryon) aimed at robotics/industrial AI.
- **[[AI-agentic-CPU-orchestration-reemergence]] — directly on-theme.** Management's central Q2 framing: "agent orchestration is predominantly **CPU-bound**, and Qualcomm has the world's best-performing CPU" (Oryon across phone/PC/auto/datacenter). That is the theme's thesis, expressed by a new participant.
- **[[TSM]] (+ Samsung, GlobalFoundries) — foundry.** Qualcomm fabs Snapdragon at TSMC on the leading edge (Samsung + GlobalFoundries secondary). A large-but-not-top-3 TSMC customer; validates the leading-edge-anchor moat from the demand side.
- **[[NVDA]] — competitor across three fronts.** Edge (Snapdragon vs Jetson), datacenter (Qualcomm's datacenter CPU + inference vs NVIDIA), and automotive (Snapdragon Ride vs NVIDIA DRIVE).
- **[[china-exposure]] — 46% of revenue.** Chinese-OEM concentration (Xiaomi/Oppo/Vivo/Honor/Transsion), the Chinese-competitor / semiconductor-self-sufficiency threat, and export-control exposure (Huawei already cut off). One of the vault's higher China-revenue concentrations.
- **Samsung** (no vault page) — a >70%-modem-share customer (a "reset" framework management calls its most stable), *and* a foundry, *and* a competitor (Exynos). **Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI, Perplexity, HUMAIN** are named as agentic orchestrators running on Snapdragon (ecosystem context, not vault pages).

## Diversification vectors (beyond the modem base)

- **Automotive** — the proven one (above): Snapdragon Digital Chassis, $5B→$6B annualized, BMW/Bosch/Wayve.
- **Datacenter re-entry** — the custom-silicon-for-a-hyperscaler play + a datacenter CPU + AI-inference accelerators (Alphawave-enabled); early, unproven, the key tier-upgrade watch item.
- **PC / Windows-on-Arm** — Snapdragon X2 (Oryon), positioned for on-device agentic AI; management claims a ~30% lead over Intel Panther Lake and the fastest laptop NPU.
- **IoT / edge AI** — $6.6B FY2025 (+22%); the agentic-device upgrade-cycle thesis.

## Open questions

1. **The Apple-modem cliff.** How fast does QCT-Apple revenue decline toward the ~$2B FY2027 assumption, and does the QTL royalty survive renegotiation intact? The dominant structural risk.
2. **Does the datacenter custom-silicon convert?** Initial shipments are guided for the Dec-2026 quarter to an unnamed hyperscaler — if it becomes material recurring revenue, that is the pre-registered trigger to revisit the `outside` placement (and a real [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] entry). Watch Qualcomm's June-2026 investor day.
3. **China (46% of revenue).** Does the Chinese-Android bottom hold, and how fast does the Chinese-competitor / self-sufficiency threat erode Qualcomm's share?
4. **QTL licensing durability.** The 72%-margin engine funds everything; periodic renegotiations and OEM royalty-avoidance are the standing risk.
5. **Automotive margin.** As auto shifts chip→SiP→software, does margin hold at the corporate average (management's claim)?

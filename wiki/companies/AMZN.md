# AMZN — Amazon.com, Inc.

## Thesis role

Amazon is the vault's **3rd hyperscaler page** (after [[MSFT]] + [[GOOGL]]) and **5th demand-side page**, the third ingest under the hyperscaler PAYER / SPENDER tracking program — the companies that *fund* the AI-datacenter buildout the rest of the vault tracks downstream (program scope: [[MSFT]] + GOOGL + AMZN + META + ORCL + CRWV). It is ingested for three purposes: (1) the **direct hyperscaler-CAPEX / AI-datacenter-expansion signal** (AWS) feeding [[AI-demand-durability]]; (2) as a **custom-silicon operator** (Trainium / Inferentia / Graviton) for [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] and [[AI-agentic-CPU-orchestration-reemergence]]; and (3) as the **buyer-side counterparty** to the "Amazon NV Investment Holdings" supplier-warrant pattern the vault already documents from five supplier pages (see Vault touchpoint). Like the other hyperscalers — and unlike [[MSFT]]/[[NOW]]/[[PLTR]] — it is *not* on the [[software-AI-moat-durability]] application-layer scorecard.

**Placement framing (honest-verdict, Section 2.1).** A hyperscaler sits *above* Layer 1 in `frameworks.md` v10.1 — it is the **demand source** (Framework 3 dependency map) and the **CAPEX deployer** (Framework 10 allocation start), not a supply-side value-capture position. Amazon carries `layer: outside` and `outside` on all five per-domain tiers — it captures no supply-side chokepoint rent; it *funds* the chokepoints. Per the [[MSFT]]/[[GOOGL]] demand-side precedent.

**`frameworks.md` gap flag (for a future codification session; human-owned, not edited).** AMZN is the 5th demand-side page (3rd hyperscaler) to press the same gap — no dedicated "demand-side / hyperscaler / CAPEX-deployer" category, and `layer: outside` extends the documented 1–6 range. The custom-silicon-commissioner role (Amazon designs Trainium via Annapurna, fabbed at TSMC) compounds it. Raised per Section 1.1; `frameworks.md`/`_thesis.md` not edited.

## CAPEX & AI-datacenter expansion

The section this ingest exists to source — Amazon's AI-datacenter spending (cross-ref [[AI-demand-durability]] + the [[hyperscaler-capex]] payer-comparison tracker). Per the Q1 2026 10-Q + call:

- **Capex run-rate.** Q1 2026 **cash capex $43.2B** (Olsavsky; $44.2B gross purchases of P&E less ~$1.0B proceeds; +~73% YoY); **trailing-twelve-month capex $151.0B** (10-Q); FY2025 capex **$131.8B (+59%)**. The Q1 run-rate annualizes to ~$177B.
- **No precise annual capex dollar-guide — an honest distinction from [[MSFT]]/[[GOOGL]].** Unlike Microsoft (~$190B CY2026) and Alphabet ($180-190B), Amazon did **not** give a clean annual capex number. Olsavsky framed it qualitatively: "We'll continue to make significant investments, especially in AI"; "the faster AWS grows, the more short-term CapEx we'll spend." The **~$200B-2026 figure that circulates in the vault aggregate is a Tier-3 / analyst number, not an Amazon primary guide.** Amazon guides quarterly *revenue* + *operating income* (Q2 2026: net sales $194–199B), not annual capex.
- **The AI-datacenter capex framing (Jassy, primary).** "AWS has to lay out cash for **land, power, buildings, chips, servers, and networking gear** in advance of when we can monetize it, typically **six to 24 months** before we start billing." Useful lives: **30+ years for data centers**, 5–6 years for chips/servers/networking. "Of the AWS CapEx we intend to spend in 2026 … we already have **customer commitments for a substantial portion** of it … it will yield compelling operating margins and ROIC."
- **The capex-ahead-of-revenue tension (stated plainly).** Jassy: "in times of very high growth like now, where the **CapEx growth meaningfully outpaces the revenue growth**, the early years free cash flow is challenged until these initial tranches of capacity are being monetized." This is the same demand-durability *timing* risk [[MSFT]] addressed as the capex-vs-revenue "disconnect" and [[GOOGL]] via its "robust ROIC framework" — Amazon frames it most structurally of the three. Free cash flow is pressured in the build years.
- **AWS revenue backlog $364B** (Q1 2026; Jassy) — "does not include the recent deal we announced with Anthropic for over $100 billion," and has "reasonable breadth … not just one customer." Forward-demand proxy.

The AWS capex figure now primary-sources a third hyperscaler component of the ~$640-720B 2026 aggregate underlying [[AI-demand-durability]] (alongside MSFT ~$190B and GOOGL $180-190B) — though, per the honest distinction above, Amazon's is the one *not* anchored to a clean annual guide. Framework 10 (CAPEX flow) treats Amazon as a top-of-diagram demand source.

## AWS segment & AI monetization

- AWS net sales **$37.6B (+28% YoY)**, the largest Q4→Q1 sequential AWS increase ever; ~$150B run rate; AWS operating income $14.2B (37.7% margin).
- **AI revenue "growing triple digits YoY"**; Bedrock a significant growth driver; "bringing more capacity online to meet high customer demand while driving efficiency gains across the installed base."
- **Bedrock Managed Agents powered by OpenAI** (preview) — a stateful agentic runtime; Amazon frames "modern agentic applications will be stateful."
- New AWS agreements since the prior quarter span OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, NVIDIA, Uber, U.S. Bank, Fox, Southwest, U.S. Army, Bloomberg, Cerebras, AT&T, Nokia (Jassy). No discrete Tier-1 "AI revenue" line — embedded in AWS (see Source audit notes).

## Custom silicon — Trainium + Graviton

Amazon's in-house silicon (Annapurna-designed, TSMC-fabbed) is the largest custom-ASIC commitment story in the vault. Cross-ref [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]].

- **Trainium: "over $225 billion in revenue commitments"** (Jassy) — the largest single custom-silicon commitment figure in the vault, with "very large multi-year, multi-gigawatt training commitments from the two leading AI labs … **Anthropic and OpenAI**," plus Uber and others.
- **Roadmap, all supply-constrained:** Trainium2 ~30% better price-performance than comparable GPUs, "largely sold out"; **Trainium3** shipping start of 2026 (30–40% more price-performant), "nearly fully subscribed"; **Trainium4** ~18 months out, "already been reserved."
- **The economic rationale (Jassy):** "Trainium will **save us tens of billions of dollars of CapEx each year** and provide several hundred basis points of operating margin advantage versus relying on others' chips for inference." Majority of Bedrock workloads run on Trainium; 4x Trainium2 token-throughput improvement in 2025.
- **Chips business ~$20B annual run rate, +~40% QoQ**, "growing triple digits" (distinct from Graviton).
- **Graviton** (Arm server CPU) is tracked vault-side at "$20B+ annual" per the [[ARM]] Q4 FY2026 letter (the CPU-orchestration angle on [[AI-agentic-CPU-orchestration-reemergence]]) — a *separate* line from the Trainium/AI-chips run rate above; Amazon's Q1 2026 call emphasizes Trainium, not Graviton.

## Vault touchpoint — the Amazon warrant pattern + Anthropic

Two distinct cross-vault entanglements, both visible only because AMZN is now a vault page:

**(1) The Amazon supplier-warrant pattern (buyer side).** The vault documents an "Amazon NV Investment Holdings LLC" warrant pattern from the supplier side across five pages — [[ALAB]] ($6.5B revenue-milestone warrant + prior), [[AAOI]] ($4B cumulative-purchase warrant), and [[FN]] ($39.3M, optical contract manufacturing) on [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]], plus [[FLEX]] ($198M notional warrant) and [[AAON]] (AWS as an explicitly named customer) on [[liquid-cooling]]. Amazon is the **buyer/grantor** behind all of these — exchanging equity warrants for multi-year supply commitments. The pattern remains 3-instance for the custom-ASIC reframing trigger ([[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] tracks a "4th Amazon warrant" reframing condition — not fired). With AMZN now canonical, "Amazon NV Investment Holdings" resolves to this page as the demand-side counterparty.

**(2) Anthropic (deep financial + compute entanglement).** Amazon invested **$8.0B in Anthropic convertible notes** (Q3 2023–Q4 2025; available-for-sale, fair-valued), with portions converting to nonvoting preferred stock. Subsequent to March 31 2026 Amazon invested **another $5.0B** in Anthropic nonvoting preferred, amended the commercial arrangement (AWS cloud + AWS-chips compute commitments), and established a **financing facility of up to $20.0B** for Anthropic (drawable against compute-capacity-delivery milestones; expiring 30 months after an Anthropic liquidity event), plus a $5.0B future-equity option. Amazon's private-company equity (primarily Anthropic + OpenAI preferred) carried at **$48.1B** (up from $16.2B). Anthropic is also Amazon's anchor Trainium training customer (the ">$100B" deal noted under CAPEX, and part of the $225B Trainium commitments). The **Q1 2026 net-income inflation traces directly to this** — the $12.3B upward adjustment on Anthropic preferred (see Financial snapshot). Amazon thus has the AI boom reflected twice on its own statements: as AWS demand *and* as a mark-up on its Anthropic stake.

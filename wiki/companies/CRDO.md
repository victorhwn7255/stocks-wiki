## Thesis role

CRDO occupies **Layer 3** (specialized designer) and **Photonics Tier 3** (specialized designers / high-quality differentiated suppliers). It designs SerDes- and DSP-based connectivity ICs — Active Electrical Cables (AECs), Optical PAM4 DSPs, PCIe retimers, line-card PHYs, SerDes Chiplets, ZeroFlap optics — for AI/ML, hyperscale, and HPC infrastructure. Fabless (TSMC for all IC manufacturing). The structural claim is competitor-equivalent performance "at a lower cost and more highly available legacy node (n-1 advantage)" via proprietary mixed-signal SerDes (CRDO 10-K FY2026). The wiki tracks CRDO as the third Optical-DSP/PHY primary-source after [[MRVL]] (S9) and [[AVGO]] (S10); `_thesis.md` does not list it as a position.

**The honest verdict (refreshed S171): a blow-out execution year — and the thesis question flips from "can it grow" to "does the optical pivot land, does concentration keep broadening, and is the +80% bar too high."** FY2026 revenue **$1,335.1M (+206% YoY)**, GAAP net income **$472.3M** (vs $52.2M; the accumulated deficit is wiped out), non-GAAP NI **$662M** (5×+); Q4 was a record **$437M** and *alone exceeded all of FY2025*. Operating leverage was the story — R&D + SG&A fell from 33.6% / 22.6% to **20.9% / 13.8%** of revenue. Three counterweights are louder than ever and belong up front: (1) **still concentrated** — the FY2026 10-K shows **two ≥10% customers** (Customer A 49% / Customer B 32% of revenue), and management's Q4 *end-customer* view was **four ≥10% at 34% / 27% / 16% / 10%** (top-4 ≈ 87%) — broadening from FY2025's single 67% customer, but not de-risked; (2) the **FY2027 +80% guide leans on a brand-new ~$600M optical ramp** — a fresh single-vector dependency replacing the de-risking that customer-broadening was supposed to provide; (3) a ~50%-net-margin / 3×-growth name guiding +80% again sits on a very high expectations bar (Tier-5 valuation signal, kept off-page per the thesis-tool discipline).

**Placement — HELD at Layer 3 / Photonics Tier 3, with the upgrade trigger pre-registered.** The structural position is still **bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations** (Framework 2.6). The optical pivot + the DustPhotonics silicon-photonics path strengthen the control-point case — but the optical/CPO revenue is *guided/forward* (FY2027–28), so a tier upgrade now would fire on momentum, not realized evidence (honest-verdict trigger discipline). HOLD; note the upward trajectory; pre-register the trigger (realized optical + CPO/NPO + scale-up revenue) in Open questions.

## Product portfolio, technology, and the optical pivot

The growth engine is shifting from **copper (AECs) to optical**. FY2026 was AEC-led (">99% of the revenue increase"); FY2027's >80% growth is ~half optical.

**The ~$600M FY2027 optical ramp (the new structural bet; CRDO Q4 call).** Three legs, **each >$100M**, **>$600M in total**:
- **Discrete optical DSPs** (Robin 100G/lane, Cardinal 200G/lane; 7nm; two-digit ASPs) — design wins + revenue contribution building.
- **Silicon-photonics PICs** (two-digit ASPs) — via the **DustPhotonics acquisition** (see below).
- **ZeroFlap Optics** (three-digit ASPs → the **largest** optical leg) — optical transceivers with continuous link-health telemetry + PILOT software / switch-SDK integration; "1,000× better reliability than commodity laser-based optics" (Brennan rhetorical claim). First production customer TensorWave (neocloud); ramp pulled into Q1 FY2027. ZeroFlap "owns the entire bill of materials," which CRDO leans on for supply-capacity commitments.

**The CPO/NPO path (a real step up the stack).** DustPhotonics' SiPho PIC roadmap (800G + 1.6T, to 3.2 Tb/s) "provides a **direct path to CPO and NPO architectures**," with **initial CPO/NPO revenue expected FY2028** (Brennan, Q4 call). Integrating SiPho into the ZeroFlap platform lets CRDO "control a significantly larger portion of the optical stack." This is in addition to the OmniConnect/Weaver displacement angle below — together they evolve CRDO from a CPO *displacement-claimant* to *also* a SiPho-PIC path *into* CPO/NPO. (Propagates to [[cpo-integration]] + [[CPO-platform-battle]].)

**Established + emerging copper / connectivity:**
- **HiWire AECs** — the de-facto in-rack/rack-to-rack standard up to 7m (CLOS/SPAN/SHIFT/SWITCH families); ZeroFlap AECs; 100G/lane at volume, 200G/lane emerging; **PCIe 6.0 AEC family** engagement strengthening.
- **PCIe retimers** — Gen5 shipping; **Gen6 sampling → mass production H1 FY2027** (design wins converting to revenue).
- **Blue Heron 200G/lane retimer + scale-up** — UALink / Ethernet / ESUN protocol support; scale-up revenue "begins" FY2027, "more substantial" FY2028.
- **OmniConnect / Weaver gearbox** — memory-IO-density gearbox; first customer **Positron** (a 2TB-LPDDR inference engine, ">10× any other"); revenue contribution **$2,000–3,000 per GPU**; production ramp FY2028. Future OmniConnect targets "near-package optics with MicroLED" addressing CPO reliability/serviceability pitfalls.
- **Active LED Cables (ALCs)** — mid-reach (≤30m) MicroLED optical via the **Hyperlume** acquisition; sample/qual FY2027, production FY2028.

**Foundry / supply (CRDO Q4 call).** TSMC across the node stack: **12nm** (100G/lane AECs, the workhorse) → **7nm** (optical DSPs) → **5nm** (program in flight) → **3nm** (all 200G/lane + the bulk of 1.6T). Significant industry supply tightness; CRDO manages it by "owning the entire BOM" and leaning in early for capacity. "Clusters don't get built without the small complementary connectivity chips."

**Three acquisitions — kept distinct (the §4.3 name-trap correction).** All real, all separate:
- **Hyperlume** — MicroLED for ALCs; closed **Sept 29 2025** (in FY2026).
- **CoMira Solutions** — protocol-IP design team (Ethernet/ESUN/UALink/PCIe); closed **Feb 25 2026** (in FY2026).
- **DustPhotonics** — silicon-photonics PIC; closed **late-May 2026** (a *subsequent event* — NOT in FY2026 financials).

> *Correction:* the prior page noted "Comira" as a *misidentification* of Hyperlume from the S27 kickoff. That has flipped — **CoMira Solutions is a real, separate, later acquisition** (Feb 25 2026), distinct from the Hyperlume (MicroLED) deal. Both now exist; do not merge them. Net Q1 FY2027 cash out for the deals ≈ $750M.

## Strategic positioning vs vault peers

Within Optical DSP/PHY, CRDO sits alongside [[MRVL]] (Spica, ex-Inphi) and [[AVGO]] (Sian3 3nm DSP) — and the FY2026 10-K names **Broadcom, Marvell, and Astera Labs ([[ALAB]])** as principal competitors. CRDO's distinct franchise is **HiWire AECs** (no direct vault peer; copper interconnect rather than optical-only) plus the emerging optical stack. Per Framework 2.6, CRDO joins MRVL at **bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations** — adjacent to MRVL but at smaller operational scale and without MRVL's Photonic Fabric platform integration. The DustPhotonics SiPho path narrows that gap on the optical/CPO axis (forward; FY2028 revenue). Five-company control-point thread: [[NVDA]] (full control point) · [[AVGO]] (control point + bottleneck) · [[MRVL]] / CRDO (bottleneck + control-point aspirations) · [[CSCO]] / [[ANET]] (bottleneck + platform-tier ambition).

## Risk factors summary (CRDO 10-K FY2026)

- **Customer concentration** — two customers ≥10% (A 49% / B 32%); top-10 ≈ 90%; "limited number of customers... for the foreseeable future" (easing but high).
- **Single-foundry dependency** — "we currently outsource **all** of our IC manufacturing to **TSMC**"; assembly/test via Amkor / ASE / KYEC / Sigurd (ICs) + BizLink (AECs); capacity-shortage + price/yield risk explicit.
- **Acquisition risk (NEW/elevated)** — reflecting FY2026 M&A (Hyperlume, CoMira, DustPhotonics): integration + dilution risk.
- **Tariffs / trade (expanded)** — "a dynamic and unpredictable trade landscape"; guidance assumes the current (fluid) tariff regime.
- **U.S. outbound-investment-in-China restrictions (NEW/elevated).**
- **AI/ML market dependence** — revenue tied to generative-AI infrastructure spend that "may not develop as expected."
- **Hong Kong / Asia** — now a structurally smaller concentration (28% revenue / 2% assets) but still flagged.
- **Standards-body / protocol-timing risk** — UALink / ESUN / Ultra Ethernet roadmap dependence.

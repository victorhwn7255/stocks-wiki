# CRDO — Credo Technology Group Holding

## Thesis role

CRDO occupies Layer 3 (specialized designer) in the value capture framework and Photonics Tier 3 (specialized designers and high-quality differentiated suppliers). CRDO designs SerDes and DSP-based connectivity ICs — including Active Electrical Cables (AECs), Optical PAM4 DSPs, PCIe retimers, line-card PHYs, and SerDes Chiplets — for AI/ML, hyperscale, and high-performance computing infrastructure. The company is fabless and uses contract manufacturing partners. CRDO's core technology positioning rests on its claim of achieving competitor-equivalent performance "at a lower cost and more highly available legacy node (n-1 advantage)" via proprietary mixed-signal SerDes architectures (CRDO 10-K FY2025).

`_thesis.md` does not yet list CRDO as a position. The wiki tracks CRDO as a Chokepoint 2 (Optical DSP/PHY) third primary-source after [[MRVL]] (Session 9) and [[AVGO]] (Session 10) and as a structurally relevant component supplier within the Ethernet AI networking ecosystem alongside [[ANET]] at Layer 5.

*Control-point distinction (per `raw/research/photonics-chokepoint-table.md`, Tier 3 source).* CRDO exhibits bottleneck-tier optical DSP/PHY component participation with control-point aspirations via three architectural commitments: (1) UALink, ESUN, and Ethernet protocol participation explicitly extended into the 1.6T AEC product family per Q3 FY2026 call; (2) Blue Heron 200G-per-lane retimer "purpose-built for scale of AI" with UALink/Ethernet/ESUN protocol support; (3) ZeroFlap optics positioned with embedded telemetry and pilot software / SDK integration delivering "real-time decisions identifying and mitigating potential link flaps before they happen" (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call). Per the framework: bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations. Honest-verdict framing per fourth discipline: control-point aspiration is real (vertically integrated system-level model claim; protocol-spanning product positioning; deep hyperscaler co-design) but realization is at smaller scale than [[MRVL]] Photonic Fabric (via Celestial $3.25-5.5B) — CRDO architectural authority is more limited than [[NVDA]]/[[AVGO]] but operationally substantive within Ethernet AI connectivity. Cross-reference: control-point thread spans [[NVDA]], [[AVGO]], [[MRVL]], [[CSCO]], [[ANET]] within vault per framework five-company set.

**Structural AI photonics exposure.** All revenue ties to AI/cloud datacenter connectivity. Bill Brennan: "Our recent performance reflects the most accelerated growth phase in Credo's history. From fiscal 2024 to fiscal 2025, we more than doubled revenue. From fiscal 2025 to current year fiscal 2026, we expect to triple revenue on top of that. That represents greater than 6x growth in just two years" (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call). The wiki does not endorse rhetorical claims of "best-in-class" or "industry-leading" in management voice — these are flagged as rhetorical claims per CLAUDE.md citation discipline.

**Scale context.** CRDO is mid-cap (Q3 FY2026 revenue $407M; FY2025 $436.8M; 507 engineers as of May 3, 2025). The company is profitable on non-GAAP basis with $208.8M Q3 FY2026 net income (51.3% non-GAAP net margin) and $1.3B cash & equivalents post-ATM offering completed October-December 2025 (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call).

## Product portfolio and technology

CRDO's product organization spans five primary product families plus three new TAM-expansion product categories announced over the past 12 months.

### Established product families

**HiWire AECs (Active Electrical Cables) — Credo's distinctive purple branded product line.** Plug-and-play copper interconnect cables for 100G/200G/400G/800G/emerging 1.6T data speeds. Four sub-families: CLOS (in-rack/HPC dense interconnect, up to 1,000 cables/rack), SPAN (rack-to-rack up to 7m, AOC replacement), SHIFT (breakout speed-shifting), SWITCH (dual-ToR Active/Standby for sub-ms failover). 50% lower power and 75% lower volume than alternatives. Up to 7m reach for liquid-cooled AI rack GPU-to-network-switch links. Microsoft HiWire Switch AEC partnership for dual-Top-of-Rack architecture (CRDO 10-K FY2025).

CEO Bill Brennan on AEC trajectory: "AECs are now the de facto standard for intra-rack and rack-to-rack connectivity up to seven meters, increasingly displacing laser-based optical modules. Their reliability and power advantages are driving broad adoption. Our ZeroFlap AECs deliver up to 1,000x better reliability than commodity laser-based optics, while consuming roughly half the power" (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call). The 1,000x and 50% claims are rhetorical claims attributed to Brennan.

**Optical PAM4 DSPs.** Fifth-generation 50G/100G PAM4 DSPs for optical transceivers and AOCs. Used in AI clusters, hyperscale data centers, service provider networks, enterprise networks, 5G wireless. Connectivity ranges 5m to 10km+ at 50Gb/s to 800Gb/s. Bill Brennan on Q3 FY2026 trajectory: "We expect strong optical DSP growth in fiscal 2026, driven by 100 gig per lane deployments with increasing traction at 200 gig as customers prepare for 1.6T transitions" (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call).

**PCIe retimers.** PCIe Gen5 32G shipping today; PCIe Gen6 64G "sampling now and will be released to mass production in first half fiscal 2027" (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call). Design wins in fiscal 2026 expected to convert to production revenue in fiscal 2027.

**Line Card PHYs and SerDes Chiplets.** SerDes Chiplets for Multi-Chip Module (MCM) package integration — production shipments include "two versions of 3.2Tbps Chiplets" and 112G XSR IP. SerDes IP licensing residual (3% of FY2025 revenue down from 15% FY2024).

### Three new TAM expansions (announced fiscal 2026)

**ZeroFlap Optics.** Optical transceivers with continuous link health telemetry and pilot software / switch-level SDK integration. CRDO claims "1,000x better reliability than commodity laser-based optics" and "the first time anybody has taken an optical transceiver up the stack to deliver real-time telemetry data" (Brennan rhetorical claim, CRDO Q3 FY2026 call). First production customer: TensorWave (neocloud). Three additional customers in qualification including hyperscalers and neoclouds. Production ramp pulled forward from second-half FY2027 to first-quarter FY2027 based on customer traction.

**Active LED Cables (ALCs).** Mid-reach optical (up to 30m) using MicroLED technology gained via the **Hyperlume acquisition** (NOT Comira; the kickoff prompt for Session 27 misidentified the acquisition as "Comira" — this CRDO.md page corrects per CRDO Q3 FY2026 call primary-source attribution). ALCs "extend our system-level AEC philosophy into mid-reach optical" combining connectivity architecture with MicroLED expertise. First sample/qualification expected fiscal 2027; production ramp fiscal 2028. Expands TAM "outward from short-reach copper into mid-reach optical, bridging the gap between AECs and conventional optical modules."

**OmniConnect.** Family of gearboxes for XPU connectivity using purpose-built VSR SerDes. First product is "Weaver" — "enables up to a 10x improvement in memory beachfront IO density with reach up to 10 inches. By converting SDR to DDR, Weaver overcomes the physical fan-out constraints of traditional memory-to-compute interconnects." First customer: Positron (inference XPU with 2TB memory capacity). Production ramp fiscal 2028. Future OmniConnect gearboxes targeting "near package optics with MicroLED that will address the reliability, serviceability, and availability pitfalls of current CPO solutions."

### Vertically integrated system-level model

CEO framing: "Our purpose-built SerDes and ICs, vertically integrated system model, and deep hyperscaler partnerships win at scale. We established leadership in high reliability copper connectivity and built strong position in optical DSPs and retimers. Now, our strategy is to lead in reliability, power efficiency, and signal integrity across the full spectrum of AI and data center connectivity, from die-to-die links to chip-to-chip and board-level links, to rack and row scale copper, to mid-reach optical and to resilient facility-wide optical solutions" (CRDO Q3 FY2026 call). The vertically integrated system-level model claim is the structural basis for Framework 2.6 control-point aspirations placement.

## Strategic positioning vs vault peers

Within Chokepoint 2 (Optical DSP/PHY) per Tier 3 framework, CRDO sits alongside [[MRVL]] (Spica DSP from Inphi acquisition) and [[AVGO]] (Sian3 3nm DSP). CRDO competitive positioning per framework Tier 3 source: "More high-beta challenger or adjacent connectivity play."

Differentiation candidates from primary sources:
- *AEC franchise leadership* — CRDO's HiWire AEC product line has no direct vault peer; MRVL and AVGO focus on optical DSP/PHY components with limited copper interconnect product extension. AEC franchise is structurally distinct from optical DSP business.
- *PCIe Gen 6 retimer + Blue Heron 200G/lane retimer* — purpose-built scale-AI products with UALink/Ethernet/ESUN protocol coverage. Competitive positioning vs MRVL/AVGO retimer offerings not directly comparable from CRDO sources alone.
- *Vertically integrated system-level model* — CRDO claims system-level integration as competitive advantage. MRVL Celestial Photonic Fabric is more aggressive vertical integration toward XPU control-point; CRDO system-level integration is more product-portfolio-coherence than control-point claim.
- *Mature node n-1 advantage* — CRDO operates on n-1 fabrication nodes for cost advantage. AVGO Sian3 at 3nm is leading-edge; this is a structural differentiation worth monitoring as 1.6T transition pressures node leadership.

Five-company control-point thread per Framework 2.6: NVDA full control point; AVGO control point with bottleneck participation; MRVL bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations; CSCO bottleneck participant with platform-tier ambition; ANET bottleneck participant with platform-tier ambition. CRDO joins MRVL at the bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations level — adjacent to MRVL but at smaller operational scale and without Photonic Fabric platform-level integration.

## Risk factors summary

Per CRDO 10-K FY2025 and 10-Q Q3 FY2026:

- **Customer concentration.** Single customer >10% (67% FY2025, 48% Q3 FY2026 contracting); top 10 = 90% FY2025; trajectory broadening but high. "We may not maintain our existing customer relationships and may fail to develop new customer relationships."
- **Wafer supply concentration.** Foundry partner concentration; long lead times; allocation risk during shortages.
- **Hong Kong / Asia geographic concentration.** 56% revenue + 43% assets; political and economic dependence on Hong Kong; National Security Law exposure.
- **Tariff exposure.** "Current tariff regime, which remains fluid" (CFO Q3 FY2026 call). Q4 FY2026 guidance based on existing tariff framework; tariff escalation risk explicit.
- **Standards body and protocol participation risk.** UALink, ESUN, Ultra Ethernet Consortium standards evolution. CRDO product roadmap dependence on protocol standardization timelines.
- **Beneficial ownership concentration.** Approximately 12.5% of outstanding ordinary shares held by directors and executive officers in aggregate (CRDO 10-K FY2025) — concentration of voting control.
- **CFC (controlled foreign corporation) US tax exposure.** US shareholders treated as having CFC interests in CRDO group structure — tax implications for certain US shareholders.

# INOD — Innodata Inc.

## Thesis role

Innodata is a global **data-engineering and AI-systems-services** company — it builds the training, post-training, alignment, evaluation, and red-teaming data that frontier AI labs and Big Tech use to develop and align their models. It is the **anchor of [[AI-data-supply-chain]]** and, per that theme's central finding, the **only liquid US-listed pure-play AI-data *supplier* of meaningful size** (the expert-RLHF peers — Scale AI, Surge AI, Mercor — are all private). It is a *demand-side* beneficiary of the AI boom, not a supplier of any datacenter-supply-chain component.

**Placement framing.** `layer: outside` + `outside` on all five per-domain tiers, following the demand-side / outside-the-frameworks precedent set by [[PLTR]] / [[MSFT]] / [[NOW]]. INOD captures no supply-side chokepoint rent; it sits at the *commodity-labor* end of the AI-data value-capture gradient, moving up-market into expert/safety/eval data. Per honest-verdict discipline (Section 2.1), its fit to the supply-chain chokepoint thesis is weak by design — inclusion is to anchor the AI-data-supplier theme.

**The three-fold honest verdict:**

1. **A high-quality business in a structurally fragile position.** INOD is *not* a weak company — revenue +54% YoY (Q1 2026), genuinely profitable ($32.2M FY2025 net income), debt-free, $117M cash, with real operating leverage (Q1 adjusted EBITDA +96% on revenue +54%). The fragility is *structural*: ~56–58% of revenue from a single customer, 63% of accounts receivable from one customer, and contracts *"generally terminable by our customer upon 30 to 90 days' notice"* (INOD 10-K FY2025). This is the "Appen tell" — the concentration that turns one procurement decision into an existential event.

2. **Picks-and-shovels *services*, not a toll booth.** INOD owns no chokepoint. Its moat narrative — "institutional knowledge compounds," "proprietary technologies for generating synthetic data" (Abuhoff, Q1 2026 call) — is management framing, not an owned asset; its services have low switching costs versus a customer's in-house team. It is a demand-side proxy for frontier-lab data budgets, valuable as a *signal* more than as a fortress.

3. **Promotional-framing caution.** The call elevates emerging vectors (agentic / physical-AI / evaluation) to "proven, monetized" and floats a **$3B "potential annual run-rate revenue"** trust-and-safety figure against $251.7M of actual FY2025 revenue — Tier-2 promotional framing attributed to management, treated skeptically here per the rhetorical-claims convention.

## AI-data-supplier positioning

On the [[AI-data-supply-chain]] value-capture gradient, INOD is the public face of the **commodity-labor tier moving up-market**. The bull case is the up-market migration (commodity bulk annotation is being automated by synthetic data; expert/safety/evaluation data — where humans set policy and audit outputs — is where value is migrating, and INOD is racing there). The bear case is that *everyone* (Scale, Surge, Mercor) is crowding the same premium tier, so margins compress as the moat stays thin. The structural read: the growth is real and the business is well-run, but the position is rented, not owned.

## Customer concentration (the centerpiece risk)

The defining fragility, verified at primary:

- **FY2025:** *"one customer in our Digital Data Solutions ('DDS') segment accounted for approximately 58% of the Company's total revenues"* (FY2024: 48%) — concentration **rose** year over year (INOD 10-K FY2025). *"As of December 31, 2025, 63% or $29.2 million of our accounts receivable were due from one customer."*
- **Q1 2026:** the top customer eased to **56%** (Q1 2025: 61%), and — for the first time — *"another customer generated approximately 17%"* (INOD 10-Q Q1 2026). That 17% second customer is the **$51M new big-tech win** management flagged on the call (*"12 months ago… our revenue from this customer was zero… this year we expect it to become our second-largest customer"*) — real, early diversification.
- **Contracts** are master service agreements with project-based statements of work, *"terminable by our customer upon 30 to 90 days' notice"* and not obligating future purchases (INOD 10-K FY2025).

The honest read: concentration is **starting** to ease (top customer 61%→56% YoY; a genuine second customer at 17%; "other big tech customers in aggregate grew 453% YoY," per the call), but it is still extreme, and the diversification narrative (Tier 2) is more optimistic than the 10-K's concentration-risk framing (Tier 1) — a Tier-1/Tier-2 framing gap (see Source audit notes). Customers are **not named** (disclosed only as percentages + "five of the Magnificent Seven") — inferable/unknown per Section 3.3.

## Segment-reporting shift (Section 2.1)

A genuine **mid-period disclosure-methodology shift** falls inside this ingest window: the FY2025 10-K reports **three reportable segments** (DDS / Synodex / Agility), while the Q1 2026 10-Q states the Company *"reassessed its operating and reportable segment structure and concluded that it operates as a single reportable segment… We now operate under one single segment"* — the CEO now reviews results on a consolidated basis. Practical effect: the FY2025 segment split (DDS 88% / Synodex 3% / Agility 9%) is the **last** three-segment disclosure; Q1 2026 onward is consolidated, so segment-level comparability breaks at the shift date (joins the NVT / MOD / NOK / AMD / NVDA Section 2.1 precedent).

## Growth vectors

What is driving the 40%+ guide (Q1 2026 call; figures are Tier 2 / management framing):

- **Pre-training / STEM data** — the $51M new big-tech customer is buying *"high-quality text-based pre-training data at scale… STEM datasets across physics, mathematics, chemistry, engineering, biology"* (graduate/PhD-grade reasoning data).
- **Post-training + evaluation** — alignment, preference optimization, and evals across the "five of the Magnificent Seven" base.
- **Agentic AI** — an **Agent Observability platform** (beta; a first ~$1M platform customer; 15 companies evaluating; two hyperscaler channel-partner discussions).
- **Trust & safety / red-teaming** — a large hyperscaler selected INOD as its global trust-and-safety partner; management floated *"approximately $3 billion of potential annual run-rate revenue"* from the initial SOW (**heavily attributed; promotional; not a forecast**).
- **Physical AI / robotics datasets** — embodied-intelligence data (part of an ~$8M cloud/commerce contract spanning agent safety, game data, responsible-AI testing, and physical AI). This is the *data feedstock* for the humanoid/embodied-AI brain — the link from [[AI-data-supply-chain]] to the vault's third domain, [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]].
- **Federal** — a dedicated Federal Practice (launched 2025); a **prime contract position under the Missile Defense Agency's SHIELD program** (part of Golden Dome) and computer-vision work *"in concert with [[PLTR]]"*. A faint, emerging defense-data adjacency — too thin to classify as a `defense_tier` (watch item).

## Moat / competitive positioning

INOD's defensibility claim is **institutional-knowledge compounding** (each engagement deepens its knowledge of a customer's stack, eval rubrics, and security posture, lowering the marginal cost of the next program) plus **proprietary synthetic-data tooling** (Abuhoff: *"proprietary technologies for generating synthetic data… enabling scaled human judgment to be applied more efficiently… more revenue for us with fewer people"*). These are attributed to management, not accepted as fact. The structural counterweights: the 10-K itself flags that INOD *"compete[s] with in-house personnel at current and prospective customers who may attempt to duplicate our offerings"* (the in-sourcing risk) and *"pricing pressures… from competition"* — the commoditization risk the up-market migration is meant to outrun. Delivery is offshore-labor-heavy (12,200 professionals across 70+ countries, principally India / Sri Lanka / Philippines; the Hong Kong subsidiary is treasury, not operations) — an honest **no-China-exposure** profile (see [[china-exposure]]).

## Open questions

1. **Concentration durability.** Does the top customer keep declining as a share while the 17% second customer keeps growing — or does a *new* concentration simply replace the old? The "Appen tell" exit signal is any disclosed large-customer step-down.
2. **The $3B trust-and-safety SOW.** Does any material fraction of the $3B "potential run-rate" convert to disclosed revenue, or does it stay a promotional figure?
3. **Moat durability.** Does the up-market migration into expert/safety/eval data hold margins as Scale / Surge / Mercor crowd the same tier, and as synthetic data automates commodity work?
4. **Federal / defense-data trajectory.** Does the SHIELD / Golden Dome / Palantir work grow into a disclosed, classifiable line (a possible future `defense_tier` consideration), or stay immaterial?
5. **Refresh trigger.** Q2 2026 10-Q (~August 2026) and FY2026 10-K (~February 2027) — watch concentration %, the second-customer trajectory, and whether the single-segment disclosure adds any product/vector breakout.

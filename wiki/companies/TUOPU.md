# TUOPU — Ningbo Tuopu Group (宁波拓普集团股份有限公司 / 拓普集团)

*Shanghai A-share **601689** (a Hong Kong H-share listing is **planned but not yet completed** — see China exposure & investability). PRC-GAAP (CAS; the company states no difference vs IFRS), RMB. The vault's **second pure A-share ingest** (after [[SANHUA]]). All figures in ¥ unless noted; source set is filings-only (no earnings-call transcript — see Source audit notes).*

## Thesis role

Tuopu is the **second name the vault has taken from the top "actuator-assembly" layer of the humanoid value chain** — Goldman's GS-14 pick, the same layer as [[SANHUA]] — but it is a **richer multi-thesis node**, because its **data-center liquid-cooling leg is sized and explicit** where Sanhua's was not. At its core Tuopu is a large, founder-controlled **Tier-1 automotive-parts supplier** (NVH/shock absorbers, interiors, chassis, auto-electronics, thermal management — "eight product lines," ~¥30,000 of content per vehicle), whose two vault-relevant legs are (1) **humanoid-robot actuators** and (2) **AI-datacenter liquid cooling**. It is anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] as a theme name and is a **confirmed component participant in the [[liquid-cooling]] chokepoint** — the first China name in the vault to earn a numeric AI-datacenter tier off a *sized* liquid-cooling disclosure.

The three questions this page exists to answer (and the connections that fall out of them):

**1 — Why Tuopu matters in the humanoid value chain (per Goldman).** Goldman's Exhibit 5 ranks **Actuator Assembly = 14** (a top-value "mechatronic core" layer; the *"adoption-certainty"* headline pick), and actuators are **~33–60% of the humanoid bill of materials** — the single biggest cost bucket. Assembly is GS's pick precisely because it rewards **manufacturing scale, cost, and integration, not proprietary IP** — and that is Tuopu's home turf. Tuopu enters from **in-house electric-motor capability** (permanent-magnet servo motors + frameless motors), integration experience (motor + reducer + controller), precision machining, and a reported (not company-confirmed) Tesla-Optimus link via its position as a top Tesla EV-parts supplier.

**2 — Legit/healthy or risky? Healthy — but a lower-quality, more-cyclical profile than Sanhua, and the contrast is the point.** FY2025 revenue **¥29.58B (+11.2%)** but **net profit ¥2.78B — DOWN 7.4%** (EPS ¥1.61, −10%; ROE 16.6%→**12.4%**): a genuine **margin-compression** story (China EV price war + ramp/investment costs), and — unlike Sanhua's *optical* Q1 dip — Tuopu's decline is **operational** (ex-non-recurring profit also fell). It is still a solid blue-chip: strong operating cash flow **¥4.48B (+38%)**, dividend-paying, heavy R&D (¥1.50B, ~5% of sales; a >4,000-person science team), audited clean. But the watch items are heavier than Sanhua's (lower margins, more leverage, **65.8% top-5 customer concentration**, EV-cycle dependence) — detailed below.

**3 — Dominant in Actuator Assembly + Linear Actuator (as GS frames it)? Materially *further along* than Sanhua — but still pre-revenue, so "dominant" is a forward bet.** Unlike Sanhua (which never used the term), Tuopu **explicitly builds linear actuators (直线执行器), rotary actuators, and dexterous-hand motors**, says its customer collaboration *"始于直线执行器"* (started with linear actuators) and rapidly *"获得客户认可"* (won customer recognition), has completed **multiple rounds of sample delivery (送样)**, stood up a **dedicated Robotics Actuator Business Division (机器人执行器事业部) with independent management**, and committed **¥5.0B to a 300-mu robotics core-components production base**. *But* FY2025 robot-actuator revenue is **negligible — ¥13.6M, ~0.05% of sales**. So per Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution: Tuopu has put **more concrete chips on the table than Sanhua** — yet GS's "dominance" still runs ahead of the disclosed reality, and the humanoid market itself is pre-volume, so no one is dominant yet.

**Placement.** `layer: 4` + `energy_power_tier: 4` + theme-anchored humanoid (no `humanoid_tier`) + `foreign_issuer: true`. The **Energy/Power Tier 4 (specialty component supplier scope)** placement reflects the genuine, *sized* data-center liquid-cooling leg (below) — the placement [[SANHUA]] was planned for but fell back from (to `outside`) on an unsized leg; Tuopu's leg is sized and explicit, so the numeric tier holds (the [[NOVT]]/[[VPG]] "real-but-minor AI leg → Tier 4" pattern). The auto-parts core (~93% of revenue) and the humanoid actuator leg (pre-revenue) are outside the AI-datacenter frameworks; humanoid stays theme-anchored. **Section 2.11 N/A** (calendar Dec-31 fiscal year). **Section 2.1 N/A** — stable reporting (auto-parts product lines + a negligible robot-actuator line; the Malaysia entity and 宁波灵御机器人部件 are scope additions, not a segment-reporting shift).

## The humanoid actuator leg (Goldman's headline pick vs Tuopu's primary disclosure)

**Why GS picks it (the value-capture logic).** Actuators are the most valuable subsystem in a humanoid (~33–60% of BOM). Goldman's "adoption-certainty" pick is the **assembly** layer — integrating motor + reducer + screw + sensor + control into a finished linear or rotary actuator — because it rewards manufacturing scale and cost rather than proprietary IP. That is exactly what a precision auto-parts manufacturer with in-house motor capability brings.

**What Tuopu actually says — and why it reads as more developed than Sanhua's.** Tuopu treats robotics as its own disclosed business segment (*"机器人业务板块全面进展"* — comprehensive progress in the robotics segment). It frames the entry as a horizontal extension of its **line-controlled brake (IBS) R&D depth** ("mechanical, reducer, motor, e-control, software"), states that **"机器人执行器…主要包括直线执行器和旋转执行器两类"** (robot actuators are core components, mainly linear and rotary actuators), lists in-house advantages (permanent-magnet servo + frameless motors; motor-reducer-controller integration; precision machining), and reports that the **customer collaboration "始于直线执行器"** (began with linear actuators), won rapid recognition, then extended to **rotary actuators + dexterous-hand motors** with **multiple rounds of sample delivery (送样)**. It is **vertically integrating the full stack** — actuators + sensors + body structural parts + foot dampers + electronic flexible skin — under a **dedicated business division**, with a **¥5.0B / 300-mu robotics core-components production base** signed with the Ningbo Economic-Tech Development Zone (and a robot-components base slated for production "本年度" / this year).

**The honest verdict (Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution; Section 4.4 finding).** Highlighted by Goldman as an actuator-assembly play, and Tuopu's disclosures substantiate that intent **more concretely than Sanhua's** (explicit linear actuators, named product stack, a dedicated division, a ¥5B base). *But* the business is still **pre-revenue** (FY2025 robot-actuator content is a rounding error), there are **no named robot customers** at primary, and the humanoid end-market is itself pre-volume — so "dominant in actuator assembly + linear actuator" is GS's **forward bet**, not a current market-share fact. The Tesla/Optimus link is widely reported in trade press but **Tuopu names only "国际国内智能电动车企" / "头部车企" (leading domestic & international NEV automakers), never Tesla** — reported-not-confirmed (Section 3.3).

## Is it a healthy business? (financial-quality assessment)

**Yes — a solid blue-chip, but a notch below Sanhua on quality, and cyclically pressured.** Tuopu is large (¥29.58B revenue), profitable (¥2.78B net profit), strongly cash-generative (operating cash flow ¥4.48B, +38%), dividend-paying, founder-controlled, audited clean, and investing heavily (¥3.5B capex + the ¥5B robot base + ¥1.50B R&D). It is a genuine platform-type Tier-1 supplier, not a speculative micro-cap.

**Watch items (heavier than Sanhua's):**
- **Profit is declining — operationally.** Revenue +11% but net profit −7.4% (ROE 16.6%→12.4%, EPS −10%); ex-non-recurring profit also fell, so this is margin compression, not optics. The decline is narrowing but not yet reversed.
- **Lower margins.** Gross ~19% / net ~9.4% (vs Sanhua's ~29% / ~13%) — auto parts is a thinner-margin business than valves/thermal.
- **More leverage.** Debt-to-assets 45%; cash ¥5.22B + ¥0.40B structured deposits against a larger debt load — **not net-cash-rich** like Sanhua.
- **High customer concentration.** Top-5 customers = **65.8% of sales** (no single customer >50%; related-party 0%) — heavy dependence on a few large EV customers (Tesla-heavy, unnamed). Much higher than Sanhua's 32%.
- **EV-cycle dependence + US-tariff exposure.** Tied to the NEV build cycle; overseas revenue was flat in FY2025; overseas plants (incl. Malaysia) double as tariff mitigation.
- **Humanoid execution risk.** The actuator leg is pre-revenue; the ¥5B base is capacity ahead of confirmed humanoid volumes — the same order-vs-capacity gamble flagged across the humanoid theme.

## Core business and competitive position

- **Auto parts (~93% of revenue; "eight product lines"; platform-type Tier-1).** Tuopu supplies NVH/shock absorbers, interiors, chassis, auto-electronics, thermal management, line-controlled brake (IBS), air suspension, and steer-by-wire/智能驾驶 systems — *"单车配套金额约 3 万元"* (~¥30,000 of content per vehicle), offering one-stop, system-level, modular supply. Fastest-growing line is **auto-electronics (+52%)**; the IBS/brake and chassis R&D is the technical base it leverages into robot actuators. This core is real and scaled but **outside the vault's AI-datacenter + humanoid scope**, and is where the margin pressure shows up.
- **Data-center liquid cooling (the genuine AI-datacenter leg; the [[liquid-cooling]] link).** Tuopu states it has extended its thermal-management technology to **液冷服务器 (liquid-cooled servers), 储能 (energy storage), and 机器人**, winning **first-batch orders of ¥1.5B (取得首批订单 15 亿元)**, and describes a full datacenter liquid-cooling system: **cold plates (液冷板) with micro-channel + folded-fin designs, coolant pumps, manifolds, flow-control valves, gas-liquid separators, and temperature/pressure sensors** — explicitly citing AI/HPC chip heat density (GPU/CPU throttling under air cooling) as the driver. This maps directly onto the [[liquid-cooling]] chokepoint's sub-component bottleneck map (cold plates, manifolds, CDUs/pumps). It is still a **component supplier** (not systems-tier), China-domiciled, and a new entrant — but it is the **most concrete data-center-cooling disclosure of any China name in the vault**, and the reason Tuopu earns Energy/Power Tier 4 where Sanhua earned `outside`.

Notably for Tuopu, Harmonic shares the **multi-thesis** shape — it too carries an AI-datacenter equipment leg (`equipment_tier 4`, semis/WFE) like Tuopu's liquid-cooling leg, plus a defense-enabler leg — but owns a *higher-quality* chokepoint (precision-grinding/metallurgy) on a fortress balance sheet, at a cyclical trough. ## China exposure, footprint, and investability

Tuopu is the vault's **second China-domiciled operating company** (after [[SANHUA]]) — it *is* a node of the China-controlled humanoid/EV supply chain, not a China-exposed foreign buyer. A structural twist worth naming: as a **China-domiciled** actuator and EV-motor maker, Tuopu sits on the **secure side of the [[rare-earth-magnet-chokepoint]]** — its in-house permanent-magnet and frameless motors run on NdFeB it can source domestically, the very magnets US robot/drone makers ([[AVAV]], [[TDY]]) are squeezed on. It is the **inverse of the "squeezed-middle" chokepoint-consumer paradox**: same chokepoint, opposite side. (Tuopu does **not** disclose rare-earth dependence as a risk — consistent with being on the protected side; the magnet link is therefore *inferable*, not disclosed, per Section 3.3.) See [[china-exposure]].

**Investability — the "second crack."** Tuopu is **A-share-only today** (SSE 601689), so it is not cleanly ownable by an international book. But on **December 1, 2025 it announced plans to issue H-shares and list in Hong Kong** ("为加速推进国际化战略…拟筹划发行 H 股并在香港联交所上市…尚存在一定不确定性") — putting it on the same A→A+H path [[SANHUA]] completed in July 2025, roughly a year behind. If it lists, it becomes the **second internationally-accessible name in the top actuator-assembly value layer** — a second crack in the value-layer map's "strongest chokepoints, worst US access" wall. Until then it remains map-but-not-yet-investable *for a US/international book.* *(Investor-access lens, per [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]]: that "not-yet-investable" is the US-brokerage lens — for an investor able to access China A-shares directly, the Shanghai A-share 601689 is already reachable, so the pending H-IPO is a future *cleaner route*, not a precondition for owning Tuopu.)*

## Open questions

1. **Does the robot-actuator business move from sample-delivery to sized revenue?** Watch for the first disclosed actuator revenue, named robot customers, or the ¥5B base reaching production — the trigger to revisit theme-anchoring (and a possible future tier). Confirming/denying the reported Tesla-Optimus relationship is the key catalyst.
2. **Does the H-share listing complete?** Completion flips Tuopu from 🔴 (map-only) to 🟡 (investable) on the value-layer map — the second top-layer name to cross that line.
3. **Is the margin compression bottoming?** The YoY profit decline is narrowing (−12% → −7.4% → −2.4%); confirm whether H1/Q2 2026 returns to profit growth, and how much is EV price war vs ramp/investment cost.
4. **How big is the data-center-cooling slice within the ¥1.5B?** The ¥1.5B spans liquid-cooled servers + storage + robotics; a sized DC-cooling line (and repeat orders) would firm up the Energy/Power Tier 4 placement and the [[liquid-cooling]] participation.
5. **Customer concentration.** Does the 65.8% top-5 (Tesla-heavy) diversify, or does EV-cycle/single-customer risk intensify?

## Power Generation data center backup + prime power positioning

The Q1 FY2026 disclosure substantively rerates Power Generation sub-application as the load-bearing AI datacenter thesis driver within CAT consolidated. Joe Creed Q1 FY2026 call: "Power generation grew 48%, driven by strong demand for large gensets and turbines used in data center applications with an increasing mix towards prime power." 10-K MD&A FY2025 confirms structural framing: "growth in Power Generation for both reciprocating engines and turbines and turbine-related services in 2026, driven by increasing energy demand to support data center build-out related to cloud computing and generative Artificial Intelligence (AI). Additionally, we are starting to see orders for prime power trend higher as data center customers look for alternative power solutions to keep pace with their growth."

**ProPower 2.1 GW agreement (April 29, 2026 announcement).** Per Joe Creed Q1 FY2026 call: "Yesterday, we announced another exciting opportunity to provide ProPower up to 2.1 GW of large gas generator sets for prime power generation in support of data center, oil and gas, and industrial applications. The orders will enter the backlog on a rolling basis. We expect to deliver generator sets over the next five years and anticipate long-term services growth opportunities in the future. This represents the sixth agreement with at least 1 GW of Caterpillar equipment for prime power applications." ProPower is named at primary as direct CAT customer (data center developer / multi-application power developer); underlying hyperscaler chain not disclosed per industry-standard non-disclosure pattern.

**Six ≥1 GW prime power agreements + multiple sub-1 GW prime power projects.** Per Joe Creed Q1 FY2026 call response to Jerry Revich (Wells Fargo): "I think 6 announcements over 1 gigawatt, but there's also multiple projects as well that are less than 1 gigawatt where we're supporting customers with prime power." Systematic multi-GW commercial commitments at primary substantiate structural elevation framing without overstating to utility-scale generation Tier 3 promotion (which CAT's commercial structure does not support — CAT remains genset/turbine component supplier to multiple distributed-scale customers vs single-utility integrated power deployment).

**Multi-product BTM advantage at primary.** Per Joe Creed Q1 FY2026 call response to Jerry Revich: "I think we do have an advantage of having, you know, when you're gonna string together a number of products behind the meter, you need multiple products, you know, us having turbines and recips is an advantage for us. We can configure it, one way or the other or a mix, and a lot of it's driven by timing too and how fast we can get them product." Multi-product behind-the-meter (BTM) offering distinct from single-product specialists at the same chokepoint terrain — [[GEV]] single-product utility-scale gas turbine; [[BE]] single-product solid oxide fuel cell; [[FCEL]] single-product molten carbonate fuel cell. CAT reciprocating engine + Solar Turbines aero-derivative gas turbine multi-product positioning creates customer-side configuration flexibility (prime vs backup mix; gas vs liquid fuel; rapid load vs continuous load).

**Backlog visibility multi-year + "well into 2028".** Per Joe Creed Q1 FY2026 call: "since we first announced our initial capacity expansion plans in January of 2024, our large reciprocating engine backlog has grown by more than 3.5x. Customers are committing to longer-term orders, with some orders well into 2028." Q1 FY2026 consolidated backlog $62 billion firm at quarter-end (10-Q) / $63 billion per Q1 call (likely post-quarter-end ProPower booking) — +$28 billion sequential / +79% YoY. Power and Energy segment estimated to represent substantial portion of multi-quarter backlog growth per Q1 FY2026 commentary.

**Prime power mix shift framing (Joe Creed Q1 FY2026 call).** "The backup power opportunity provides a good base for us, but it is fungible capacity, and we are seeing a lot more mix towards prime. That drives, you know, when it's gas compression or prime power drives a lot of aftermarket, which this capacity will also allow us to serve that aftermarket opportunity, which I think gives us great services growth opportunity beyond 2030." Prime power positioning (utility-grade reliability + continuous-load operation) materially distinct from commodity backup genset (intermittent emergency standby) scope; substantiates structural elevation framing.

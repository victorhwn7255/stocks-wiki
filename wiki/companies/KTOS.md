# KTOS — Kratos Defense & Security Solutions, Inc.

## Thesis role

Kratos is the **second Defense & Drones platform-prime page** (after [[AVAV]]) and the broadest defense industrial-base name in the vault — a multi-franchise prime spanning jet-powered drones, target drones, hypersonics, propulsion (it builds both the airframe *and* the engine), solid rocket motors, space/satellite, and microwave electronics. Placement is **`defense_tier 1`** (prime with programs of record) per `frameworks-defense-drones.md` Framework D2 value-capture tiers, with one honest nuance: several of its flagship programs (Valkyrie, the largest hypersonic award) are still **pre-LRIP / verbal / framework-agreement**, not yet obligated production contracts. Kratos carries no AI-datacenter supply-chain role; the AI `*_tier` fields are not applied (pure-defense page, per the [[AVAV]] precedent).

**Why it matters to the thesis.** Kratos is the public-market embodiment of the Defense thesis's **"affordable mass"** demand driver — a scale manufacturer of low-cost, attritable systems (drones, loitering-class missiles, hypersonic test vehicles, cheap jet engines) explicitly aligned with the Department's "Arsenal of Freedom" / "first-to-market, good-enough-today" procurement shift. Q1 FY2026 was a **beat-and-raise**: revenue $371.0M (+15.8% organic), record backlog $2.011B, a 1.6x book-to-bill, adjusted EBITDA $38.7M (above guidance), and a raised FY2026 revenue guide to $1.70–1.76B (Kratos Q1 FY2026 10-Q + call, May 6 2026). Demand is real and accelerating.

**Honest-verdict framing (Section 2.1 host-tensions-honestly).** Unlike [[AVAV]] (whose Q3 stress-test was a *demand-is-real-but-margin-broke* story), Kratos's tension is **not** operational — it is **capital structure and timing**. The company is *capitalized and priced for the budget tailwind to fully materialize*: GAAP profitability is thin (Q1 operating margin 1.3%; net income is interest- and tax-aided), free cash flow is **negative**, dilution has been **heavy and serial** (two equity raises totaling ~$1.9B net in eight months; share count up ~44% since FY2023), and capacity is being built **ahead of orders** against a wall of verbal / pre-production awards. On Framework D5 (chokepoint quality gradient), Kratos is **not** a geology/physics chokepoint owner — its moat is *affordable mass + first-to-market + propulsion vertical integration*, which sits on the policy/execution end of the gradient; its propulsion / solid-rocket-motor second-sourcing and hypersonic test infrastructure are the most chokepoint-like pieces, while the airframes themselves are the commoditizing layer.

## Defense & Drones thesis role — how Kratos benefits from the themes

Kratos maps onto `frameworks-defense-drones.md` across an unusually wide span of the value chain. All program facts below are primary (Kratos FY2025 10-K + Q1 FY2026 10-Q + Q1 FY2026 call, May 6 2026, as cited).

**Framework D1 (value-chain position) — Kratos spans platforms, propulsion, and C5ISR:**

- **Unmanned / drones (platform layer).** XQ-58A **Valkyrie** (jet-powered, CCA-adjacent "loyal wingman"); **Tactical Firejet** ("Mighty Hornet"), which Kratos now flies with its *own* jet engine — full plane-plus-engine vertical integration; **target drones** (BQM-167, BQM-177A). Management frames these as the **"Family of Affordable Mass"** — 30,000+ low-cost missiles/drones over the next several years (Q1 call). This is the same attritable / ~commoditizing dynamic the thesis flags for [[AVAV]]'s one-way-attack drones, but Kratos plays it as a *scale jet-aircraft manufacturer* rather than a small-UAS house.
- **Hypersonics.** **MACH-TB 2.0** — a $1.45B-ceiling, 5-year award (the largest contract in Kratos history), providing affordable hypersonic flight-test infrastructure for the National Hypersonic Initiative 2.0 (FY2025 10-K); plus Erinyes, Dark Fury, and Zeus solid rocket motors. Management guides hypersonics revenue from ~$400M (2026) to ~$700M (2027), and reported a verbally-awarded "$1B+ sole-source hypersonic expansion" (Q1 call — flagged as **verbal**, not yet contracted).
- **Propulsion / solid rocket motors (the closest-to-chokepoint franchise).** The **GEK turbofan** was selected for the SLCM-N submarine-launched cruise missile and a ~4x production expansion; small jet-engine LRIP is expected to begin late 2026, scaling to "several thousand engines" in 2027 at $40,000–60,000 each (Q1 call). The **Prometheus** solid-rocket-motor joint venture with Rafael ($50M Kratos + $50M Rafael + a $100M DoD facility commitment) is an explicit attempt to **second-source the solid-rocket-motor oligopoly** — a genuine supply chokepoint the broader munitions base is short of. DeMarco's "only company in the world that builds the plane *and* the engine" (Q1 call) is the vertical-integration claim that underpins the moat.
- **Space / satellite.** **OpenSpace** — an open, virtualized C2/RF-processing software platform management characterizes as the company's "crown jewel" and an analog to Anduril's Lattice (Q1 call); a **$447M US Space Force MEO Resilient Missile Warning & Tracking** prime award (Golden Dome adjacency); a stated 3:1 satellite book-to-bill.
- **Microwave / C5ISR + directed energy / counter-UAS.** Kratos describes itself as the largest independent merchant supplier of microwave electronics outside the US, transitioning from parabolic to AESA / phased-array antennas (the Orbit acquisition); and reported a new **multi-hundred-million-dollar directed-energy / counter-UAS program with Kratos as prime**, ramping 2027 and "very big in 2028" (Q1 call).

**Framework D2 (tier):** `defense_tier 1` — prime with programs of record. Honest nuance: Valkyrie is still **pre-LRIP** (LRIP Phase I being negotiated in 2026) and several large awards are framework agreements or verbal, so the "programs of record" depth is more *forward* than [[AVAV]]'s already-booked task orders.

**Framework D5 (chokepoint quality gradient):** Kratos's defensible edges are **production scale**, **affordability / first-to-market**, and **propulsion + solid-rocket-motor vertical integration**. The propulsion and SRM second-sourcing are the most chokepoint-like positions (they address a genuine industrial-base shortage); the airframes are the commoditizing layer. This is *policy/execution* moat, not *geology/physics* — placing Kratos below the chokepoint owners (e.g., [[MP]]'s rare-earth magnets) on the quality gradient.

## US-defense-budget connection + the enacted-vs-requested caveat

Kratos frames its entire outlook around the US defense budget far more explicitly than the AI-side vault names do — it is a **direct prime beneficiary** of the demand the Defense thesis tracks (Framework D3). On the Q1 call, management tied the guidance raise to the **$156B FY2025 reconciliation defense funds** beginning to flow: DeMarco noted that **only ~$30B of the $156B had been obligated into contracts by April**, with the remainder now being put on contract — his stated source of "increased confidence" in the full-year plan. He further pointed to an FY2027 outlook of a **$1.15T base budget plus a potential $350B reconciliation = ~$1.5T national-security spend, "an approximate $411B increase above 2026,"** and to the Department's shift toward multi-year (up-to-seven-year) weapon-system framework agreements.

**Honest framing (per `_thesis-defense-drones.md`'s budget-contingency discipline).** DeMarco characterized the spending increase as **"bipartisan… regardless of which party controls Congress"** (Q1 call) — this is a **Tier 2 management characterization** (Section 3.3 rhetorical attribution), and it collides directly with the Defense thesis's own **enacted-vs-requested caveat**: the FY2025 reconciliation is enacted law, but the FY2027 surge is largely a **request / future-reconciliation-contingent** number, dependent on appropriations that the November 2026 midterms could reshape. The page records DeMarco's framing as a management claim, cross-referenced to the caveat — not as established fact. This is the same contingency that gates [[AVAV]]'s forward trajectory.

## Honest-verdict centerpiece — capital structure, dilution, FCF, and pre-production timing

The beat-and-raise is genuine, so the analytical tension lives in how the company is financed and timed.

**Serial dilution.** Kratos has run two large equity offerings in eight months: **14,935,065 shares at $38.50 (~$555.9M net) in June 2025**, used to extinguish its Term Loan A, and **16,428,571 shares at $84.00 (~$1,348.6M net) in February 2026** (Kratos FY2025 10-K; Q1 FY2026 10-Q). Shares outstanding rose from 168.9M (Dec 28 2025) to **187.4M (Mar 29 2026) — ~+11% in a single quarter** — and from ~130M (FY2023) to ~187M today (~+44%). Weighted-average diluted shares went 156.2M → 179.4M year-over-year. Cash ballooned to **$1,464.3M** with zero debt.

**Thin GAAP profitability.** Q1 operating income was just **$4.7M (1.3% operating margin**, down from 2.2%). Net income of $11.9M is **interest- and tax-aided**: the income statement shows **$4.5M of net interest income** (earned on the $1.46B cash pile) plus a **$2.1M tax benefit** bridging $4.7M of operating income to $11.9M of net income (Q1 FY2026 10-Q statement of operations) — i.e., operating leverage is not yet visible at the bottom line. At the full-year level, FY2025 operating income actually **fell 12%** ($29.0M → $25.6M) despite +18.5% revenue, as gross margin compressed from 25.3% to 22.9% on mix and inflation.

**Negative free cash flow.** Q1 operating cash flow was **−$27.4M**; after $19.9M capex (and $4.2M of Valkyrie asset-sale proceeds) free cash flow was **≈ −$43M** (Q1 FY2026 10-Q; call). FY2026 capex is guided to ~$160M (call). Capacity is being built **ahead of orders** — Valkyrie production lots before a contract award, plus the Prometheus / Anaconda / Helios / GEK facilities — a deliberate bet that the demand converts. When pressed on the second-half margin/cash trajectory, management's framing was that it is "trying to be conservative" and that the constraint is government contracting offices' obligation backlog, with no specific free-cash-flow-inflection date offered (Goldman question, Q1 call).

**Pre-production / verbal-award timing.** Valkyrie is still pre-LRIP; the "$1B+ hypersonic expansion" is a **verbal** award; many other "wins" are framework agreements rather than obligated contracts. The conversion of this pipeline depends on the appropriated-but-unobligated budget actually flowing — a real execution and timing risk that management itself surfaced ($30B of $156B obligated by April).

## Acquisitive roll-up + Framework D6 financial-quality read

Kratos is also a serial acquirer. In the last ~15 months it closed **Norden Millimeter** (microwave electronics, Feb 2025, $32.2M stock; +$22.3M FY2025 revenue), **Nomad Global Communication Solutions** (mobile operations centers, Feb 2026, ~$89M stock + $37M cash; +$7.3M Q1 revenue), and **Orbit Technologies** (microwave / antenna, Mar 2026, ~$352.7M cash; +$13.3M Q1 revenue, +$3.4M operating income). Goodwill rose from $595.7M to $884.1M and intangibles to $219.7M over Q1 (Q1 FY2026 10-Q).

**Framework D6 read.** Kratos is **not** a going-concern / distress case — the D6 "LASE discipline" screen (going-concern, burn, dilution-as-survival) does not apply to a well-funded, zero-debt, profitable prime with a record backlog. The D6 flags that *do* apply sit at the opposite end of the spectrum: **integration quality** across three back-to-back acquisitions, **dilution** as the funding mechanism, and **capacity-ahead-of-orders capital intensity**. There is **no goodwill impairment** (a contrast with [[AVAV]]'s $151.3M BlueHalo Space write-off). **Section 2.1 segment shift: N/A** — Kratos's two reportable segments (Kratos Government Solutions + Unmanned Systems) are unchanged, so there is no mid-fiscal-year disclosure-methodology shift to reconcile (simpler than [[AVAV]] or [[LSCC]]).

## Supply chain / chokepoint position

Kratos is unusually **vertically integrated** for a platform prime — it builds its own jet engines (Tactical Firejet, GEK turbofan) and is standing up in-house solid-rocket-motor capacity (Prometheus). That integration *reduces* its dependence on some of the propulsion/energetics chokepoints the Defense thesis tracks and arguably makes Kratos a chokepoint *builder* (second-sourcing SRMs) rather than a pure consumer.

Notably, **neither the FY2025 10-K nor the Q1 FY2026 10-Q discloses rare-earth / magnet / critical-mineral supply dependence** — the filings' references to China are to China as a *peer adversary driving demand*, not a supply vulnerability. This is a clean contrast with [[AVAV]], whose 10-K explicitly flags China-sourced rare-earth magnets in its motors and batteries. Per Section 3.3 (disclosed / inferable / unknown), Kratos's drones and missiles structurally use the same NdFeB-magnet and secure-microelectronics components, but the dependence is **inferable-only, not disclosed** — so this page does not assert a supply-chain link to [[MP]] or [[LSCC]]. If a future filing discloses it, the link can be added then. KTOS sits as the vertically-integrated *chokepoint builder* on the [[defense-drone-supply-chain]] map, and its non-disclosure of magnet dependence is recorded on the consumer side of [[rare-earth-magnet-chokepoint]].

## Competitive positioning & cross-prime adjacency

Among US-listed pure-plays, Kratos sits alongside [[AVAV]] as the two **Defense & Drones platform primes** — but at a different position. [[AVAV]] is the broad small-UAS / loitering-munition / counter-UAS house (post-BlueHalo, also space/cyber/DE); Kratos is the jet-drone + hypersonics + **propulsion (plane-and-engine)** + space/microwave prime, and sits further *upstream* via its engine and solid-rocket-motor integration. The two are structural co-positioned peers (Section 3.14 parallel-but-independent) rather than direct same-product competitors — they overlap most at the CCA-adjacent / affordable-mass drone layer. The most capable pure drone-autonomy companies (Anduril, Shield AI) remain private and un-investable; Kratos's public-market edge is scale manufacturing and affordability across many franchises rather than a single defensible chokepoint. The affordable-mass commoditization dynamic both primes face — and Kratos's vertical-integration response to it — is the subject of [[drone-platform-commoditization]].

A forward cross-thesis curiosity: management mentioned an expected **industrial gas-turbine program "for AI-related data centers"** with an unnamed global industrial-technology partner by end-2026 (Q1 call). This is a single prospective program with no disclosed revenue — broad-cycle, failing the Section 3.10 four-criterion test — so it does **not** earn an AI `*_tier`; it is noted only as a potential future cross-thesis node to watch.

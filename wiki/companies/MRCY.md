# MRCY — Mercury Systems, Inc.

## Thesis role

Mercury Systems is the Defense & Drones domain's **trusted/secure "processing at the edge" specialist** — a mid-cap defense-electronics company (RF/microwave, mission computing, electronic warfare, data management, and **secure microelectronics**) that turns commercial silicon into trusted, mission-ready subsystems for aerospace-and-defense primes across radar, EW, missiles, space, and C4I. It is the vault's **first `defense_tier 2`** placement (mid-cap pure-play specialist per `frameworks-defense-drones.md` Framework D2) — filling the structural slot between the platform primes ([[AVAV]]/[[KTOS]], tier 1) and the chokepoint enablers ([[MP]]/[[LSCC]]/[[TDY]]/[[AMPX]], tier 4). Mercury carries no AI-datacenter supply-chain role (the AI `*_tier` + `layer` fields are not applied); its "AI" is *defense-edge* AI, not the datacenter kind (see below).

**Why it matters — and the two honest tensions.** Mercury's differentiated moat is **trusted, onshore, secure microelectronics** (DMEA-certified trusted design + manufacturing; BuiltSECURE/BuiltSAFE; secure chiplets) — the chokepoint-grade capability that makes it the **trusted-domestic pole of the secure-microelectronics node**, the structural inverse of [[LSCC]]'s 64%-China-revenue / Taiwan-fab profile. But two tensions, both evidence-led per Section 2.1, define the page:

- **(a) The turnaround is in *adjusted* terms; GAAP is still a loss.** Under CEO Bill Ballhaus, Mercury has staged a credible operational turnaround — record bookings + backlog, expanding margins, adjusted EBITDA from $9.4M (FY2024) to $119.4M (FY2025) — *but it remains GAAP loss-making* (FY2025 net loss $(37.9)M / diluted loss per share $(0.65); 9M FY2026 net loss $(30.5)M / $(0.51)) under ~$33M/yr interest on a ~$590M revolver plus heavy intangible amortization, with **lumpy free cash flow** (Mercury FY2025 10-K + Q3 FY2026 10-Q + call, May 5 2026).
- **(b) Strong on Defense, weak on Drones.** Within "Defense & Drones," Mercury is a **Defense name, not a Drones name** — missiles/radar/EW/space/C4I, with *no disclosed* drone or counter-UAS business (see the domain mapping).

**Honest-verdict framing.** `defense_tier 2` mid-cap specialist; inclusion is for the **trusted-microelectronics chokepoint moat + defense-budget exposure**, not drone-centrality or AI-datacenter exposure. The turnaround, the drones-weakness, and the still-GAAP-loss balance sheet are surfaced plainly.

## Defense & Drones thesis role — relationship to the four domains

Per the vault's two theses + the AI/energy domains, Mercury's fit is **asymmetric** — strong on one axis, inferable or absent on the others. All program facts are primary (Mercury Q3 FY2026 call, May 5 2026, unless noted).

**Defense — strong (the core).** Maps onto `frameworks-defense-drones.md` Frameworks D1–D6:
- **D1/D3 (value chain + demand):** Mercury sits at the **secure-processing subsystem layer**, selling to primes. Q3 FY2026 **domestic revenue was ~88% of the total and grew +17% YoY**; end-markets span **radar, EW, C4I, missiles, space, and sensors/effectors** (Land + Space + Naval up; Airborne down). Named programs: **LTAMDS** (air defense), **SEWIP** (shipboard EW), **PWSA** (space), **KC-46**, and the internal **Common Processing Architecture (CPA)**. Management ties the outlook to **Golden Dome, munitions acceleration, and global rearmament** — "we're starting to see those tailwinds manifest in the form of multi-year strategic agreements at increased quantities… with the primes" (Ballhaus). This is the strong, central fit, and the basis for the `defense_tier 2` placement.
- **D2 (tier):** `defense_tier 2` — mid-cap pure-play defense-electronics specialist (the first in the vault).
- **D5 (chokepoint quality):** the **trusted-microelectronics moat** is the chokepoint-grade piece — see the dedicated section.
- **D6 (financial quality):** the turnaround read — see Framework D6 section.

**Drones — weak / inferable-only (the honest tension).** Neither the FY2025 10-K nor the Q3 FY2026 call discloses a drone or **counter-UAS** business. The 10-K names "UAVs / unmanned systems / fixed wing and rotorcraft" only as *platform categories* its SWaP-constrained processing *can* ride — not named drone programs or revenue. Per Section 3.3 (disclosed/inferable/unknown), Mercury's processing structurally *could* sit on unmanned platforms, but the dependence is **inferable, not disclosed** — so no drone-centric narrative is asserted. Within "Defense & Drones," Mercury anchors the **Defense** half (missiles/radar/EW/space), not the unmanned half. (The honest-verdict inverse of [[LSCC]], which was AI-strong / defense-thin; Mercury is defense-strong / drones-thin.)

**AI — defense-edge-AI only; NOT AI-datacenter; NOT dual-thesis.** Ballhaus frames Mercury's AI angle as tactical: "taking our mission-critical processing to the edge… provide the compute infrastructure needed to have **AI distributed across the battlespace**." That is *defense edge-AI* (sensor/autonomy processing at the tactical edge) — there is **zero** hyperscaler / datacenter / HPC commercial exposure. So Mercury's "AI" maps to the **Defense** thesis (autonomy/edge processing), **not** the AI-datacenter vault — Mercury is **not a dual-thesis page** (the clean contrast with [[LSCC]], whose AI exposure is genuine hyperscaler-server demand). → no AI `*_tier`.

**Energy — none.** No energy / power / grid / datacenter-power exposure of any kind. Pure aerospace-and-defense. → no `energy_power_tier`.

## The trusted-domestic-microelectronics moat (and the LSCC contrast)

Mercury's structural moat is **trusted, secure, onshore microelectronics** — and it is the cleanest cross-vault connection:
- **DMEA-certified trusted design + manufacturing facilities** ("advanced secure system-on-chip design, assembly, packaging and test"); a "trusted supply chain, with products designed and manufactured **onshore**" (FY2025 10-K Item 1).
- **BuiltSECURE** (reverse-engineering mitigation) + **BuiltSAFE** (safety-certifiable) security IP; on the call, Ballhaus: "there are certain security standards that **we're the only ones that can meet** — we've got a nice moat around this business," and flagged **secure chiplets** as "a big TAM."

This is a genuine Framework D5 chokepoint-quality capability (the secure/trusted-processing layer the Defense thesis prizes), and it makes Mercury the **trusted-domestic pole of the secure-microelectronics node**, alongside [[LSCC]] — but as its *inverse*: [[LSCC]] is a fabless secure-FPGA *designer* with 64% Greater-China revenue and TSMC-Taiwan fabrication; Mercury is an **onshore-trusted secure-processing integrator** with minimal direct China/Taiwan exposure (tariff risk only). On the [[china-exposure]] theme, Mercury is the **low-exposure, reshoring-aligned "trusted-domestic" entry** — the structural beneficiary of the de-China secure-microelectronics push. Two names, two poles of the same node. A dedicated trusted-microelectronics chokepoint page is now a closer candidate (MRCY + LSCC = two participants); deferred pending a third.

## Framework D6 — the turnaround read

The honest-verdict's financial half: a credible operational inflection that is **not yet GAAP-profitable or clean on cash**:
- **Bookings/backlog momentum:** record Q3 bookings **$348.3M (1.48 book-to-bill)**, record backlog **~$1.6B**, record TTM bookings $1.23B — the demand side is strong and converting faster.
- **Margin expansion:** gross margin FY2025 27.9% (+440 bps) → Q3 29.3%; adjusted EBITDA margin to mid-teens, guided toward low-to-mid-20s longer term, as lower-margin legacy development backlog converts out and new bookings come in at target margins.
- **The net-working-capital story:** Ballhaus "swung the pendulum hard" to just-in-time material, cutting **NWC ~$225M from its ~$659M peak** (to ~$434M) — the engine behind FY2025's strong FCF, and now normalizing (FY2026 FCF is lumpier: Q3 a ~$2M outflow vs a $24M inflow a year prior, with full-year guided positive).
- **But still GAAP-loss-making + levered:** ~$33M/yr interest on the ~$590M revolver + heavy amortization keep GAAP negative; net debt ~$260M (improving — a $150M April-2026 paydown). **No going-concern, no goodwill impairment, no material weakness** (the FY2024 restructuring is largely behind it; ~220 positions cut FY2025–FY2026). The D6 read: *the turnaround is real and inflecting in adjusted terms, but GAAP profitability and a clean run-rate FCF are the open items, on a mid-cap balance sheet.*

## Company structure & end-markets

One reportable segment (the **defense-electronics business**), organized as a single integrated operating unit since the 2024 reorganization, with four business units (Signal Technologies, Processing Technologies, Integrated Processing Solutions, + an international unit; the Switzerland manufacturing operation was divested to Cicor Group in FY2025). Mercury discloses **product** (Components / Modules & Sub-assemblies / Integrated Solutions), **end-market** (Radar / EW / C4I / Sensors & Effectors), and **platform** (Land / Space / Naval / Airborne) breakdowns in lieu of P&L segments. ~88% domestic revenue; sells to top US + European defense primes, the US Government, and commercial-aerospace OEMs.

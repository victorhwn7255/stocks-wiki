# ARM — Arm Holdings plc

## Thesis role

Arm Holdings occupies **Layer 1 (Platform Definers)** as the CPU IP architecture platform owner whose ISA, microarchitecture, and Neoverse Compute Subsystem ("CSS") reference designs define the dominant non-x86 compute architecture across mobile, edge, and an increasingly substantive share of datacenter compute. ~50% of CPU compute among top hyperscalers now runs on Arm (Q4 FY2026 shareholder letter); Arm-based hyperscaler custom silicon (AWS Graviton + Google Axion + Microsoft Cobalt + NVIDIA Vera/Grace + Meta-internal) is the central vector by which CPU spend is shifting away from x86 — and Arm captures royalty on every chip shipped under its IP. FY2026 data center royalty more than doubled YoY (Q4 FY2026 letter); FY2026 total revenue $4.92B (+23%) marked the third consecutive >20% year since the September 2023 NASDAQ IPO.

ARM is the **first vault canonical placed at pure compute-domain Layer 1 scope** — Layer 1 (Platform Definer) with ALL `*_tier outside` (photonics + energy_power + equipment + materials Outside; `memory_tier` field omitted per Section 3.2(c)). Substantively unique among vault canonicals: prior Layer 1 placements [[NVDA]] (photonics_tier 1) and [[AVGO]] (photonics_tier 2) carry cross-domain photonics-platform participation; ARM does not. Pure compute-domain Layer 1 honest framing.

**Commercial structure is dual-vector and shifting.** Vector 1 (mature, dominant): IP licensing + royalty — License/other revenue $2.31B FY2026 (+25%); royalty $2.61B FY2026 (+21%). Vector 2 (emerging): Arm AGI CPU first-party silicon — Arm's first datacenter chip; $2B+ committed customer demand across fiscal 2027 + 2028 (more than double the $1B at March 2026 Arm Everywhere launch per Q4 FY2026 call); first production-silicon revenue guided for Q4 fiscal 2027 (~$90M expectation held flat at Q4 FY2026 call despite demand doubling, pending supply chain capacity expansion); $15B AGI CPU revenue trajectory by FYE31 per Child Q4 FY2026 call ("$15 billion in AGI CPU revenue and $10 billion in IP revenue for a total of $25 billion... more than $9 in EPS"). **Caveat #3(b) Layer 1-2 hybrid notation candidate deferred at S76** — current revenue mix substantively IP-licensing dominant; AGI CPU production revenue first-window Q4 fiscal 2027; revert decision at future ARM refresh when AGI CPU production revenue substantively material.

**Power efficiency reinforces the binding 2026 power chokepoint thesis (rare alignment).** Q4 FY2026 letter framing: agentic-AI datacenters will require "more than 4x current CPU capacity per gigawatt" — Arm AGI CPU delivers ">2x performance per rack vs x86" and potential "$10B/gigawatt" AI datacenter capex reduction. Google Axion: "up to 2x better performance-per-watt" vs x86; "50% less power"; TPU8i "up to 80% better inference performance-per-dollar" vs prior x86 generation (Q4 FY2026 letter). NVIDIA Vera: "up to 75% faster performance and 2x higher efficiency than x86 CPUs" (Q4 FY2026 letter). ARM is one of few vault canonicals where the binding 2026 power-infrastructure chokepoint per `_thesis.md` 2026-04-30 rework *reinforces* the bull case rather than constrains it.

**Honest-verdict framing on controlling shareholder + related-party customer concentration.** SoftBank Group beneficially owned **approximately 86.4% of total issued and outstanding share capital as of May 21, 2026** (FY2026 20-F; down from 87.1% as of May 20, 2025 per the FY2025 20-F) — Arm is a Nasdaq-defined "controlled company" exempting it from majority-independent-director, fully-independent-comp/nominating-committee, and certain related-party-approval requirements. **Arm China (Arm Technology (China) Co. Limited) is Arm's largest customer at 16% of total revenue FY2026 and is a related party** (17% FY2025; 21% FY2024 — a declining share as data-center royalty grows) under the IPLA structure governing Arm China's sublicensing to PRC end-customers; neither Arm nor SoftBank Group control Arm China's operations per FY2026 20-F Risk Factors. This is a distinctive related-party + foreign-controlled-distribution-channel concentration archetype within Framework 11.1 customer concentration scope. Aggregate related-party revenue (Arm China + SoftBank Group) rose to 30% of total revenue FY2026 ($1,499M) from 21% FY2025 ($823M), driven by the growing SoftBank related-party technology licensing + design services agreement (FY2026 20-F).

## CPU IP architecture commercial structure (dual-vector primary)

**Vector 1 (IP licensing + royalty) is the mature, dominant cash engine.** Royalty model: per-chip royalty (typically % of customer ASP per IPLA terms) recognized on accrual basis when customer ships chips containing Arm IP (Q4 FY2026 letter Royalty revenue note). License model: Arm Total Access (subscription access to broad IP portfolio; 56 customers Q4 FY2026 including >50% of top 30) + Arm Flexible Access (broader access for emerging customers; 329 customers Q4 FY2026) + per-architecture license + Compute Subsystem ("CSS") license (RTL or GDSII reference designs covering Neoverse server, mobile, and infrastructure variants). ACV $1.66B (+22%) is management's preferred normalized licensing trend indicator; consistently above long-term licensing growth expectations per Child Q4 FY2026 call.

**Armv9 + Neoverse CSS adoption is the royalty rate driver.** Per Q4 FY2026 letter: royalty growth was "driven by the continued adoption of Arm technology with higher royalty rates per chip, such as Armv9 architecture and Arm CSS, and increased deployment of Arm-based chips in data centers." Armv9 (and successor architectures led by Chief Architect Richard Grisenthwaite per FY2025 20-F) commands a higher per-chip rate than the v8 baseline; CSS reference designs (introduced Feb 21, 2024 per FY2025 20-F; targeted initially at infrastructure space) command a further premium and shorten customer time-to-silicon. Royalty mix by architecture is estimated at the SoC level per FY2026 REPORT Royalty technology mix note (subject to change as additional product detail surfaces). Per Child Q4 FY2026 call, data center networking chips — particularly DPUs and SmartNICs — represent ~100% Arm market share; this is a structurally durable royalty stream distinct from the smartphone-cyclicality scope.

**SoftBank related-party license $200M Q4 FY2026** (Child Q4 FY2026 call). SoftBank-Arm technology licensing + design services agreement; flat with prior quarter; counts within the $819M Q4 license + other revenue line. Related-party scope distinct from Arm China.

**FY2026 royalty end-market concentration (NOT single-customer):** "Royalty revenue from mobile applications processors constituted approximately 43% of our royalty revenue for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026" (FY2026 20-F; down from ~46% FY2025 — the decline reflects data-center-royalty mix growth, with data-center royalty more than doubling YoY). Per FY2026 20-F: Arm has "maintained market share in the mobile applications processor market of greater than 99% for many years, by virtue of all key mobile operating systems depending on Arm processors." This 43% is end-market concentration scope (mobile apps processors of royalty), not single-customer scope.

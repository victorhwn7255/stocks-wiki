# SIVE — Sivers Semiconductors AB

## Thesis role

SIVE occupies Layer 4 (critical component supplier) as a maker of **CW/DFB semiconductor lasers** — the external light source that feeds pluggable transceivers and co-packaged optics (CPO). This places it inside the same `datacenter-laser-supply` chokepoint the vault tracks via [[LITE]] and [[COHR]], on the merchant-challenger side. Its lasers are made at Sivers Photonics (Glasgow, Scotland) plus an external high-volume foundry partner (WIN Semiconductors).

**AI-datacenter thesis fit — genuine positioning, pre-revenue commercially.** SIVE's photonics technology is a real fit for the laser chokepoint, and management confirms the chokepoint thesis from the challenger's vantage: it expects *"a CW laser supply shortage in the next few years as the industry transitions from 800G to 1.6T and eventually to 3.2T"* (Annual Report FY2025, Photonics). But AIDC laser revenue today is zero — production readiness is targeted for end-2026 and revenue *"in 2027 and beyond"* (Annual Report FY2025; Q4 FY2025 call). Honest framing per Section 2.1: SIVE is **NOT thesis-central**. Its inclusion is as a **merchant new-entrant / trajectory counterpoint** at the laser chokepoint — and a live case study in how hard that chokepoint is to enter (its pre-revenue timeline, sub-scale, and going-concern flag illustrate the entry barrier that [[datacenter-laser-supply]] already names).

**Two unrelated businesses; the majority is out of vault scope.** SIVE runs two segments: **Wireless (~70% of revenue; +53% FY2025)** — mmWave beamforming chips for satellite communications, defense, and fixed-wireless — and **Photonics (~30%; +17%)** — semiconductor lasers. Only Photonics is thesis-relevant; the Wireless majority (SATCOM/defense/RF) is outside the vault's AI-datacenter scope and is covered here only for completeness. A reader should not mistake SIVE for a datacenter pure-play: it is a SATCOM/defense RF chip company with an early-stage AIDC-laser bet attached.

**photonics_tier 4 placement (pre-revenue; with a pre-registered tier-3 upgrade trigger).** SIVE's *product* — the CW laser chip — is as chokepoint-central as some tier-3 names ([[LITE]] sits at tier 3), which argues for a higher placement on technology-directness alone. But commercial standing pulls it to tier 4: zero AIDC laser revenue, sub-scale photonics (~SEK 92M / ~$9M FY2025), and a going-concern flag. The placement mirrors the pre-revenue framing applied to [[XFAB]] photonics and [[VECO]] (F5 tier 4). **Upgrade-to-tier-3 trigger pre-registered** (Open question #1) if AIDC laser production revenue materializes (2027+); **downgrade pressure** if the inflection slips. Per honest-verdict discipline, conviction about the CPO supercycle does not move this placement — the upgrade trigger gives the bull case a clear, evidence-gated path.

**No energy/power, equipment, or materials placement.** SIVE has no datacenter-power business (its Wireless segment is mmWave RF, not power semis), supplies no equipment, and sells no materials. Per Section 3.2(c), those per-domain fields are omitted rather than marked.

### Going concern + financing

- **Going-concern material uncertainty disclosed** (Annual Report FY2025, "Financial risks and risks related to going concern"): the Group "has not yet achieved an earnings capacity through sales revenue sufficient to fully cover the Group's development plans and operating costs," depends on "continued ability to obtain additional capital," and "there is a material risk and uncertainty factor that the Group and the Parent Company may not be able to continue operations to the planned extent." Statements prepared on a going-concern basis.
- Serial dilution: two directed share issues in 2025 raised SEK 202.8M (before costs); share count rose ~31% to 311,333,572; board authorization for up to 47.9M further new shares (~15% dilution) (Annual Report FY2025; The Share).
- USD 12M loan from a US credit institution (May 2025). Post-period (February 2026), a **$17M refinancing with Bootstrap Europe** (a specialist technology lender) replaced ~$11.5M of existing loans via a committed three-year facility, alongside a **$12M convertible struck at a premium to the share price** (converts only at a materially higher valuation) (Q4 FY2025 call; Heine Thorsgaard). This extends the runway but adds leverage rather than an equity cushion.
- CFO long-term model (Q4 FY2025 call; Heine Thorsgaard): cash-flow breakeven at ~$50–55M annual revenue with ~65% of revenue from products, targeted "in roughly two years" (~2028); long-run ambition 25–30% revenue CAGR, ~65% gross margin, ~30% EBITDA margin. Against FY2025's ~$30M revenue, breakeven requires roughly doubling revenue and shifting the product mix.

## Photonics / laser franchise

SIVE's Photonics segment designs and makes **high-power DFB lasers, CW DFB laser arrays, and semiconductor optical amplifiers**, manufactured at Sivers Photonics (Glasgow) with a high-volume foundry partnership at **WIN Semiconductors** (announced March 2025) to scale laser-array production (Annual Report FY2025). Photonics grew 17% YoY in FY2025 (audited) to ~SEK 92M, with Q4 up 52% YoY — but the base is small and weighted toward NRE (engineering fees), automotive LIDAR, bio-sensing, and existing SME customers, not datacenter lasers.

Management frames the datacenter laser opportunity around the convergence of scale-up and scale-out networking into "optics-first" datacenter architectures, and a coming **CW laser supply shortage** as bandwidths move 800G → 1.6T → 3.2T (Annual Report FY2025, Photonics). Production-readiness for AIDC lasers is targeted for end-2026; the company expects "a subset" of the transceiver makers it is sampling "to commit to production plans with Sivers lasers in 2027 and beyond" (Annual Report FY2025).

## AI-datacenter & CPO exposure

SIVE's CPO angle is the **external-light-source (ELS) layer**: it supplies the laser; partners supply the interposer / optical engine. All of the named relationships below are **counterparty-attribution / over-claim** in vault terms (Section 3.5) — SIVE-side disclosed, not bilaterally confirmed in vault primary sources (none of POET, O-Net, or Ayar Labs is a vault canonical):

- **POET Technologies** (November 2025): Sivers DFB laser arrays + POET's Optical Interposer to deliver scalable external light engines for AI infrastructure; prototypes targeted H1 2026, production readiness by year-end 2026 (Annual Report FY2025). *Named in Sivers disclosure; not reciprocally confirmed in vault primary sources.*
- **O-Net Technologies** (April 2025): partnership to produce high-performance external laser sources for next-generation CPO in switches and NICs; product demos expected at OFC (March 2026) (Annual Report FY2025; Q4 FY2025 call). *Counterparty-attribution only.*
- **Ayar Labs** (Q4 FY2025 call): named alongside O-Net and POET as among the "innovative young companies" SIVE has engaged on CPO. *Counterparty-attribution only.*
- **LIGHTIUM / EU INGENIOUS** (October 2025): thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) transceiver-engine platform with Sivers CW lasers and photodetectors integrated onto TFLN wafers (Annual Report FY2025). *Counterparty-attribution only.*
- **WIN Semiconductors** (March 2025): high-volume compound-semiconductor foundry to scale laser-array production (Annual Report FY2025).

On the Q4 FY2025 call, the CEO added that beyond the young CPO companies, *"a big household name brand"* is now in discussions about SIVE supporting its technology roadmap. This is an **over-claim**: the name is undisclosed and it is "discussions," not a design-win or order — treated as management signaling, not confirmed demand.

SIVE is sampling CW DFB lasers to "several pluggable transceiver manufacturers worldwide" for scale-out, and CW DFB laser arrays for scale-up (Annual Report FY2025; Q4 FY2025 call). For CPO laser manufacturing capacity, management says it is working with partners (WIN) to secure capacity and is qualifying lasers at both the Glasgow fab and an external fab (Q4 FY2025 call). Competitively, SIVE positions itself as an aspiring merchant challenger to the incumbents — the CEO explicitly cites Lumentum ([[LITE]]) as a company "we aspire to grow like" (Q4 FY2025 call). It is a sub-scale entrant against [[LITE]] and [[COHR]], whom NVDA backed with $4B to secure laser supply.

## Other photonics (outside AIDC scope)

- **Automotive LIDAR** is the nearest concrete photonics revenue: the lead LIDAR customer is set to begin production ramps in Q4 2026, deploying Sivers optical amplifiers and DFB lasers across its product platforms; management gives a non-binding revenue potential of ~$23M (baseline) to ~$58M (upside) over 2026–2030, with automotive life-cycle tails of ~$53–138M beyond that horizon (Annual Report FY2025; Q4 FY2025 call). This is not AI-datacenter demand, but it is the clearest near-term proof point for the laser franchise.
- **Bio-sensing**: lasers and detectors for wearable health/fitness devices (a long-cycle Fortune-100 customer application) (Annual Report FY2025; Q4 FY2025 call).

## Wireless segment (out of vault scope)

Roughly 70% of FY2025 revenue, and the actual near-term production engine — but outside the vault's AI-datacenter scope, so noted only briefly: mmWave beamforming ICs, transceivers, and antenna modules for SATCOM, defense, and fixed-wireless/5G. Lead customer **ALL.SPACE** reached Technology Readiness Level 6 with the US Army (Next Generation Tactical Terminal), with production from 2026; the European **IRIS²** SATCOM program advanced three user-terminal vendors to RFP prototyping with Sivers beamformers; **Tachyon Networks** placed a first fixed-wireless production order; the US Chips Act Electronic-Warfare (EW-STAR) program was extended into year two at $6.6M; Korea's Doosan engaged Sivers for Ka-band SATCOM panels; and Sivers launched standard products (Cloudchaser beamformer chipset + Maverick antenna panel) shown at MWC Barcelona (Annual Report FY2025, Wireless; Q4 FY2025 call). No theme or chokepoint pages are created for this segment — it is out of scope.

## Open questions

1. **AIDC laser revenue inflection (2027+).** AIDC laser revenue is zero today; production readiness is targeted end-2026, revenue 2027+. **photonics_tier 4 → tier 3 upgrade trigger** if AIDC laser production revenue materializes at scale; **downgrade pressure** if the inflection slips beyond the 2027 window.
2. **POET / O-Net / Ayar Labs bilateral verification.** All three are counterparty-attribution only today (none is a vault canonical). Bilateral confirmation from any of their primary sources would move the partnerships from over-claim toward reciprocal-confirmation.
3. **"Big household name brand" identity.** The unnamed roadmap-discussion partner cited on the Q4 call is an over-claim pending any Tier 1 substantiation.
4. **Automotive LIDAR ramp execution.** The Q4 2026 LIDAR ramp ($23–58M 2026–2030) is the nearest real photonics revenue; execution is the clearest near-term proof point for the laser franchise.
5. **Going-concern / financing dependence.** Cash ~$3M against a ~SEK 50M+ adjusted-EBITDA burn; the Bootstrap facility extends runway via debt + convertible. Monitor runway, further dilution, and progress toward the ~$50–55M breakeven.
6. **Financial restatement resolution.** The "correction of financial figures," internal-control material weaknesses being remediated, and a possible Nasdaq New York dual listing are quality-of-earnings items to track.
7. **Wireless out-of-scope boundary.** SATCOM/defense/RF is the majority of revenue but out of vault scope; revisit only if a genuine wireless-AIDC convergence emerges.
8. **Merchant-challenger traction vs incumbent capacity.** Does a sub-scale merchant entrant win CPO/pluggable design-wins, or do incumbent capacity expansion ([[LITE]]/[[COHR]]) and integrator vertical integration ([[AAOI]]) absorb the shortage before SIVE reaches production? This is the core test of the bull thesis.

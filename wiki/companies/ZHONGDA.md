# ZHONGDA — Ningbo Zhongda Leader Intelligent Transmission (宁波中大力德智能传动股份有限公司 / 中大力德)

*Shenzhen A-share **002896**. PRC-GAAP (CAS), RMB. The vault's **fourth Chinese-filing ingest** (after [[SANHUA]] A+H, [[TUOPU]] A, and [[SHUANGHUAN]] A) and the **cohort's first small-cap** (FY2025 revenue ¥1.04B). All figures in ¥ unless noted; source set is filings-only (no earnings-call transcript — see Source audit notes).*

## Thesis role

Zhongda Leader is the **fourth China name the vault has taken from the top of the humanoid value-layer map** — the reducer / rotary-actuator layer (Goldman's top-ranked component, score 16/18) — and it is the one that resolves the layer's two open questions in *opposite* directions. On positioning it is the **strongest** of the four: it is the only one of the cohort that mass-produces **all three precision-reducer types — planetary, RV (cycloidal), and harmonic (strain-wave)** — and the only one that makes the **harmonic** type Goldman's row is actually named after; it is an **integrated actuator/joint-module maker** (reducer + motor + driver in one), spanning two value layers rather than one; and it is by far the **most humanoid-forward** of the four in its own disclosure. On financial quality it is the **weakest** — the smallest, with profit declining and accelerating downward, a collapsed operating cash flow, and a swing into net debt. That tension — *best positioning, weakest financials* — is what this page exists to document. It is anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] as a theme name, **outside the AI-datacenter frameworks** (the [[SHUANGHUAN]]/[[SANHUA]]/[[OUST]] precedent — assessed, but zero datacenter/cooling/semis exposure).

The two questions this page exists to answer (Vic's, from the readiness review):

**1 — Value-layer placement: integrated actuator + the only full-category (incl. harmonic) reducer maker.** Unlike [[SHUANGHUAN]] (a pure RV-reducer leader whose reducer leg is a minority of a gear-dominated company), Zhongda is a **transmission-components specialist** whose revenue is concentrated in the actuator/reducer space and spans the full stack. Its largest line is **智能执行单元 (integrated smart-actuator units) — ¥391.7M, 37.6% of revenue** (industrial-robot body components + 减速器+电机+驱动器 all-in-one modules), then **减速电机 (geared motors) ¥373.5M (35.9%)**, then **精密减速器 (precision reducers) ¥252.4M (24.2%)** (ZHONGDA FY2025 年报). It is one of the few Chinese firms to **批量生产 (mass-produce)** all three reducer types — planetary, RV, *and* harmonic — and the **harmonic** capability is the differentiator from Shuanghuan's RV: harmonic is the strain-wave type Goldman headlines for the rotary-actuator row, and the vault's primer names "Zhongda Leader (002896.SZ)" directly in its rotary-actuator / harmonic-reducer section (`raw/research/humanoid-robot-primer-report.md`). *The full value-layer analysis is below ("Value layer — integrated, and the only one doing harmonic").*

**2 — Healthy or risky? Risky — the weakest financial profile of the four China names, and deteriorating.** FY2025 revenue grew modestly (**¥1,040.9M, +6.6%**) but **net profit fell to ¥62.7M (−13.6%)**, ex-non-recurring profit **−20.3%**, with **ROE down to 5.29%** and **operating cash flow collapsed −85.3%** to ¥30.7M on a receivables blowout. The decline *accelerated* through the year (Q4 net profit near-breakeven) and **continued into Q1 2026** (net profit −36%, ex-items −43%, ROE 0.92%, negative operating cash flow). The audit is clean and the company is profitable and dividend-paying — it is not distressed — but on the financial-quality screen it is clearly the cohort's marginal name. *The full assessment is below ("Is it a healthy business?").*

**Placement.** `layer: outside` + AI `*_tier` absent + theme-anchored humanoid (no `humanoid_tier`) + `foreign_issuer: true`. Zhongda has **zero AI-datacenter exposure** — 数据中心 / 液冷 / 算力 / 服务器 / 散热 = **0 mentions** (a transmission-components maker; no thermal/cooling/datacenter business) — so, like [[SHUANGHUAN]] and unlike [[TUOPU]] (which earned `energy_power_tier 4` for a sized liquid-cooling leg), it carries **no AI `*_tier`**: honest-verdict `layer outside` + theme-anchored humanoid. ## Is it a healthy business? (financial-quality assessment — the spine)

**Risky — the weakest of the four China actuator-layer names, and deteriorating across the board.** As the cohort's first small-cap (revenue ¥1.04B vs the others' multi-billion), the "healthy vs risky" question was genuinely open going in; it resolves clearly to the marginal end:

- **Profit declining and accelerating downward.** FY2025 net profit −13.6%, ex-items −20.3%, with the decline back-half-loaded (Q4 ex-items near-breakeven) and continuing into Q1 2026 (−36% / ex-items −43%). The fall is *operational* — ex-non-recurring profit fell faster than headline, and management attributes Q1 2026 to gross-margin compression + higher expenses (ZHONGDA Q1 2026 季报).
- **ROE collapsing** — 5.29% FY (from 6.35%), 0.92% in Q1 2026.
- **Operating cash flow collapsed −85.3%** to ¥30.7M (negative −¥9.8M in Q1 2026). The cause is a **receivables blowout**: trade receivables jumped to **¥365.3M from ¥252.7M (≈+45%)** against revenue up only +6.6%, as sales shifted toward the distribution channel (经销 +27.5%) amid industry-wide collection difficulty the MD&A flags directly (账款回收难). Cash conversion, not headline profit, is the sharper problem — it ran all year (H1 op CF already −91.8%).
- **Net debt, not net cash** (the contrast with Sanhua/Shuanghuan). Short-term borrowings roughly **doubled to ¥209.5M** (11.31% of assets, from 6.11%) against cash of ¥92.5M, with fixed assets and intangibles pledged as collateral for those borrowings.
- **R&D pulling back** — R&D investment fell and R&D headcount declined −5.7% (281 → 265 people, 12.49% of the workforce) — a notable signal for a name whose thesis leans on a technology-intensive, full-category precision-reducer capability.
- **But not distressed.** The audit is a clean standard-unqualified opinion (标准无保留意见, 中汇/ZhongHui, signed 2026-04-14); the company is still profitable, founder-controlled (岑国建), and dividend-paying (¥1.20/10 shares). The honest read is *deteriorating quality on a stable base*, not *impairment* — the financial-quality screen flags it as the cohort's marginal name, the inverse of its positioning strength.

## Value layer — integrated, and the only one doing harmonic

**The rotary-actuator reducer layer, and why it is the prize.** A humanoid rotary joint (shoulder, elbow, wrist, hip) is driven by a stack of **frameless motor + precision reducer + encoder + sensor + controller**, and the **reducer is the precision bottleneck** — Goldman scores the reduction-gear layer **16/18, the top of its Exhibit-5 ranking**, on content value + technology barrier + manufacturing barrier; the reducer is ~30–50% of an actuator's cost and the actuator is ~33–60% of the whole robot, making the reducer roughly **机器人成本最高核心部件之一 (one of the highest-cost core robot components)** (see [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] and the primer, `raw/research/humanoid-robot-primer-report.md`). Reducers need scarce high-precision grinding, specialized metallurgy (harmonic flexsplines / RV cycloidal gears), and long qualification cycles — a durable chokepoint.

**Where Zhongda sits — broader than Shuanghuan, and integrated.**
- **The only full-category reducer maker in the cohort.** Zhongda is "国内少有实现行星减速器、RV 减速器、谐波减速器三大品类批量生产的公司之一" (one of the few Chinese companies mass-producing all three precision-reducer types — planetary, RV, and harmonic) (ZHONGDA FY2025 年报). Critically, that includes **harmonic (谐波 / strain-wave)** — the type Goldman's row is named after, and the one [[SHUANGHUAN]] (an RV leader) does not lead. The vault's primer lists "Zhongda Leader (002896.SZ)" in its rotary-actuator / harmonic-strain-wave section, so Zhongda is a *more direct* match to Goldman's harmonic framing than Shuanghuan was.
- **Integrated — it spans two value layers, not one.** Zhongda's largest segment is 智能执行单元 (integrated smart-actuator units, 37.6% of revenue): industrial-robot body components plus **减速器+电机+驱动器 一体机 (reducer + motor + driver all-in-one) modules** — "精密行星减速器+伺服电机+驱动" / "RV减速器+伺服电机+驱动" / "谐波减速器+伺服电机+驱动" 一体机. It therefore sits at both the **reducer layer (GS-16)** and the **actuator-assembly layer** (the [[SANHUA]]/[[TUOPU]] layer), doing the full stack rather than a single component. Its legacy core is 减速电机 (small/micro geared motors), where it has a stable, established domestic market position.

**The honest refinement (Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution — Goldman's framing vs primary): humanoid positioning, not yet humanoid revenue.** Zhongda is the *most* humanoid-forward of the four in its own disclosure (next section), but the precision-reducer line (¥252.4M) is overwhelmingly **industrial-robot** reducers today, the humanoid-specific products are at the developed/sampling stage, and **no humanoid OEM customer is named at primary**. So Goldman's "rotary actuator for humanoid robots" is, as with the other three, a forward extrapolation — here built on a real, full-category, *integrated* industrial transmission base. Because Goldman is Tier-3 forward analysis, this is a finding, not a kickoff (g') variance.

## The humanoid positioning (the most explicit of the cohort)

Where [[SHUANGHUAN]]'s filings carry **zero** humanoid terms, Zhongda's annual mentions 人形机器人 (humanoid robot) **16 times** and engages the theme directly (ZHONGDA FY2025 年报):

- **A dedicated forward-outlook section** — "人形机器人产业化加速，减速器需求空间巨大" (humanoid industrialization accelerating, reducer demand space huge) — positioning Zhongda as a **全品类精密减速器制造商 (full-category precision-reducer maker) that "将深度受益于人形机器人产业" (will deeply benefit from the humanoid robot industry)**, and citing GGII's humanoid TAM (~$1.0B 2024 → ~$15B 2030, >56% CAGR). (Tier-3 TAM figures relayed by the company; attributed, not vault-verified.)
- **Actual humanoid-specific products developed** — harmonic-reducer models **ZSHF-11 and ZSHD-14** for humanoid-robot joint precision, plus dexterous-hand (灵巧手) reducers for operating small objects. These exist (developed/sample stage); they are not yet a sized revenue line.
- **Humanoid industrialization disclosed as a formal *risk factor*** — "人形机器人产业化不及预期" (humanoid industrialization falling short of expectations): the MD&A states the industry is still early-commercialization, the technology route is unsettled, and mass-production timing is uncertain, so the related business ramp is directly exposed to that uncertainty. An honest two-sided framing — opportunity and risk on the same page.

## Core business, integrated modules, and the reducer leg

- **Geared motors + integrated units (the bulk; the core).** The legacy business is small and micro geared motors (减速电机, 35.9%), where Zhongda holds an established domestic position; the largest line is now the integrated 智能执行单元 (37.6%) — industrial-robot body components and reducer-motor-driver modules. Both are real, scaled businesses, but **outside the vault's AI-datacenter scope** (industrial transmission, not datacenter/semis).
- **The precision-reducer leg (24.2%, the vault-relevant one).** Planetary + RV + harmonic, ¥252.4M, +4.0% — the layer that earns the page. Smaller and lower-growth than Shuanghuan's reducer leg (¥795M, +21%), and lower-margin (GM 21.4%) — but broader on type (the only harmonic-capable one) and embedded in the integrated modules.
- **No subsidiary spin-off / IPO in train** (the contrast with Shuanghuan's Huandong). Zhongda's reducer business is in-house, not a separately-listing entity — so there is no domestic-IPO event to track, and no near-term Section 2.1 deconsolidation trigger.

## China exposure and investability

Zhongda is the vault's **fourth China-domiciled operating company** (after [[SANHUA]], [[TUOPU]], [[SHUANGHUAN]]) — it *is* a node of the China-controlled humanoid/industrial-robot supply chain, not a China-exposed foreign buyer. It is the **most domestically concentrated** of the four (≈91% domestic revenue; overseas only ~9%, though +35%). Like Shuanghuan and unlike Sanhua/Tuopu (which make permanent-magnet motors and so consume NdFeB), Zhongda makes the **reducer/gears — the precision-machining, non-magnet half of the actuator** — so it is *not* a direct rare-earth-magnet consumer, and the [[rare-earth-magnet-chokepoint]] squeeze does not apply to it. **Investability:** A-share-only (SZSE 002896), with **no pending Hong Kong H-listing and no subsidiary-IPO crack at all** — making it the **least accessible of the four** (more locked than even Shuanghuan, whose Huandong has a domestic STAR-Market IPO in train). *(Investor-access lens, per [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]]: that "access wall" is a US/international-brokerage lens — for an investor able to reach China A-shares directly, the Shenzhen A-share 002896 is ownable now, and under that lens the operative ranking is the chokepoint-quality gradient and business health, not the listing flag. Structural framing only; no buy/sell.)* See [[china-exposure]].

## Open questions

1. **Does the profit/cash-flow deterioration bottom — or is it structural?** Net profit fell through 2025 (Q4 near-breakeven) and again in Q1 2026 (−36%); operating cash flow collapsed −85% on a +45% receivables build. Watch H1 2026 for whether margin and collections stabilize, or whether the distribution-channel shift keeps stretching cash conversion.
2. **Does the harmonic / humanoid-reducer capability turn into revenue?** Zhongda has developed humanoid-joint harmonic models (ZSHF-11/ZSHD-14) and full-category coverage, but the reducer line is industrial today and humanoid is unsized. Watch for the first sized humanoid-reducer revenue or a named humanoid OEM — the trigger that would move "positioning" to "business."
3. **Is the R&D pullback a one-year cost response or a capability signal?** R&D spend and headcount both fell in FY2025 — unusual for a name whose thesis is technology-intensive precision reduction. Watch whether it reverses as profit recovers.
4. **Integrated-module strategy vs pure-component peers.** Zhongda sells modules (reducer+motor+driver) as well as components; does the integration win share against pure-reducer peers (Shuanghuan, LeaderDrive) and pure-assembly peers (Sanhua, Tuopu), or compress margins by competing on both fronts?
5. **Does the access picture ever change?** A-share-only with no H-listing or subsidiary IPO in train — the least accessible of the four to an international book (though directly ownable under the China-access lens).

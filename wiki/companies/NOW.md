# NOW — ServiceNow, Inc.

## Thesis role

ServiceNow is the vault's **first pure application-layer / SaaS company page** — an enterprise software platform (IT/HR/security/CRM workflows) that is a *consumer* of AI compute, not a supplier of any datacenter-supply-chain component. It is included narrowly, for the [[software-AI-moat-durability]] research goal: NOW is the canonical **system-of-record + workflow-lock-in** archetype, and the primary-source test of whether a seat-billed incumbent can survive the AI transition. Per honest-verdict discipline (Section 2.1), its fit to the broader supply-chain thesis is weak by design — this page is verdict-oriented around the research goal rather than supply-chain centrality.

**Placement framing.** NOW carries `layer: outside` + `outside` on all five per-domain tiers, following the demand-side placement precedent set by [[MSFT]] (the vault's first hyperscaler). NOW sits even further off the supply-side value-capture ladder than MSFT: a hyperscaler at least deploys the CAPEX and commissions custom silicon that the vault tracks downstream; NOW is an application-layer SaaS that merely *rents* that infrastructure. It captures no supply-side chokepoint rent.

**`frameworks.md` gap flag (for a future codification session; human-owned, not edited).** `frameworks.md` v10.1 has no application-layer / SaaS / software positioning category — the same gap family the [[MSFT]] hyperscaler page raised. Whether to add an explicit demand-side / application-layer placement (and how to bound it so the vault does not drift into general software coverage) is raised here per Section 1.1; `frameworks.md` and `_thesis.md` were not edited.

**Research-goal verdict (validated at primary; see [[software-AI-moat-durability]]):** **CONTESTED, leaning durable** — held. Durability metrics are strong and NOW is *actively converting* its billed unit from seat to consumption; the contested side is real (AI-revenue disclosure is call-only/bookings-based, and the market is skeptical of organic AI attribution).

## Billed-unit lens & AI monetization

This is the page's reason for inclusion — the primary-source test of the [[software-AI-moat-durability]] **billed-unit discriminator** (seat / consumption / outcome). NOW is the canonical **seat-billed incumbent actively converting to consumption**:

- **The conversion is real and quantified.** "50% of net new business now comes from a non-seat-based pricing model, including tokens and other assets" (CPO Amit Zavery, Q1 2026 call). Management frames a **hybrid model** — "predictable foundational seat licenses combined with usage-based scalability" (McDermott) — and announced the entire portfolio is "AI native… not a separate purchase. This is a deliberate break from sidecar AI." This is the seat→consumption transition the theme describes, observed in motion at a system-of-record incumbent.
- **But AI revenue is call-only bookings, not a clean line.** Now Assist net-new ACV was disclosed as ">$600M tracking $1B FY2026" (McDermott floated "$1.5B on run"), with Now Assist deals including 3+ products up ~70% YoY and the largest Now Assist deal >$15M NNACV — all on the **call (Tier 2)**. Management confirmed the methodology captures "only the incremental contribution from the AI capabilities" (NNACV bookings, not total consumption) and the **filings disclose no separate Now Assist revenue line** (cRPO $12.64B is not broken out by AI). This is the same disclosure-quality gap the theme flags for [[MSFT]] (call-only AI-ARR) and Salesforce (bookings vs consumption) — see Source audit notes.
- **Seat deflation — substantiated by case, not aggregate.** Management cited Robinhood "deflecting 70% of employee requests before human intervention… 2,200 hours of manual effort eliminated monthly" (Q1 2026 call) — a single customer case, evidence that AI auto-resolution reduces the human/fulfiller workload, but not a portfolio-aggregate seat-reduction figure.

## Durability & the system-of-record moat

The durable side of the verdict rests on retention plus a proprietary-workflow-data moat:

- **Retention/backlog:** 98% renewal rate (FY2025 10-K; 97% Q1 2026 including the Moveworks consolidation); RPO $27.7B (+23.5% cc) and cRPO $12.64B (+21% cc) at Q1 2026; subscription gross margin >80% (10-K). Customers spending >$1M grew >130% YoY (call); 16 deals >$5M and 5 >$10M NNACV in Q1.
- **The moat argument (system-of-record archetype):** management's "AI Control Tower" + "Context Engine" framing — "grounding each AI agent action in live context… we've now trained over 95 billion annual workflows and more than 7 trillion transactions" (McDermott, call) — is the claim that agents need NOW's proprietary, governed workflow context, i.e. the data layer is amplified rather than disintermediated. This is the durable-leg thesis the theme assigns to the system-of-record archetype; it remains a claim, not yet a disclosed revenue moat.
- **The contested side (priced in real time):** the stock fell ~12% after the print despite beats. Keith Weiss (Morgan Stanley) pressed on a "lack of clarity on the inorganic contribution" and when ServiceNow itself — not the acquired AI assets — sees "organic positive revisions"; Gabriela Borges (Goldman Sachs) pressed on whether customers "negotiate down… the more classic parts of the ServiceNow stack… to free up budget for the newer products." Management defended the core ("we're not getting negotiated down on the core") but did not break out organic vs inorganic AI uplift. The contested verdict is the market's verdict.

## Agentic AI & acquisitions

NOW's AI build-out is heavily inorganic, which is the root of the analyst-attribution skepticism:

- **Moveworks** (front-office AI agents) — closed Q1 2026, rebranded "EmployeeWorks," reportedly grew 5x YoY and "closed more deals than they did the entire year last year" (call).
- **Veza** (identity governance — "maps access across people, machines, and AI agents") and **Armis** (security/asset discovery; closed earlier than expected, accelerating subscription growth), plus **Pyramid Analytics**.
- CRM NNACV grew >5x YoY with deal count +80% (call). Single operating segment; ~$600B+ stated TAM ("AI Control Tower for business reinvention").

## Open questions

1. **Clean AI revenue line.** Will NOW ever disclose Now Assist *revenue* (Tier 1), or does it stay a call-only NNACV-bookings construct on an "incremental-only" methodology? A clean consumption line would strengthen the durable verdict.
2. **Seat deflation at portfolio scale.** Does the Robinhood-style 70% deflection generalize into a measurable fulfiller-seat reduction across the base — and does the consumption overlay offset it (the central contested-leg test)?
3. **Organic vs inorganic AI uplift.** Can NOW show organic AI revisions, or does the AI growth remain attributable to Moveworks/Veza/Armis (the analyst skepticism)?
4. **Core-module cannibalization.** Do customers negotiate down the seat-priced core to fund AI, compressing the foundation?
5. **Placement codification.** Resolution of the application-layer / SaaS placement gap (with [[MSFT]]).

# Astera Labs (ALAB)

## Thesis role

Layer 3 specialized designer — the second Layer 3 company page in the vault alongside [[MRVL]]. ALAB designs connectivity silicon (PCIe/CXL retimers, Ethernet cable modules, CXL memory controllers) and AI fabric switches (Scorpio) for hyperscaler data center customers. Fabless model; sole IC fabrication partner is [[TSM]] (ALAB 10-K FY2025).

**CPO platform contestant.** One of five named contestants in [[CPO-platform-battle]] via Scorpio X-Series scale-up switching and the aiXscale photonics acquisition (November 2025). Unlike [[MRVL]]'s commercially aggressive CPO framing ($500M/$1B revenue targets) and [[AVGO]]'s active dismissal ("bright, shiny objects"), Mohan positions ALAB in a phased-coexistence framework: copper and optical "coexisting from a system standpoint" with scale-up optical in 2028 (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). See [[CPO-platform-battle]] for Mohan's phased-coexistence perspective and the three-way scale-up technology disagreement.

**photonics_tier 4 — revised from frameworks.md Tier 3.** frameworks.md places ALAB at photonics_tier 3 based on the aiXscale acquisition and optical roadmap. Source evidence warrants a downgrade to Tier 4: aiXscale was acquired for $31.1M cash — a small bolt-on, not a platform investment (cf. [[MRVL]]'s ~$3.25B-$5.5B Celestial AI acquisition). The 10-K describes aiXscale as "a privately held company specializing in fiber-chip coupling technologies" with $14.5M in IPR&D and $16.9M in goodwill (ALAB 10-K FY2025). Zero photonics revenue is disclosed. The 10-K contains zero mentions of CPO, co-packaged optics, silicon photonics, or photonic — the aiXscale acquisition paragraph is the only photonics-adjacent content. On the call, Tate frames new optical opportunities as "18-24 months on the earlier side" from start to revenue (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). ALAB's photonics capability is nascent and pre-revenue; Tier 4 reflects the current state. This revision is flagged for frameworks.md cleanup.

**NVLink Fusion ecosystem partner.** ALAB announced custom connectivity solutions enabling NVIDIA's NVLink Fusion scale-up architecture for hybrid racks (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). Both Amazon and NVIDIA chose Astera as a partner — Mohan emphasizes this dual endorsement. The NVLink Fusion opportunity attaches to XPUs on a 1:1 basis, with revenue "in line with the switched opportunity" of a UALink switch (Mohan, ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). This positions ALAB as the first vault company explicitly in the NVDA NVLink ecosystem as a connectivity enabler rather than a competitor.

### Scorpio X-Series 320-lane scale-up fabric — Q1 FY2026 inflection

Scorpio X-Series moved from "pre-production shipments to initial volume ramp during Q1" (Mohan, Q1 FY2026 call). In-network compute "offloads critical accelerator-to-accelerator communication and computation directly onto the switch, dramatically reducing the networking overhead during large-scale training and inference" (Mohan, Q1 FY2026 call). Scorpio P-Series PCIe 6.0 portfolio also expanded to 32-320 lanes "diverse system topologies, making it the broadest in the industry" (Mohan, Q1 FY2026 call). PCIe Gen 6 across AI fabric + signal conditioning contributed >1/3 of total Q1 revenue.

Production volumes ramp H2 2026 (Lynch, Q1 FY2026 call): "we expect Scorpio X-Series shipments to increase in Q2, along with initial shipments of our new Scorpio X 320 lane, then ramp to full volume production in the H2 of 2026." Lynch projects: "we would expect to see that Scorpio will become our largest product line by the end of the year, which is strong performance for a product line that was only 15% of total company revenue last year. As we go throughout the year, I would expect to see X-Series revenue, exceeding P-Series."

## Product portfolio

**Aries PCIe/CXL smart retimers and cable modules.** Signal conditioning for PCIe and CXL links between processors, storage, and network controllers. Commercial since 2020. Revenue grew ~70% YoY in FY2025 (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). Active Electrical Cable (AEC) assemblies provide the cable module form factor.

**Taurus Ethernet smart cable modules.** Copper Ethernet connectivity modules. Revenue grew approximately 4x YoY in FY2025 (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). Positioned for rack-level network connectivity.

**Scorpio smart fabric switches.** Two sub-families:
- **Scorpio P-Series:** PCIe Gen 6.0 scale-out switching. Mohan: "the only PCIe 6 fabric shipping in volume in the market" (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). Exceeded 15% of FY2025 revenue, up from a 10% initial target (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call, Tate).
- **Scorpio X-Series:** Scale-up GPU-to-GPU fabric switching. 10+ customer engagements. Initial quantities shipping H2 2026, volume ramps in 2027 (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). Tate: "will put us on a trajectory for it to be our biggest product line" (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call).

**Leo CXL memory controllers.** Memory bandwidth expansion and pooling over CXL serial links.

**COSMOS software suite.** Embedded in all hardware products. Link management, fleet management, and RAS (reliability/availability/serviceability) capabilities.

## Scale-up fabric and competitive positioning

**TAM sizing.** Merchant scale-up switching market: ~$20B annually by 2030 (Gajendra, ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). Total served addressable market across all product families: $25B by 2030, a >10x expansion from current (Mohan, ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). ALAB claims "pole position to service at least half of this market" near-to-medium term (Gajendra, ALAB Q4 FY2025 call).

**UALink positioning.** AWS announced Trainium 4 (2027 ramp) will support UALink; AMD MI500 also announced UALink support. Mohan: "UALink ecosystem is humming. We've got great availability of IP, a lot of vendor announcements" (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). ALAB's UALink solution targets the 2027 ramp.

**NVLink Fusion.** Custom connectivity solutions translating native XPU protocols into NVLink for hybrid racks. Revenue content per unit "in line with the switched opportunity" of a UALink switch. Both Amazon and NVIDIA chose Astera as partner — Mohan frames this as an endorsement of trust (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). The exact mix of NVLink Fusion vs. native UALink deployment at the lead hyperscaler customer "remains to be seen" (Mohan, ALAB Q4 FY2025 call).

**NVLink Fusion Q1 FY2026 substantive engagement — A1 mode strengthening, counterparty-attribution-only preserved.** Q1 FY2026 evidence at primary substantively strengthens NVLink Fusion ecosystem partner positioning while retaining counterparty-attribution-only A1 mode per [[nvidia-supply-chain-commitments]] classification. Gajendra (Q1 FY2026 call): "we are engaging with multiple customers to enable NVIDIA Fusion scale-up architecture for hybrid racks. Our strong historical execution delivering intelligent connectivity solutions for NVIDIA-based systems positions us well to develop and design within these new custom programs." On competitive landscape: "this is an ecosystem that NVIDIA is creating with NVLink Fusion. There are others, but for us, you know, the main thing is that we have been engaged with real customers, real applications" (Gajendra, Q1 FY2026 call). On specific design win commitment: "We're very deep in engagement for an initial design win in collaboration with NVIDIA and then the hyperscaler. That project is going well... we do expect that this will start providing some meaningful revenue in 2027 and then growing from there" (Gajendra, Q1 FY2026 call). The framing positions NVIDIA as ecosystem architect with ALAB as connectivity enabler executing custom design within that ecosystem — A1 mode classification remains counterparty-attribution-only because no bilateral NVDA-side reciprocal-confirmation surfaces in this ingest scope (would require NVDA Q1 fiscal 2027 refresh per [[nvidia-supply-chain-commitments]] Open question #9). Revenue 2027+ remains forward-looking commercial commitment vs current operational scope.

**Microsoft Azure named hyperscaler at primary — first named hyperscaler customer.** Mohan (Q1 FY2026 call) on Leo CXL memory controller deployment: "our Leo memory controller is on track for an early ramp of CXL-attached memory with Microsoft Azure M-series virtual machines." Follow-up framing: "We are on track for deploying this with Microsoft Azure for their M-series instances at the data center. That's in private beta now, expected to go into general availability end of the year" (Mohan, Q1 FY2026 call). Microsoft Azure named for the first time at ALAB primary — closes the prior gap whereby ALAB's >70% end-customer concentration disclosure was structurally inferable but unnamed at all primary venues. Microsoft Azure named ≠ confirmation that Microsoft is the >70% end customer (Leo CXL is one product line; revenue scale unspecified); but the naming establishes Microsoft Azure as named hyperscaler customer at primary — distinct from inferable-only baseline. Combined with prior Amazon warrant disclosure (S14 baseline), ALAB primary venues now name two of the four major hyperscalers (Amazon + Microsoft).

**KV cache offload — second custom hyperscaler design win at primary.** Mohan (Q1 FY2026 call): "we picked up our second design win of a custom application for CXL earlier this quarter. We are working with our customer, which is an additional new hyperscaler. We're working with them on at scale performance test and expect that one to ship for revenue in 2027." Custom solutions business now ≥2 hyperscaler design wins: (1) NVLink Fusion (NVIDIA + hyperscaler initial design win), (2) Leo CXL KV cache offload (additional new hyperscaler). Gajendra positions custom solutions as "a new multi-billion dollar market opportunity for Astera Labs" (Q1 FY2026 call). Business model distinct from standard products: "We are developing a product for a given customer under a business model that would include NRE and other ways of paying for the development and of course the product revenue that comes when the product starts shipping" (Mohan, Q1 FY2026 call).

**UALink 2.2 specification + UAL switch ASIC.** UALink Consortium published 2.2 specification in early April 2026 defining in-network compute chiplets manageability + 200 gig performance (Gajendra, Q1 FY2026 call). ALAB UAL switch positioned for hyperscaler ASIC/GPU launches "sometime in 2027" — Amazon (Trainium) + AMD (MI500) named publicly (Gajendra, Q1 FY2026 call). UAL switch vs PCIe switch dollar content distinction: "the value that we are able to charge for these devices will be substantially higher in terms of what we are able to do for the PCIe switches" (Mohan, Q1 FY2026 call) — value uplift via combination of higher complexity, increased radix/lane count, and media attach evolution from majority copper to copper+NPO.

**Silicon dollar content >$1,000 per XPU.** Mohan (Q1 FY2026 call): "This trend is quickly increasing our silicon dollar content opportunity beyond $1,000 per XPU within AI racks and positions Astera Labs to outperform our end market growth rates." Verified at primary; first explicit market sizing claim quantification at ALAB primary venues. Drivers: AI fabric rack-scale architecture + higher compute capabilities + PCIe Gen 6 transition + 800-gig + 1.6T Ethernet connectivity transitions.

**UALink vs. ESUN protocol coexistence.** Mohan frames scale-up networking as a multi-protocol market: "hyperscalers are going to leverage the type of solutions that their software stack is designed for." Memory-centric protocol users (NVLink, PCIe) are "likely to continue to use that and transition to UALink." Ethernet users "are likely to stay with Ethernet and then maybe move to ESUN." ALAB is developing solutions "where our customers are asking us to, which happens to be PCI Express, UALink, and now increasingly on NVLink Fusion" (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call). This implicitly concedes the Ethernet/ESUN market segment to Ethernet-heritage players.

**Named competitors.** Broadcom ([[AVGO]]), Credo Technology, Marvell ([[MRVL]]), Microchip Technology, Montage Technology, Parade Technologies, and Rambus (ALAB 10-K FY2025). ALAB names both Layer 1 and Layer 3 peers as direct competitors — consistent with Layer 3 competitive dynamics.

## Customer concentration

ALAB presents the most complex customer concentration picture in the vault — a significant divergence between billing-address data (Tier 1 filings) and end-customer concentration (Tier 1 risk factor disclosure).

**End-customer concentration (10-K risk factor).** "In 2025, one end customer represented more than 70% of our revenue; the top three end customers represented an aggregate of approximately 86% of our revenue" (ALAB 10-K FY2025). The 10-K uses purchase order end-customer designations to estimate this figure. The >70% single end-customer concentration is the highest in the vault, surpassing [[AVGO]]'s 42% single-distributor concentration and [[AAOI]]'s 53.1% DigiComm concentration.

**Billing-address customer data (10-K Note 2).** Five customers exceeded 10% of FY2025 revenue by billing address: A (20%), B (20%), C (17%), D (16%), E (11%) — totaling 84% (ALAB 10-K FY2025). These are distributors and manufacturing partners, not end customers. The fragmentation across five billing-address customers that consolidate to one >70% end customer reveals the depth of a single hyperscaler relationship flowing through multiple supply chain channels.

**Q1 FY2026 billing-address customer concentration refresh (Session 55).** Five customers exceeded 10% in Q1 FY2026 by billing address: A (29%), B (21%), C (16%), D (12%), E (12%) — totaling 90% (ALAB 10-Q Q1 FY2026). Customer A shifted from 20% (FY2025) to 29% (Q1 FY2026), consolidating concentration into the lead billing-address position. Customer F (19% in Q1 FY2025 comparative) fell below 10% disclosure threshold. Billing-address concentration remains a 5-customer landscape but with greater Customer A weight. Per the prior baseline disclosure that billing-address customers consolidate to a single >70% end customer, the Q1 FY2026 concentration shift may reflect manufacturing partner routing changes rather than end-customer composition changes; no Q1 FY2026 end-customer concentration disclosure (10-Q filings do not require risk factor refreshes that the 10-K provides). Reciprocally, Microsoft Azure named at primary as Leo CXL hyperscaler customer (private beta to GA end of 2026) — first explicit non-Amazon hyperscaler naming at ALAB primary. No analyst Q&A on Q1 FY2026 call addressed >70% end-customer concentration risk — analyst silence pattern from S14 Q4 FY2025 call persists across two consecutive calls.

**Analyst silence on concentration risk.** Across 8 analysts on the Q4 FY2025 earnings call, zero asked about the >70% single end-customer concentration disclosed in the 10-K filed the same day. Multiple analysts reference "lead hyperscaler customer" or "lead customer" in technology architecture questions without framing concentration as a risk. Management frames customer diversification positively — Mohan: "we continue to diversify our business profile with several new design wins across multiple customers" (ALAB Q4 FY2025 call) — without acknowledging the current extreme concentration. The analyst silence on >70% concentration is among the most conspicuous in the vault, comparable to the [[AAOI]] CPO displacement analyst silence. Per the Analyst silence as signal convention: either the covering analysts view single-customer concentration as structural and non-actionable for a design-win-driven Layer 3 company, or the coverage universe is not pricing concentration risk at this stage of ALAB's growth trajectory.

**Amazon relationship.** The Amazon warrant (see below) confirms a deep commercial relationship. The 10-K identifies the warrant holder as "Amazon NV Investment Holdings LLC" but does not explicitly name Amazon as the >70% end customer, though the structural inference is strong. See [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] for the cross-company warrant pattern.

## Supply chain

**Sole-source fabrication.** "We use a fabless manufacturing model and partner with TSMC to fabricate all of our ICs" (ALAB 10-K FY2025). Single manufacturing partner dependency — the most explicit sole-source statement in the vault alongside [[AVGO]]'s 95% TSMC figure.

**Geographic revenue shifts.** Revenue by billing address shows dramatic geographic shifts: Taiwan dropped from 68% (Q3 FY2024) to 16% (Q3 FY2025) while Singapore surged from 11% to 42%. FY2025 full-year: Singapore $277M (33%), China $256M (30%), Taiwan $247M (29%), US $27M (3%) (ALAB 10-K FY2025). The filing caveats that billing address is not end-customer location — the shifts reflect supply chain routing through different manufacturing partners, not changes in end-customer demand geography.

**Zero debt.** $1.19B cash and marketable securities with no borrowings. Total liabilities $168M, primarily operating leases and accounts payable (ALAB 10-K FY2025).

## Open questions

1. **>70% end-customer concentration risk.** Is ALAB's growth trajectory sustainable with one end customer representing >70% of revenue? The Amazon warrant ($6.5B) deepens the relationship but also deepens dependency. Watch for diversification evidence: Scorpio X at 10+ customer engagements may broaden the base if volume ramps materialize in 2027.
2. **aiXscale acquisition cost — kickoff hypothesis falsification.** The kickoff prompt hypothesized aiXscale at $70-150M. Source evidence shows $31.1M cash (ALAB 10-K FY2025, Note 3) — materially lower than hypothesized. A separate ~$70M asset acquisition (Israel Design Center) closed in February 2026 per subsequent events disclosure. The hypothesis may have conflated the two transactions; external reporting at the time may have combined both figures. The $31.1M is authoritative (Tier 1). The modest size reinforces the photonics_tier 4 assessment — this was a technology tuck-in, not a platform acquisition. However, absence of evidence at scale is not evidence of absence — aiXscale's strategic value may exceed its purchase price if the fiber-chip coupling technology enables ALAB's optical roadmap.
3. **Scorpio X execution risk.** Initial quantities H2 2026, volume ramp 2027. 10+ customer engagements but zero disclosed design wins. Tate projects Scorpio (P + X combined) to become "our biggest product line" — requires significant ramp from the current >15% base.
4. **NVLink Fusion mix uncertainty.** The exact split between UALink native deployment and NVLink Fusion custom solutions at the lead hyperscaler "remains to be seen" (Mohan). Revenue content is similar per unit, but the technology choice affects ALAB's competitive positioning — NVLink Fusion locks ALAB into the NVIDIA ecosystem; UALink preserves multi-vendor optionality.
5. **Optical revenue timeline.** Tate: 18-24 months from start to revenue for optical. aiXscale acquired November 2025 implies earliest revenue mid-2027. Mohan: scale-up optical in 2028. [[MRVL]] targets $500M CPO run rate by Q4 FY2028 — what is ALAB's equivalent?
6. **Material weaknesses.** Two material weaknesses (risk assessment process, IT general controls) were unremediated as of September 30, 2025 (ALAB 10-Q Q3 FY2025). Monitor for remediation disclosure in future filings.
7. **Gross margin trajectory.** Declining: 77.7% → 76.2% → 75.7% → guided 74% → actual 76.4% Q1 FY2026 (+70 bps QoQ surprise to upside) → guided ~73% Q2 FY2026 (incl. 200 bps warrant impact). Q1 FY2026 expansion reflects "lower mix of hardware sales across our signal conditioning portfolio" per Lynch — Q2 reversion to ~73% includes the first full-quarter warrant impact. Can ALAB sustain >70% non-GAAP gross margins as hardware mix increases and warrant contra-revenue ramps?
8. **NVLink Fusion design win revenue 2027+ — bilateral verification deferred.** Q1 FY2026 substantive engagement at primary; A1 mode counterparty-attribution-only preserved per [[nvidia-supply-chain-commitments]]. NVDA Q1 fiscal 2027 refresh (post-May 20, 2026 release) candidate venue for bilateral verification. Revenue 2027+ is forward-looking commercial commitment — track at subsequent ALAB refreshes for actualization trajectory.
9. **Custom solutions multi-billion dollar market sizing — execution + concentration tension.** Gajendra: "new multi-billion dollar market opportunity for Astera Labs." Two custom hyperscaler design wins disclosed (NVLink Fusion + Leo CXL KV cache). NRE-based business model distinct from standard products. Mohan: "we will not take any opportunity that comes our way because sometimes the custom business can lead into, you know, could be so unique to one customer and with lot of risk and margin and so on" — execution discipline framing. Custom solutions ramp may either diversify customer base (multi-hyperscaler design wins) OR amplify concentration (additional single-hyperscaler dependency). Open question pre-registered for subsequent refresh cycles.
10. **Optical roadmap commercial revenue 2027 — first photonics revenue stream.** High-density detachable reusable fiber connectors expected to ship volume starting 2027; pre-NPO + pre-CPO commercial revenue stream emerges first.

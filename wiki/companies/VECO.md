# VECO — Veeco Instruments Inc.

## Thesis role

VECO is a semiconductor equipment company whose vault inclusion rests on two analytical rationales, neither of which positions it as thesis-central:

1. **InP supply chain enabler:** VECO's Lumina+ MOCVD (metal-organic chemical vapor deposition) tools grow the epitaxial layers on InP and GaAs substrates that become laser devices. This positions VECO one step downstream of [[AXTI]] (substrates) and one step upstream of [[LITE]], [[COHR]], and [[AAOI]] (device fabrication) in the InP photonics supply chain. However, Compound Semiconductor is VECO's smallest segment at 9% of FY2025 revenue ($60M), and the company does not break out InP-specific equipment revenue within that segment.

2. **AI semiconductor equipment exposure:** VECO's dominant Semiconductor segment (72% of revenue) serves advanced packaging — a direct beneficiary of AI chip demand. Advanced packaging revenue doubled from $75M to $150M in FY2025, driven by the same [[TSMC-CoWoS]] capacity buildout tracked elsewhere in the vault.

The photonics fit is weak. VECO's photonics-relevant revenue is a small fraction of a small segment, and the company does not mention CPO, co-packaged optics, silicon photonics, or InP substrates by name in any of its filings or earnings call. The vault value is primarily as a cross-reference node in the [[InP-supply]] chain and as a corroborating data point for AI-driven semiconductor equipment demand — not as a photonics thesis play.

## Product portfolio

VECO manufactures semiconductor process equipment across four technology areas:

- **Ion beam and laser processing:** Deposition, etch, and surface treatment for semiconductor and data storage. The dominant revenue contributor.
- **MOCVD (Lumina+):** Epitaxial growth systems for compound semiconductors — GaAs and InP-based materials for photonics, RF, and power devices. The photonics-relevant product line.
- **Lithography:** Advanced packaging lithography for fan-out wafer-level packaging and heterogeneous integration.
- **MBE (molecular beam epitaxy):** Research-grade epitaxial growth for scientific and specialty applications.

## Semiconductor equipment and AI demand

VECO's Semiconductor segment is the primary financial driver and the strongest AI demand signal in the company's disclosures. Advanced packaging revenue doubled from $75M to $150M in FY2025 (VECO Q4 FY2025 call), directly tied to advanced packaging capacity expansion for AI accelerators.

Management referenced a "leading Taiwanese semiconductor customer" multiple times as the primary demand driver (VECO Q4 FY2025 call) — a pattern consistent with [[TSM]] as the dominant advanced packaging customer, though VECO does not name TSMC directly. This aligns with the reciprocal non-naming pattern documented in [[TSM]].

Backlog of $555M (+35% YoY) is heavily weighted toward Semiconductor. The FY2026 guidance of ~$550M for Semiconductor (+15%) implies continued AI-driven demand without the doubling trajectory of FY2025.

## Compound semiconductor and photonics

Compound Semiconductor is VECO's smallest reporting segment and the only one with direct photonics supply chain relevance. FY2025 revenue was $60M, declining 25.9% from $81M in FY2024. This is the segment housing the Lumina+ MOCVD tools that grow epitaxial layers on InP and GaAs substrates for laser and photodetector manufacturing.

The 10-Q provides the clearest photonics connection: "photonics orders driven by demand for... optical communication solutions for datacom industry" (VECO 10-Q Q3 FY2025). However, the 10-K describes the segment broadly as serving "advanced communications networks, 5G, next-generation autonomous vehicles, and other consumer electronics" without singling out datacenter photonics.

Lumina+ is described on the call as handling "arsenide phosphide" materials (VECO Q4 FY2025 call). InP is not specifically named in any filing or call. The company does not disclose what share of Compound Semiconductor revenue comes from photonics vs. RF, power, or LED applications.

**Epiluvac impairment:** VECO took a $28.1M impairment charge on its Epiluvac SiC epitaxy business in FY2025 (VECO 10-K FY2025), reflecting weakness in the SiC power semiconductor market. This is analytically irrelevant to the photonics thesis but explains the operating income decline.

## Axcelis merger

In January 2026, VECO announced a merger with Axcelis Technologies (ACLS) in an all-stock transaction (0.3575 VECO shares per AXLS share). Expected close: H2 2026, subject to regulatory approval and shareholder vote (VECO 10-K FY2025).

The merger creates a broader semiconductor equipment platform combining VECO's deposition and etch capabilities with Axcelis's ion implantation tools. The combined entity would have enhanced exposure to advanced packaging and AI semiconductor manufacturing. For the photonics thesis, the merger is neutral — Axcelis has no photonics-relevant product lines, and the combination does not change VECO's Compound Semiconductor segment positioning.

**Axcelis merger close trajectory at Q1 FY2026 (Session 58 refresh).** Per CEO Miller Q1 FY2026 call: *"The transaction has been approved by shareholders of both companies, and all regulatory approvals have been received other than antitrust approval in China. We remain engaged with the authorities in China and continue to expect the transaction to close in the H2 of 2026. Integration planning is progressing well, and we remain excited about the strategic fit and long-term potential for value creation."* China antitrust approval is the sole remaining regulatory gate; H2 2026 close maintained.

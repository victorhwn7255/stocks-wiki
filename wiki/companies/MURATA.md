## Thesis role

Murata is the **clear, full-stack leader of the MLCC oligopoly** — the tiny multilayer ceramic capacitors that stabilize power right next to the GPU/HBM/ASIC, which Goldman dubbed the AI server's "new memory" (the 3rd-largest server BOM item after compute and memory). It is the only player that owns the *whole* chain — in-house + JV barium-titanate powder, the ~1,000-layer stacking process, and the SimSurfing design-tool lock-in that designs Murata parts into the power-delivery network. Capacitors were **¥936.4bn (+12.6%), 51% of revenue**, on a **fortress balance sheet** (85% equity ratio, ¥653.7bn cash, ~91% of net income returned to holders). On the vault's chokepoint-quality gradient it sits at precision-manufacturing/yield — and these primary sources upgrade the MLCC theme's best evidence from Tier-3 rumor to Tier-1/2 fact.

But the honest verdict has two halves that must be read together, and the second is the brake. **Murata is a diversified, AI-*minority* name whose chokepoint is softer than [[IBIDEN]]'s substrate.** The *whole company* grew just **+5% with flat operating profit** (¥281.8bn, +0.8%) — because servers+PCs are only **~17% of revenue** (smartphones 36%, −3%; autos 26%, +5%), and the Devices & Modules segment's ROIC turned **negative (−3.5%)** on smartphone/comms-module weakness. The MLCC chokepoint itself is, per [[MLCC-oligopoly]]'s verdict, **"quality-but-cyclical with one genuinely structural kernel"** — the durable, pricing-power part is *only* the top-bin chip-adjacent parts; the rest of the BOM is cyclical and price-eroding (company-wide capacitor prices are still *falling*, below). So this is a **fortress quality-compounder with an AI kicker, not a pure AI-chokepoint play** — the *opposite* trade-off from a pure-but-fragile single-material name like Nittobo.

**Placement — why `outside`, not a numbered tier.** Layer 4 (component/material supplier; no integrated-systems; Caveat #9 N/A). The per-domain tiers are **`materials_tier: outside`, theme-anchored to [[MLCC-oligopoly]]** — deliberately *not* a numbered `materials_tier` like [[IBIDEN]]'s 2. The difference is honest, not arbitrary: Ibiden's tier is *thesis-anchored* (it's named in `_thesis.md` Rank 5 Materials), whereas **MLCC / capacitors / Murata are named nowhere in `_thesis.md` or `frameworks.md`** (the Materials ranks are GOES / gases / rare-earths / copper / substrate only). Assigning a number would be inventing a thesis placement the human-owned anchors don't carry — so this lands `outside` + theme-anchored (the [[INOD]]/humanoid precedent), with a Phase-4 proposal to Vic to add an MLCC / passive-components node to `_thesis.md` Materials (at which point a real tier could follow). Single-domain (AI-datacenter).

## The MLCC chokepoint — Murata's structural position

The durable kernel, in Murata's own words. MLCCs decouple power for AI silicon: GPU load swings cause microsecond current spikes the far-away voltage regulator can't answer, so chip-adjacent capacitors bridge the gap. Murata's moat is **full-stack** — it self-supplies the sub-100nm barium-titanate powder (most rivals buy merchant, e.g. from Sakai Chemical), runs the ~1,000-layer process, and locks designs in with its SimSurfing tool. The structural kernel is the **top bin**: management says of data centers, *"the structure… is one where procurement is prioritized over price… the number of manufacturers able to supply it is very limited… we have not received any requests at all to lower prices"* (Omori, EVP Ceramic Capacitor BU, full-year conference, Apr 30 2026).

**Share — carry the range, with the correction.** Estimates conflict and are sell-side, not disclosure: Korean press ~Murata 45% / SEMCO 40%; Chinese media ~Murata 70%. **Correction to the prior [[MLCC-oligopoly]] framing:** Murata's ">50%" claim (Nakajima) in these sources is for **high-frequency *modules* (smartphones), NOT MLCC** — Murata discloses *no* specific MLCC share %. So: clearly #1, but the precise number is unconfirmed; the goal stated is "No.1 in capacitors."

## AI-datacenter demand — server, ASIC, the top bin

The single most important upgrade: **Murata now breaks out data-center sales as its own line for the first time** (the disclosure-grade split [[MLCC-oligopoly]] pre-registered as the thing that would "re-anchor the theme"): data-center revenue **+¥75bn / +74%** in the year ended Mar 2026, guided **+¥148bn / +84%** for FY2027. And the headline server-MLCC guide is primary-confirmed: *"we had previously said a volume increase of approximately 30%, but… we now see something closer to about 80% on an annualized basis"* — **~85-90% server-capacitor growth** (Omori, Apr 30 2026). *"Data center investment centered on AI servers is clearly entering a major expansion cycle"* (Nakajima). The product pull is toward small-case, high-cap parts: *"near TPUs and GPUs, small-size, high-capacity, lower-voltage products are required"* (Omori) — plus high-voltage parts for the 800V rack transition and **embedded MLCC** (capacitors buried in the package substrate next to the die; see [[pcb-interconnect-substrate-chokepoint]]).

## Capacity & pricing — the buyer dynamic, honestly framed

- **Capacity:** an **¥80bn "emergency additional capacity investment, mainly for server-use MLCCs"** (≈¥40bn recognized; Nakajima, Apr 30 2026), stepping Murata's expansion pace from ~10%/yr to **~20% over FY26-27**, utilization targeted **90-95%** (Q4 ~95%). Total capex ¥247.8bn (FY2026) → ¥250bn (FY2027), "primarily… servers." (Note: this *breaks* the GS "~10%/yr capacity ceiling" the MLCC theme cited — a mild knock to the scarcity-durability case.)
- **Pricing — the bull-hype debunked at primary.** The rumored "15-35% MLCC hike effective April 1" is **rejected**: *"we will not adopt a policy of simply raising prices… at this point, we are not thinking about raising prices in the near term"* (Omori). The profit bridge shows capacitor prices still **falling** — a **−¥105bn** price hit (FY2025) narrowing to a guided **−¥73bn** (FY2026); the gain is **mix + volume, not price** (Omori confirmed to Goldman's analyst that it's "mix improvement," not hikes). The genuine pricing power is confined to the top bin (no price-cut requests there), and even there Murata chooses relationship/share defense over gouging — the same restraint seen at [[IBIDEN]] and Nittobo.

## Multi-segment honest-verdict — the dilution

Per the [[CAT]]/[[ENS]] multi-segment pattern: the AI story is one slice of a diversified electronics giant. **Components (capacitors + inductors) = the AI engine** (22.4% ROIC, +12.3%); **Devices & Modules = the drag** (−5.9%, ROIC −3.5%) on smartphone/comms-module weakness (HF/comms module −11%). End-market: smartphones (36%, −3%) and autos (26%, +5%) are the *bulk*; servers+PCs (17%, +28%) are the *growth*. So the AI inflection is genuine but **doesn't yet move the whole company** — the flat operating profit is the proof.

## Cyclicality & the bear clock

MLCC is the *most* cycle-exposed of the durable AI-materials nodes. Two honest brakes:
- **The cycle template is real.** The 2017-18 MLCC super-cycle (prices +5-10×) unwound brutally in 2019 (Yageo revenue −34.5%). The bear case (per [[MLCC-oligopoly]]): a funded supply wave (Murata's own ¥80bn + 20% expansion; SEMCO/Walsin/China adds) lands **2027-28** into a hyperscaler-capex deceleration — and the FY2027 +34.8%-OP-on-+7.1%-revenue leverage bet reverses if server demand disappoints. Management is candid about the uncertainty: *"what we still cannot see clearly is whether it stops there… could remain a doubling-type situation for the next two to three years"* (Omori).
- **But the double-booking is quarantined.** The channel hoarding/double-booking the bear case flags is in **consumer-grade** parts — at the AI top bin Murata says the *opposite*: order intake **exceeds** capacity and inventory *"did not build up"* because it shipped aggressively. The bear risk is real for the low end, not (yet) the top bin.

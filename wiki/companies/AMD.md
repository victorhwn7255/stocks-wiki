# AMD — Advanced Micro Devices, Inc.

## Thesis role

AMD occupies **Layer 1 (Platform Definers)** as the merchant alternative to [[NVDA]] across AI accelerator (Instinct MI series) + merchant GPU (Radeon) + x86 server CPU (EPYC) + x86 client CPU (Ryzen) — a multi-product-line ecosystem moat distinct from prior vault Layer 1 placements ([[NVDA]] platform-integrated photonics_tier 1; [[AVGO]] custom ASIC photonics_tier 2; [[ARM]] pure-compute-domain Layer 1). 57-year operating history (founded 1969); x86 architecture co-licensee since 1982; CEO Lisa Su 11+ year continuity since October 8, 2014. FY2025 revenue $34.6B (+34% YoY); Data Center segment $16.6B (+32%) = 48% of consolidated revenue; Q1 2026 Data Center +57% YoY to $5.8B representing a "clear inflection in our growth trajectory and a structural shift" per Lisa Su Q1 2026 call.

AMD is **2nd in the 3-canonical CPU sub-domain coverage trajectory** (S76 [[ARM]] → S77 AMD → S78 [[INTC]] per Vic-confirmed sequence) and **first vault canonical with multi-product-line 3-segment commercial structure** (Data Center + Client and Gaming combined + Embedded; restructured from prior 4-segment scope at Q1 FY2025 per 10-K Notes "All prior period segment data were retrospectively adjusted").

**Commercial structure: multi-product-line at consolidated Layer 1 scope.** Three reportable segments per 10-K Notes Basis of Presentation: (1) Data Center — AI accelerators (Instinct MI300/MI325/MI350/MI355X/MI450) + server CPUs (EPYC 5th gen / 6th Gen Venice) + GPUs + APUs + DPUs (Pensando-origin) + AI NICs + FPGAs (Xilinx-origin) + SoC products; (2) Client and Gaming — client CPUs (Ryzen + Ryzen AI 300/400) + APUs + chipsets + discrete GPUs (Radeon RX 9000 / RDNA 4) + semi-custom SoC (Sony PlayStation 5 + Microsoft Xbox Series S/X + Valve Steam Machine PC); (3) Embedded — embedded CPUs + APUs + FPGAs + SOMs + adaptive SoCs (Xilinx + Pensando-origin). The Client and Gaming "businesses do not qualify as a separate reportable operating segment" but per-business revenue is separately disclosed (Client $10.6B + Gaming $3.9B FY2025).

**Two-tier bilateral disclosure depth (analogous to [[ARM]] S76 methodology; 2-instance precedent post-S77).** **(a) Hyperscaler-customer-commitment tier:** OpenAI 6GW + Meta 6GW substantively material multi-year deployment commitments per 10-K + Q1 2026 10-Q Notes (each with 160M-share warrant at $0.01 exercise price vesting per Instinct GPU purchase milestones); Microsoft Azure + Oracle MI deployments + EPYC hyperscaler cloud + enterprise (record Q1 2026 server CPU revenue +50% YoY across both cloud and enterprise per Q1 2026 call). **(b) Chip-vendor/foundry ecosystem tier:** TSMC primary foundry (advanced-node + advanced packaging at CoWoS); Sanmina preferred partner for AI systems manufacturing post-ZT Manufacturing Business divestiture October 2025.

**Honest-verdict framing on ROCm software platform "challenger" positioning.** ROCm (open-source GPU software ecosystem) is substantively less mature than NVDA CUDA per `frameworks.md` Section 1.4 "challenger" framing + `_thesis.md` Rank 1 Compute "execution gap to NVDA remains material — MI300 generation underperformed expectations; MI400 generation positioning improving but enterprise adoption lagging."

# META — Meta Platforms, Inc.

## Thesis role

Meta is the vault's **4th hyperscaler page** (after [[MSFT]] + [[GOOGL]] + [[AMZN]]) and **6th demand-side page** — the fourth ingest under the hyperscaler PAYER/SPENDER tracking program, and the one that completes the **four big hyperscalers** underlying the ~$640-720B 2026 AI-infrastructure aggregate the chokepoint thesis depends on. It is ingested for: (1) the **AI-datacenter capex / demand signal** feeding [[AI-demand-durability]]; (2) as a **custom-silicon operator** (MTIA, built with Broadcom) for [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]]; and (3) for its dense cross-vault footprint — [[AVGO]] (MTIA), [[AMD]] (MI450 co-developer), [[ARM]] (AGI-CPU lead partner), and [[GLW]] ($6B fiber). Like the other hyperscalers — and unlike [[MSFT]]/[[NOW]]/[[PLTR]] — it is *not* on the [[software-AI-moat-durability]] application-layer scorecard.

**Placement framing (honest-verdict, Section 2.1).** A hyperscaler sits *above* Layer 1 in `frameworks.md` v10.1 — the **demand source** (Framework 3) and **CAPEX deployer** (Framework 10), not a supply-side value-capture position. Meta carries `layer: outside` and `outside` on all five per-domain tiers — it captures no supply-side chokepoint rent; it *funds* the chokepoints. Per the [[MSFT]]/[[GOOGL]]/[[AMZN]] precedent.

## CAPEX & AI-datacenter investment

The section this ingest exists to source — Meta's AI-datacenter spending (cross-ref [[AI-demand-durability]] + the [[hyperscaler-capex]] payer-comparison tracker). Per the Q1 2026 10-Q + call:

- **Capex run-rate.** Q1 2026 capex (incl. finance-lease principal) **$19.84B** ($18,997M purchases of P&E + $843M finance leases; +~45% YoY), "driven by investments in servers, data centers, and network infrastructure" (Li). Free cash flow $12.4B; ~$81.2B cash & marketable securities vs $58.7B debt. FY2025 capex was $72.2B.
- **FY2026 capex guidance RAISED to $125–145B** (incl. finance leases), up from the prior **$115–135B** (Li). At the ~$135B midpoint that is **~+87% over FY2025's $72.2B**. Unlike [[AMZN]] (qualitative-only), Meta gives a clean annual range — primary-anchored.
- **The raise is memory-pricing-driven (a direct chokepoint tie).** Zuckerberg: "we are increasing our infrastructure CapEx forecast for this year. **Most of that is due to higher component costs, particularly memory pricing.**" Li adds the increase reflects "higher component pricing this year and, to a lesser extent, additional data center costs to support future year capacity." → the capex raise is a cost-push from memory pricing, not only volume — cross-ref [[HBM-oligopoly]] (a consumer-side validation of the memory-pricing signal, alongside the CSCO "unprecedented memory pricing" note).
- **FY2026 total expenses $162–169B** (unchanged); operating income expected **above 2025**.
- **AI-DC expansion (Zuckerberg/Li).** "Substantially expanding our own data center footprint and striking deals throughout the supply chain to secure necessary components for future capacity," plus **multi-year cloud deals** "that will come online over … 2026 and 2027, allowing us to scale more quickly" — Meta builds its own datacenters *and* rents external capacity. "Every sign … in our own work and across the industry gives us confidence in this investment," paired with a stated focus on "increasing the efficiency of our investments" (the Meta Compute initiative).

Meta's ~$135B-midpoint capex is the fourth — and final — of the four big hyperscalers now primary-sourced into the ~$640-720B 2026 aggregate (MSFT ~$190B + GOOGL $180-190B + AMZN ~$170B-run-rate + META $125-145B). Framework 10 (CAPEX flow) treats Meta as a top-of-diagram demand source.

## Custom silicon — MTIA / Meta Compute

- **Meta Compute initiative — a three-vendor stack.** Zuckerberg: "we are rolling out **more than 1 GW of our own custom silicon that we're developing with Broadcom**, as well as a **significant amount of AMD chips** to complement the new **NVIDIA systems** that we're rolling out" — the stated goal being "to lead the industry in efficiency of building compute."
- **[[AVGO]] ↔ META reciprocal-confirmation (A1).** AVGO names Meta as a custom XPU customer ("Meta MTIA 'alive and well… we're shipping now'; … multiple gigawatts in 2027 and beyond," AVGO Q1 FY2026 call). Meta primary now confirms the program — ">1 GW of our own custom silicon … with Broadcom" — bilaterally confirming the counterparty disclosure. (Meta does not use the "MTIA" label on the call; the name is the AVGO/10-K-side label — honest framing.) Cross-ref [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]].
- **[[AMD]] relationship.** Meta is an AMD MI450/Helios co-developer (6GW commitment + a 160M-share AMD warrant, vesting per Instinct-GPU purchases) — documented AMD-side; Meta primary corroborates the "significant amount of AMD chips" deployment. The multi-vendor mix (Broadcom custom + AMD + NVIDIA) matches the "custom and merchant grow concurrently" pattern on [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]].
- **[[ARM]] AGI-CPU.** Per the ARM Q4 FY2026 letter, Meta is the AGI-CPU "lead partner and co-developer" (Arm-based internal CPU). Meta's Q1 2026 call does **not** mention this relationship — it emphasizes the Broadcom accelerator + AMD + NVIDIA — so the ARM AGI-CPU partnership remains ARM-letter-sourced, not reciprocally confirmed at Meta primary (see [[AI-agentic-CPU-orchestration-reemergence]]).

## AI models & monetization

- **Muse family of models (Meta Superintelligence Labs).** The flagship release is the **Muse family** — first model **Muse Spark** — "the first release from **Meta Superintelligence Labs**," with a significantly upgraded Meta AI. (Note: the "Llama" branding does not appear on the call — Meta's models are now the Muse/MSL line.) Management frames MSL as "on track to build a leading lab."
- **Ad-monetization ROI (the GEM/Lattice models).** Advances in the **GEM** ads model architecture (with Lattice modeling techniques) "drove a more than **6% increase in conversion rate for landing page view ads**" (Q1 2026 call) — a fresh primary GEM-ROI datapoint that complements the vault's existing NVDA-cited "Meta GEM 3.5x ad-click" signal on [[AI-demand-durability]]. Ad revenue + engagement growth (video at all-time-high engagement; Instagram/Facebook/WhatsApp/Threads) is the FoA engine funding the capex build.
- AI-talent hiring drove compensation growth; Q1 headcount ~77,900 (−1% QoQ on optimization). No discrete Tier-1 "AI revenue" line — AI monetization is embedded in FoA advertising.

# ORCL — Oracle Corporation

## Thesis role

Oracle is the vault's **5th hyperscaler page** (after [[MSFT]] + [[GOOGL]] + [[AMZN]] + [[META]]) and **7th demand-side page**, the fifth ingest under the hyperscaler PAYER / SPENDER tracking program — the companies that *fund* the AI-datacenter buildout the rest of the vault tracks downstream (program scope: [[MSFT]] + GOOGL + AMZN + META + ORCL + CoreWeave). It is ingested for two purposes: (1) the **direct hyperscaler-CAPEX / AI-infrastructure signal** (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure) feeding [[AI-demand-durability]] and the [[hyperscaler-capex]] payer-comparison tracker; and (2) as the cohort's **structural outlier** — a late-but-fastest-accelerating entrant whose AI-infra growth is the most explosive in the group (AI-infrastructure revenue +243% YoY) but whose model differs from its peers in three ways: it is **contracted-backlog-driven** (a $552.6B RPO, not ad/retail/subscription cash flow), it **buys merchant silicon** rather than building captive accelerators (the exception to [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]]), and it is **financing the buildout off its own balance sheet** ("uncoupling CapEx from capital requirements"). Like the other hyperscalers — and unlike [[NOW]]/[[PLTR]] — it is *not* on the [[software-AI-moat-durability]] application-layer scorecard, though its Fusion-vs-SAP SaaS franchise is noted below.

**The honest verdict (refreshed S178, FY2026): the demand is enormous and the financing is now the story.** RPO exploded to **$638B (+363% YoY)** — years of contracted future revenue in the backlog — and OCI grew **+93%** in Q4. But FY2026 also made Oracle the cohort's clearest "funding-regime-flipped" case: **capex $55.7B (+162%)** drove **free cash flow to −$23.7B**, and FY2027 capex is guided to **~$70B net cash outlay (~$90-95B reported** once the $20-25B of customer prepayments/timing is added back). Management pairs this with strong forward guidance (**FY2027 +34% revenue / $8.05 non-GAAP EPS; 31% revenue + 28% EPS CAGR through FY2030**) and balance-sheet reassurance (~$40B FY2027 financing incl. a **$20B ATM-equity** program; investment-grade maintained; no more debt in calendar 2026). Placement HELD (`outside` — demand-side payer). The durability question is unchanged and sharper: a **$638B backlog whose counterparty Oracle still will not name** (OpenAI/Stargate: zero mentions at both venues), funded increasingly off the balance sheet.

**Placement framing (honest-verdict, Section 2.1).** A hyperscaler sits *above* Layer 1 in `frameworks.md` v10.1 — it is the **demand source** (Framework 3 dependency map) and the **CAPEX deployer** (Framework 10 allocation start), not a supply-side value-capture position. Oracle carries `layer: outside` and `outside` on all five per-domain tiers — it captures no supply-side chokepoint rent; it *funds* the chokepoints. Per the [[MSFT]]/[[GOOGL]]/[[AMZN]]/[[META]] demand-side precedent.

## CAPEX & AI infrastructure

The section this ingest exists to source — Oracle's AI-infrastructure spending (cross-ref [[AI-demand-durability]] + the [[hyperscaler-capex]] payer tracker). Oracle is the cohort's **percentage-growth leader**, and its capex story has a structure none of the other four share.

- **The capex ramp.** Purchases of property & equipment: **$6.9B (FY2024) → $21.2B (FY2025, +209%) → $39.2B in nine months of FY2026 (+223% YoY)**, with trailing-twelve-month capex ~$48.3B (ORCL FY2025 10-K + Q3 FY2026 10-Q). PP&E net doubled to $43.5B in FY2025. This is the steepest capex acceleration among the program's payers. **FY2026 full-year capex landed at $55.7B (+162%)** — above the prior ~$50-52B estimate — driving **free cash flow to −$23.7B** (OCF $32.0B − capex $55.7B; the page's earlier Tier-3 −$24.7B is now primary-confirmed). FY2027 capex is guided to **~$70B net cash outlay / ~$90-95B reported** (CFO Maxson + the Materne Q&A — the $20-25B gap is customer-prepayment / third-party-manufacturer timing; "the sum of those is our reported CapEx").
- **Two distinct ~$50B figures (avoid conflating them).** Oracle's FY2026 *capex* (~$50-52B, derived above) and its **$50B *financing* envelope** (debt+equity, below) are different things that happen to be similar in size: one is what it spends on property; the other is what it raises to pay for it.
- **AI-infrastructure & multicloud are the engines.** In Q3 FY2026, **AI-infrastructure revenue grew 243% YoY and multi-cloud database revenue grew 531% YoY** (Magouyrk, ORCL Q3 FY2026 call); both segments have **"demand that exceeds supply."** AI-capacity gross margin held at **32%** (above Oracle's 30% guide), with database/other OCI services at higher margins.
- **The financing structure — Oracle's distinctive row.** In February 2026 Oracle announced intent to raise **up to $50B in debt and equity financing** (no additional bonds beyond this in calendar 2026) and **raised $30B within days** (investment-grade), driving cash from $10.8B (May-31) to $38.5B (Feb-28); management reaffirmed commitment to maintaining the investment-grade rating (Kehring, ORCL Q3 FY2026 call). Tier-3 reporting (`ai-capex-map-2025-2027.md`) adds the scale of the leverage building underneath: **total debt above $100B**, **$72B+ of partner-debt packages**, trailing free cash flow around **–$24.7B**, and a lender (Blue Owl) reportedly **walking away from a $10B Oracle data-center financing on spending-pace concerns** — context for the forward financing/leverage risk, not a primary disclosure.
- **"Uncoupling of CapEx from Oracle's capital requirements" (the key analytical nuance).** Kehring flagged that "additional funding mechanisms" mean "there may be additional CapEx, but it doesn't require out-of-pocket cash from Oracle." Oracle is funding part of the AI-infra buildout through partner/off-balance-sheet structures — so **Oracle's reported capex understates the total datacenter capacity being built in its name.** This is a structural difference from the self-funded peers ([[MSFT]]/[[GOOGL]]/[[AMZN]]/[[META]] capex ≈ their build) and the reason ORCL's tracker row carries an understatement caveat.
- **Forward guidance — now given (FY2027; the Q3-era "deferred" posture is resolved).** Oracle guides **~$70B net capex, +34% revenue, $8.05 non-GAAP EPS, and 31% revenue / 28% EPS CAGR through FY2030** — the cohort's largest absolute AI-infra capex. Financing: **~$40B FY2027 (incl. a $20B ATM-equity program); no additional debt in calendar 2026; investment-grade reaffirmed.** **BYOH/prepaid structure now quantified:** $67B of AI-infra contracts signed in Q4, **$75B cumulative** "bring-your-own-hardware / prepaid" contracts "with no degradation in margin" (Magouyrk/Maxson) — customers fund part of the build, so reported capex still understates total capacity, and the net-cash-outlay framing (Maxson) lowers Oracle's funding requirement.
- **Power — a [[BE]] (Bloom Energy) datapoint.** The **Doña Ana County, NM** data center is designed around **gigawatt-scale Bloom clean fuel cells** (Magouyrk, Q4 call) — a primary, Oracle-side confirmation of a hyperscaler deploying on-site fuel-cell power. Five named DC sites (Abilene TX, Shackelford TX, Doña Ana NM, Saline MI, Port Washington WI); >1.2 GW delivered FY2026; FY2027 Q1 alone ~1 GW; **97.5% global GPU utilization** (multi-tenant, dynamically reallocated).
- **Where the capex flows (Framework 10).** Magouyrk's buildout chain: "AI infrastructure begins with **data centers and power generation** … Compute, networking, and storage has to be designed, manufactured, delivered, and installed" — Oracle as a demand source feeding the supply-side chokepoints the vault tracks (compute/[[NVDA]], networking, **on-site power generation**, cooling). "AI infrastructure, both **GPU and CPU**, continues to exceed supply" (ORCL Q3 FY2026 call).

## RPO / backlog & customer concentration

Oracle's AI-infra thesis is, more than any peer's, a **contracted-backlog** story:

- **RPO reached $638B at May 31, 2026** — up from **$137.8B** a year earlier (**+363%**) and $552.6B at Q3 (Feb 28) (ORCL FY2026 10-K + Q4 call; Maxson: "$638 billion, up 363%… exceptional visibility… all supported by long-term contractual customer commitments"). Still **long-dated**: ~12% within 12 months, 34% over months 13–36, 34% over 37–60, ~20% beyond — "expected to accelerate." **~12% of RPO is now the BYOH/prepaid AI-infra deals** (Materne Q&A). The bulk converts years out, which is why current OCI revenue is a small fraction of the backlog.
- **The recognized-vs-contracted concentration distinction (honest framing).** The FY2025 10-K states **no single customer was ≥10% of recognized revenue** in FY2025/24/23 — Oracle's *billed* base is diversified. But the *contracted* RPO surge is attributed only to **"certain significant contracts"** (Q3 FY2026 10-Q) — a heavily concentrated backlog. The two facts are not in tension: today's revenue base is broad; tomorrow's backlog is narrow.
- **OpenAI / Stargate — disclosed / inferable / unknown (Section 3.3).** The RPO jump is **widely reported** (Tier 4) to be driven by a multi-year OpenAI / Stargate compute contract. **Oracle names neither OpenAI nor Stargate** anywhere in the **FY2026 10-K, the Q4 call, the Q3 10-Q, or any call** (zero mentions across all venues, refreshed S178). *Inferable* (the "certain significant contracts" framing + timing + scale are consistent with the publicly reported OpenAI/Stargate deals); *not disclosed* (Oracle does not name the counterparty or quantify single-customer backlog concentration); *unknown* (the exact counterparty split of the $552.6B). This counterparty-concentration risk — a backlog disproportionately reliant on a single, itself-capital-hungry customer — is the central durability question for the Oracle AI-infra thesis, and Oracle's silence on it is itself a source observation.

## Multicloud database & OCI

- **Multi-cloud database** (Oracle Database@Azure / @Google / @AWS) is the +531%-growth segment: **33 regions live with Microsoft, 14 live with Google** as of Q3 FY2026, with AWS added; "global region coverage in all of our partner clouds" achieved this quarter (Magouyrk, ORCL Q3 FY2026 call). The partnerships convert Oracle's installed database base into multi-cloud-hosted recurring revenue. **Q4 FY2026 update:** multicloud DB revenue **+404% YoY** (bookings +325%; tokens +3.25×), cloud database +29%, "very early days… continue to unlock new regions and partnerships" (Sicilia, Q4 call) — an "outsized growth engine."
- **Fusion SaaS vs SAP.** Oracle cited a run of competitive ERP/SCM/HCM wins "over SAP" (Investec, HID Global, a "major Wall Street bank," others) and continued Fusion go-lives (ORCL Q3 FY2026 call). This is the application-layer franchise that *could* warrant a [[software-AI-moat-durability]] lens later (Open questions); this ingest treats Oracle as a hyperscaler payer, not an app-SaaS scorecard entry.
- **Sovereign AI** (OCI Alloy / Alloy partner cloud) is cited as a non-knee-jerk, structural pipeline vector (Sicilia, ORCL Q3 FY2026 call).

## Competitive positioning

- **vs the captive-silicon hyperscalers.** [[MSFT]] (Maia), [[GOOGL]] (TPU), [[AMZN]] (Trainium) and [[META]] (MTIA/Broadcom) all build in-house AI accelerators to cut capex and lift margin; **Oracle does not** — it deploys merchant silicon (NVIDIA/AMD GPUs; merchant/Ampere ARM CPUs) and competes instead on **multicloud neutrality** (running Oracle Database inside Azure/Google/AWS) plus speed of OCI capacity build. Oracle is therefore the [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]] counterpoint — the hyperscaler whose lever is *neutrality + merchant procurement*, not captive design.
- **vs the neoclouds.** Oracle is a **build-side** hyperscaler (it owns/operates OCI capacity), distinct from rent-side neoclouds (CoreWeave, the program's pending 6th payer) that lease GPU capacity to labs and hyperscalers. Both are AI-infra capex payers; the structural roles differ.

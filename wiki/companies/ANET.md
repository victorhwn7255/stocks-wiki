# ANET — Arista Networks, Inc.

## Thesis role

ANET occupies Layer 5 (integrated systems) in the value capture framework and Photonics Tier 5 (integrated systems with structural AI exposure but commoditized at the integrated-systems level, exposed to long-term CPO displacement risk). ANET designs and sells high-speed Ethernet switching platforms and the EOS (Extensible Operating System) software stack, primarily for cloud and AI titans, AI specialty providers, and enterprise customers. ANET is fabless and "primarily reliant upon a predominant merchant silicon vendor" (ANET 10-K FY2025 risk factors) — almost certainly [[AVGO]] Tomahawk per ecosystem context.

## AI Center architecture — three-domain framing

ANET 10-K FY2025 articulates an "AI Center" architecture spanning three connectivity domains:

**Scale Up.** "Currently dominated by proprietary technologies, this domain involves high-bandwidth, low-latency interconnects linking multiple XPUs within a single rack. We believe Scale Up represents a future incremental opportunity as the market shifts toward open standards like Ethernet for Scale Up Networks (ESUN)" (ANET 10-K FY2025). ESUN founding member positioning is the platform-tier ambition mechanism for Scale Up. Currently proprietary technologies (NVLink) dominate; ANET positions for displacement timeline 2026-2028 via ESUN standardization.

**Scale Out.** "This network connects XPUs across multiple racks to support massive training or inferencing clusters. The industry trend toward replacing legacy, proprietary approaches, such as InfiniBand, with Ethernet, as defined by the Ultra Ethernet Consortium, creates an opportunity for us to gain share while enabling customers to scale from thousands to a million XPUs and beyond." Ultra Ethernet Consortium 1.0 specification completed 2025 per Ullal Q4 2025 call — ANET founding member. InfiniBand displacement is the immediate Scale Out opportunity.

**Scale Across.** "Power and space constraints, along with the need for AI inference closer to the edge, are driving the need for distributed clusters spread across large geographic distances. Our Scale Across solutions deliver the capabilities necessary to enable long-distance routed access while accounting for factors such as packet loss and delays, as well as data security on the wire." Distributed inference across geographic clusters; addresses inference latency + power/space constraints at metro scale.

**Etherlink portfolio.** 20+ products spanning the three AI Center domains. Three families of Etherlink: spine, leaf, fabric. Key platforms:
- **Arista 7800R AI Spine** — modular AI spine with deep packet buffer architecture and virtual output queuing
- **Arista 7060 AI Leaf** — fixed-form AI leaf for back-end and front-end compute interconnect
- **Arista 7700R4 Distributed Etherlink Switch (DES)** — 460 terabits capacity for scale-across, DCI, multi-service routing, AI workloads

100+ customers cumulatively for Etherlink products with 800G adoption per Q4 2025 call. "Co-designing several AI rack systems with 1.6T switching emerging this year" (Ullal Q4 2025 call) — suggests partial design-tier control-point participation rather than pure component supplier role.

**Q1 2026 Etherlink + 1.6T trajectory update (Session 55 refresh).** Per Ullal Q1 2026 call: ">100 cumulative customers to date in 800 GB Ethernet deployments, and we expect the addition of 1.6 TB in 2027 at production scale." Scale-up entry timeline: *"Scale-up will be a new entry for Arista in 2027 and beyond, where we will be working closely with our customers to build AI racks with very fast interconnects for co-packaged copper, CPC, or open co-packaged optics, CPO, as well as supporting collectives and memory acceleration"* (Ullal, Q1 2026 call). Ullal direct framing of NVLink current dominance: *"today's scale-up is mostly limited to NVLink from Nvidia and maybe some PCIe switching. Majority of the Ethernet scale-up will only really happen in 2027 and 2028"* — concedes near-term scale-up to NVIDIA proprietary technology; positions Ethernet scale-up via ESUN displacement timeline 2027-2028 (consistent with S27 baseline ESUN founding member positioning). Multi-accelerator diversification: TPU recognized at Q1 2026 ("I will definitely give a nod to the TPUs"; "multi-tenants connecting to different AI accelerators, including TPUs"); previously named AMD MI-series + NVIDIA + AMD accelerators on Q4 2025.

## Extended Pluggable Optics (XPO) at Optical Fiber Conference — Session 55 refresh

ANET unveiled the Extended Pluggable Optics (XPO) form factor at the Optical Fiber Communication Conference (Q1 2026, March 2026). XPO is the structurally most material new commercial product in the Q1 2026 refresh — first ANET-architected optical form factor announcement at vault canonical, and substantively reframes the photonics_tier 5 placement narrative without triggering Caveat #9 vertical integration reclassification.

**Technical specifications at primary (Ullal, Q1 2026 call):**
- 12.8 TB per pluggable module — record-breaking throughput
- 204.8 TB per OCP rack unit — "unprecedented rack density"
- Integrated cold plate "capable of cooling up to 400 W power per module"
- Universal flexibility across "range of pluggable optics copper, as well as linear half-retimed or retimed interfaces"
- Endorsed by ">100 vendors" at unveiling — industry-wide multi-vendor standard
- "10-year run, especially at 1.6T and 3T where you need liquid cooling and you need that kind of capacity" (Ullal, Q1 2026 call)

**Architect attribution and "industry at large" framing (Ullal + Duda, Q1 2026 call):**
- "A special kudos to Andy Bechtolsheim, Arista's Chief Architect, for driving from OSFP 10 years ago to this next generation XPO, bringing structural improvements in power, footprint, and cost reductions" (Ullal)
- Ullal: "This is a very crucial invention and innovation, not just for Arista, but the industry at large"
- Duda: "What XPO unlocks is a standard interoperable multi-vendor way to get to 4x the network density and liquid cooling, which is absolutely critical for these AI use cases... We're really enabling the future growth of our industry this way, which we benefit and others benefit as well."

**XPO vs CPO competitive framing (Ullal Q1 2026 call):**
- "We embrace open CPO few years from now, but we think XPO has a 10-year run"
- "you should look at XPO as a partner to OSFP. At 400 Gb and 800 Gb, you'll fine with OSFP. As we go to higher speeds in 2027, 2028 or even beyond, you know, OSFP will run out of steam, and this will be the new connector of choice"
- "I think XPO in terms of density will be another alternative. I don't rule out open CPO as well over there if they're really looking to maximize their density in a minimum amount of space"
- "while the industry has been talking a lot about co-package optics, these are still science experiments and they're very proprietary with individual vendors doing their own thing" — Ullal explicit CPO dismissal as commercial scale technology; pluggable optics ecosystem positioning preserved

**Honest-verdict scope: photonics_tier 5 placement preserved.** XPO is pluggable optics form factor + density innovation — ANET enables the ecosystem rather than entering photonics manufacturing/design at IC or substrate level. Per Section 3.10 four-criterion test: ANET does NOT meet criteria for photonics_tier upgrade (no photonic foundry customer disclosure; no SiPh-direct revenue stream; XPO is connector form factor not photonic IC; AI exposure remains broad networking switching scope). XPO substantively strengthens ANET's structural ecosystem-enablement role within the photonics-adjacent supply chain but does NOT shift placement. Per Ullal explicit (Q1 2026 call): "we're not a classic optics vendor, but almost always whenever we're selling our switches, it has to connect to something, and usually it's some form of copper or optics." Caveat #9 Layer 5 upgrade trigger NOT met — XPO is pluggable optics commercial product enabler, not vertical integration into optics business; ANET continues to source merchant silicon and rely on third-party optics vendors for transceiver-level production. Cross-vault context: [[LITE]], [[COHR]], [[FN]], [[AAOI]] photonics laser/transceiver suppliers retain photonics_tier placements; ANET as pluggable optics form factor architect operates structurally upstream at connector standardization level rather than at IC manufacturing.

# SANHUA — Zhejiang Sanhua Intelligent Controls (浙江三花智能控制股份有限公司 / 三花智控)

*Shenzhen A-share **002050** + Hong Kong H-share **2050** (H-IPO July 2025). PRC-GAAP (CAS; the company states no difference vs IFRS), RMB. The vault's first pure A/H-share ingest. All figures in ¥ unless noted; source set is filings-only (no earnings-call transcript — see Source audit notes).*

## Thesis role

Sanhua is the **top-value layer of the humanoid value chain** — Goldman's actuator-assembly headline pick — and the **first name from that layer the vault can actually own**, because it completed a **Hong Kong H-share IPO in July 2025** (the value-layer map had flagged the entire actuator layer as un-investable China A-shares; Sanhua's H-listing partially breaks that "strongest-chokepoints / worst-US-access" tension). It is a **multi-thesis but mostly out-of-AI-scope** name: a dominant global thermal-management components manufacturer whose vault-relevant legs — (1) **humanoid actuators** and (2) **data-center cooling** — are both *real but emerging*, sitting on top of a HVAC&R-valve core and an EV-thermal business that are largely outside the vault's AI-datacenter scope. It is anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] as a theme name (the [[OUST]] precedent — assessed but outside the AI-datacenter frameworks), with an emerging-participant link to [[liquid-cooling]].

The three questions this page exists to answer:

**1 — Why Sanhua matters in the humanoid value chain (per Goldman).** Goldman's Exhibit 5 ranks **Actuator Assembly = 14** (a top-value "mechatronic core" layer; GS's *"adoption-certainty"* headline pick), and actuators are **~33–60% of the humanoid bill of materials** — the single biggest cost bucket. GS names Sanhua because actuator assembly is a **scale / integration / cost game, not a proprietary-IP game**, and Sanhua brings exactly that: mass-precision-manufacturing scale (it ships hundreds of millions of precision thermal/mechatronic parts a year), **electric-motor manufacturing expertise** that it explicitly leverages to enter actuators, deep cost control, vertical integration, and a reported (not company-confirmed) Tesla-Optimus link via its position as a top Tesla EV-thermal supplier. The bet: whoever assembles actuators at scale captures the largest BOM slice, and Sanhua's manufacturing machine makes it a natural assembler.

**2 — Legit/healthy or risky? Healthy — a high-quality blue-chip industrial, the opposite of a risky micro-cap.** FY2025 revenue **¥31.0B (+10.97%)**, net profit **¥4.06B (+31.10%)**, ~29% gross margin, **15.8% ROE**, **net cash** (~¥14.3B cash vs ~¥3.8B debt → ~¥10.5B net cash), **~zero goodwill**, dividend-paying, heavy R&D (3,671 R&D staff, 19% of headcount; 4,680 patents), plus a fresh H-IPO war chest. Detailed below, with the genuine watch items (Q1 2026 deceleration, customer concentration, related-party governance, EV-cycle dependence, and execution risk on the pre-revenue humanoid leg).

**3 — Dominant in Actuator Assembly + Linear Actuator (as GS frames it)? Dominant in the core it leverages *from*; an early contender in the actuators GS highlights — "dominant" overstates the present.** In Sanhua's own filings the actuator business is a **"战略新兴" (strategic-emerging) business at the R&D / 试制 (trial-production) / 送样 (sample-delivery) stage** — not mass production, not separately sized in revenue, with no named robot customers; it does **not even use "linear actuator," "dexterous hand," "harmonic reducer," or "roller screw,"** referring only to "机电执行器" (electromechanical actuators) generically. Where Sanhua *is* genuinely dominant is its **core**: a **near-exclusive global supplier of air-conditioning electronic-expansion and solenoid valves** (management: *"维持…几乎独家配套地位"* — "maintaining a near-exclusive supply position") and a top-tier global EV-thermal supplier. So Goldman's "actuator-assembly headline pick" is a **forward-looking bet on Sanhua translating core dominance into actuator leadership** — credible and exceptionally well-positioned, but *not* a current market-share fact (a counterparty-attribution gap per Section 3.5: named/highlighted by Goldman, not yet substantiated in Sanhua's primary disclosure).

**Placement.** `layer: outside` + `energy_power_tier: outside` + theme-anchored humanoid (no `humanoid_tier`) + `foreign_issuer: true`. Both vault-relevant legs are emerging/unsized: the humanoid actuator business is R&D-stage, and the data-center-cooling push is described as *"advancing data-center-domain projects"* with no sizing and no explicit "liquid cooling" (液冷) product disclosure — so honest-verdict `outside` on the AI-datacenter frameworks, with a registered promotion trigger (a sized data-center-cooling business → numeric `energy_power_tier`; sized humanoid actuator revenue → revisit). Sanhua's *dominant* businesses (HVAC&R valves, EV thermal) are real but outside the vault's AI-datacenter + humanoid scope. **Section 2.11 N/A** (calendar Dec-31 fiscal year). **Section 2.1 N/A** — two stable reported product segments (制冷空调电器零部件 + 汽车零部件); the bionic-robot actuator business is "新兴," not yet a reported segment (note: intra-group absorption of 绍兴三花新能源汽车部件, Apr 2025, a related-party restructuring, not a segment-reporting shift).

## The humanoid actuator leg (Goldman's headline pick vs Sanhua's primary disclosure)

**Why GS picks it (the value-capture logic).** Actuators are the most valuable subsystem in a humanoid (~33–60% of BOM). Goldman splits the actuator stack into the high-tech-barrier pieces (harmonic gears, roller screws — where Japanese/German/Chinese specialists lead) and the **assembly** layer — integrating motor + reducer + screw + sensor + control into a finished linear or rotary actuator. Assembly is GS's "adoption-certainty" pick precisely because it rewards **manufacturing scale and cost**, not proprietary IP — and that is Sanhua's home turf.

**What Sanhua actually says.** Sanhua frames the business as *"向仿生机器人机电执行器等新兴领域进行业务拓展"* (expanding into emerging areas such as bionic-robot electromechanical actuators) and *"依靠在电机制造专业知识、规模化和成本控制能力等方面的优势，成功切入仿生机器人机电执行器制造领域"* (leveraging motor-manufacturing expertise, scale, and cost control to enter bionic-robot actuator manufacturing), aiming to deliver *"重量更轻、体积更小、精度更高的机电执行器"* (lighter, smaller, higher-precision actuators). As of H1 2025 the business is *"配合客户进行全系列产品研发、试制、迭代、送样"* — co-developing a full product line with customers through R&D, trial production, iteration, and **sample delivery** (SANHUA H1 2025 半年报).

**The honest verdict (Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution).** Highlighted by Goldman as the actuator-assembly play; **not yet substantiated in Sanhua's primary disclosure** — the actuator business is pre-mass-production, pre-revenue-disclosure, and not broken into the "linear actuator / dexterous hand / harmonic reducer / roller screw" categories Goldman uses (those terms are absent from Sanhua's filings). Sanhua is a **credible, exceptionally well-positioned early entrant** (its motor-manufacturing scale + precision + cost base + reported Tesla-Optimus relationship make it a natural assembler-at-scale), but it is **not a dominant actuator incumbent** — and the humanoid market itself is pre-volume, so no one is. The Tesla/Optimus link is widely reported in trade press but **Sanhua names only "新能源汽车头部车企" (leading NEV automakers), never Tesla** — reported-not-confirmed (Section 3.3).

## Is it a healthy business? (financial-quality assessment)

**Yes — high quality.** Sanhua is large (¥31B revenue), highly profitable (¥4.06B net profit, +31%; ~29% gross margin; 15.8% ROE), cash-generative (9M operating cash flow ¥3.06B, +53%), **net cash** (~¥10.5B), carries **~zero goodwill** (no acquisition-impairment overhang), pays dividends, invests heavily in R&D, and just added an H-IPO war chest for capacity. This is a blue-chip global industrial — the financial-quality opposite of the speculative micro-caps the vault screens hard elsewhere.

**Watch items (not red flags):**
- **Q1 2026 deceleration** — headline +1.4% revenue / +2.7% profit, though ex-non-recurring +15.5% (FX/derivative optics + tough comps + US-tariff drag). Confirm the operating trend holds.
- **Customer concentration** — top-5 = 32% of revenue, with some related-party sales; EV-thermal is tied to a small set of leading automakers.
- **Related-party governance** — the Sanhua Holding group (三花控股集团 22.5% + 三花绿能 15.8%, a concerted party) controls ~38%; extensive related-party transactions (disclosed and governed, with related-shareholder recusal).
- **EV-cycle dependence** — ~40% of revenue is auto thermal; NEV growth is decelerating in some markets, and US EV tax-credit changes are a headwind.
- **Humanoid execution risk** — the actuator leg is pre-revenue; building capacity ahead of confirmed humanoid volumes is the same order-vs-capacity gamble flagged across the humanoid theme.
- **China / geopolitical + US tariffs** — China-domiciled; actively running US-tariff cost-sharing negotiations.

## Core business and competitive position

- **制冷空调电器零部件 (HVAC&R / refrigeration components — 60% of revenue, dominant).** Valves (electronic expansion, four-way reversing, stop, solenoid, ball), microchannel heat exchangers, Omega pumps, controllers, sensors for residential/commercial AC, commercial/industrial refrigeration, cold chain, heat pumps. Sanhua is a **near-exclusive global supplier of AC electronic-expansion and solenoid valves** and a 30-year industry leader, "多款产品于全球市场中位居前列" (multiple products rank top globally). This is the genuine global-#1 dominance.
- **汽车零部件 (auto / EV thermal — 40%, top-tier).** Integrated thermal management for NEVs (cabin, battery, e-motor/e-control) — auto electronic expansion valves, electronic water pumps, battery chillers, integrated assemblies — "成功将大量关键产品整合至新能源汽车头部车企的供应链" (integrated into leading NEV automakers' supply chains). A top global EV-thermal supplier (Tesla among them, unnamed).
- **Data-center cooling (emerging, within HVAC&R; the [[liquid-cooling]] link).** Sanhua reports *"推进数据中心领域项目… 围绕全球数据中心及算力产业"* (advancing data-center projects around the global data-center & computing-power industry), and the AI/data-center build-out is a named demand tailwind for its commercial-AC exports. But the leg is **not sized**, and the filings do **not** use "液冷" (liquid cooling) explicitly — so it's an emerging direction, not a substantiated revenue line. As a thermal-management components leader (valves/pumps/heat exchangers), Sanhua is structurally one of the most capable potential suppliers into data-center liquid cooling — hence an **emerging-participant** note on [[liquid-cooling]], not a numeric tier yet.

## China exposure, global footprint, and investability

Sanhua is the vault's most directly **China-domiciled** humanoid-chain name — the embodiment of the "China controls the actuator chokepoint" thesis — yet it is unusually **globally diversified in operations**: ~57% China / ~43% overseas revenue, 4 overseas production bases (Mexico/Poland/Vietnam/Thailand) and 3 overseas R&D bases (US/Germany), serving 80+ countries. It carries direct **US-tariff exposure** (actively negotiating cost-sharing). The **H-share listing (HKEX 2050) is the investability unlock** — the actuator chokepoint that the value-layer map flagged as un-ownable for a US/international book is now accessible via Hong Kong. *(Investor-access lens, per [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]]: the "un-ownable" framing is a US/international-brokerage lens — for an investor able to access China A-shares directly, the Shenzhen A-share 002050 was already reachable, so the H-listing is a cleaner *route* rather than the thing that makes Sanhua ownable.)* See [[china-exposure]].

**Peer — [[TUOPU]] (拓普集团).** The vault's second China actuator-assembly (GS-14) pick and the natural comparison: a Tier-1 auto-parts maker whose actuator program is **more developed than Sanhua's** (explicit linear/rotary/dexterous actuators + a dedicated division + a ¥5B base) but still pre-revenue, and whose **data-center liquid-cooling leg is *sized* (¥1.5B, explicit 液冷服务器 → `energy_power_tier 4`)** where Sanhua's is unsized (`outside`). The trade-off runs the other way on quality/access: Sanhua is the higher-margin, net-cash, **already-H-listed** name, while Tuopu is more cyclically pressured (profit declining operationally; leveraged; 65.8% top-5 concentration) with an H-IPO still only *pending*. Full side-by-side on [[TUOPU]].

**Peer — [[SHUANGHUAN]] (双环传动).** The third China actuator-layer name (S143), one layer *up* the value chain: the **reducer / rotary-actuator core** (GS-16, the highest-value layer) vs Sanhua's actuator-assembly. The Chinese **RV-reducer leader** (via subsidiary 环动科技/Huandong), with the healthiest financials of the three (profit +23%) and a real reducer-revenue business (¥795M) — but A-share-only (even Huandong's IPO is the *domestic* STAR Market, so no international access) and *industrial*-robot-framed. The 3-way SHUANGHUAN-vs-SANHUA-vs-TUOPU comparison lives on [[SHUANGHUAN]].

**Peer — [[ZHONGDA]] (中大力德).** The fourth China actuator-layer name (S144), at the reducer / rotary-actuator layer (GS-16) like Shuanghuan but an **integrated** maker — the only one of the cohort doing **all three reducer types incl. harmonic** plus reducer+motor+driver modules, and the most humanoid-forward in disclosure. The trade-off is stark: the **best positioning but the weakest financials** (the cohort's first small-cap, ¥1.04B, profit −13.6% and deteriorating, op cash flow −85%, net debt) — the inverse of Sanhua's healthy, net-cash profile — and the **least accessible** of the four (A-share-only, no H/IPO crack). The 4-way cohort comparison lives on [[ZHONGDA]].

**Benchmark — [[HARMONIC]] (Harmonic Drive Systems, TSE Prime 6324).** The cohort's gold standard (S145), one layer *up* at the harmonic reducer (GS-16) — the Japanese **GS-Buy** incumbent that invented the strain-wave gear, and the **first cohort name with booked, quantified humanoid revenue** (vs Sanhua's pre-revenue actuator leg). What it means for Sanhua: both are net-cash and reasonably accessible (Sanhua via H 🟡, Harmonic via Japan 🟡), but Harmonic owns a *higher-quality* chokepoint (precision-grinding/metallurgy vs Sanhua's assembly integration) with the GS thesis confirmed at primary — though it is a cyclically-depressed incumbent, not a growth story. The 5-way gold-standard-vs-China-cohort comparison lives on [[HARMONIC]].

Confirming/denying the reported Tesla-Optimus relationship is the key catalyst.
2. **Does the data-center-cooling push get sized?** If Sanhua quantifies a data-center thermal/liquid-cooling business, it promotes from `energy_power_tier outside` to a numeric tier and a confirmed [[liquid-cooling]] participant.
3. **Is the Q1 2026 deceleration operating or optical?** Ex-non-recurring +15.5% suggests mostly optical (FX/derivatives) — confirm against H1/Q3 2026.
4. **EV-cycle + tariff exposure** — how much does decelerating NEV growth + US tariff/EV-credit changes pressure the 40% auto leg?
5. **Related-party + concentration** — does customer concentration (top-5 32%) and the ~38% parent-group control evolve?

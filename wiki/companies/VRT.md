# Vertiv Holdings Co (VRT)

## Thesis role

VRT occupies Layer 5 (integrated systems) in the value capture framework and Photonics Tier 4 (specialized fit, narrower exposure). Vertiv designs, manufactures, sells, installs, and services critical digital infrastructure — power management, thermal management, switchgear, busbar, racks, integrated modular solutions, and liquid cooling — for data centers, communication networks, and commercial/industrial environments. Vertiv is **photonics-adjacent rather than photonics-core**: revenue base is broader datacenter cooling and power infrastructure that exists independently of photonics, with AI datacenter exposure as the photonics-relevant subset.

This page is the **first thermal/liquid cooling and power infrastructure layer vault coverage**, closing the Section 2.8 (Thermal / liquid cooling) zero-vault-coverage gap surfaced in [[datacenter-photonics-supply-chain]] (Session 19). Vertiv is the third post-Session-19 cross-validation cycle following [[GLW]] (Session 20 over-claim mode) and [[FN]] (Session 21 inversion mode). Vertiv presents a **third structural mode — reciprocal-confirmation**: the Session 19 Tier 3 NVIDIA-aligned claim is bilaterally confirmed in Vertiv's primary sources, including a specific deployment example.

**Key thesis-relevant findings:**

1. **NVIDIA partnership reciprocally confirmed** at Tier 1 level — first instance in the vault for an NVIDIA-named ecosystem partner across three Tier 3 cross-validation cycles (Sessions 20-22). 10-K FY2025 Item 7 Outlook and Trends — Strategic Partnerships: "Our partnership with NVIDIA supports the development of advanced power and thermal infrastructure aligned with next-generation AI and high-performance computing architectures" (VRT 10-K FY2025). 3. **Backlog more than doubled** — $15.0B at December 31, 2025 vs $7.2B at December 31, 2024 (+108% YoY). 12-18 month customer-requested delivery windows.

## Datacenter infrastructure portfolio

Vertiv reports by **geography only** (Americas, Asia Pacific, EMEA). The 10-K and 10-Q do not disaggregate revenue by product line — no separate disclosure of liquid cooling vs air cooling vs UPS vs switchgear revenue. Disaggregation in filings is limited to Products vs Services & spares (FY2025: Products 80.2%, Services & spares 19.8%) and timing of revenue recognition (point-in-time vs over-time).

**Power management:** AC and DC power management, low/medium voltage switchgear, busbar, single phase UPS, rack power distribution, integrated power solutions, energy storage solutions. "More than doubled manufacturing capacity for switchgear, busbar and integrated power solutions" since late 2021 (VRT 10-K FY2025).

**Thermal management:** Air cooled and liquid cooled thermal management products, rack thermal systems. "The complexity of hybrid air and liquid cooling created by AI workloads presents significant opportunities for innovation within, and expansion of, the entire thermal chain" (VRT 10-K FY2025). Pune India thermal facility opened 2024.

**Integrated solutions:** Vertiv OneCore — standardized data center solution encompassing power, thermal, IT white space, and services (selected for EcoDataCenter Sweden NVIDIA Vera Rubin deployment per Q1 2026 call). SmartRun overhead infrastructure portfolio — pre-engineered modular power, busway, integrated infrastructure for prefabricated/factory-integrated deployment.

**Services & spares:** Pre-sale and after-sales services including preventative maintenance, project management, acceptance testing, engineering and consulting, performance assessments, remote monitoring, specialized fluid management, training, spare parts, and critical digital infrastructure software. Over 300 service centers globally; approximately 5,000 service engineers.

**Brand portfolio:** Vertiv, Liebert, NetSure, Geist, Energy Labs, ERS, Albér, Avocent.

### 800V DC architecture

**Programs on time per Q1 2026 call.** Christopher Snyder (Morgan Stanley) directly asked about 800-volt architecture transition, Vertiv content implications, shipping timing, and TAM expansion beyond chip cooling. Albertazzi response:

> "Clearly, we wouldn't see necessarily as a transition, a wholesale transition to 800 volt. Clearly, 800 volt is going to be an important portion of the total market as we go into 2027 and beyond. We are on time with our programs. We were talking about second half this year launches of our portfolio. We are pleased with where we are in terms of the customer feedbacks with the prototypes and validation activities that we have ongoing. Shipping will be a little bit further away, but I think it's a little bit premature to elaborate too much, but we see it as a 2027 thing." (Albertazzi, VRT Q1 2026 call)

**Liquid cooling correlation.** Albertazzi continued:

> "When it comes to liquid cooling and the influence of 800-volt, I would say that there will be a correlation, not a causation necessarily, simply because 800-volt DC is applied for very high-density compute. That very high-density compute will see not just liquid cooling for the chip, but for a much bigger array of electronics across the entire IT stack. Of course, that has then influenced the entire power train, thermal chain. We see that as an opportunity for us. We're very excited, very pleased with where we are with the 800-volt DC programs, and we're getting ready for it." (Albertazzi, VRT Q1 2026 call)

**Tier 1 framing.** 10-Q Q1 2026 MD&A references "high voltage direct current power architectures" generically without 800V-specific naming: "Customer requirements are evolving toward higher-density, more complex infrastructure environments, including, but not limited to, hybrid air and liquid cooling architectures, converged physical infrastructure systems, and high voltage direct current power architectures" (VRT 10-Q Q1 2026). Tier 1 disclosure is consistent with but not specific to 800V; specific naming surfaces only on the Q1 2026 call in response to direct analyst Q&A. Disclosure-lifecycle pattern: 800V DC programs are commercially imminent (H2 2026 portfolio launches) but not yet revenue-generating in the reported period.

**CPO transition enabler framing — partial confirmation.** Tier 3 sources framed 800V DC as "structural CPO transition enabler" (Session 19 architecture primer).

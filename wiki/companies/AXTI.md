# AXTI — AXT, Inc.

## Thesis role

AXTI occupies Layer 6 (substrate and materials manufacturing) as the only publicly listed Western-headquartered InP substrate supplier. The company's thesis relevance is structural rather than financial — AXTI's substrate output gates the capacity of every downstream InP device maker ([[LITE]], [[COHR]], [[AAOI]], [[AVGO]]). If AI datacenter photonics demand is real and scaling, AXTI's InP order book is an early-stage demand signal that precedes device-level and module-level revenue recognition by 6-12 months.

The structural position is complicated by two factors: (1) 100% of manufacturing is in China, creating a geopolitical chokepoint that mirrors the value chain's downstream dependency on TSMC in Taiwan; and (2) the company's financial profile ($88.3M FY2025 revenue, negative net income) is characteristic of a niche materials supplier rather than a structurally dominant platform player. AXTI's vault inclusion is for what its order trajectory reveals about the AI photonics supply chain, not for standalone investment thesis strength.

Layer 6 classification note: AXTI is the first Layer 6 company in the vault. Layer 6 extends the value capture framework below Layer 5 (component assembly) to substrate and materials manufacturing — the foundational input layer where crystal growth determines what downstream fabrication is possible. This layer classification is provisional and should be reviewed when `frameworks.md` is updated.

## Product portfolio and InP demand

AXTI manufactures compound semiconductor substrates via vertical gradient freeze (VGF) crystal growth:

- **Indium phosphide (InP):** Primary thesis-relevant product. Used as the substrate for lasers and photodetectors in datacenter optical transceivers and, increasingly, coherent optics for AI networking. InP substrates are the foundational input for [[LITE]], [[COHR]], and [[AAOI]]'s laser fabrication.
- **Gallium arsenide (GaAs):** Used in wireless communications, LED, and solar applications. Q4 revenue $7.0M — roughly comparable to InP but with weaker structural growth trajectory.
- **Germanium (Ge):** Small and declining ($231K in Q4). Solar and infrared applications.
- **Raw materials:** $29.4M FY2025. In-house production of gallium, germanium, and pyrolytic boron nitride (pBN) crucibles used in crystal growth. Sold both internally and to third parties.

InP demand trajectory: InP backlog reached $60M+ as of the Q4 FY2025 call — Fischer described this as "a new high" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call). Management attributed the backlog growth to datacenter AI networking demand, specifically citing 800G and 1.6T transceiver deployments. Young characterized demand as driven by "hyperscaler, enterprise AI data center buildout" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call).

Demand attribution caveat: Management's claim that InP demand is "AI-driven" is a rhetorical characterization, not a verified demand decomposition. InP substrates serve multiple end markets (telecom, defense, sensing) and the 10-K does not provide end-market revenue disaggregation for InP. The $60M+ backlog figure is factual; its attribution to AI networking specifically is management's framing.

## InP capacity and competitive position

### Competitive landscape

The 10-K identifies "three primary suppliers of InP substrates worldwide" and names Sumitomo Electric Industries (Japan) and JX Nippon Mining & Metals (Japan) as the principal competitors (AXTI 10-K FY2025). On the Q4 earnings call, Fischer stated: "Today there are two InP substrate suppliers in the world, and we're one of them" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call).

**Tier 1/Tier 2 framing gap:** The filing's "three primary suppliers" vs. the call's "two... and we're one of them" is a significant divergence. The filing includes a third competitor that the call erases from the competitive landscape. This pattern — a filing acknowledging competitive breadth that the call narrows to assert market dominance — is comparable to or exceeding prior vault instances of the Tier 1/Tier 2 framing gap convention (see Source audit notes). The analytical question is whether the third supplier identified in the filing is commercially marginal (making Fischer's "two" directionally correct) or whether the call's framing strategically minimizes competition.

### Capacity expansion

Current InP capacity supports roughly $8-10M/quarter in substrate revenue based on Q3-Q4 FY2025 run rate. Two expansion phases are planned:

1. **Phase 1 (brownfield, $30M, by end 2026):** Doubles current InP capacity. Fischer described this as "relatively capital-light" using existing facilities in Beijing (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call). The $30M figure comes from call commentary; the 10-K references capacity expansion plans but does not quantify them.

2. **Phase 2 (greenfield, $100-150M, potential 2027):** Fischer described a possible second doubling: "we have the option to build a brand-new facility... in the range of $100 to $150 million" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call). This is conditional on sustained demand and would bring total InP capacity to approximately $35M/quarter — the stated run rate target.

### 6-inch transition

Industry transition from 4-inch to 6-inch InP wafers is underway. Larger wafers improve downstream device yield. Young stated AXTI is "qualifying 6-inch InP substrates with multiple customers" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call). The 10-K does not mention 6-inch qualification status.

## Vertical integration

AXTI operates a vertically integrated model through its Tongmei subsidiary structure in China:

- **Crystal growth:** VGF technique for InP, GaAs, and Ge boules
- **Raw materials:** In-house production of gallium, germanium, and pBN crucibles
- **Wafer processing:** Slicing, polishing, and epitaxy-ready surface preparation

The 10-K identifies six partially owned subsidiaries in China producing raw materials, collectively contributing $29.4M in FY2025 revenue. Vertical integration reduces input cost dependency but concentrates all manufacturing in a single jurisdiction.

**Tongmei STAR Market IPO:** AXTI's primary operating subsidiary, Beijing Tongmei Xtal Technology Co., Ltd., filed for a STAR Market IPO in 2021. The filing has been pending for over four years with no resolution date disclosed. If the IPO does not occur by the contractual deadline, AXTI faces a contingent redemption obligation of approximately $49M to minority shareholders (AXTI 10-K FY2025). This overhang is material — $49M against FY2025 revenue of $88.3M — and could force dilutive financing or asset restructuring.

North America revenue collapsed from $7.9M (FY2024) to $1.8M (FY2025) — a 77.5% decline. The 10-K does not explain this collapse; the call does not address it directly. This trajectory merits monitoring: if North America represents downstream InP laser makers ([[LITE]], [[COHR]]), the decline may reflect supply chain restructuring or customer inventory correction rather than demand weakness.

### Export controls — dual exposure

AXTI faces export control pressure from both sides of the US-China technology decoupling:

- **China outbound controls (Feb 4, 2025):** China added InP substrates to its export control list, requiring AXTI to obtain permits for all non-China shipments. As of the Q4 call, permits had been received for Europe, Japan, UK, and Canada. First permit denials were received for US-destined shipments (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call). The 10-K, filed before the call, noted permits were "pending" without specifying denials.
- **US inbound controls:** AXTI's US headquarters and listing expose it to potential CFIUS scrutiny and Entity List risk for its China-based operations.

The geopolitical position is structurally significant: AXTI is one of two or three global InP substrate suppliers, and 100% of its production is in China. Any sustained export permit denial for US-bound InP would force downstream customers to source exclusively from Japanese suppliers (Sumitomo, JX Nippon), tightening the [[InP-supply]] chokepoint. See [[InP-supply]] for the broader geopolitical dimension.

## CPO and technology transitions

AXTI is the first company in the vault to express explicit bullishness on co-packaged optics (CPO) as a demand driver. Young stated: "We see CPO as yet another inflection point for us beginning in late 2027 and beyond. We believe it could be a sizable additional revenue opportunity for us" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call).

This contrasts sharply with the vault's 26-data-point CPO silence pattern documented in [[CPO-platform-battle]]. The structural logic differs from higher layers: at the substrate level, CPO does not displace InP — it potentially amplifies demand by requiring more InP laser die per switch package than pluggable transceivers. AXTI's bullishness is therefore structurally consistent even if higher-layer companies view CPO with ambivalence or silence.

The 10-K contains zero mentions of CPO, co-packaged optics, or silicon photonics (AXTI 10-K FY2025). The CPO commentary exists only in Tier 2 call sources, consistent with the Disclosure lifecycle awareness convention — CPO is strategic intent, not yet financial materiality for AXTI.

**Substrate-peer cross-reference (S101).** [[SOI]] (Soitec; ingested S101) is the silicon-photonics analog to AXTI at Layer 6 — the engineered-SOI-substrate supplier on which silicon-photonics PICs are fabricated, just as AXTI supplies the InP substrate for lasers. Soitec confirms the same substrate-tier CPO demand-amplifier dynamic on the SOI leg (CPO inserts interconnect at the substrate level → more Photonics-SOI content per unit; Photonics-SOI >$100M FY2026, supply-constrained — Soitec Q3 FY2026 call). The two form a **geopolitical mirror** at the substrate layer: AXTI is 100% China-manufactured and under InP export-control pressure; Soitec is France-based with near-monopoly Smart Cut IP (and its own China exposure via the NSIG licensing framework). See [[SOI]] + [[CPO-platform-battle]] substrate-tier demand-amplifier.

## Open questions

1. **InP yield advantage — unverified.** The kickoff prompt hypothesized a 15-20% yield advantage for AXTI's VGF process over competitors' Czochralski method. Neither the 10-K, 10-Q, nor earnings call quantify yield rates or claim a specific yield advantage. The 10-K describes the VGF process and notes it produces "low-defect-density substrates" but does not compare yield to competitors. The 15-20% figure remains unverified and should not be treated as established until corroborated by a primary or Tier 3 source.

2. **Customer identities — unverified.** The kickoff prompt suggested specific customer relationships (e.g., Lumentum as a major customer). No filing or call names individual InP customers. Customer A (12%) and Customer B (10%) in the 10-K are anonymous. The call references "hyperscalers" and "some of the biggest companies in the world" without identification. Customer identity hypotheses remain working hypotheses pending corroboration.

3. **Lumentum 85% market share — not corroborated.** The kickoff prompt referenced Lumentum holding 85% InP laser market share. No AXTI filing or call references Lumentum by name or provides any InP laser market share figures. This figure, if accurate, would be significant for understanding AXTI's customer concentration risk, but it is not sourced from AXTI's primary disclosures and should be attributed to its original source (likely Tier 3) if cited elsewhere in the vault.

4. **North America revenue collapse.** FY2025 North America revenue fell 77.5% to $1.8M. The cause is undisclosed. Possible explanations include: customer inventory correction, export-control-driven supply chain restructuring, or loss of specific customer relationships. This is a high-priority monitoring item for the next quarterly filing.

5. **Phase 2 capacity decision.** The $100-150M greenfield expansion is conditional. What demand level or backlog threshold triggers the decision? Fischer described it as an "option" without committing to a timeline or trigger. The gap between $30M brownfield (committed) and $100-150M greenfield (conditional) is large — the second phase represents a transformative capital commitment for a company with $88.3M annual revenue.

6. **Tongmei IPO resolution.** The STAR Market IPO has been pending since 2021. If it does not occur, the $49M contingent redemption is material. What is the contractual deadline, and what are the financing options if triggered?

7. **InP substrate concentration two-claim reconciliation.** Two layered Tier 3 claims about InP substrate concentration require future primary-source verification:

Framework characterization: "Qualify 60-70% claim. AXTI is the closest US-listed InP substrate exposure, but it is not a clean 'low-risk bottleneck' because its operational footprint and customer base carry China-related risk."
   
   The two claims address different supply chain layers and are not contradictory if interpreted at different layers — JX dominates indium feedstock; AXTI may dominate downstream InP wafer production from that feedstock. Both treated as over-claim mode per A1 three-mode framing convention pending future primary-source cross-validation. ## Source audit notes

### AXTI 10-K FY2025 (Tier 1, filed Feb 2026)

Standard annual filing with comprehensive risk factor disclosure. InP is not broken out as a separate revenue line — the filing reports only "substrates" ($58.9M) and "raw materials" ($29.4M). This means the InP demand narrative that dominates the earnings call cannot be verified against Tier 1 financial data at the product level. The filing identifies "three primary suppliers" of InP substrates, naming Sumitomo Electric and JX Nippon. Zero mentions of CPO, co-packaged optics, or silicon photonics. The filing's risk factors include detailed China regulatory, export control, and Tongmei IPO risks that the call largely does not address.

**Tier 1/Tier 2 framing gap (CLAUDE.md v6 convention):** The filing's "three primary suppliers" directly contradicts Fischer's call claim of "two InP substrate suppliers in the world." The framing gap is structurally significant: the filing acknowledges a three-player competitive landscape consistent with SEC disclosure obligations, while the call asserts a duopoly narrative that positions AXTI as more dominant. This instance is comparable to or exceeding prior vault instances — the MRVL Celestial acquisition disclosure gap and AVGO customer-owned tooling risk dismissal both involved selective Tier 2 reframing of Tier 1 risk disclosures, but the AXTI instance involves a direct factual claim (number of competitors) rather than a risk-framing difference. The divergence directly affects how an investor assesses AXTI's competitive moat.

### AXTI 10-Q Q3 FY2025 (Tier 1, filed Nov 2025)

Quarterly filing providing mid-year baseline. Notable for the North America revenue collapse ($140K in Q3, down from $1.8M prior-year Q3) and the $100M shelf registration filing in November 2025 — one month before the equity raise. Customer concentration increased (19% and 13% vs. full-year 12% and 10%), suggesting growing reliance on fewer large customers as InP demand concentrates.

### AXTI Q4 FY2025 earnings call (Tier 2, Feb 19, 2026)

**Promotional tone caveat:** This call exhibits markedly promotional language that warrants heightened rhetorical-claims discipline. Fischer described demand as "very unusual and actually intense" and stated "we're too excited." Young called CPO "yet another inflection point." Bettles described demand as "enormous." Three analysts participated. The December 2025 equity offering ($95.2M net from 8.16M shares at $12.25) closed approximately five weeks before this call. While no causal link is established, the proximity of a completed equity raise to a promotional-toned earnings call is relevant context for evaluating management's incentive structure during the call. All bullish demand claims from this call are attributed to the specific speaker rather than paraphrased in wiki voice.

**Analyst silence observation:** Only three analysts participated. No analyst questioned the competitive landscape claim ("two suppliers"), the North America revenue collapse, the Tongmei IPO timeline, or the discrepancy between filing and call competitor counts. The Q&A focused on capacity timing and customer demand, accepting management's framing without challenge. This coverage gap may reflect AXTI's small-cap status and limited sell-side coverage rather than deliberate avoidance, but it means Tier 2 source quality is lower than for heavily covered companies.

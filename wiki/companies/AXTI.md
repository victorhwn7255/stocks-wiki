# AXTI — AXT, Inc.

## Thesis role

AXTI occupies Layer 6 (substrate and materials manufacturing) as the only publicly listed Western-headquartered InP substrate supplier. The company's thesis relevance is structural rather than financial — AXTI's substrate output gates the capacity of every downstream InP device maker ([[LITE]], [[COHR]], [[AAOI]], [[AVGO]]). If AI datacenter photonics demand is real and scaling, AXTI's InP order book is an early-stage demand signal that precedes device-level and module-level revenue recognition by 6-12 months.

**Order book converting to results (Q1 FY2026 refresh).** Through FY2025 the order-book signal was visible (rising InP backlog) but unconfirmed at the income statement (negative net income, sub-13% FY2025 gross margin). Q1 2026 is the inflection: revenue +39% YoY to $26.9M, non-GAAP gross margin rebounding to 29.9%, non-GAAP net loss narrowed to ~$585K, and **Q2 2026 guided to both GAAP and non-GAAP profitability** (AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). InP is now north of 50% of revenue and the backlog reached a new high of $100M+. The early-stage demand signal has begun converting to financial results — the order book is no longer the only evidence.

The structural position remains complicated by two factors: (1) 100% of manufacturing is in China, creating a geopolitical chokepoint that mirrors the value chain's downstream dependency on TSMC in Taiwan — and US export permits for InP remain pending (only ~2% of FY2025 revenue reached North America); and (2) even after the inflection, AXTI is a ~$27M/quarter materials supplier, small relative to the downstream device makers it feeds, and the long-term supply agreements management describes with Tier 1 customers and hyperscalers are still unsigned and unnamed. AXTI's vault inclusion remains for what its order trajectory reveals about the AI photonics supply chain rather than for standalone investment thesis strength; the Q1 2026 inflection strengthens that trajectory without changing the Layer-6 substrate-input role.

The April 2026 capital raise ($632.5M gross — roughly 6.6x the December 2025 raise, and priced at $64.25/share vs $12.25 five months earlier) funds and de-risks a materially escalated multi-year capacity roadmap (see capacity section), though the 2027-2028 phases are direction-plus-funding rather than committed contracts.

Layer 6 classification note: AXTI is the first Layer 6 company in the vault. Layer 6 extends the value capture framework below Layer 5 (component assembly) to substrate and materials manufacturing — the foundational input layer where crystal growth determines what downstream fabrication is possible. This layer classification is provisional and should be reviewed when `frameworks.md` is updated.

`materials_tier 2` added (Q1 FY2026 refresh): AXTI is a genuine engineered-substrate and raw-materials operator — InP/GaAs/Ge substrates plus in-house gallium, germanium, and pBN crucibles, now extended to high-purity indium refining (JinMei). This mirrors [[SOI]] (Soitec; materials_tier 2, engineered-substrate materials leader) and sits below the nuclear/rare-earth materials tier-1 and above broad-cycle materials suppliers such as [[MKSI]] (tier 3). The field codifies a classification the page has implied since creation; it predates the multi-domain `*_tier` convention.

## Product portfolio and InP demand

AXTI manufactures compound semiconductor substrates via vertical gradient freeze (VGF) crystal growth:

- **Indium phosphide (InP):** Primary thesis-relevant product. Used as the substrate for lasers and photodetectors in datacenter optical transceivers and, increasingly, coherent optics for AI networking. InP substrates are the foundational input for [[LITE]], [[COHR]], and [[AAOI]]'s laser fabrication.
- **Gallium arsenide (GaAs):** Used in wireless communications, LED, and solar applications. Q4 revenue $7.0M — roughly comparable to InP but with weaker structural growth trajectory.
- **Germanium (Ge):** Small and declining ($231K in Q4). Solar and infrared applications.
- **Raw materials:** $29.4M FY2025. In-house production of gallium, germanium, and pyrolytic boron nitride (pBN) crucibles used in crystal growth. Sold both internally and to third parties.

InP demand trajectory: InP backlog reached a new high of **$100M+** as of the Q1 FY2026 call (up from $60M+ at the Q4 FY2025 call) (AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). InP is now north of 50% of total revenue. Management attributes the growth to two demand legs: (1) US AI infrastructure build-out and cloud/AI-platform CapEx driving demand for EML and silicon-photonics-based transceivers plus high-speed photodetectors ("material being used in multiple U.S. hyperscalers"); and (2) a newly emphasized China leg.

**China demand surge (new structural angle, Q1 FY2026 call).** AXTI's China InP-based-laser-market revenue more than doubled QoQ in Q1 2026 and is expected to double again in Q2. Bettles estimated China at ~30% of the overall InP global market demand AXTI sees in Q2, rising toward ~40% by Q4, as China builds its own optical-transceiver and CPO supply chain. This leg is structurally important because in-China shipments require no export permit — China demand is unconstrained by the export-control gate that holds US-destined shipments. Management also cited industry expectations of a 4x-6x increase in the overall optical-component substrate market over the next three to five years, driven by both scale-out and scale-up (Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call).

**Customer base broadening.** AXTI now reports "supporting nearly all leading customers in the optical space" — Tier 1 laser manufacturers and transceiver module makers, globally and in China. Hyperscalers ("the customer's customer") are now engaging AXTI directly on supply-chain guarantees, and AXTI is in long-term-supply-agreement discussions with "nearly all of the larger customers," encouraged by hyperscalers and hardware companies, expecting "to come to resolution with some... in the very near future" (Bettles / Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). Per counterparty-attribution discipline, these relationships are AXTI-side disclosed: no customer is named, no agreement is yet signed, and no customer exceeded 10% of revenue in Q1 2026 (top 5 ~32%). See Open question on customer identities.

Demand attribution caveat: Management's claim that InP demand is "AI-driven" remains a rhetorical characterization, now partly corroborated by InP-specific commentary (China laser-market doubling; InP >50% of revenue) and the US-hyperscaler-material claim — but still not a Tier-1 end-market disaggregation. The $100M+ backlog figure is factual; its attribution to AI networking specifically is management's framing. InP substrates also serve telecom, defense, and sensing markets the filings do not break out.

## InP capacity and competitive position

### Competitive landscape

The 10-K identifies "three primary suppliers of InP substrates worldwide" and names Sumitomo Electric Industries (Japan) and JX Nippon Mining & Metals (Japan) as the principal competitors (AXTI 10-K FY2025). On the Q4 earnings call, Fischer stated: "Today there are two InP substrate suppliers in the world, and we're one of them" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call).

**Tier 1/Tier 2 framing gap:** The filing's "three primary suppliers" vs. the call's "two... and we're one of them" is a significant divergence. The filing includes a third competitor that the call erases from the competitive landscape. This pattern — a filing acknowledging competitive breadth that the call narrows to assert market dominance — is comparable to or exceeding prior vault instances of the Tier 1/Tier 2 framing gap convention (see Source audit notes). The analytical question is whether the third supplier identified in the filing is commercially marginal (making Fischer's "two" directionally correct) or whether the call's framing strategically minimizes competition.

### Capital raise — April 2026 ($632.5M)

In April 2026 AXTI completed an underwritten offering of 8,560,311 base shares at $64.25/share plus a 1,284,046-share over-allotment exercised in full on April 22, 2026, for total gross proceeds of approximately $632.5M (AXTI Q1 FY2026 10-Q, subsequent event). Use of proceeds per management: Tongmei InP capacity expansion, R&D in new products including 6-inch InP, and working capital (Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). The raise is roughly 6.6x the December 2025 offering ($95.2M net) and was priced at $64.25 vs $12.25 five months earlier — a ~5x re-rating reflecting the AI-demand narrative. Pro forma Q2 share count is ~63.5M (vs Q1 basic 53.3M). The raise funds the multi-year capacity roadmap below and removes the financing overhang that previously made Phase 2 conditional.

### Capacity expansion — multi-year roadmap (escalated, Q1 FY2026 call)

The capacity plan escalated from the prior two-phase framing (a $30M brownfield doubling plus a conditional $100-150M greenfield) to a primary-direction multi-year roadmap, now funded by the April raise:

1. **2026 (brownfield, ahead of plan):** doubling InP capacity from Q4 2025 levels to ~$35M/quarter (~$140M/year) by end-2026, in a Beijing site formerly used for GaAs crystal growth. FY2026 CapEx ~$35M (range $30-40M), mostly crystal-growth furnaces plus polisher backend (Fischer, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). Management reports running ahead of this plan.
2. **2027 (adjacent Beijing facility):** doubling again via a newly acquired site next to the existing factory dedicated to InP wafer production — to ~$65-70M/quarter (~$280M/year) by end-2027/early-2028 ("double, double"). Estimated ~$100M; greenfield build ~1-1.5 years.
3. **2028 (greenfield):** a further meaningful expansion under planning, potentially **outside China**; a meaningful greenfield estimated at ~$220-250M.

Honest framing: the 2026 doubling is committed and funded; the 2027 doubling is planned and funded but not yet contracted; the 2028 greenfield is direction. Management repeatedly cautioned that "adding capacity versus able to deliver wafers is two different things" and that demand "is 10x" the planned doubling — i.e., even the escalated roadmap is supply-constrained against stated demand (Young / Bettles, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call).

### Wafer-size transition (3-inch → 4-inch → 6-inch)

Industry transition toward larger InP wafers continues; larger wafers improve downstream device yield. At the Q1 FY2026 call, management reported "important progress" on 6-inch InP for both iron-doped (laser) and sulfur-doped (photodetector) specifications, with a significant part of the capacity expansion focused on 6-inch crystal growth to support customers' 6-inch roadmaps (Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). Current mix is roughly 4:1 in favor of 3-inch, with 4-inch ramping fastest in the next 6-12 months and 6-inch ~1 year out; high-speed photodetectors have largely moved to 4-inch while lasers still carry significant 3-inch (Young / Bettles, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). Iron-doped (laser) volume is rising relative to sulfur-doped (photodetector) — Young put the large-diameter mix near 40/60 iron-to-sulfur, up from a prior ~10:1 favor of sulfur-doped. This advances the Q4 FY2025 "qualifying with multiple customers" status toward production scaling.

## Vertical integration

AXTI operates a vertically integrated model through its Tongmei subsidiary structure in China:

- **Crystal growth:** VGF technique for InP, GaAs, and Ge boules
- **Raw materials:** In-house production of gallium, germanium, and pBN crucibles; as of Q1 FY2026 the JinMei subsidiary has begun refining **high-purity indium** — extending vertical control to the critical indium input for InP substrates (Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call)
- **Wafer processing:** Slicing, polishing, and epitaxy-ready surface preparation

The 10-K identifies six partially owned subsidiaries in China producing raw materials, collectively contributing $29.4M in FY2025 revenue. Vertical integration reduces input cost dependency but concentrates all manufacturing in a single jurisdiction. Management frames the raw-material subsidiaries (JinMei, BoYu for crucibles) as a "choo-choo train" whose profit contribution grows with AXT volume and characterizes the integrated indium-to-substrate chain as a competitive differentiator (Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call).

**Pricing (Q1 FY2026 call).** AXTI is raising prices and globalizing/standardizing pricing across regions (historically more aggressive in lower-end markets such as GPON), citing indium raw-material cost increases and a mix shift to larger-diameter, higher-specification wafers where its low-defect-density material commands a premium (Bettles / Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). Pricing effects are expected to show in margins later in 2026.

**Tongmei STAR Market IPO:** AXTI's primary operating subsidiary, Beijing Tongmei Xtal Technology Co., Ltd., filed for a STAR Market IPO in 2021. The filing has been pending for over four years with no resolution date disclosed. If the IPO does not occur by the contractual deadline, AXTI faces a contingent redemption obligation of approximately $49M to minority shareholders (AXTI 10-K FY2025). This overhang is material — $49M against FY2025 revenue of $88.3M — and could force dilutive financing or asset restructuring.

### Export controls — dual exposure

AXTI faces export control pressure from both sides of the US-China technology decoupling:

- **China outbound controls (Feb 4, 2025):** China added InP substrates to its export control list, requiring AXTI to obtain permits for all non-China shipments. Non-US permits (Europe, Japan, UK, Canada) continue to be received readily, and Q1 2026 permits "came in slightly better than guidance" (Young, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call).
  - **US-permit framing update (Section 4.3 contradiction note).** The prior page state (from the Q4 FY2025 call) recorded that "first permit denials were received for US-destined shipments." The Q1 FY2026 call reframes the US status as **still pending** rather than denied: management states it has not given up on the US, that US permits remain pending, and that China's Ministry of Commerce has contacted AXTI to submit more data on a number of US applications — characterized as "an encouraging sign... still a possibility to get a permit for the U.S. in the near future" (Young / Bettles, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). Per source-tier discipline this is a Tier 2 framing shift; the underlying Tier 1 risk-factor language continues to flag export-permit risk and US-revenue impact (only ~2% of FY2025 revenue to North America). The "denials → still pending + active dialogue" shift is recorded here rather than overwriting the prior claim.
- **US inbound controls:** AXTI's US headquarters and listing expose it to potential CFIUS scrutiny and Entity List risk for its China-based operations. The 10-Q also notes a February 20, 2026 US Supreme Court ruling invalidating certain IEEPA-based tariffs, with replacement tariffs possible under other authorities — adding to forecasting uncertainty (AXTI Q1 FY2026 10-Q).

The geopolitical position is structurally significant: AXTI is one of two or three global InP substrate suppliers, and 100% of its production is in China. Any sustained export permit denial for US-bound InP would force downstream customers to source exclusively from Japanese suppliers (Sumitomo, JX Nippon), tightening the [[InP-supply]] chokepoint. See [[InP-supply]] for the broader geopolitical dimension.

## CPO and technology transitions

AXTI is the first company in the vault to express explicit bullishness on co-packaged optics (CPO) as a demand driver. Young stated: "We see CPO as yet another inflection point for us beginning in late 2027 and beyond. We believe it could be a sizable additional revenue opportunity for us" (AXTI Q4 FY2025 call). The Q1 FY2026 call reaffirmed this timing ("another inflection point... beginning in late 2027 and beyond"), framed CPO within an expected 4x-6x increase in the overall optical-component substrate market over three-to-five years (scale-out + scale-up), and added that China is entering the CPO supply chain — AXTI is "actively engaging in discussions with customers about their technical and timing requirements" (Young / Bettles, AXTI Q1 FY2026 call). The late-2027 timing has held across two consecutive cycles.

This contrasts sharply with the vault's 26-data-point CPO silence pattern documented in [[CPO-platform-battle]]. The structural logic differs from higher layers: at the substrate level, CPO does not displace InP — it potentially amplifies demand by requiring more InP laser die per switch package than pluggable transceivers. AXTI's bullishness is therefore structurally consistent even if higher-layer companies view CPO with ambivalence or silence.

The 10-K contains zero mentions of CPO, co-packaged optics, or silicon photonics (AXTI 10-K FY2025). The CPO commentary exists only in Tier 2 call sources, consistent with the Disclosure lifecycle awareness convention — CPO is strategic intent, not yet financial materiality for AXTI.

**Substrate-peer cross-reference (S101).** [[SOI]] (Soitec; ingested S101) is the silicon-photonics analog to AXTI at Layer 6 — the engineered-SOI-substrate supplier on which silicon-photonics PICs are fabricated, just as AXTI supplies the InP substrate for lasers. Soitec confirms the same substrate-tier CPO demand-amplifier dynamic on the SOI leg (CPO inserts interconnect at the substrate level → more Photonics-SOI content per unit; Photonics-SOI >$100M FY2026, supply-constrained — Soitec Q3 FY2026 call). The two form a **geopolitical mirror** at the substrate layer: AXTI is 100% China-manufactured and under InP export-control pressure; Soitec is France-based with near-monopoly Smart Cut IP (and its own China exposure via the NSIG licensing framework). See [[SOI]] + [[CPO-platform-battle]] substrate-tier demand-amplifier.

# SOI — Soitec SA

## Thesis role

Soitec occupies Layer 6 (substrate and materials manufacturing) as the world leader in **engineered substrates** — the specialized wafers chips are built *on*. Its proprietary **Smart Cut™** atomic-layer-transfer process gives it a near-monopoly position in silicon-on-insulator (SOI). Like [[AXTI]] (the InP-substrate peer), Soitec's thesis relevance is structural: it sits one layer upstream of the foundries and chipmakers, and its order book is an early-stage demand signal that precedes downstream device and module revenue.

**AI-datacenter relevance — the Photonics-SOI substrate (real revenue, not a promise).** Soitec's AIDC hook is **Photonics-SOI**: every silicon-photonics PIC — whether it ends up in a pluggable transceiver or a co-packaged optics (CPO) engine — is fabricated on an SOI wafer, and Soitec is the dominant merchant supplier. Photonics-SOI exceeded **$100M revenue in FY2026** ("earlier than initially anticipated"), is supply-constrained, and is ramping for CPO qualification (FY2026 results; Q3 FY2026 call). This is the SOI analog to [[AXTI]]'s InP substrate — and a stronger placement than the pre-revenue photonics of [[SIVE]] or [[XFAB]], because the revenue and the near-monopoly position are real.

**Honest framing per Section 2.1 — a cyclical-trough company with a structural AIDC option.** The AIDC-relevant slice (Photonics-SOI, ~16% of revenue) sits inside a company that is **deeply cyclically depressed**: FY2026 revenue fell 34% to €592M, gross margin collapsed to 16.3%, and the net result was a €(220)M loss (FY2026 results). Roughly **52% of revenue is mobile RF-SOI**, mid-way through a customer inventory correction that does not normalize until ~end-CY2026, with recovery a CY2027 story (Q3 FY2026 call). The page should not read as an AI pure-play: most of Soitec is mobile/cyclical, and the AI thesis is the Photonics-SOI substrate position plus the structural Smart Cut moat — not the whole company.

**Per-domain placement.** **Layer 6** mirrors [[AXTI]] (substrate manufacturing). **photonics_tier 3** reflects the real-revenue silicon-photonics substrate position (same tier as [[AXTI]] InP substrate and [[GLW]]/[[LITE]]; the Smart Cut moat could argue tier 2, but tier 2 is reserved for broader optical/foundry leaders like [[COHR]]/[[TSEM]], so a substrate placement at tier 3 is the honest call). **materials_tier 2** captures Soitec's engineered-substrate (semiconductor materials) leadership — below the nuclear-fuel/rare-earth tier-1 criticality of [[LEU]]/[[MP]], above [[MKSI]] semi-materials at tier 3. No energy/power or equipment placement: the SmartSiC datacenter-power angle is impaired and not materializing (below), Power-SOI is automotive, and Soitec consumes equipment rather than supplying it.

## Engineered-substrate franchise and the Smart Cut moat

Soitec sells engineered substrates to foundries, IDMs, and fabless customers via the proprietary **Smart Cut™** process (atomic-scale layer transfer), plus Smart Stacking and epitaxy (URD FY2025). The moat is genuine and was reinforced this year:

- **Near-monopoly IP.** Soitec has only **one** Smart Cut technology licensee (Shin-Etsu / SEH), and it **terminated GlobalWafers' license in 2024** — GlobalWafers loses the right to use any Soitec IP by July 2027, even as it builds US SOI capacity (Q3 FY2026 call). Management sees "no big changes in segment shares" and R&D was doubled from €60M to €120M over four years (Q3 FY2026 call).
- **Product lines across three end-markets** (URD FY2025): **Mobile** — RF-SOI (industry-standard RF in nearly all 4G/5G phones), FD-SOI, POI (Piezoelectric-on-Insulator for SAW filters); **Edge & Cloud AI** — Photonics-SOI, FD-SOI, Imager-SOI (phasing out), LNOI; **Auto & Industrial** — Power-SOI, SmartSiC, FD-SOI.
- **Q4 FY2026 commercial events:** a 10-year licensing-framework extension with China's NSIG (Simgui + Simwings; Mar 13, 2026) and a multi-year POI supply agreement for Skyworks' Sky5 platform (Mar 5, 2026) (FY2026 results).

## AI-datacenter and CPO exposure

Soitec is the **upstream SOI substrate underneath the silicon-photonics leg** of the optical supply chain. It supplies the SOI wafer; silicon-photonics foundries ([[TSEM]]'s PH18DA platform, plus GlobalFoundries and Intel — non-vault) build the PICs on it; those PICs go to the transceiver/CPO designers ([[MRVL]], [[AVGO]]) and the laser/optics ecosystem ([[LITE]], [[COHR]]).

- **Photonics-SOI is real, supply-constrained, and growing.** >$100M revenue in FY2026; "the optimal solution for high-speed, high-bandwidth optical interconnections, for both plug-in optical transceivers and co-packaged optics systems (CPOs)" (H1 FY2026 report). On the Q3 call: "more than five customers today engaged in our silicon photonic solutions, and more and more are coming… each asking for more and more volumes," a **20–30% market CAGR**, and "the demand for AI is higher than what the industry today can provide" (Q3 FY2026 call).
- **CPO is a content uplift, and it is pulling forward.** Because CPO inserts the optical interconnect "at the substrate level," it pulls **more Photonics-SOI content per unit** than today's pluggables; and the CPO timeline has accelerated — "first designs [are] gonna be commercially available by end of this [calendar] year [2026]" (Q3 FY2026 call). This makes the CPO transition a positive mix/volume shift for Soitec specifically — the same substrate-tier demand-amplifier dynamic the vault documents for [[AXTI]] at the InP leg, now extended to the SOI/silicon-photonics leg (see [[CPO-platform-battle]]).
- **LNOI (lithium-niobate-on-insulator)** is the next-generation substrate Soitec is accelerating to extend bandwidth beyond 1.6T, complementing silicon photonics (Q3 FY2026 call; FY2026 results).
- **FD-SOI** is a secondary edge-AI enabler (FD-SOI + Photonics-SOI combined +25% YoY in FY2026; FY2026 results).
- **Counterparty-attribution discipline (Section 3.5).** Soitec describes accelerating its Photonics-SOI roadmap "in collaboration with global leaders in the industry" (H1 FY2026 report) without naming them — treated as over-claim (Soitec-side disclosed; specific customers not named or bilaterally confirmed).

## SmartSiC / power (impaired)

Soitec markets **SmartSiC™** (engineered SiC substrate via Smart Cut) for power, including a datacenter power-supply-efficiency angle — but management says those datacenter opportunities are "not expected to materialize in the short term," and Soitec took a **€41M impairment** on SmartSiC assets in FY2026 driven by Chinese monoSiC competition (H1 FY2026 report; FY2026 results). Power-SOI (for EV battery management) also declined. The power/SiC angle is documented honestly as a disappointment, not an AIDC driver — hence no energy/power tier placement.

## Mobile / cyclical core (out of AIDC scope)

Roughly 52% of revenue is **RF-SOI / mobile**, the cyclical engine currently in a customer inventory correction. Management quantified the bottom unusually precisely (Q3 FY2026 call): RF-SOI customer inventories (8-inch equivalent) fell **2.5M → 2.3M (Sept) → ~2.0M (Dec)**, with Soitec undershipping 200–300K wafers/quarter, targeting **~1M by end-CY2026** (the pre-COVID, ~6–9-month normal level). RF-SOI growth likely resumes in **CY2027** ("but we don't know exactly when"), and FY2027 fab loading stays below normal — keeping margins pressured into FY27. This base is out of vault scope; it is covered because it gates the whole company's earnings recovery.

## Cross-vault adjacencies

- **[[AXTI]]** — the substrate-peer analog. AXTI = InP substrate for lasers; Soitec = SOI substrate for silicon-photonics PICs. Both Layer 6 Western-headquartered substrate suppliers, on different material legs of the optical supply chain — and a **geopolitical mirror**: AXTI is 100% China-manufactured and under InP export-control pressure, while Soitec is France-based with near-monopoly Smart Cut IP (and its own China exposure via NSIG licensing).
- **[[TSEM]]** — silicon-photonics PDK foundry (PH18DA) that builds PICs on SOI substrate; Soitec sits upstream of it (alongside non-vault GlobalFoundries and Intel).
- **[[LITE]] / [[COHR]] / [[datacenter-laser-supply]]** — Soitec's SiPh substrate underpins the silicon-photonics route to optical interconnect, complementary to (and partly competing with) the InP/EML laser route LITE and COHR dominate. The more silicon-photonics/CPO wins share versus InP pluggables, the more Soitec benefits.
- **[[MRVL]] / [[AVGO]]** — design the silicon-photonics chips (transceivers, CPO engines) fabricated on Soitec SOI via foundries.
- **[[VECO]]** — epitaxy/process equipment adjacent to engineered-substrate manufacturing.
- **[[MKSI]]** — the closest vault semiconductor-materials peer (materials_tier 3); the nuclear-fuel/rare-earth materials names ([[LEU]], [[MP]], [[CCJ]]) are a different materials sub-domain.
- **[[datacenter-photonics-supply-chain]]** — Soitec is the vault's SOI/silicon-photonics-substrate participant in Section 2.9 (upstream materials and substrates), parallel to [[AXTI]] at the InP leg.
- **[[cpo-integration]]** — Photonics-SOI is the substrate beneath the CPO optical engine; CPO content uplift + pull-forward.
- **[[CPO-platform-battle]]** — Soitec extends the Layer 6 substrate-demand-amplifier finding to the SOI/SiPh leg (architecture-agnostic: benefits whether pluggables or CPO win).
- **[[InP-supply]]** — the InP-substrate chokepoint; Soitec is the SOI-substrate parallel (different material), not part of the InP chain.

## Open questions

1. **RF-SOI recovery timing.** Inventory normalizes ~end-CY2026; RF growth resumes ~CY2027 per management — the dominant near-term earnings swing factor. Track the wafer-inventory drawdown (toward ~1M 8-inch-equivalent) and FY27 fab-loading trajectory.
2. **Photonics-SOI revenue trajectory + CPO ramp.** >$100M FY2026, supply-constrained, 20–30% CAGR. **photonics_tier 3 → 2 upgrade trigger** if Photonics-SOI scales into a materially larger revenue mix as CPO ramps (first designs end-CY2026).
3. **LNOI commercialization.** Timing and scale of lithium-niobate-on-insulator beyond 1.6T.
4. **SmartSiC strategy post-writedown.** Whether the €41M-impaired SiC franchise is repositioned, exited, or the datacenter-PSU angle eventually materializes.
5. **CEO transition.** Laurent Rémont took office April 1, 2026 (succeeding Pierre Barnabé); strategy continuity at the trough.
6. **China / NSIG licensing.** The 10-year NSIG framework extension (Simgui + Simwings) and "China for China" exposure; competitive and geopolitical implications.
7. **GlobalWafers IP termination (July 2027).** Whether GlobalWafers' loss of Smart Cut IP access materially affects the competitive landscape or Soitec's share.
8. **SOI-substrate chokepoint promotion candidate.** Soitec's near-monopoly, supply-constrained Photonics-SOI is a potential dedicated chokepoint, but evidence is single-source today — deferred per Section 3.15 (future candidate, pending a second source or Tier 3 anchor).

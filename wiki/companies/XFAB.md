# XFAB — X-FAB Silicon Foundries SE

## Thesis role

XFAB occupies Layer 2 (irreplaceable infrastructure) at the **specialty foundry sub-domain** — a structural peer to [[TSEM]] at mature/specialty process nodes, distinct from [[TSM]] Layer 2 leading-edge logic + advanced packaging scope. XFAB is a pure-play analog/mixed-signal foundry (1.0μm down to 110nm; 150mm + 200mm wafers) across six fabs in four countries, serving automotive + industrial + medical end markets (94% of revenue per Q1 2026 press release). Customer migration is cost-prohibitive: long product lifecycles (>15 years), sole-source positioning on most products, and AEC-Q100 automotive qualification create the durable-incumbency profile typical of Layer 2 specialty foundries.

**AI datacenter thesis fit — emerging, not central.** XFAB is a broad-cycle specialty foundry (automotive-led) where AI datacenter exposure is a thin but growing slice via two channels: wide-bandgap (SiC + GaN) power conversion and a pre-revenue silicon photonics pipeline. The Q1 2026 earnings call (Damien Macq) is the first time management framed "AI-driven infrastructure" as a strategic anchor with named datacenter use cases. Honest framing per Section 2.1: XFAB is NOT thesis-central; it earns Tier 4 placement on both Framework 5 (Photonics) and Framework 7 (Energy/Power), with the photonics placement carrying explicit pre-revenue framing.

**Framework 7 energy_power_tier 4 placement (datacenter SiC + GaN exposure growing).** Section 3.10 four-criterion test: criteria 1+2 met (Navitas named SiC customer + datacenter power management named segment + 800V→48V/12V/6V conversion explicit); criteria 3+4 not met (wide-bandgap is ~4% of FY2025 revenue + customer concentration is automotive-driven, not datacenter-specific). Tier 4 = differentiated component supplier with growing AIDC exposure — below the designer-tier scope of [[NVTS]]/[[POWI]]/[[VICR]]/[[MPWR]]/[[ON]] but above Outside. Upgrade-to-Tier-3 trigger pre-registered if datacenter SiC revenue mix exceeds ~15% by FY2026 end.

**Framework 5 photonics_tier 4 placement (pre-revenue; genuinely marginal).** Section 3.10 four-criterion test 2-of-4 met: criteria 1+2 satisfied (Ligentec named silicon photonics partner + EU photonixFAB project named segment); criteria 3+4 fail (photonics is pre-revenue ~$7M NRE in 2025 + broad-cycle customer concentration). Placement parallels [[VECO]] F5 Tier 4 at ~9% compound-semi revenue. Carried with explicit "pre-revenue / development-phase" framing; downgrade-to-Outside trigger pre-registered if silicon photonics product revenue does not inflect by 2027-2028 (management's own guided window). This is a borderline Tier 4 / Outside call — Vic decision flagged at Open questions.

**Outside Framework 8 (Equipment) + Outside Framework 9 (Materials).** XFAB is a foundry — it consumes equipment (lithography, deposition, etch) and materials (raw wafers, SiC substrates, gases) rather than supplying them. Outside placement on both per Section 3.10 four-criterion test inverse. Multi-domain Outside strengthens honest-verdict-correctness on these two domains.

## Specialty foundry positioning

Six wafer fabs across four countries (Annual Report FY2025): **Erfurt + Dresden + Itzehoe (Germany) + Corbeil-Essonnes (France) + Kuching (Malaysia) + Lubbock, Texas (USA)**. ~4,300 employees; >400 customers; >100 active customers. Listed on Euronext Paris since April 2017; market cap EUR 676.1M at December 31, 2025 (Annual Report FY2025 + Shareholder information).

**Technology portfolio (Annual Report FY2025):** Smart CMOS & SOI (1.0μm → 110nm; up to 700V breakdown; deep trench isolation; 180nm + 110nm BCD-on-SOI) + Microsystems/MEMS + Photonics + Wide-bandgap (SiC + GaN). The 110nm BCD-on-SOI platform is gaining traction across automotive, robotics, and — newly — data center and medical ultrasound applications.

**Three-BU reorganization (Q1 2026 call; finalized prior quarter):** Damien Macq described a global reorganization into three dedicated business units — **Smart CMOS & SOI**, **Microsystems & Photonics**, and **Wide-bandgap (SiC + GaN)** — each built around a core technology family to bring development engineers + roadmaps + business development closer to customers. Reorganization is operational realignment, not a segment-reporting change (XFAB reports a single operating segment per IFRS 8).

**CEO transition (Annual Report FY2025; Q4 2025 press release):** Damien Macq succeeded Rudi De Winter (15-year tenure) effective February 6, 2026. Q1 2026 call was Macq's first as CEO.

**Erfurt → Microsystems hub transition (Annual Report FY2025):** Erfurt, Germany is transitioning from CMOS wafer manufacturing to the group's Microsystems hub, supported by EU Chips Act funding; advanced capabilities cited include wafer-level packaging, heterogeneous integration, and 3D stacking.

## Customer concentration

**Tier 1 disclosure (Annual Report FY2025 Risk Factors + Segment note):** Largest customer **Melexis accounted for 43% of FY2025 revenue ($377.2M)**; top 3 = 53%; top 5 = 58%. One customer exceeded 10% of consolidated revenue ($377.2M in 2025 vs $364.2M in 2024). None of XFAB's customers are prohibited by contract from purchasing from other foundries; customers have switched with little or no notice historically (Annual Report FY2025 Risk Factors).

This sits in tension with the Layer 2 incumbency framing in Thesis role and should be read together with it: the migration-cost moat operates at the **product/requalification level** (a >15-year automotive part is costly and slow to requalify at another foundry), not at the contractual level (no exclusivity). The low-60% Q1 2026 utilization shows the moat is not currently translating into pricing power. The Layer 2 placement therefore rests on requalification friction rather than contractual lock-in — a weaker basis than the [[TSEM]] SiPh-PDK precedent it mirrors, and the reason the Layer 2-vs-4 call is flagged as a Vic decision.

**Tier 1/Tier 2 framing gap candidate (Section 3.4; customer-concentration sub-pattern).** The Annual Report Risk Factors disclose the Melexis concentration plainly as a risk. On the Q1 2026 call, Damien Macq framed the largest customer's share declining "from 41% to 35%" (Q4 2025 → Q1 2026 quarterly) as "important customer mix evolution" and "strategically important... diversification," attributing the move partly to top-20 customers (ex-largest) growing revenue 21% QoQ. Honest framing: the share decline is driven in large part by involuntary Melexis destocking (automotive -10% YoY in Q1), not purely deliberate diversification — the call frames a concentration risk realizing as diversification progress. Both readings hold: the top-20 ex-largest growth is genuine, while the largest-customer decline is destocking-driven. This is the **2nd instance** of the Section 3.4 customer-concentration sub-pattern (after [[MRVL]] S9); codification threshold is 3+.

## Datacenter exposure (Framework 7 Energy/Power Tier 4)

**Wide-bandgap growth (Q1 2026 press release + call):** Wide-bandgap revenue reached $15.1M in Q1 2026 (+152% YoY); SiC wafer shipments hit 14,300 units (+195% YoY, +28% QoQ). Wide-bandgap was ~4% of FY2025 revenue. Management attributes the SiC ramp partly to data center power management demand. The high YoY growth is partly a low-base recovery: FY2025 SiC revenue was $33.8M, down 34% YoY after an exceptionally strong Q1 2024, so Q1 2026 is rebounding off a depressed comparison rather than off a flat base — read the +152%/+195% with that context.

**SiC for AIDC power architecture (Q1 2026 call; Damien Macq):** XFAB's SiC is used "going from power switch for solid-state breakers, but also utilizing energy conversions from 800V to system voltage down to 48V, 12V or 6V" — the 800V DC datacenter power conversion chain. SiC drives the bulk of this; GaN interest is also partly datacenter-linked.

**Navitas named customer — A1 reciprocal-confirmation (Section 3.5).** Damien Macq named Navitas as a publicly known SiC customer of XFAB on the Q1 2026 call. This is **reciprocally confirmed** in [[NVTS]] primary sources: the Navitas FY2025 10-K Item 1 names "X-Fab (SiC; US-based with Texas operations)" as its SiC foundry partner (per S92 NVTS canonical). Annotated per Section 3.5: named in XFAB Q1 2026 earnings call (counterparty); reciprocally confirmed in [[NVTS]] 10-K Item 1 fabless-foundry partner disclosure. First reciprocal-confirmation A1 instance at a first-ingest baseline.

**GaN-on-Si (Q1 2026 press release + call):** XFAB delivered a first 1,200V GaN prototype on a customized 200mm engineered substrate and started a next-generation vertical GaN customer project; GaN is positioned for datacenter power supply units. GaN remains primarily in the prototyping/development stage.

**Atomic clock timing chips for GPU rack synchronization (Q1 2026 call; NEW disclosure).** Damien Macq: data center growth is also driving "other technologies like... timing clock to synchronize GPU in racks. The idea is to reach really atomic clock precisions with the technology, and this is driven from a chip that was built out of X-FAB technology." Also flagged: design wins for higher-voltage ICs to drive power transistors. This GPU-rack-timing use case is novel and not yet captured in [[NVDA-platform-integration]] or [[AI-agentic-CPU-orchestration-reemergence]]; customer identity undisclosed (Open question).

**SiC capacity (Q1 2026 call):** XFAB has built SiC capacity to run up to 10,000 wafers/month; currently loading ~6,000 (≈60% in the SiC corridor) to leave headroom for QoQ growth.

## Photonics development pipeline (Framework 5 Photonics Tier 4 — pre-revenue)

**Silicon photonics activity (Annual Report FY2025):** Three silicon photonics projects with key partners were in development in 2025, accounting for 8% of total prototyping revenue. Photonic ICs combine silicon photonics with advanced photonics, fully compatible with XFAB's CMOS, supporting ultra-fast data transmission for datacenter + telecom + quantum-computing applications.

**Ligentec partnership (Annual Report FY2025):** Switzerland-based Ligentec is XFAB's named strategic silicon photonics partner, specializing in high-performance materials such as lithium niobate and silicon germanium. XFAB is also engaged in the EU-funded **photonixFAB** project, aimed at strengthening a European industrial-grade scalable photonics platform (European sovereignty initiative).

**Revenue timing (Q1 2026 call):** Photonics is mostly NRE today — ~$7M in 2025 (≈5.5-7% of group NRE per CFO Alba Morganti). Damien Macq: "we anticipate product revenues from Photonics to hit more around the end of 2027, 2028, so not yet visible outside of NRE at this moment." Photonics is pre-revenue at this baseline — the basis for the explicit pre-revenue framing on the F5 Tier 4 placement.

## Capacity, utilization, and cost structure

**Capacity program (Annual Report FY2025 + Q1 2026 call):** The three-year capacity expansion program completed mid-2025. A new Kuching, Malaysia cleanroom (6,000 sq m) launched production September 2025, raising monthly wafer-start capacity from 30,000 toward a target of 40,000 by end-2026 — more than doubling capacity for the popular 180nm BCD-on-SOI platform. FY2026 capex is guided to ~$100M (roughly half FY2025's $204.1M), reflecting the move past the investment-cycle peak.

**Utilization + cost structure (Q1 2026 call):** Global utilization is in the "low 60%" range; XFAB is idling some tools and managing factory cost control to align with reduced loading. Cost structure is ~70% fixed / 30% variable (CFO Alba Morganti) — so gross margin recovery is leverage-dependent on higher utilization. The fixed-cost weight is why Q1 profitability compressed despite only a modest revenue decline.

**Cost-saving program (Q1 2026 call):** High single-digit percentage headcount reduction + gradual operating-cost decline, targeting ~$6M/quarter savings by Q4 2026. Effects were not yet visible in Q1; management reiterated confidence the plan materializes by year-end. Near-term G&A is elevated by a SAP ERP implementation (CFO: G&A peaks in 2026, declines from 2028).

**One-off items (Q4 2025 + Q1 2026):** A SiC raw-wafer LTA renegotiation drove a $9.3M one-off in Q4 2025 ($6.0M settlement + $3.3M SiC substrate inventory revaluation). The expiration of most customer LTAs (including the largest customer) at December 31, 2025 triggered the IFRS 15 -$10.3M over-time revenue impact in Q1 2026; business engagements with these customers continue post-LTA.

## Cross-vault adjacencies

- **[[TSEM]]** — closest specialty-foundry peer (both multi-domain foreign-issuer foundries at Layer 2). Non-overlapping sub-domains: TSEM is silicon-photonics-PDK-focused; XFAB is wide-bandgap-power-focused. TSEM does not manufacture SiC/GaN; XFAB does not run a silicon photonics PDK at TSEM's scale. Structural analog, not competitor.
- **[[NVTS]]** — SiC customer (A1 reciprocal-confirmation, above). XFAB is the merchant SiC foundry behind Navitas's fabless SiC line.
- **[[ON]] / [[MPWR]] / [[VICR]] / [[POWI]]** — power-semi designer/IDM ecosystem. XFAB is a pure merchant foundry (capacity supplier); ON is an IDM with internal manufacturing — the foundry-manufacturer-vs-designer distinction is the cross-vault contrast documented at [[power-semis]].
- **[[TSM]]** — leading-edge foundry counterpart; XFAB sits at the specialty/mature-node end of the foundry spectrum.
- **[[VRT]] / [[ETN]]** — datacenter power-infrastructure integrators (Energy/Power Tier 1) consuming the power-conversion ICs that ride on XFAB SiC. XFAB sits component-tiers upstream.
- **[[liquid-cooling]] + [[AIDC-cooling-architecture-transition]]** — the 800V DC rack architecture XFAB SiC serves is the same architecture transition driving the cooling chokepoints.
- **[[transformer-supply]]** — adjacent datacenter power-infrastructure constraint.
- **[[datacenter-photonics-supply-chain]]** — XFAB silicon photonics development pipeline (pre-revenue) placed at theme-level observation.

## Open questions

1. **Datacenter SiC revenue mix trajectory** — wide-bandgap was ~4% of FY2025 revenue; Q1 2026 +152% YoY. What share is datacenter-specific (vs automotive/industrial SiC)? **F7 Tier 4 → Tier 3 upgrade trigger** if datacenter SiC mix exceeds ~15% by FY2026 end.
2. **Photonics product revenue inflection 2027-2028** — management guides product revenue (beyond NRE) to the end of 2027/2028. **F5 Tier 4 confirmation OR downgrade-to-Outside trigger** depending on whether the inflection materializes.
3. **Melexis concentration normalization** — 43% FY2025 / 35% Q1 2026 quarterly. How much of the decline is voluntary diversification vs forced automotive destocking? Track largest-customer share + top-20 ex-largest growth at H2 2026.
4. **Atomic clock timing chip customer identity** — GPU rack synchronization chip "built out of X-FAB technology"; customer undisclosed. Tier 1 substantiation needed. Cross-vault implication for [[NVDA-platform-integration]].
5. **SiC merchant foundry competitive positioning** — XFAB 10,000 wafers/month max at ~60% load vs Wolfspeed / onsemi / STMicro / Infineon SiC capacity. Candidate input to a future SiC merchant foundry capacity chokepoint (deferred; multi-source threshold not yet met).
6. **Silicon photonics partners beyond Ligentec** — three projects in development; only Ligentec named. Who are the other two partners, and are any datacenter-direct?
7. **110nm BCD-on-SOI datacenter design-win pipeline** — Damien Macq cited design wins "spanning data center and medical ultrasound applications." What is the datacenter-specific revenue ramp from this platform?
8. **Belgium / Erfurt EU Chips Act funding scope + timing** — funding supports the Erfurt Microsystems hub transition; scope and milestones not fully disclosed.

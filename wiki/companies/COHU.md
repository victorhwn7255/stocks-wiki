# COHU

## Thesis role

COHU is a broad-cycle semiconductor test and inspection equipment company. It is the structurally weakest thesis fit in the seed set and the only company positioned at photonics_tier "outside" — meaning it has no photonic device exposure, no CPO relevance, and no mechanism by which its products intersect the photonics supply chain.

`_thesis.md` states: "This is the most analytically uncertain of my holdings and the wiki's honest verdict matters." The honest verdict is that COHU's filings do not support AI-specific thesis fit. COHU serves all semiconductor end markets — mobile, automotive, industrial, computing — with test handlers, semiconductor testers, and interface solutions. AI is mentioned aspirationally in filings but is not quantifiable in the revenue base. No customer above 10% annually, no [[TSMC]] or [[NVDA]] relationship disclosed, no advanced packaging specificity.

COHU is included in the vault for **equipment-tier completeness and as a cyclical context counterpoint.** Placed beside [[AEHR]] (structurally AI-exposed via KGD burn-in for advanced packaging, photonics_tier 4) and [[ONTO]] (structurally AI-exposed via packaging substrate metrology with quantified HBM programs, photonics_tier 4), COHU shows what a non-AI-specific equipment company looks like. The contrast itself is analytically valuable — the equipment tier is not monolithic.

COHU is the only seed-set company documented solely from Tier 1 filings (10-K and 10-Q), with no Tier 2 earnings call available. The Tier 2/Tier 1 divergence pattern that characterized [[LITE]], [[COHR]], [[AEHR]], and [[AAOI]] — where filings are silent on CPO while calls discuss it — cannot be tested for COHU. See Source audit notes for implications.

### Customer concentration

On an **annual basis**, COHU's customer base is the most diffuse in the vault. No customer exceeded 10% of revenue in FY2025 or FY2024. Top 10 customers accounted for 60% of total revenue. Only STMicroelectronics exceeded 10% historically (12% in FY2023) — an automotive/industrial IDM, not an AI-adjacent customer (COHU 10-K FY2025).

On a **quarterly basis**, concentration is lumpy. In Q3 FY2025, three customers each exceeded 10%, with the largest at 34% of quarterly net sales — up from one customer at 12% in prior-year Q3 (COHU 10-Q Q3 FY2025). For the full nine months, only one customer reached 10%. The quarterly spike is consistent with lumpy handler system deliveries to specific customers rather than sustained concentration. Customers are unnamed.

The diffuse annual pattern is the honest-verdict signal: COHU's revenue is spread across end markets and customers, the opposite of AI-thesis concentration (compare: [[ONTO]] at 21% TSMC, [[AAOI]] at 81.9% top-2).

## Product and market position

COHU operates three segments — Test Handler (TH), Semiconductor Tester (ST), and Interface Solutions (IS) — aggregated into one reportable segment, "Semiconductor Test & Inspection" (COHU 10-K FY2025). The aggregation prevents end-market or segment-level revenue disaggregation from filings.

**Product lines:** Test automation equipment (pick-and-place handlers with active thermal control), post-singulated die inspection and metrology (WLCSP, bare die, six-sided optical + infrared), automated test equipment (RF, digital, mixed signal, power management), test contactors and power probe cards (consumable, driving 60% recurring revenue), and AI-driven process control software (Tignis acquisition) (COHU 10-K FY2025).

**Named competitors:** Advantest, Teradyne, Hon Precision, KLA (COHU 10-K FY2025). These are broad-based equipment companies, not AI-specific niche players.

**Geographic footprint:** Manufacturing in Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Japan, and Germany. Transitioning out of Poway, California — restructuring charges $10.1M in FY2025 (COHU 10-K FY2025). Approximately 2,857 employees across 25 countries, 75% in Asia-Pacific (COHU 10-K FY2025).

**China exposure:** Q3 FY2025 China revenue $14.2M (11.2% of quarterly revenue), down from $17.2M prior year. Nine-month China revenue $45.4M (13.7%), down from $49.1M (16.1%) (COHU 10-Q Q3 FY2025). Neither filing discusses export controls or entity list restrictions — COHU's handler equipment may be below the control threshold for semiconductor equipment export restrictions.

**Taiwan spike:** Q3 FY2025 Taiwan revenue surged to $23.4M from $3.5M prior year (+561%) (COHU 10-Q Q3 FY2025). Likely reflects a large handler system delivery. No explanation provided in filings.

## The AI-adjacent question

This is the central analytical section for COHU's wiki page. The question per `_thesis.md`: is the AI tailwind "real and specific, or whether the position is better understood as a general semi cycle play with automotive/analog drag"?

**The evidence does not support AI-specific exposure.**

COHU's 10-K states the company is "capitalizing on high-growth opportunities in HBM inspection, high performance processor test in AI applications" and attributes FY2025 revenue growth to "stronger demand for AI-based computing applications" (COHU 10-K FY2025). The 10-Q mentions AI only twice, in generic "AI data centers" language (COHU 10-Q Q3 FY2025). But neither filing quantifies AI-specific revenue, names an AI-adjacent customer above 10%, or provides end-market disaggregation that would permit AI identification.

**The single AI-adjacent investment: Tignis.** COHU acquired Tignis ($34.9M, January 7, 2025), an AI process control software company whose technology was integrated into COHU's DI-Core defect inspection platform for HBM inspection applications (COHU 10-K FY2025). Tignis revenue was "not material" in FY2025 (COHU 10-K FY2025). This is COHU's most concrete AI-specific positioning — and the company itself discloses it as immaterial.

**Broad-cycle indicators dominate.** Mobile sales declined three consecutive years (54%, 10%, 17%). Automotive/industrial declined 24% in FY2023, 65% in FY2024, then recovered 45% in FY2025 (COHU 10-K FY2025). This is classic semiconductor cycle behavior. Revenue declined 29% from FY2023 peak ($636.3M) to FY2025 ($453.0M). Gross margins compressed from 47.6% to 42.7% over the same period — atypical for a recovery phase, driven by product mix and manufacturing transition.

## Source audit notes

**COHU 10-Q Q3 FY2025 (Tier 1, quarter ended September 27, 2025):**
AI is mentioned exactly twice in the entire filing — both generic "AI data centers" language with no quantification, no technology specificity, and no customer identification. Zero mentions of HBM, CoWoS, advanced packaging, silicon photonics, CPO, co-packaged optics, TSMC, NVDA, Advantest, Teradyne, AEHR, or ONTO. The three-customer quarterly concentration spike (34% largest) contrasts sharply with the diffuse annual customer base — lumpy handler equipment orders create episodic concentration without structural dependency. Tone: a purely operational filing with no AI-positioning effort. The contrast with Layer 4 10-Qs (LITE, COHR — which at least contain generic photonics language) is itself informative.

**COHU 10-K FY2025 (Tier 1, fiscal year ended December 27, 2025):**
The 10-K contains aspirational AI language — "capitalizing on high-growth opportunities in HBM inspection, high performance processor test in AI applications" — that is never quantified. No end-market revenue disaggregation is provided. Three operating segments are aggregated into one reportable segment, preventing analytical identification of AI-specific revenue streams. Named competitors (Advantest, Teradyne, Hon Precision, KLA) are all broad-based equipment companies. Mobile revenue declined three consecutive years; automotive/industrial shows classic cyclicality. Tignis revenue is "not material" — the company's own disclosure of its most AI-specific acquisition. Tone: a filing that acknowledges AI exists but cannot demonstrate material participation in it. The opacity — aggregated segments, unnamed customers, unquantified end markets — is itself a data point. Companies with structural AI positioning typically highlight it aggressively (compare [[NVDA]], [[COHR]] management commentary).

**COHU Q4 FY2025 earnings call — not available.**
The transcript file (`raw/transcripts/COHU-earnings-2026-01-31.pdf`) contains a paywalled Q2 FY2025 call summary (July 31, 2025), not the Q4 FY2025 call (January 31, 2026) specified in the session kickoff. This absence is analytically informative rather than merely a data gap. The Tier 2/Tier 1 divergence pattern — where Tier 1 filings are silent on emerging themes like CPO while Tier 2 calls discuss them extensively — was one of the most significant findings across Sessions 4-7. At Layer 4 (LITE, COHR), calls broke the CPO silence that filings maintained. At Layer 5 (AAOI), the call introduced asymmetric CPO reframing absent from filings. The inability to test this pattern for COHU means the vault cannot assess whether COHU management discusses AI exposure more specifically or substantively in Tier 2 venues than their Tier 1 filings suggest. However, the broad-cycle honest verdict rests on what the filings do disclose — diffuse customers, unquantified AI revenue, aspirational language — which is sufficient evidence for the "outside" photonics classification regardless of what an earnings call might add.

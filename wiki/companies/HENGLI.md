## Thesis role

Hengli is the vault's **first roller-screw-layer name** — Goldman scores the **planetary roller screw 14/18** (tied with actuator assembly, behind only the harmonic reducer 16 and dexterous hand 15) and calls it **"the most acute physical chokepoint alongside harmonic gears."** With this ingest the vault covers **all four of Goldman's top value layers.** It is anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] as a theme name, **outside the AI-datacenter frameworks** (the [[SLING]]/[[ZHAOWEI]]/[[ZHONGDA]] precedent — assessed, but zero datacenter/cooling/semis exposure).

This page exists to document one specific, validated thing — and to be honest about everything it is *not*:

**What's validated — the precision-grinding-credibility case.** The roller-screw chokepoint *is* precision grinding (Goldman scores its capacity-flexibility the lowest possible, 1, because grinding gates supply). Hengli is the one Chinese name that has **demonstrably built that capability at scale** — its linear-drive project is in production with capacity for **70,000 sets/yr of precision-*ground* ball screws** (年产精密磨削滚珠丝杠 7 万套) + 360,000 m/yr of high-precision rails, 300+ customers, and **planetary roller screws at sampling + initial mass production** — *and* it carries the pedigree to back it: it broke the international-brand monopoly in high-end excavator hydraulics. So Goldman's "credible precision-grinding entrant" framing is confirmed at primary, not asserted.

**What it is not — a humanoid play, on the evidence.** As a *humanoid* name Hengli is the **most diluted in the entire cohort.** In its own 183-page annual: 液压 (hydraulic) appears **311 times**, 丝杠 (screw) **3 times**, **人形 (humanoid) zero**, 机器人 (robot) twice (a board-member bio and a business-license scope line — not operations). The roller-screw business is **bundled into a ~3.5%-of-revenue "accessories & castings" line**, the humanoid-relevant PRS is a just-started sliver of that, and the **humanoid end-market is never named** — the linear-drive products serve "中高端工业设备" (mid-high-end industrial equipment). Hengli is, first and overwhelmingly, a **world-class hydraulics blue-chip in a construction-machinery upcycle**; humanoid roller screws are early, unsized, unnamed optionality on a credible precision base.

## Is it a healthy business?

**Yes, emphatically — it is the highest-quality business of the China cohort.** Record revenue and profit, ~25% net margin, 16.6% ROE, ~¥8.85B net cash / essentially debt-free, and in a cyclical *upswing*. The clean unqualified audit (容诚/Rongcheng), the fortress balance sheet, and the oligopoly-breaking competitive position are all genuine strengths. Two honest qualifiers: (1) it is **cyclical** — earnings track the construction-machinery / excavator cycle (currently a tailwind, but the swing factor), and the recent quarter shows **margin compression** on the volume recovery; and (2) **none of this health is humanoid-driven** — the balance-sheet strength and the cyclical recovery are a hydraulics story. The financial-quality screen is the inverse of the page's humanoid-fit screen: a superb business, but not a humanoid business.

## Why Goldman puts Hengli on the roller-screw layer — the precision-grinding-credibility case (the centerpiece)

**Why the layer is the prize.** A planetary roller screw converts a motor's rotation into linear motion in the **load-bearing linear joints** (knee, ankle, arm) — threaded rollers (vs. balls) take the shock loads of walking with higher load capacity and durability. Goldman scores it **14/18** and calls it **"the most acute physical chokepoint alongside harmonic gears"** (narrow supplier base, long lead times, limited substitution). An Optimus uses **~14**, each **$1,350–$2,700**; Goldman's one public per-component figure is that the roller-screw linear joint = **20–30% of high-spec component cost** (JPM: reducers + screws ≈ 33% of BOM). Its capacity-flexibility score is the *lowest possible* (1) — because **precision grinding gates supply**, which is exactly what makes it a pricing-power chokepoint. Goldman's near-term playbook names **harmonic gears + planetary roller screws** as "the parts with the most durable pricing power."

**Where Hengli sits:**
1. **Goldman names it directly** — *"Hengli Hydraulic (China) — leveraging hydraulics/precision-machining base to localize roller-screw production"* (`humanoid-robot-high-value-subsystems-research.md` §3d) — one of only four highlighted roller-screw names (the others: Schaeffler, [[SANHUA]] ✓ in-vault, and LeaderDrive).
2. **The grinding capability is real, at scale, and primary-validated.** The linear-drive project is in production with **precision-ground (精密磨削) ball screws at 70,000 sets/yr** + 360,000 m/yr of high-precision rails, 300+ customers, and **PRS at sampling + initial mass production** (HENGLI FY2025 年报). The "精密磨削" language maps exactly onto Goldman's grinding-gated chokepoint — Hengli isn't *announcing* a roller-screw facility, it has the grinding base in production.
3. **The oligopoly-breaking pedigree backs the credibility.** Hengli broke the international-brand monopoly in high-end hydraulics — FY2025 mid-large excavator pumps/valves +40%, *"填补国内高端超大挖用液压泵领域的市场空白，进一步打破国际品牌垄断"* (filled the domestic high-end super-large-excavator-pump gap, further breaking the foreign-brand monopoly). It has *demonstrably* mastered a comparable precision-machining chokepoint against entrenched foreign incumbents — which is *why* Goldman names it over the smaller pivots.
4. **The honest verdict (Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution).** The precision-grinding *chokepoint capability* is validated at primary; the *humanoid application* is Goldman's forward extrapolation. PRS is at initial production, bundled in the accessories line, and the humanoid end-market is never named (人形 ×0). A finding, not a (g′) variance — Goldman is Tier-3. **Honest contrast:** Hengli is *more credible* than the focused pivots — **Beite** (announced a ~$260M dedicated PRS plant) and **Wuzhou Xinchun** (reverse PRS purpose-built for hip/knee joints at ~half Rollvis's price) — but *more diluted* than either, because for them PRS is a strategic focus while for Hengli it is a rounding error on a hydraulics business.

## Core business — the hydraulics champion

The ~88% base that funds the option and provides the precision-machining competency:
- **Products:** hydraulic cylinders (incl. excavator-specific + heavy-equipment non-standard cylinders) + pumps/valves/motors (incl. the high-end excavator main-control pumps/valves that broke the foreign oligopoly) + systems. The end-market is **construction machinery / excavators**, plus a diversification push into marine engineering, water conservancy, injection molding, forging, **wind power**, and agricultural machinery.
- **Position + cycle:** the dominant Chinese hydraulics name, in a cyclical recovery (excavator hydraulics sales +20% in FY2025; mid-large +40%). ~25% net margins reflect the premium, oligopoly-breaking position.
- **Globalization:** a Mexico manufacturing base in production + overseas service points (UK/Italy/Indonesia/Guinea); "从产品出海向制造出海" (product export → manufacturing export) — relevant to overseas/tariff exposure (overseas assets ~13.7%).

## Open questions

1. **Does the PRS leg ever get broken out — or a named robot/humanoid OEM?** Today it's bundled in accessories and the humanoid end-market is unnamed. A separately-disclosed roller-screw/linear-actuator revenue line or a named robotics customer is the trigger that would move "credible capability" to "humanoid business" (and trip the cohort-wide `humanoid_tier` codification trigger).
2. **Goldman's own falsifier — does precision-grinding capacity scale faster than expected?** If grinding capacity (the chokepoint constraint) scales quickly — and Hengli at 70k sets/yr is part of that scaling — lead times and margins compress and the chokepoint thesis weakens, shifting value downstream. Hengli is on both sides of this (a beneficiary if it holds; a contributor to its erosion).
3. **How does the construction-machinery cycle turn?** Earnings are cyclical; FY2025–Q1 2026 is an upswing with margin compression. Watch whether volume strength holds and whether margins stabilize.
4. **Overseas / tariff exposure** — overseas assets ~13.7% and a Mexico base; watch how globalization and trade policy interact.

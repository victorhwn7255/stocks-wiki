## Segment restructuring (FY2026) — FAS/FRS → ITS/RMS/CPI

Effective for FY2026 (as of March 31, 2026), FLEX reorganized from two segments (Flex Agility Solutions / Flex Reliability Solutions) into **three**, with prior-period segment data recast — a Section 2.1 mid-fiscal-year disclosure-shift instance (CFO Krumm, Q4 FY2026 call):

- **Integrated Technology Solutions (ITS)** — former Agility-side businesses minus the data-center carve-out: **Communications** (former non-cloud CEC) + **Lifestyle** (now including former Consumer Devices). Fast-moving industries, shorter product life cycles. FY2026 $11,109M (40%); adj op margin 5.4%.
- **Regulated Manufacturing Solutions (RMS)** — former Reliability Solutions: **Industrial + Automotive + Healthcare**. Longer life cycles, higher precision/consistency. FY2026 $10,191M (36%); adj op margin 6.0%.
- **Cloud and Power Infrastructure (CPI)** — the **data-center business**, previously split across Industrial (critical/embedded power) and CEC (cloud), now consolidated. Two business units: **Cloud and Cooling** (integrated compute for power-dense infrastructure + advanced liquid cooling) + **Power** (utility/facility electrical infrastructure + high-density rack/board power for compute). FY2026 $6,614M (24%); adj op margin 9.2%; **+38% YoY** (Power outpacing Cloud). This is the segment being spun off (SpinCo).

**Comparability boundary (Section 2.1).** Cross-period comparison crosses the restructuring date — the old FAS/FRS tables in the financial snapshot are retained as historical; the FY2026 annual table uses the recast ITS/RMS/CPI structure. FLEX will provide segment-level disclosures until the spin closes (~Q1 calendar 2027).

## CPI spin-off + CEO transition (announced May 5, 2026)

FLEX announced its intent to **spin off the CPI segment** as a separate publicly traded company ("SpinCo"), targeted for completion in **Q1 calendar 2027**, intended to be **tax-free** for U.S. shareholders. Management framing (Advaithi, Q4 FY2026 call): SpinCo will be "a global critical digital infrastructure company delivering **end-to-end power and thermal management from grid to chip** for AI data centers and mission-critical applications like utilities," differentiated by "depth across power, thermal, and compute integration" — positioned to replace the fragmented multi-vendor approach with a single grid-to-chip partner. Rationale tied to two drivers: (1) AI compute density requiring power + thermal "engineered as a unified system, not bolted on"; (2) the generational electrical transformation (solid-state transformers + 800V DC distribution "from grid to chip" — call-disclosed; not in the 10-K).

**CEO transition.** Revathi Advaithi (Flex CEO since 2019) will become **CEO of SpinCo**; **Michael Hartung** — current Chief Commercial Officer, with Flex since the 2007 Solectron acquisition — becomes **CEO of Flex (RemainCo)**. Post-spin, RemainCo guides to low-to-mid-single-digit revenue growth, reinvesting in healthcare, robotics, warehouse automation, and networking.

**EP² (Electrical Power Products) acquisition.** Closed ~May 4, 2026 (subsequent event in the FY2026 10-K); utility-grade, specification-driven solutions for grid modernization and electrification, folded into CPI/Power. Strengthens "long-cycle, margin-accretive programs supporting grid resiliency" and end-to-end grid-to-chip capability. (Purchase price not disclosed in the 10-K; ~$1.1B per the Tier 3 liquid-cooling report.)

**Google multi-year contract.** FLEX named **Google** (Tier 2 primary) as a multi-year CPI customer underpinning the 65-75% FY2027 / 80%+ FY2028 CPI growth — "not single-product manufacturing engagements... power infrastructure, thermal systems, and complex hardware manufacturing deployed at scale." Per Advaithi, CPI acceleration spans "Google and multiple other hyperscalers... across colos and neoclouds... and a wide variety of utility customers." This is a concrete primary-source customer disclosure (contrast the NVIDIA over-claim below).

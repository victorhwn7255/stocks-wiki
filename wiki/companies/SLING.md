## Thesis role

Sling is the **fifth China name the vault has taken from Goldman's humanoid reducer / rotary-actuator layer** — the top-ranked component in Goldman's Exhibit-5 ranking (score 16/18) — and it is the name where the **gap between the Goldman label and the underlying business is widest.** Goldman lists Sling on its **Exhibit-2 "Rotary actuators" map** (full weight, alongside Harmonic Drive / LeaderDrive / Zhongda Leader / Shuanghuan / Reach Machinery / FORE Intelligent), and the vault primer files it in the same rotary-actuator section. But that is the **map level**, a tier below Goldman's *thesis-level* "Buy" picks (Harmonic Drive for reducers; [[SANHUA]] + [[TUOPU]] for assembly) — and Sling's *core* business is not rotary actuators at all. Sling is, first and overwhelmingly, an **automotive wheel-hub-bearing maker** (~99% of revenue), whose rotary-actuator / harmonic-reducer business is a **real but still pre-material pivot** begun in 2024. It is anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] as a theme name, **outside the AI-datacenter frameworks** (the [[SHUANGHUAN]]/[[ZHONGDA]]/[[SANHUA]]/[[OUST]] precedent — assessed, but zero datacenter/cooling/semis exposure).

What makes the page worth writing is **how** Sling enters the layer, and the honest distance it still has to travel:

**The distinctive angle — Sling enters the rotary-actuator layer *bearing-first*.** Where [[SANHUA]]/[[TUOPU]] come at the actuator from thermal / auto-assembly, [[SHUANGHUAN]] from gears (RV), and [[ZHONGDA]] from geared motors, **Sling comes from precision *bearings*** — its decades-deep wheel-hub-bearing competency. Its 2024 "robot-components" project builds a three-step ladder, deepest-first: the **reducer-specific bearings** that go *inside* a harmonic reducer (its home turf) → the **harmonic reducer** itself → the integrated **actuator module**. That makes Sling the one cohort name approaching the chokepoint *from the bearing inward* — and it puts Sling at the same reducer-bearing sub-component as [[HARMONIC]]'s Harmonic Precision cross-roller bearings. *Full analysis below ("Sling's connection to Goldman's reducer / rotary-actuator layer").*

**The honest verdict — healthiest-but-earliest, the near-inverse of [[ZHONGDA]].** Sling is **financially healthy** — profitable (~22% net margin), strongly cash-generative (operating cash flow above net profit), net cash, clean unqualified audit. So unlike Zhongda (best-positioned, weakest financials), Sling's tension is **not** financial quality; it is that the rotary-actuator business Goldman names it for is **the smallest, earliest part of the company, and humanoid is not even named in its filings** (zero 人形 / 灵巧手 mentions; industrial framing throughout). The chokepoint placement is on the *prize* layer; the business behind it is an auto-bearing maker with an emerging reducer leg. *Full assessment below ("Is it a healthy business?").*

## Is it a healthy business? (financial-quality assessment)

**Healthy — and the near-inverse of [[ZHONGDA]] on the financial-quality screen.** As a small-cap (revenue ¥788M) the "healthy vs risky" question was open going in; it resolves to the sound end:

- **Profitable, at a strong margin.** ~22% net margin (¥172.6M on ¥788.2M) and ~32% gross margin — high for an auto-parts maker, reflecting the aftermarket / hub-bearing mix and an export book.
- **Strongly cash-generative.** Operating cash flow ¥185.8M **exceeds** net profit ¥172.6M — the opposite of Zhongda's −85% cash-flow collapse. Receivables are ¥266.8M and **98.16% aged under one year**, with export receivables covered by PICC short-term export-credit insurance.
- **Net cash, lowly levered.** Cash ¥1,076.8M (43% of assets) against a single ¥223.6M long-term bank loan (funding the US/Thailand/Yinqiu build-out) and no material short-term debt; debt ratio only 27.18%, equity ratio ~73%.
- **Clean audit, founder-aligned, paying out.** Standard unqualified opinion (标准的无保留意见, 天健 / Tianjian, signed 2026-04-20); dividend plus repeated bonus-share issues.
- **The watch item is the cycle, not the balance sheet.** Net profit −9.2% FY and −15.6% in Q1 2026, ROE stepping down (18.2% → 11.5% → 9.6%), on margin compression while revenue still grows. This is cyclical/margin pressure in the auto-bearing core, not deterioration in quality — the inverse of Zhongda, where the positioning was strong but the financials were the problem.

## Sling's connection to Goldman's reducer / rotary-actuator layer (the centerpiece)

**Why the layer is the prize.** A humanoid rotary joint (shoulder, elbow, wrist, hip) is driven by a stack of **frameless motor + precision reducer + bearing + encoder + controller**, and the **reducer is the precision bottleneck** — Goldman scores the reduction-gear layer **16/18, the top of its Exhibit-5 ranking**, on content value + technology barrier + manufacturing barrier (see [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] and the primer, `raw/research/humanoid-robot-primer-report.md`). Reducers — and the precision bearings inside them — need scarce high-precision grinding, specialized metallurgy and long qualification cycles: a durable chokepoint.

**1 — Goldman provenance: map-named, not Buy-rated.** Sling appears on Goldman's **Exhibit-2 "Rotary actuators" enabler map** at full weight, and in the vault primer's rotary-actuator section (§2A: "Sling … niche/private," 🔴). This is the **map level** — the same level as [[ZHONGDA]] and [[SHUANGHUAN]], a tier **below** Goldman's Buy-rated, thesis-level picks (Harmonic Drive for reducers; [[SANHUA]] + [[TUOPU]] for assembly). Being on the map places Sling on the highest-value layer; it does not make Sling a Goldman conviction call.

**2 — The bearing-first entry vector (the distinctive part).** Sling's 2024 "机器人零部件智能化技术改造项目" (robot-components intelligent technical-upgrade project) is the pivot, and it climbs the layer from the bearing inward — three products, deepest-first by maturity (SLING FY2025 年报):
- **减速器专用轴承 (reducer-specific bearings) — in production.** Cross-roller (交叉滚子) and flexible (柔性) bearings that sit *inside* precision reducers and high-precision rotary joints, carrying radial / axial / overturning-moment loads. This is the bearing that **gates harmonic-reducer assembly** — and it is exactly Sling's home discipline (precision bearings), leveraged straight into the chokepoint.
- **谐波减速器 (harmonic reducer / strain-wave gear) — second-phase production complete.** The strain-wave gear itself (柔轮 flexspline + 刚轮 circular spline + 波发生器 wave generator). The annual states Sling "完成谐波减速器的第二期投产工作" by period-end.
- **执行器模组 (actuator module) — R&D-reserve, pre-production.** An integrated unit combining 谐波减速器 + 无框电机 (frameless motor) + 伺服驱动板 (servo driver) + 磁性编码器 (magnetic encoder) + 刹车片 (brake) — i.e. a complete rotary actuator, "研发储备完成" but not yet in production.

**3 — The cross-roller-bearing overlap with [[HARMONIC]].** Sling's reducer-specific cross-roller bearing is the **same sub-component** as the cross-roller bearings made by [[HARMONIC]]'s Harmonic Precision arm — the precision bearing that is itself a chokepoint inside a strain-wave reducer. Sling and Harmonic meet at that reducer-bearing sub-layer. It is the cleanest illustration in the cohort of "the precision parts inside the assembly are the moat": Sling is climbing toward the rotary-actuator chokepoint from the most defensible direction it already owns.

**4 — The honest refinement (Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution; the widest gap in the cohort).**
- **Humanoid is not named.** Across all four filings, **人形 (humanoid) = 0 mentions and 灵巧手 (dexterous hand) = 0** — against Zhongda's 16× 人形. The robot framing is **industrial**: 高端智能终端设备 (high-end smart-terminal equipment), 核心运动关节 (core motion joints), machine tools, medical devices, automation lines. Sling itself calls the field "仍处于起步期" (still in its nascent stage). Goldman's "rotary actuator" placement is thus a forward read on a real but **industrial-framed, pre-humanoid** base.
- **The reducer leg is pre-material.** It is not broken out in revenue (the ~99% auto-bearing split absorbs it) — genuine and in production, but not yet a sized business.
- **"Niche/private" refined against primary.** The primer's "niche/private" tag is imprecise: Sling is a **profitable, listed ChiNext company** (¥788M revenue, ~22% net margin). The "niche" describes its *reducer* business, not the company. (Tier-3-vs-primary refinement; a finding, not a kickoff (g′) variance — the source is Tier-3 and the Goldman map-naming is itself vault-confirmed.)

## The robot-components pivot — bearing-first, and still early

- **Origin and framing.** Sling describes itself as "专业生产汽车零部件和高精密零部件的制造企业" (a maker of auto parts *and* high-precision components), "积极布局高精密零部件业务，为核心运动关节提供关键技术支持" (building out a high-precision-components business, providing key support for core motion joints) — while its stated identity remains "成为具有全球竞争力的汽车轴承制造商" (to become a globally competitive **auto-bearing** maker) (SLING FY2025 年报). Auto bearings is the identity; robot components is the expansion.
- **Reinforced by M&A.** In November 2025 Sling agreed to acquire a **24.34% stake in Ningbo Yinqiu Technology (宁波银球科技)**, an industry-leading maker of micro / small / integrated precision bearings for home appliances, industrial motors, auto parts and **high-end smart-terminal equipment** — explicitly to deepen the precision-bearing business and the smart-terminal-component push (SLING FY2025 年报). It is a precision-bearing tuck-in that strengthens the bearing-first robotics angle, not a reducer/actuator acquisition.
- **R&D.** R&D expense ¥37.65M, 137 new products developed, 21 projects in progress; a central research institute established 2024.

## Core business — automotive wheel-hub bearings

The base the page sits on is a real, scaled, export-oriented auto-parts business — outside the vault's AI-datacenter scope, but the source of the cash and the manufacturing competency the robotics pivot leans on:
- **Products.** Wheel-hub bearing units, wheel-hub bearings, tapered bearings, clutch bearings, tensioner/idler bearings, one-way pulleys, heavy-truck bearings, drive-motor bearings — applied to vehicle brake / power / transmission systems. ~99% of revenue (brake-system bearings alone 78.7%).
- **Global footprint + a tariff dimension.** ~71% of revenue is export, across North America / Europe / Asia. Sling runs two production bases (Zhejiang Xinchang + Thailand Ayutthaya, four plant areas), completed a third-phase Thailand expansion in 2025, and is **building a new US plant in Michigan** (foundations complete, production pending) — a localization response to the trade/tariff exposure that an export-heavy book carries.

## Open questions

1. **Does the reducer/actuator leg become a sized business?** Harmonic reducers and reducer bearings are in production but unbroken-out; the actuator module is still R&D. Watch for the first separately-disclosed reducer/actuator revenue or a named OEM — the trigger that moves "pivot" to "business."
2. **Does the bearing-first vertical integration win?** Sling makes both the cross-roller bearing *and* the harmonic reducer that uses it. Does owning the precision bearing inside the reducer translate into a cost/quality edge against pure-reducer peers (Shuanghuan, Zhongda, LeaderDrive) — or does it stay a niche line bundled into an auto-parts company?
3. **Does the auto-bearing cycle bottom?** Net profit −9.2% FY and −15.6% in Q1 2026 on margin compression while revenue grows. Watch whether margins and ROE stabilize through 2026 or keep stepping down.
4. **Does export concentration / tariffs bite — and does the US plant de-risk it?** ~71% export with a heavy US/Europe book; watch how the Michigan plant ramp and trade policy interact.
5. **Does the access picture change?** A-share-only with no H-listing or subsidiary IPO — among the least accessible of the cohort to an international book (directly ownable under the China-access lens).

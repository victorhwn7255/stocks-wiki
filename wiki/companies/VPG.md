# VPG — Vishay Precision Group, Inc.

## Thesis role

VPG is the second US name in Goldman's humanoid force/torque sensor row (alongside [[NOVT]]) and the next ingest off the [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] value-layer map. Like NOVT, the primary sources reframe it: VPG is a **small, diversified, barely-profitable precision-measurement company** whose vault-relevant exposure is an **AI-datacenter precision-resistor leg, with humanoid as a disclosed-but-tiny optionality** — plus a genuine broad aerospace/defense leg. The honest-verdict contrast with NOVT is the useful product: VPG ties its demand to "AI infrastructure" more *explicitly* and even *quantifies* its humanoid revenue (NOVT did neither), but it is roughly a third of NOVT's size, structurally lower-margin, and posted a **GAAP net loss in Q1 2026**.

**What VPG is.** A precision sensing and measurement company in three reportable segments (FY2025): **Sensors** ($115.6M, 38% — precision foil resistors + foil strain gages), **Weighing Solutions** ($111.1M, 36% — load cells, weighing modules and systems; largely industrial/medical/ag, **out of vault scope**), and **Measurement Systems** ($80.4M, 26% — DTS/DSI data-acquisition instrumentation). Revenue has been roughly flat-to-declining ($355.0M in 2023 → $306.5M 2024 → $307.2M 2025) and is now inflecting up: Q1 2026 revenue +18% YoY, bookings **$102.1M — the first quarter above $100M since 2022** (book-to-bill 1.21) (VPG Q1 2026 call).

**The vault-relevant leg — AI-infrastructure precision resistors (in Sensors).** Management's strongest-demand callout: *"particularly robust demand related to AI infrastructure. Orders grew for precision resistors used in semiconductor front-end and back-end equipment, supporting the manufacturing and testing of AI-related chips and systems, as well as in data centers and fiber optics equipment"* (Shoshani, VPG Q1 2026 call). Sensors bookings hit their highest level in 15 quarters (B/B 1.36). The AI-datacenter slice is not separately sized, but it is the named growth driver of the segment.

**Placement — Equipment Tier 4 (differentiated-component / indirect-exposure scope), Layer 4, defense Outside, theme-anchored humanoid.**
- **`equipment_tier 4`.** VPG's precision foil resistors are differentiated passive components going **indirectly** into semiconductor front+back-end equipment + data-center + fiber-optics equipment — a near-exact match to Framework 8 Tier 4 ("differentiated components and indirect exposure"). Decisive precedent [[VECO]] (Tier 4 on 9% relevant revenue); the AI-infrastructure tie here is named and growth-driving, so `outside` would under-place. Structure analog [[NOVT]] (the paired sensor-layer name) and [[MKSI]].
- **`defense_tier: outside`.** VPG has a real, named aerospace/defense/space leg — "avionics & military, space communications" is a core precision-resistor market, and Measurement Systems' DTS data-acquisition modules hit record sales on defense missile-test, hypersonic, and military-jet-engine programs. But this is **broad aerospace/defense test & instrumentation, not unmanned systems** — weak fit for the drones-first Defense & Drones thesis, so `outside` is the honest-verdict-correct designation (assessed; real A&D exposure; weak unmanned-systems fit). Detailed below.
- **`photonics_tier` absent (—).** VPG makes no photonics/optical products; the fiber-optics tie is a customer end-market captured under the equipment placement (distinct from NOVT, which actually makes lasers → `outside`). memory / energy_power / materials absent — no substantive direct exposure (Section 3.2 (c)).
- **No `humanoid_tier`** (held under YAGNI) → humanoid relevance is theme-anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] (the [[INOD]] / [[NOVT]] precedent), detailed below.
- **Layer 4** (component supplier / differentiated integrator), matching NOVT/MKSI.

**Period + filing discipline.** VPG runs a **52/53-week fiscal year ending ~December 31** (FY2025 ended December 31, 2025; Q1 2026 ended **April 4, 2026**). Per Section 2.11.1 this is a **week-scope offset → Section 2.11 N/A**; Q1 2026 is treated as calendar Q1 2026, joining the [[NOVT]] / [[AMD]] / [[INTC]] week-scope precedent — though *not* an exact-period-parity match with NOVT (Apr 4 vs NOVT's Apr 3). **Section 2.1 N/A** — three reportable segments, stable across both periods. **`foreign_issuer false`** — incorporated in **Delaware** (Aug 28, 2009); HQ Chesterbrook, PA; 10-K/10-Q; US GAAP; USD (the company carries heavy Israel operations via its Vishay heritage, but is a straightforward US-domestic filer — no edge case).

## The AI-datacenter precision-resistor leg (the vault-relevant core)

VPG's foil resistors (VFR, Alpha Electronics, Powertron, APR brands) are high-precision, high-stability passive components. The AI-relevant demand spans two equipment classes:
- **Semiconductor front- and back-end equipment** — resistors used in the tools that manufacture *and test* AI chips ("test & measurement including semiconductor test and production" is a core resistor market per the FY2025 10-K).
- **Data-center and fiber-optics equipment** — supporting the AI-datacenter build-out (Shoshani, VPG Q1 2026 call).

This is the Sensors-segment growth engine: in Q1, Sensors revenue rose 23% YoY with bookings at a 15-quarter high. The leg is not separately quantified, but management's framing is unambiguous that AI infrastructure (alongside defense) is the dominant booking driver — *"very strong demand around AI infrastructure in addition to defense"* (Shoshani). The new three-year model names "semiconductor equipment used in AI processing, and data center and fiber optics infrastructure" as a core secular driver.

## Humanoid / sensor optionality (theme-anchored)

Unlike NOVT, VPG **discloses and quantifies** its humanoid exposure — though it is tiny and entirely in management commentary (the word "humanoid" appears **zero** times in the 10-K and 10-Q; the FY2025 10-K mentions only "industrial and medical robotics" as an emerging strain-gage application):
- Foil **strain gages used in humanoid pre-production prototypes** (VPG Q1 2026 call).
- **~$600K shipped to humanoid makers in Q1 2026**, with Q2 *"expected to more than double that amount."* The three-year model assumes humanoid revenue grows **~50% annually from 2025 levels** — analyst math of *~$5M in 2026 toward low-teens-$M in the out years* was confirmed as a reasonable baseline by management.
- **Two established (pre-production) humanoid customers**, plus early engineering discussions with a third and a **fourth — a startup building humanoid platforms for defense, home, and industrial use**.
- Management framed it honestly: *"the adoption rate is still fairly low… we are still in pre-production levels… anything can happen"* (Shoshani). At ~$5M of ~$340M projected 2026 revenue, humanoid is **~1.5% optionality**, not a base case.

This is the [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] needle-mover screen confirming again — a clean US sensor name where humanoid is real but small. The disclosed-magnitude contrast with NOVT (which shipped to humanoid makers but never sized it) is itself the finding.

## Aerospace, defense & space leg

VPG's defense exposure is broader and more material than NOVT's, which is why the page carries `defense_tier: outside` rather than absent:
- **Precision resistors** — "avionics & military, space communications" is one of the named core resistor markets (VPG FY2025 10-K).
- **Measurement Systems / DTS** — ruggedized miniature data-acquisition modules hit **record Q1 sales on defense missile-test projects**; orders grew for testing of **military jet engines and hypersonic missiles**. DTS data loggers flew on **NASA Artemis II** (lunar launch) and have been used on **SpaceX Dragon** and **Blue Origin** platforms.

This is genuine, recurring aerospace/defense test-and-instrumentation exposure — but it is **broad A&D, not unmanned systems**, so it does not meet the drones-first Defense & Drones thesis. `defense_tier: outside` is the honest-verdict designation (assessed; real exposure; weak unmanned-systems fit). A drone- or counter-UAS-specific disclosure would be the trigger to revisit.

## Segments and the new three-year operating model

The headline of the Q1 call was a **new three-year target operating model**, set by a refreshed leadership structure (new Chief Business & Process Officer + Chief Operating Officer organizations):
- **8–10% compounded annual organic growth** (Sensors and Measurement Systems above that rate; Weighing Solutions below — a favorable mix shift).
- Targets: **gross margin 46.5%**, operating margin 14.5–15.5%, EBITDA margin 18.5–20.5% — a substantial step-up from current levels (FY2025 38.9% gross margin; Q1 2026 0.4% GAAP operating margin).
- **$20M+ of cost reductions over three years** (manufacturing-footprint optimization, automation, procurement) against ~$5M of new annual org cost; up to 50% incremental-EBITDA flow-through.
- Two top-line drivers: secular growth (advanced robotics, AI-processing semiconductor equipment, data-center/fiber-optics infrastructure, A&D) + a cross-company sales/marketing/BD transformation. Business-development initiatives booked $10M in Q1 toward a $45M 2026 goal.

Management was explicit the model rests on a conservative 2025 baseline given limited visibility ("we have no visibility, we decided to take 2025 as a baseline").

## Open questions

1. **Does the AI-infrastructure Sensors demand sustain the booking surge?** Q1 bookings (>$100M, B/B 1.21; Sensors B/B 1.36) are the strongest since 2022 — but off three years averaging below 1.0. Verify the AI-infra/semi-equipment/data-center slice converts to sustained revenue, and whether VPG begins sizing it.
2. **Humanoid conversion.** Does the disclosed pre-production revenue ($600K Q1 → ~$5M 2026 target) scale, and do the 3rd/4th customer discussions (incl. the defense-humanoid startup) convert? Trigger to revisit theme-anchoring / a `humanoid_tier` if VPG materially sizes the leg.
3. **The margin-recovery model.** The new three-year targets (46.5% gross / 14.5–15.5% operating) are a large step-up from a 0.4% GAAP-operating-margin quarter. Track gross-margin progression and whether the $20M cost program lands.
4. **Q1 GAAP loss vs adjusted earnings.** Confirm the GAAP loss is a seasonal + new-org-cost + FX artifact (not structural deterioration) as full-year 2026 progresses.
5. **Defense leg.** Does any drone / counter-UAS-specific DTS or resistor disclosure emerge that would move it from `defense_tier outside` toward a numeric defense placement?

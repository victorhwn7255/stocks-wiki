# PLTR — Palantir Technologies Inc.

## Thesis role

Palantir is an AI-native data/analytics platform company (Gotham, Foundry, AIP) — application-layer software, a *consumer* of AI compute, not a supplier of any datacenter-supply-chain component. It is the third [[software-AI-moat-durability]] scorecard name ingested (after [[MSFT]] and [[NOW]]), chosen to test the *other end* of the billed-unit spectrum: the scorecard's cleanest **Durable / amplified** verdict (vs the two Contested-ish seat names), the **AI-native data-fusion** archetype, and a **deal/outcome-based** billed unit.

**Placement framing.** `layer: outside` + `outside` on all five per-domain tiers, following the demand-side / application-layer precedent set by [[MSFT]] and [[NOW]]. PLTR captures no supply-side chokepoint rent; its vault role is the research goal plus one genuine cross-vault touchpoint (see Vault touchpoint below). Per honest-verdict discipline (Section 2.1), its fit to the broader supply-chain thesis is weak by design.

**`frameworks.md` demand-side placement — RESOLVED (codified S189, 2026-07-19).** The application-layer placement gap is codified — **CLAUDE.md Section 3.21 + frameworks.md Framework 10.1** (PLTR = the **app-layer-consumer** sub-role, the third of the four flag instances). `_thesis.md` unchanged.

**Research-goal verdict (validated at primary; see [[software-AI-moat-durability]]):** **Durable / amplified (execution-dependent)** — held. The "amplified" leg is strongly supported by Q1 2026 (85% revenue growth, Rule of 40 of 145, NDR 150%); the "execution-dependent" caveat is real (government concentration, high-expectations valuation, heavy SBC, FDE-led GTM that is hard to scale).

## Billed-unit lens & AI monetization

PLTR adds the **third billed-unit flavor** to the [[software-AI-moat-durability]] discriminator (after MSFT's seat-vs-consumption split and NOW's seat→consumption conversion): **deal / outcome-based** contract value, neither per-seat nor pure consumption. Per Q1 2026:

- TCV bookings **$2.4B** (+61% YoY); US commercial TCV bookings $1.2B (+45%); trailing-12-month US commercial TCV $4.7B (+115%).
- Remaining performance obligations **$4.5B** (+134% YoY); total remaining deal value **$11.8B** (+98%); NDR **150%**.
- Customers commit multi-year contract values reflecting outcome-driven AIP deployments (bootcamp land-and-expand), rather than seat licenses or metered consumption.
- **AIP revenue is not separately disclosed** — it is embedded in total revenue (no discrete AI line in the filings); the AI contribution is visible only through the 100%+ US growth and the TCV/booking metrics surfaced on the call (Tier 2). A mild Tier-1/Tier-2 framing gap (the call adds Rule of 40, NDR, TCV; the filings carry the GAAP financials) — see Source audit notes.

## Moat — ontology + agentic execution

PLTR's moat is better described as **ontology depth + agentic execution** than as a classic usage-data flywheel (a refinement of the scorecard's "data fusion + flywheel" framing). The argument, per the Q1 2026 call:

- The **ontology** (Foundry's semantic model of an enterprise's data, processes, and decisions) is the substrate agents act on — "the body of the AI brains" (CTO Shyam Sankar). AIP orchestrates agents across the ontology with governance ("a true AI no-slop zone… converting potential AI leverage into compounding real-world value without risking enterprise disaster," CRO Ryan Taylor).
- Management frames "**operational AI**" (outcome-producing, ontology-governed) against "models alone" / generic LLMs, and invokes a Jevons-paradox demand thesis: "tokens are the new coal, AIP is the train" (Sankar) — cheaper tokens → more AIP usage.
- Customer evidence (call): AIG agentic underwriting/claims, GE Aerospace agentic production, Moder/Freedom Mortgage, USDA ($300M), Ondas/WorldView.

This is the Durable/amplified leg — defensible, but the defensibility is tied to continued execution (the ontology/FDE motion does not commoditize, customers absorb the deployments) rather than a passive data-network effect.

## Growth & the execution-dependent caveats

The "amplified" metrics are strong: Q1 2026 revenue +85% YoY (highest as a public company), US revenue +104% (>100% for the first time since the DPO), US commercial +133%, Rule of 40 of 145, customer count 1,007 (+31% YoY; US commercial customers 615, +42%), FY2026 revenue guidance raised to **$7.65–7.66B (+71%)**.

The honest counterweight (the "execution-dependent" qualifier):
- **Government concentration ~42% (FY2025) → ~53% (Q1 2026)** — material, though below the scorecard's prior ~55% assumption, and government grows slower than commercial. US-government-budget/appropriations risk is a named 10-K risk factor.
- **High-expectations valuation** — Karp himself referenced market "hyperbolic rhetoric"; the stock prices in sustained hyper-growth, leaving little margin for execution slips.
- **Stock-based-comp dilution** — the 46%-GAAP-vs-60%-adjusted operating-margin gap is SBC; diluted shares ~2.57B and rising.
- **FDE-led GTM** — the forward-deployed-engineer motion that drives the ontology moat is talent-intensive and structurally hard to scale.

## Vault touchpoint

PLTR is the only software-moat scorecard name with a pre-existing vault footprint: it is the **Palantir Foundry / AI partner at Centrus ([[LEU]])** for the Piketon HALEU enrichment supply chain — "$300M identified cost savings" and a $5M Q1 prepayment, alongside Fluor (EPC) and Geiger Brothers (construction). Documented at primary on [[LEU]] and [[HALEU-fuel-chokepoint]]; LEU's primary sources name Palantir (an A1 reciprocal-confirmation). This is a vault-internal example of AIP/Foundry deployed in a tracked supply chain — a commercial-customer data point, not a change to PLTR's application-layer placement.

A second, lighter touchpoint surfaced S135: [[INOD]] (the anchor of [[AI-data-supply-chain]]) disclosed on its Q1 2026 call that its Federal practice does computer-vision work *"in concert with Palantir"* for US government customers — emerging and immaterial to PLTR (INOD-side disclosure, not reciprocally confirmed in PLTR primary sources), noted for provenance.

## Open questions

3. **SBC dilution.** Does the GAAP/adjusted margin gap narrow, or does dilution persist as a structural drag?
4. **Valuation/expectations.** Can execution sustain the hyper-growth the valuation embeds?
5. **FDE-led GTM at scale.** Does the ontology/FDE motion scale, or is talent the binding constraint?

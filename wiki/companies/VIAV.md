# VIAV — Viavi Solutions Inc.

## Thesis role

VIAV occupies Layer 4 (specialized equipment supplier) in the value capture framework and Photonics Tier 4 (specialized equipment supplier with AI photonics ramp tailwind). VIAV designs and sells network test, monitoring, and assurance solutions for telecommunications, cloud, enterprises, first responders, military, aerospace, and critical infrastructure. The company also operates an Optical Security and Performance Products (OSP) segment for anti-counterfeiting pigments and 3D sensing. VIAV's core photonics-thesis exposure is via finished-module optical test equipment supporting AI data center build-out — fiber field instruments, lab and production test products, and Spirent-acquired high-speed Ethernet (HSE) + network security + channel emulation testing businesses.

`_thesis.md` does not yet list VIAV as a position. The wiki tracks VIAV as the **first vault Chokepoint 6 (Finished-module optical test) primary-source baseline** per Tier 3 framework Rank 6 / medium-high conviction. Vault previously held Keysight, Anritsu, and VIAVI as plain-text Tier C references per Session 24 Integration 4.

*Control-point distinction (per `raw/research/photonics-chokepoint-table.md`, Tier 3 source).* VIAV exhibits pure bottleneck participant Framework 2.6 placement — no architectural authority over AI compute platform decisions. Spirent acquisition (closed October 16, 2025; $425M; from Keysight Technologies) and multi-quarter visibility extension (one-and-a-half quarter historical visibility to up-to-three quarters ahead) are commercial scaling, not control-point aspirations. VIAV supplies test equipment to NEMs, hyperscalers, and service providers downstream of platform decisions; the Etherlink 1.6T transition test methodologies (IEEE 802.3dj) are standards-body-derived rather than VIAV-architected. Cross-reference: control-point thread is at Chokepoint 7 (Switch ASIC / platform architecture) and spans [[NVDA]], [[AVGO]], [[MRVL]], [[CSCO]], [[ANET]] within vault per framework five-company set.

**Structural AI photonics with broader test-equipment cyclicality.** Data center ecosystem has expanded from prior-quarter ~40% of NSE revenue to ~45% in Q2 FY2026 (per Khaykin Q2 call). Aerospace and defense PNT (positioning, navigation, timing) products are structural growth drivers via Inertial Labs acquisition (completed January 2025, $121.7M). Service provider segment (~40% of NSE) is cyclical recovery with stable/slightly-improving demand from cable operators (DAA architecture, Access 4.0); wireless infrastructure test demand is "weak but stable."

**Scale context.** VIAV is mid-cap (FY2025 revenue $1,084.3M; ~5,000+ employees; $772.1M cash & ST investments end of Q2 FY2026 driven by Spirent-related Term Loan B financing and convertible note management). Net loss Q2 FY2026 -$48.1M is non-recurring (driven by -$38.7M loss on convertible note extinguishment); operating margin Q2 FY2026 was 19.3% non-GAAP — above guidance.

## Product portfolio

### Network and Service Enablement (NSE)

Effective March 30, 2025, VIAV realigned segment reporting: previously-separate NE (Network Enablement) and SE (Service Enablement) segments merged into single NSE segment. Reasons disclosed: Inertial Labs and Spirent acquisitions reduced SE revenue as percentage of total; common leadership and shared customers/suppliers (VIAV 10-K FY2025).

NSE product families:
- **Fiber test instruments** — high-performance integrated circuits, telecom/datacom equipment, modules, networks; lab and production environments
- **Wireless products** — flexible application software + multi-functional hardware for wireless infrastructure development and management
- **AIOps solutions** — AI/ML/big data analytics for network operations automation
- **NPTS (Network Performance and Threat Solutions)** — enterprise IT and security monitoring
- **Aerospace and Defense** — mission-critical communications + avionics test equipment + PNT products (Inertial Labs)
- **Spirent product lines (post-October 16, 2025 closing):** High-Speed Ethernet (HSE) + network security + channel emulation testing — acquired from Keysight Technologies for $425M

### Optical Security and Performance Products (OSP)

- Anti-counterfeiting pigments
- 3D sensing
- High-performance optical applications: free-space optical communications, quantum key distribution, quantum computing, automotive LiDAR

OSP is structurally distinct from NSE — different end markets (currency/document security; consumer 3D sensing) with different growth and cyclicality patterns. FY2025 OSP improved YoY with growth in Anti-Counterfeiting and Other products.

## Strategic positioning

### Co-packaged optics (CPO) momentum substantive subsection — Q3 FY2026 (Session 58 refresh)

The Q3 FY2026 disclosure materially strengthens VIAV's co-packaged optics test equipment positioning vs S28 baseline implicit framing. CEO Khaykin Q3 FY2026 call surfaces CPO momentum as substantive Q3 driver:

**CPO test equipment positioning at semiconductor vendor scope.** Khaykin Q3 call: *"On the production, we are seeing a lot of momentum on this whole co-packaged optics area, and that plays extremely well to the traditional Viavi strength with the, you know, the old JDS Uniphase products that go into the production line where you are measuring a spectral performance, the optical performance of all the various optics. It's also, we are selling now a lot of that equipment to the semiconductor vendors as they develop their integrated packaged optics solution. I mean, it's pretty much everything we do in that, in that area relating to the advanced silicon for both training and inference applications and all the optical gear that goes into the data centers is playing and perfectly aligned to our portfolio."* — CPO test equipment selling to semiconductor vendors developing integrated packaged optics; "perfectly aligned to our portfolio" framing strengthens vs S28 implicit framing.

**1.6T + 3.2T trajectory.** Khaykin Q3 call: *"the 800G is still very much a high volume driver. A lot of the new development is using our 1.6T. By the way, we released 1.6T a year and a half ago. You just see. Actually, ironically, it was initially the NEMs, the optical equipment vendors who deployed it first in development, and now it's spreading to the semis and the whole packaged optics. Yeah, I mean, 1.6T is ramping in the lab, and we're also having some of the early production product to the module vendors."* — 1.6T ramping (lab + early production); 3.2T trajectory next (analyst Ruben Roy Q3 follow-up: "things like 1.6T co-packaged optics").

**Field instruments data center mix to 40-50% (vs Q2 ~33%).** Khaykin Q3 call: *"as all these data centers come up, they are putting a lot of investment into ensuring peak performance.

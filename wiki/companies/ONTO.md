# ONTO — Onto Innovation

## Thesis role

ONTO occupies Layer 3-4 (advanced packaging metrology) in the value capture framework and Photonics Tier 4 (real but indirect — clean, no tension). ONTO provides process control metrology, inspection, and lithography systems for semiconductor manufacturing, with approximately 50% of FY2025 revenue from advanced packaging and specialty devices. The company's metrology and inspection tools enable yield and quality for advanced packaging processes including 2.5D/3D integration, bump metrology, and HBM stack inspection — directly adjacent to [[TSMC-CoWoS]] and HBM production workflows.

`_thesis.md` tracks: CoWoS/COUPE-adjacent revenue, metrology share, memory and logic cyclicality offset. The disconfirming signal: "The advanced packaging test/metrology opportunity is smaller than expected. If CoWoS yields improve faster than predicted, AEHR and ONTO's positioning is less leveraged."

**Photonics_tier 4 (clean).** ONTO does not discuss silicon photonics, CPO, co-packaged optics, or photonic packaging in any of three sources. Its advanced packaging metrology inspects the physical package (bump height, alignment, defects) regardless of whether photonic or electrical interconnect is used. This is packaging-substrate-agnostic metrology — real AI exposure through packaging volumes, but no direct photonics engagement. Compare with [[AEHR]], which has a photonics_tier 4 *with tension* due to direct silicon photonics burn-in discussion.

### AI inspection tension: FY2025 decline vs. FY2026 growth guidance

**The 10-K and the earnings call present structurally divergent signals on AI-related inspection demand that the wiki preserves without adjudicating.**

The ONTO 10-K FY2025 MD&A states that FY2025 systems/software revenue decreased in part due to "a decrease in units shipped of our inspection product line to customers in support of advanced packaging needs for chips used in AI applications" (ONTO 10-K FY2025). This is a reported-fact finding: inspection unit shipments for AI advanced packaging fell in FY2025 relative to FY2024.

The ONTO Q4 FY2025 earnings call (February 19, 2026) guides advanced packaging growth of >30% in 2026 and describes demand as strong, with backlog nearly doubled (ONTO Q4 FY2025 call).

**Possible reconciliations (not adjudicated):**

1. **Mix shift toward metrology.** The 10-K notes the inspection decline was "partially offset by an increase in metrology product line units shipped to customers in Advanced Nodes." The growth may be rotating from inspection (Dragonfly) toward metrology (Atlas G6 for GAA/HBM DRAM), changing the product composition without reducing total AI-related demand.

2. **Cycle trough recovery.** FY2025 may represent a digestion cycle between initial AI packaging buildout (FY2024 was the "first large wave") and the next expansion phase. The $240M+ VPA and >30% growth guidance reflect committed future demand, not current-period shipments.

3. **Guidance-vs-reported-fact disclosure lifecycle.** The 10-K reports what happened (Tier 1, backward-looking); the call guides what management expects (Tier 2, forward-looking). Both statements can be simultaneously true. The Tier 1/Tier 2 divergence follows the established disclosure lifecycle pattern: Tier 2 calls are more optimistic on forward trajectory while Tier 1 filings report the actual period results, which may include timing gaps.

**This tension is flagged as an open question for tracking.** If Q1 FY2026 results (expected May 2026) show inspection unit recovery, reconciliation #2 is supported. If inspection continues declining while metrology grows, reconciliation #1 is the operative dynamic. If neither, the >30% growth guidance requires re-evaluation.

If export controls tighten further (the "50% Rule" Affiliates Rule, suspended until November 2026), ONTO's China revenue could decline further. Monitor regulatory developments.

5. **Semilab integration.** Post-acquisition operating loss of $6.2M in partial quarter. Integration risk cited as a risk factor. Revenue contribution of $8.6M implies ~$35M annualized — modest relative to the $527M consideration.

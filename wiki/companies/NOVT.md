# NOVT — Novanta Inc.

## Thesis role

NOVT entered the vault through the **humanoid-robot value chain** — Goldman names it in the sensor layer ("Novanta (ATI Industrial Automation)") and the two commissioned humanoid reports flag it as one of the cleanest US-listed picks Goldman underweights. **Primary sources falsify the humanoid-pure-play framing: humanoid is NOVT's *smallest* AI leg, not its thesis.** The 10-K mentions "humanoids" exactly once — as one item in a list of servo-drive applications (NOVT FY2025 10-K). On the call, management explicitly tempered it: the NVIDIA-ecosystem robotics opportunity is *"still in a prototype phase… we're tempering the excitement… we expect it to be more meaningful 2027"* (Glastra, NOVT Q1 2026 call). The page therefore lands as an **AI-datacenter semiconductor-equipment component supplier with humanoid as theme-anchored optionality** — which *confirms* the humanoid theme's pre-registered needle-mover screen (verify revenue mix before calling any name a humanoid play) rather than refuting it. This is a Section 4.4 kickoff-hypothesis finding, not a marketing-tier factual variance.

**What NOVT actually is.** A diversified precision-technology compounder supplying differentiated photonics, motion-control, and sensing **components and subsystems to OEMs** across medical and advanced-industrial end markets (~53% medical / ~47% advanced industrial of Q1 2026 sales; NOVT Q1 2026 call). Two reportable segments: **Automation Enabling Technologies** ($500.8M, 51% of FY2025 revenue — Robotics & Automation $318.6M + Precision Manufacturing $182.3M) and **Medical Solutions** ($479.8M, 49% — Precision Medicine $237.3M + Advanced Surgery $242.4M) (NOVT FY2025 10-K). The ~49% medical half is **outside vault scope**; the vault-relevant exposure sits inside the industrial half.

**The genuine vault-relevant leg — the GenAI-datacenter collective (~15% of revenue, growing ~20% YoY).** Management quantified, for the first time, a cross-segment "collective" of applications pulled by GenAI infrastructure: *"DUV and EUV lithography, advanced packaging, probe card production, precision robotics, GPU drilling, metrology for advanced semiconductor wafer nodes… approximately 15% of total company revenue in the first quarter… grew about 20% year-over-year, and we expect this growth rate to increase"* (Glastra, NOVT Q1 2026 call). Within that, "semiconductor" ≈ 10% of sales and robotics ≈ 20% (Buckley, same call). This is a **differentiated-component / indirect-exposure** position into semiconductor capital equipment — NOVT sells to the OEMs and to fabs' tool chains, not the fabs directly.

**Placement — Equipment Tier 4 (differentiated-component / indirect-exposure scope), Layer 4, theme-anchored humanoid.**
- **`equipment_tier 4`.** Framework 8 Tier 4 is "differentiated components and indirect exposure" — a near-exact structural match (air-bearing spindles for GPU-board drilling; laser beam steering for DUV/EUV light engines and advanced packaging; metrology and precision motion for advanced wafer nodes). The decisive precedent is [[VECO]], placed at `equipment_tier 4` on only **9%** Compound-Semiconductor revenue; NOVT's ~10–15% AI-datacenter semiconductor exposure clears that comfortably. Page-structure analog: [[MKSI]] (Layer 4, `equipment_tier 2`; a larger, more diversified precision/photonics supplier into semi equipment).
- **Considered and rejected: `outside`.** The Outside case is real — Framework 8's Outside example is "diversified industrial test/measurement with semi as a minor segment," and NOVT is ~49% medical. But the four-criterion test (Section 3.10) splits ~2-2: NOVT *does* name and quantify a specific, growing AI-datacenter served segment (15% / +20% YoY) rather than enumerating broad-cycle exposure. Given the VECO precedent, `outside` would *under*-place NOVT; Tier 4 (the lowest numeric tier) honestly signals "real but indirect and minor."
- **`photonics_tier: outside`.** NOVT makes lasers/photonics, but for medical, industrial, and lithography end uses — **not datacenter optics** (no transceivers / silicon photonics / CPO). The DUV/EUV light-engine and laser-beam-steering photonics are captured under the equipment placement; assigning a numeric photonics_tier as well would double-count the same leg. All four Outside-Framework-5 criteria are met → honest-verdict `outside`.
- **No `humanoid_tier`** (held under YAGNI per the v0.1 humanoid anchors) → humanoid relevance is theme-anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] (the [[INOD]] / demand-side precedent), with the optionality detailed below, not a frontmatter field.
- **Absent (—): memory / energy_power / materials / defense** — no substantive direct exposure (Section 3.2 (c) substantive-exposure discipline). Note: the laser-additive-manufacturing line touches *aerospace and drone production* (Glastra, NOVT Q1 2026 call), but it is an undifferentiated sliver of the GenAI/industrial collective — not a `defense_tier`-warranting unmanned-systems position.

**Filing-status note (convention edge case).** Novanta is **incorporated in New Brunswick, Canada** but headquartered in Bedford, Massachusetts and **files as a US domestic filer** (Form 10-K + 10-Q, US GAAP, USD) — *not* a foreign private issuer (20-F) and *not* an MJDS filer (40-F like [[CCJ]]). `foreign_issuer: false` is set because the field's operational purpose (Section 4.2) is to signal a non-standard source structure, and NOVT's source set is the standard domestic 10-K + 10-Q + call. The domicile-vs-filing-regime distinction is flagged as a possible convention clarification (first Canadian-incorporated US-domestic-filer in the vault).

**Period discipline.** NOVT runs a **52/53-week fiscal year ending on the Sunday nearest December 31** (FY2025 ended December 31, 2025; Q1 2026 ended April 3, 2026). Per Section 2.11.1 this is a **week-scope offset → Section 2.11 N/A**; Q1 2026 is treated as substantively calendar Q1 2026, joining the [[AMD]] / [[INTC]] / [[NVDA]] week-scope precedent. **Section 2.1 N/A** — two reportable segments, stable across both periods (no mid-year segment shift).

## The GenAI-datacenter semiconductor-equipment leg (the vault-relevant core)

This is why NOVT belongs in the vault. The ~15%-of-revenue, ~20%-growth GenAI "collective" (Q1 2026) spans the tool chain for advanced-node and AI-GPU manufacturing — and NOVT claims niche leadership in several pieces:
- **GPU-board drilling (air-bearing spindles).** The legacy "Westwind" air-bearing-spindle business (PCB/semiconductor drilling, ~300,000 RPM) — depressed to ~$2M/quarter after the 2023 China downturn — has caught a GPU tailwind. Thick (~40-layer) AI-GPU boards favor precise, fast mechanical + laser drilling; management claims it is *"the number 1, by far"* (Glastra, NOVT Q1 2026 call). This makes NOVT a **board-fab equipment enabler** of the [[pcb-interconnect-substrate-chokepoint]] (the drilling tool gating high-layer board fabrication, Layer 1) — enabler, not chokepoint owner; NOVT is listed in that page's vault-adjacent `tickers`.
- **Lithography light engines + laser beam steering.** "Intelligent laser beam steering subsystems" for DUV/EUV lithography light engines, advanced packaging/substrate, and **probe-card production for AI-GPU testing** — described as market-leading proprietary subsystems for advanced (≤2nm) nodes (NOVT Q1 2026 call).
- **Metrology + precision robotics** for advanced wafer-fab nodes.

Management is conspicuously disciplined about *not* overclaiming this leg: *"it should not materially change as a % of sales… nothing is going to outpace something else dramatically"* (Buckley); the message is repeatedly that the strength is the *collective* of niche positions, not any single hyped business. Honest framing per Section 2.1 — this is structural diversification, not a semiconductor pure-play. NOVT frames a ~**$4B incremental market opportunity by 2030** across its four growth platforms (AI-driven robotics & automation; minimally invasive & robotic surgery; digital/AI-driven manufacturing; Precision Medicine).

## Humanoid / Physical AI optionality (theme-anchored)

The humanoid linkage that put NOVT on the radar is real but early and immaterial to current revenue:
- **NVIDIA Holoscan AI Systems Inspection Lab (March 2026).** NOVT says it is *"the only servo drive manufacturer… that actually got selected"* for the rigorous certification; its drives are certified in the NVIDIA ecosystem, so *"whenever there is humanoids and warehouse automation or other precision robotics OEMs that want to use the NVIDIA infrastructure,"* they specify NOVT-certified drives (Glastra, NOVT Q1 2026 call).
- **Magnitude — optionality only.** *"These applications are still in a prototype phase… we're tempering the excitement… still early in the adoption cycle. We expect it to be more meaningful 2027, but we do see some more meaningful prototype orders coming our way"* (Glastra). Management characterized the certification's near-term value as *credibility* (third-party validation of its safety-rated servo-drive technology), not revenue.
- **ATI Industrial Automation** — the six-axis force/torque entity Goldman names — was **not mentioned on the call**; the call's robotics framing is servo drives + precision robotics + "Physical AI," not force/torque sensing specifically. Per Section 3.3 (disclosed/inferable/unknown), NOVT's humanoid *content* is disclosed only at the application-list level, not sized.

Net: NOVT's humanoid exposure is genuine optionality embedded in a broader "Physical AI / precision robotics" tailwind, sequenced behind 2027 — exactly the kind of legacy-business-with-an-AI-option the [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] needle-mover screen is designed to catch. The paired US sensor-layer name is **[[VPG]]** (Vishay Precision Group, ingested S139) — the smaller, lower-margin sibling that, unlike NOVT, actually *quantifies* its humanoid revenue; see the NOVT-vs-VPG comparison on that page.

- **Medical Solutions** (Q1 2026 $126.5M, +15%; adj. gross margin ~43%): Advanced Surgery (insufflators, smoke evacuation, robotic-surgery components, +11%) + Precision Medicine (life-science/multi-omics + the 2025 Keonn RFID acquisition, +18%). **Out of vault scope** but the larger growth engine; medical consumables ~15% of total company sales. Life-science exposure guided to <10% of revenue in 2026.

## Balance sheet and the TEU recapitalization

NOVT exited Q1 2026 in a **net-cash position** (cash $388.8M; gross debt $249M; net debt −$139M; gross leverage 1.1x, net leverage −0.6x). The 2025 **tangible equity unit** offering (~$614.4M net proceeds; amortizing-note + stock-purchase-contract structure, mandatory settlement Nov 2028) recapitalized the balance sheet, retiring the revolver and adding ~4.7M minimum shares to the diluted count (~41M diluted shares guided for 2026). The trade-off is explicit: **stronger balance sheet, lower GAAP EPS** — the source of the headline EPS decline despite stable operations. Capital priority is acquisitions first, opportunistic buybacks second (~$18M repurchased in Q1) (NOVT Q1 2026 10-Q + call).

## Open questions

1. **Does the GenAI-datacenter collective hold ~15% and accelerate?** Management expects the ~20% growth rate to increase through 2026; the GPU-drilling and litho-light-engine ramps (the "delayed business" Glastra flagged as more pronounced in 2027) are the watch items. Verify the collective's size and growth at the next refresh.
2. **Humanoid — does the prototype-phase NVIDIA-ecosystem position convert to disclosed revenue?** Trigger to revisit theme-anchoring (or a `humanoid_tier` codification) only if NOVT begins *sizing* humanoid/Physical-AI content, plausibly 2027.
3. **Gross-margin recovery.** The price-cost/tariff timing gap (−60 bps in Q1) is expected to reverse via surcharge repricing + two plant closures in 2H. Did margins return to the prior full-year path?
4. **Operating cash flow durability.** FY2025 operating cash flow fell to $64.1M from $158.5M; Q1 2026 rebounded (+63% YoY, >200% conversion). Confirm the rebound is structural, not a working-capital swing.
5. **Equipment-framework numeric coverage.** NOVT is only the second numeric `equipment_tier` placement (after [[MKSI]]); does the equipment domain warrant fuller positioning-table treatment as more component suppliers enter?

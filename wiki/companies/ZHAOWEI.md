# ZHAOWEI — Shenzhen Zhaowei Machinery & Electronics (深圳市兆威机电股份有限公司 / 兆威机电)

*Shenzhen **main board** A-share **003021**. PRC-GAAP (CAS), RMB. The vault's **sixth Chinese-filing ingest** (after [[SANHUA]] A+H, [[TUOPU]] A, [[SHUANGHUAN]] A, [[ZHONGDA]] A, [[SLING]] A) and the **first name at the dexterous-hand layer** — the highest-value humanoid component the vault had not yet covered. ## Thesis role

Zhaowei is the vault's **first dexterous-hand-layer name** — Goldman scores the **dexterous-hand module 15/18, the second-highest of any humanoid subsystem** (behind only the harmonic reducer), and Zhaowei is the cohort name that genuinely *occupies* that layer rather than being placed on a map. Where [[SLING]] was Goldman-map-named but **absent in its own disclosure** (zero humanoid mentions), Zhaowei is the inverse: it engages the layer **at product level** — 灵巧手 (dexterous hand) is named **38 times**, 人形 (humanoid) **6 times**; it ships dexterous hands with named degrees-of-freedom (A17/B06/B20); it runs a **dedicated subsidiary** for them; and Goldman describes it **by name** as a *"cross-over entrant … integrated dexterous-hand drive modules."* It is anchored to [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]] as a theme name, **outside the AI-datacenter frameworks** (the [[SLING]]/[[ZHONGDA]]/[[SHUANGHUAN]] precedent — assessed, but zero datacenter/cooling/semis exposure).

**The honest-verdict spine.** The question for Zhaowei is *not* "is the connection real" — it is. The tensions are three: (1) the dexterous-hand/humanoid leg is **product-deep but not yet a sized revenue line** (the needle-mover); (2) the dexterous-hand **architecture is unsettled** — Goldman's *lowest* certainty-of-tech-choice score — so there is genuine route-risk; and (3) the financials are strong on the balance sheet and through FY2025 but **reversed sharply in Q1 2026** (net profit −25%). So Zhaowei is the cohort's most genuinely-engaged dexterous-hand name, on a healthy micro-drive core, with a real-but-early and recently-wobbling humanoid frontier.

**The `humanoid_tier` decision (settled at ingest): held theme-anchored — no `humanoid_tier`.** Zhaowei is the **strongest `humanoid_tier` candidate the vault has seen** — but even here there is **no sized or broken-out humanoid revenue**, so by the same test (CLAUDE.md §3.2(c): apply a per-domain tier only for substantive, *sized* exposure) that kept the other six cohort names theme-anchored, Zhaowei stays theme-anchored. Codifying the field now would mean inventing a 1–5 scale with a single speculative entry. The codification trigger is **pre-registered in Open questions**: the first cohort name to disclose a *sized* humanoid/robot revenue segment is the moment to add `humanoid_tier`, with real data to calibrate it.

**Placement.** `layer: outside` + AI `*_tier` absent + theme-anchored humanoid (no `humanoid_tier`) + `foreign_issuer: true`. **Zero AI-datacenter exposure** — 数据中心 / 液冷 / 服务器 = 0 mentions (a micro-transmission/drive maker; no datacenter/cooling/semis business) — so, like the China cohort, **no AI `*_tier`** and **no `equipment_tier`**. **Section 2.11 N/A** (calendar Dec-31). **Section 2.1 N/A** — stable product-line reporting; the dexterous-hand subsidiary is a new *business line*, not a segment restructuring.

## Is it a healthy business?

**Healthy balance sheet and a strong 2025, but two honest qualifiers — a sharp recent-quarter reversal and low capital efficiency.**
- **Fortress balance sheet.** ~81% equity ratio, net cash, no long-term debt, a large IPO-funded wealth-management buffer, clean unqualified audit (立信/BDO). The strongest balance sheet of the China cohort.
- **Strong FY2025, real growth.** Revenue +12.5% / net profit +13% with accelerating quarters and operating cash flow +28.9% — a genuinely good year on a diversified micro-drive base.
- **But Q1 2026 reversed.** Net profit −25.2% on flat-to-down revenue (−2.7%) — the growth stalled in the most recent quarter. This is the watch item: a clean grower would not give back a quarter like this, so the page does not call Zhaowei a clean grower. Watch H1 2026 for whether margin/volume recover.
- **Low capital efficiency.** Weighted ROE just **7.14%** — depressed because the 2021 IPO left the company **over-capitalized** (¥3.48B equity, ¥4.32B assets against only ¥1.72B revenue), with much of the proceeds sitting in low-return wealth-management. The flip side: it is a large, self-funded war chest to bankroll the dexterous-hand / robotics build-out without external capital — the option is cheap to hold.

## Why Goldman puts Zhaowei on the dexterous-hand layer (the centerpiece)

**Why the layer is the prize — and why it is contested.** A dexterous hand is the densest mechatronic object on a humanoid — dozens of degrees of freedom in a palm-sized package. Goldman scores the **dexterous-hand module 15/18, second only to the harmonic reducer**, on content value + technology and manufacturing barriers. But it is **explicitly *not* a Goldman headline pick**, because it has the **lowest certainty-of-tech-choice** in the cohort: the winning hand architecture is unsettled (tendon-driven vs linkage vs direct-drive; how many DOF; where the motors sit), so there is real route-risk. That unsettledness *is* the layer's defining feature.

**Where Zhaowei sits (the analytical core):**

1. **Goldman describes it by name, at two layers.** The subsystems research names Zhaowei directly as a **"cross-over entrant … [supplying] integrated dexterous-hand drive modules combining micro-motors, reduction gearboxes and lead screws; cost-performance positioning leveraging its precision micro-transmission base"** (`humanoid-robot-high-value-subsystems-research.md` §3b, Tier 3), and again at the **coreless-motor** layer (§3g; GS-13 — coreless motors are ~50% of a hand's actuator cost). The primer lists it (003021.SZ, 🔴) in the dexterous-hand section. This is a real Goldman characterization, not a bare map-listing — the contrast with [[SLING]].

2. **Vertically integrated — the moat is the micro-drive platform, not the hand shell.** Zhaowei makes the integrated hand **plus the parts inside it**: micro-motors (incl. a 4mm brushless coreless motor in mass production), micro-gearboxes/reducers, lead screws, and the motor-control electronics — its **"1+1+1"** platform (微型传动系统 + 微型电机系统 + 电控系统). The durable position is that precision micro-transmission/drive base. It is the dexterous-hand analogue of [[SLING]]'s bearing-first integration — and further along.

3. **It hedges the architecture risk with a product range.** Rather than bet one design, Zhaowei ships several: **A17** (17 active DOF, finger-joint built-in drive units, for fine operations; July 2025), **B06** (6 DOF, linkage transmission, heavy-duty; July 2025), and **B20** (20 active DOF, 600g whole-hand, grip ≥30N, fingertip force 5–12N; launched at **CES January 2026**), plus the **ZWHAND** platform. A dedicated subsidiary (深圳市兆威灵巧手技术有限公司, ¥50M registered capital) and a 4–42mm full-stack self-developed motor series back it. Offering multiple architectures is a rational response to Goldman's "unsettled architecture" caveat.

4. **The honest verdict (Section 3.5 counterparty-attribution).** Zhaowei is the cohort's most genuinely-engaged dexterous-hand name on a healthy core — **but**: (a) the humanoid leg is **product-real yet unsized/immaterial** in revenue today (the hands launched H2 2025–CES 2026; mass production is "持续推进" / still ramping); (b) Goldman frames it as a **"cross-over entrant / cost-performance"** player, *not* a premium leader; (c) the dexterous-hand **architecture is unsettled** — Zhaowei's hands could be obsoleted by whatever design wins; and (d) the **coreless-motor** half is commoditizing (GS-13, low moat). Real and credible; early and contested.

## The dexterous-hand / humanoid leg — real products, unsized revenue

- **The products are genuine and shipping/sampling**, with named specs and CES visibility (A17/B06/B20/ZWHAND, above) and a dedicated subsidiary — far beyond the positioning-only or absent engagement of the rest of the China cohort.
- **But it is not a disclosed revenue line.** Zhaowei reports by product *type* (micro-transmission 60.1% / precision parts 34.7% / molds 5.2%), and humanoid/dexterous-hand sales are embedded, not broken out — so the leg is, on the evidence, still small. Management frames humanoid as the **strategic frontier**: it aims to leverage its micro-motor-drive platform to "establish a position in **humanoid-robot core components**" serving 具身智能 (embodied AI). The honest read: a real, product-backed option on the dexterous-hand layer, not yet a sized business.

## Core business — micro-transmission & drive systems

The ~¥1.7B base the humanoid option sits on is a real, diversified, profitable micro-drive business — outside the vault's AI-datacenter scope, but the source of the cash and the platform competency:
- **"1+1+1" micro-drive platform** — micro-transmission (gearboxes/reducers) + micro-motors + motor-control, sold as integrated drive systems and as precision components.
- **End-markets:** smart auto (the largest — central-control screen drives, e-parking-brake actuators; **Bosch, BYD, Changan** named as core customers), consumer & medical (eyewear IPD adjustment, surgical staplers, insulin pumps, lawn-mower-robot drives), advanced industrial (antenna/feeder, valve actuators), and robotics. Described as low-cyclicality; ~88% domestic.

## Open questions

1. **`humanoid_tier` codification trigger (pre-registered).** Zhaowei is the strongest `humanoid_tier` candidate to date but has no *sized* humanoid revenue. The trigger to codify the field is the **first cohort name to disclose a sized/broken-out humanoid or robot revenue segment** (ideally 2–3 at once, to calibrate the 1–5 scale). Until then the whole cohort, Zhaowei included, stays theme-anchored.
2. **Is the Q1 2026 reversal a blip or a slowdown?** Net profit −25% on −2.7% revenue after a strong, accelerating 2025. Watch H1 2026 for whether margin and volume recover, or whether the auto/consumer core is rolling over.
3. **Does the dexterous-hand leg get sized — or a named humanoid OEM?** The products are real but embedded in revenue. A broken-out line or a named robot/humanoid customer is the signal that moves "frontier" to "business" (and trips Open Question 1).
4. **Which hand architecture wins (route-risk)?** Goldman's lowest tech-choice-certainty score is the layer's defining risk. Zhaowei hedges with a product range (A17/B06/B20), but a winning design it does not hold could strand part of that range.
5. **Does the micro-drive-platform integration hold the moat as coreless motors commoditize?** The durable edge is the integrated precision platform, not the commoditizing coreless-motor half — watch whether integration sustains pricing.
6. **Does the IPO-parked balance sheet get deployed?** ~¥3.5B equity, much in wealth-management, against a ¥1.7B-revenue business. Watch whether the over-capitalization funds a real robotics build-out (capex/R&D/M&A) or stays idle (the ROE drag).

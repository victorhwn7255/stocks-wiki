# AMPX — Amprius Technologies, Inc.

## Thesis role

Amprius is the Defense & Drones domain's **silicon-anode battery enabler** — a maker of high-energy-density lithium-ion cells (its commercial "SiCore" platform; the legacy "SiMaxx" line is being phased out) whose value to the thesis is the **energy-density chokepoint** that gates drone endurance: longer loiter, longer range, more payload per kilogram. Placement is **`defense_tier 4`** (supply-chain / payload enabler) per `frameworks-defense-drones.md` Framework D2 — the *highest-conviction* tier, where the chokepoint enablers sit ([[MP]] magnets, [[LSCC]] secure FPGAs, [[TDY]] EO/IR sensors). Amprius carries no AI-datacenter supply-chain role (the AI `*_tier` + `layer` fields are not applied; pure-defense page, per the [[AVAV]]/[[KTOS]]/[[TDY]] precedent). It graduates the **battery node** on the [[defense-drone-supply-chain]] map from a forward candidate to a live vault page.

**Why it matters — and the two honest tensions.** Amprius is the public-market way to own the battery layer the thesis prizes, and it is on the **right side of platform commoditization**: it supplies energy-density cells to *many* drone makers ([[AVAV]], [[TDY]]'s FLIR, AALTO Airbus, BAE, Kraus Hamdani), so it captures value as airframes commoditize toward the ~$2,000 floor (see [[drone-platform-commoditization]]). But two tensions, both evidence-led per Section 2.1, define the page:

- **(a) Financial-quality (Framework D6 — the screen biting as designed).** Amprius is a small-cap *capitalized and timed* for the demand to materialize: it is **loss-making** (FY2025 net loss $(44.0)M; Q1 FY2026 net loss $(5.0)M), **burning cash fast** ($(37.3)M operating cash flow in Q1 alone; cash fell from $90.5M to $62.4M in one quarter), funding itself through **equity dilution** (an at-the-market program + serial warrant exchanges), and it just **abandoned its own gigawatt-scale fab** (a $22.5M Brighton, Colorado impairment + a $20.0M lease-termination payment). It is *not* distressed — no going-concern qualification, gross margin swung positive, and it guides to FY2026 adjusted-EBITDA-positive — but runway and execution are the live questions.
- **(b) The supply-chain irony (the [[TDY]] pattern, for batteries).** Amprius is positioned as the **NDAA-compliant *domestic* battery answer** for the US drone supply chain — yet it is *itself* China-dependent today (battery materials "sourced primarily from China," silicon-anode IP from a China-based affiliate, exposure to China's new lithium-battery export controls), and is racing to qualify NDAA-compliant sources under its Defense Innovation Unit contract. It is positioned to *own* the compliant-battery node while *consuming* the China battery-material chokepoint.

**Honest-verdict framing.** `defense_tier 4` enabler, honest-verdict-**qualified** (like [[LSCC]]): the energy-density advantage is real and the defense pull is accelerating, but the "chokepoint" is partly *policy/capability* (the NDAA battery mandate) rather than geology, the company is loss-making and cash-burning, and it is an upstream-China consumer itself. Inclusion is for the battery-enabler position the thesis has been pointing at, with the financial-quality and supply-chain tensions surfaced plainly.

## Defense & Drones thesis role — how Amprius benefits from the themes

Mapped onto `frameworks-defense-drones.md`. All program facts are primary (Amprius FY2025 10-K + Q1 FY2026 10-Q + Q1 FY2026 call, May 7 2026, as cited).

**Framework D1 (value-chain position):** Amprius sits at the **component / energy layer** — upstream of the platform primes, supplying the cells that determine drone endurance. It is a *consumer* of the upstream lithium-ion materials chain (anode, cathode, separator, electrolyte) and a *supplier* to the platform layer.

**Framework D2 (tier):** `defense_tier 4` — supply-chain / payload enabler (the battery node).

**Framework D3 (demand map / programs of record) — the benefit channels:**
- **DIU standardization contract** — a US DoD **Defense Innovation Unit** development contract, **$14.8M at FY2025 year-end, increased to $18.1M by Q1 FY2026** (amended three times). Scope: deliver three silicon-anode cylindrical cell types + four standard pouch cells to standardize the government's battery supply, and "research and adapt our supply chain" to NDAA-compliant sourcing (FY2025 10-K; Q1 call). CEO Stepien: standardization "reduces cost, simplifies logistics, and ensures systems can use the same safe, reliable, NDAA-compliant power sources."
- **The NDAA battery mandate** — the FY2026 National Defense Authorization Act adds provisions restricting the source of battery components/materials for US-government suppliers; Amprius frames its roadmap to "U.S.-made batteries and U.S. content" as the structural tailwind (Q1 call).
- **Named drone-program pull-through** — management cited multi-million-dollar awards to its customers: **Kraus Hamdani Aerospace** (a US Department of War award for the K1000ULE + a contract up to $270M from US Air Force Central Command); **[[AVAV]]** (a March-2026 ~$117M firm-fixed-price US Army contract for the P550 UAS); **[[TDY]]'s Teledyne FLIR** (a European order for the **Black Hornet 4** nano-drone) — each of which "boosts visibility into future purchase orders for SiCore cells" (Q1 call).
- **Drone-as-First-Responder (DFR) + Gauntlet** — Amprius positions on endurance ("longer loitering time") and says it is "really close with that community"; it tracks the **Gauntlet II** program (the same affordable-mass competition [[AVAV]] and [[KTOS]] compete in — see [[drone-platform-commoditization]]).

**Framework D4 (structural vs cyclical):** Amprius rides the structural drone-endurance demand build, but its forward trajectory is **execution- and capital-cyclical** — it depends on contract-manufacturer ramp (Korea + US), gross-margin recovery, and not out-running its cash. The defense surge is also subject to the thesis's enacted-vs-requested caveat (the CFO flagged a *proposed* ~$58B Defense Autonomous Working Group budget as upside "not captured" in current analysis — i.e., a request, not enacted).

**Framework D5 (chokepoint quality gradient):** the battery is an **energy-density + policy chokepoint**, not a geology/physics one. Amprius's edges are its silicon-anode energy density (the technical moat) and its NDAA-compliant-supply positioning (a *policy* chokepoint created by the NDAA mandate + DIU standardization) — which places it *below* the geology/physics chokepoint owners ([[MP]]'s rare-earth magnets) on the quality gradient, and makes it vulnerable to the same caveat as the NDAA "Blue UAS" supply chain: policy chokepoints can shift.

**Framework D6 (financial-quality screen):** the screen that was built for speculative names actually applies here — see the dedicated read below.

## The NDAA-compliant-battery-vs-China-dependence centerpiece (honest-verdict)

Amprius is positioned to *own* the compliant-battery node the drone supply chain needs, but its own filings show it is **currently dependent on China** for the inputs — the same structural irony [[TDY]] embodies (owning the EO/IR chokepoint while consuming the magnet chokepoint):

> "Our battery materials are sourced primarily from China and we rely on contract manufacturing partners from China and South Korea for our SiCore batteries." (Amprius FY2025 10-K Item 1A.)

Three distinct dependencies fall out:
- **Silicon-anode IP from a China-based affiliate.** SiCore's proprietary silicon-anode material was developed with — and is supplied by — **Berzelius (Nanjing) Co., Ltd.**, "a former affiliated company." Amprius's Exclusive Supply Agreement gives it exclusive rights to purchase Berzelius's anode material only for the **US, Canada, and Mexico**, and "does not include any commercial terms" until the parties agree them (FY2025 10-K Item 1) — a single-source, China-origin dependence for the core technology.
- **China export controls on the batteries themselves.** "Recent regulatory developments in China have introduced new export controls on certain lithium-ion batteries, the materials used in their production, and related manufacturing equipment and technologies. Enforcement of these controls has been suspended until at least November 2026" (FY2025 10-K Item 1A) — a direct, China-controlled supply risk with a known timer.
- **China sales exposure too.** Amprius formed a wholly-owned China subsidiary (**Amprius Energy Co., Ltd.**, January 2025) for China commercial sales, and won a **$21M multi-quarter light-EV order from a China-based customer** (Q1 call) — so China is both a supply risk *and* a revenue channel.

The **DIU contract is the friend-shoring pull against this**: it "requires [Amprius] to secure future sources or qualify individual lithium-ion battery components from NDAA-compliant suppliers," and management says the Department of War is "encouraging" the move to US-made content with a stated roadmap (Q1 call). The honest read: Amprius is *racing to become* the NDAA-compliant domestic battery it is marketed as, from a starting point of genuine China dependence. That race — its cost, timeline, and whether it converts to material US-government revenue — is the thesis-central question.

## Framework D6 financial-quality read

This is the honest-verdict's financial half — the D6 screen applied at the small-cap scale it was built for:
- **Loss-making + cash-burning.** FY2025 net loss $(44.0)M; Q1 operating cash burn $(37.3)M; cash $90.5M → $62.4M in a quarter. At the Q1 burn rate, runway is the live constraint, though management asserts cash is "sufficient to fund our obligations over twelve months" and the FY2026 adjusted-EBITDA-positive guide implies the burn moderates as revenue scales.
- **Dilution is the funding mechanism.** All-equity, no debt. In Q1 Amprius sold ~2.6M shares via an at-the-market program (~$8.4M net) and, in May 2026, exchanged **7.1M public warrants** (a $11.50 strike from the 2022 SPAC) into common stock — framed by the CFO as "saving shareholders ~$70M of dilution" versus a cash exercise, but dilutive nonetheless. The 10-Q flags it may "be unable to raise additional funds… on favorable terms or at all."
- **The execution scar.** Amprius abandoned its planned gigawatt-scale **Brighton, Colorado** fab — a $22.5M FY2025 impairment + a $20.0M lease-termination payment (January 2026) — pivoting from own-capex to an **asset-light contract-manufacturing** model (see Capacity).
- **But not distressed.** No going-concern qualification; gross margin swung positive (FY2025 11.3%, Q1 20.1%, full-year target 25%); revenue +153–202%. The D6 read is *demand-real, profitability-inflecting, still cash-burning* — the [[KTOS]] "capital structure, not operations" tension, but at micro-cap scale where runway genuinely matters.

## Customers & end-markets

Amprius serves five principal end-markets — defense/drones, public-safety drones, satellites/space, light EVs, and robotics/eVTOL — with the defense/UAS and aviation segments the current center of gravity. Named customers (FY2025 10-K Item 1): **AALTO Airbus** (the Zephyr high-altitude pseudo-satellite), **[[AVAV]]** (AeroVironment), **BAE Systems**, **Kraus Hamdani Aerospace**, **Nokia Drone Networks**, **Nordic Wing**, **[[TDY]]'s Teledyne FLIR**, **ESAero**, and the **U.S. Army**. SiCore is now ~97% of product revenue (the SiMaxx line is winding down).

**Customer concentration (Section 3.3 disclosed/inferable/unknown).** *Disclosed:* "during the year ended December 31, 2025, one customer represented $27.1 million of our total revenue" (≈37.1%); FY2024 had three customers >10%. *Inferable:* the unnamed 37% customer is consistent with a large aviation/HAPS battery buyer (AALTO Airbus's Zephyr is a heavy battery consumer, and the 10-K calls AALTO a "tier-one customer"), but the filing does not name it — so no identity is asserted. *Unknown:* the customer's name and the defense-vs-commercial revenue split (single segment; not broken out). Geographic mix Q1 FY2026: ~58% EMEA / 21% North America / 21% Asia-Pacific, with North America rising (the call expects US to accelerate in 2H).

## Capacity & manufacturing — the asset-light pivot

Amprius shifted from building its own fab to an **asset-light contract-manufacturing** model after the Brighton, Colorado exit. As of December 31, 2025 it had access — through manufacturing supply agreements with global contract manufacturers, including the **Amprius Korea Battery Alliance** (a consortium of South Korean companies across the lithium-ion value chain) — to **annual production exceeding 2.0 GWh** of SiCore cells (pouch, cylindrical, prismatic). It is adding US contract manufacturers (one cylindrical CM operational, more coming) and validating cells with **Nanotech Energy** (California). The **Fremont, California** facility is being converted from a kWh-scale SiMaxx line to a **10 MWh-scale SiCore pilot line**, accelerated by the DIU contract (capex <$10M guided for FY2026). The Brighton GWh fab was the abandoned own-capex bet (impairment + lease settlement above).

## Supply chain / chokepoint position

Amprius is best read as a **battery-node enabler that is itself an upstream consumer** — distinct from [[MP]]'s rare-earth magnet chokepoint (a geology/physics chokepoint Amprius does *not* materially depend on; silicon-anode cells are not rare-earth-intensive). Its dependence runs to the **lithium-ion materials chain** (anode, cathode, separator, electrolyte) and to **China + South Korea contract manufacturers** (see the centerpiece). On the [[defense-drone-supply-chain]] map it occupies the previously-vacant battery node, downstream of the materials chain and upstream of the platform primes [[AVAV]] and [[TDY]] (FLIR) that consume its cells. The NDAA-compliant-sourcing build-out (DIU-driven) is the attempt to convert that consumer position into an owned, compliant chokepoint. A dedicated battery / energy-density chokepoint page is a future candidate (deferred — single primary source today; provisional at most per Pathway 1).

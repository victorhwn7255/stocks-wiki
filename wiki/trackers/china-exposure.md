*Vault-wide cross-cutting theme spanning **both** thesis domains (AI-datacenter supply chain + Defense & Drones). The structural framing — that China/Taiwan/Korea concentration is **correlated, not diversifiable** — is Vic-authored scaffolding paraphrased in wiki voice (AI `_thesis.md`: "correlated geopolitical risk across photonics (Taiwan TSM), memory (Korea Samsung/SK Hynix), and power infrastructure (China transformers/rare earths)"; AI `frameworks.md` Framework 11.10; Defense `_thesis-defense-drones.md` "China-pacing" + Framework D5). Per-company placements are cross-referenced to the primary-source evidence on each company page. Concentration percentages (China ~85–90% magnets, ~60% transformers, etc.) are Tier-3 (IEA Nov 2025 via the anchors); verify against primaries before treating as load-bearing. This page **consolidates** a frame already scattered across ~36 pages — it cross-references the China/Taiwan chokepoint pages rather than duplicating them.*

## Thesis — China exposure is correlated, not diversifiable, and direction matters more than magnitude

Two structural facts organize the whole map:

**1. The concentration is correlated.** A single "China exposure" number per name is misleading because the exposures are not independent. The vault's binding nodes — **Taiwan** (TSMC leading-edge logic + advanced packaging), **China** (rare-earth magnets, transformers, battery materials, InP substrates), and **Korea** (HBM memory) — would move *together* in a geopolitical event. A Taiwan crisis does not hit one stock; it hits the *entire* compute/photonics cluster at once (every fabless AI name routes through TSMC), and "diversifying" to an alternate geography usually buys a *parallel* concentration (Korea memory, China materials) rather than real diversification. This is why the vault treats geopolitical risk as a portfolio-level, cross-chokepoint dimension (Framework 11.10), not a per-name footnote.

**2. Direction matters more than magnitude.** China is not one exposure but four, and they point in opposite directions:
- a **supply risk** (the input you can't get) — most AI semis, the materials chokepoints;
- a **demand market being lost** (the customer you can't sell to) — NVDA/AMD under export controls;
- a **demand *driver*** (the adversary whose threat funds the budget) — the Defense names;
- a **revenue/market exposure** (the customer base that could be weaponized) — the high-China-revenue names.

A name can sit on several at once. The most important analytical move on this page is therefore not "how much China?" but "**which direction, and is it a tailwind or a landmine?**"

## The master matrix — every exposed vault name × the eight axes

**Axes:** **A** China revenue · **B** supply/sourcing from China (incl. rare-earth/germanium/gallium/battery-materials/InP) · **C** Taiwan-fab / China-assembly (TSMC/UMC/OSAT) · **D** outbound export-control (US→China selling bans) · **E** inbound export-control (China→US mineral/battery bans) · **F** Chinese competitor threat · **G** China-as-adversary-demand (Defense) · **H** China operations. ## The six exposure clusters

**(C) The Taiwan-fab master node.** The single largest concentration in the vault is not "China" — it is **Taiwan (TSMC)**, the thing China *threatens*. Every fabless AI name routes through it: [[AVGO]] (95%), [[ALAB]] (sole-source), [[AMD]], [[NVDA]], [[MRVL]], [[MU]] (CoWoS), [[MPWR]], [[NVTS]], [[INTC]] (partial), the hyperscaler custom-ASIC silicon (Trainium/TPU, all TSMC-fabbed — see [[hyperscaler-custom-ASIC]]), and the Defense secure-FPGA node ([[LSCC]]) sits on it too. This is the apex of the correlated-risk insight — a Taiwan event is a *cluster* event. Consolidated on [[TSMC-CoWoS]] and the photonics chokepoints ([[cpo-integration]], [[advanced-optical-packaging]], [[optical-dsp-phy-supply]]).

**(B/E) China critical-minerals & materials.** The multi-thesis cluster: rare-earth magnets (China ~85–90%; [[MP]] reshores, [[AVAV]]/[[TDY]] consume — see [[rare-earth-magnet-chokepoint]]), germanium/gallium ([[TDY]]), battery materials ([[AMPX]], [[ENS]]), InP substrates ([[AXTI]] 100% China-manufactured — see [[InP-supply]]), and **transformers** (China ~60% of global capacity; the AI `_thesis`'s Rank-2 *binding 2026 constraint* — see [[transformer-supply]]). This is where the AI and Defense theses physically share inputs.

**(A) China revenue / market-access.** The "selling *into* China" exposure — two-edged if China weaponizes supply, and a tariff/de-rating risk: [[LSCC]] **64%**, [[AAOI]] ~50–60%, [[CRDO]] 28% HK (down from 56%), [[ON]] 28%, [[POWI]] China-dominant, [[ARM]] 16%, [[COHR]]/[[COHU]]/[[GLW]]. **Caveat (load-bearing):** several "China revenue" figures are **ship-to / billing-address routing, not end-market demand** ([[ALAB]] ~30%, [[LITE]]/[[COHR]] China growth = contract-manufacturer routing). The page distinguishes genuine China *end-demand* from supply-chain *routing artifacts* — they carry very different risk.

**(D) Outbound export controls (US→China).** A real, *quantified* P&L driver, not a tail risk: [[NVDA]] (China compute zeroed; multi-$B H20 write-downs), [[AMD]] (MI308 $440M net charge), [[ONTO]] (China revenue roughly halved), [[VECO]] (BIS license headwind), [[AXTI]] (InP added to China's *outbound* list — the inverse). The US-China tech-decoupling shows up directly in earnings.

**(F) Chinese competitors — the "China builds its own" erosion.** SMIC, **CXMT/YMTC** ([[MU]], [[SNDK]], [[ONTO]] 14% YMTC exposure), Huawei ([[CSCO]]/[[COHR]]/[[LITE]]/[[VRT]] competitor), **JL MAG** (vs [[MP]]), Shenzhen photomask makers ([[PLAB]]), Chinese battery makers ([[AMPX]]). The risk that today's chokepoint is tomorrow's commodity once China domesticates it.

**(H) China operations footprint.** [[AXTI]] (100% mfg + indium refining), [[GLW]]/[[PLAB]]/[[AMPX]]/[[MU]]/[[FN]]/[[SOI]] footprints, [[MP]] (now zero, post-cessation) — the physical assets exposed to a China action.

## Cross-cutting payoff

**The demand-vs-supply direction matrix.** The master tension, by group:
- **AI semis / materials** → China is a **supply risk** (Taiwan fab, rare-earth, InP) and, for some, a **lost demand market** (export controls). Net headwind from escalation.
- **The 6 Defense names** → China is **both** the **demand driver** (China-pacing budgets) **and** the **supply risk** (rare-earth/germanium/battery materials) — the benefit and the hurt run through the same country.
- **The reshoring plays** → China is a **tailwind**: [[MP]] (export restrictions *raise* its value), [[AMPX]] (NDAA-compliant battery), the US-fab buildout ([[overseas-fab-expansion]]). These are the inverse-exposure positions.

**The "squeezed-middle" / chokepoint-consumer paradox — a vault-wide pattern, not Defense-only.** The sharpest names *own* one chokepoint while *consuming* a China/Taiwan chokepoint they don't control: [[TDY]] (owns EO/IR sensors, consumes magnets/germanium), [[LSCC]] (owns secure-FPGA design, consumes TSMC fab + 64% China revenue), [[AMPX]] (owns the NDAA-battery position, consumes China materials), [[AXTI]] (owns an InP-substrate position yet is **100% China-manufactured and export-controlled** — the AI-side twin of the defense pattern), and even [[MP]] (owns the magnet chokepoint, but China controls ~100% of the production *equipment*). "Positioned as the Western alternative while structurally China/Taiwan-dependent" is the recurring honest-verdict tension across both domains.

**The humanoid-robot value chain is the most China-concentrated supply chain in the vault** — and reinforces the correlated-concentration thesis from a third domain. Morgan Stanley puts China at ~63% of the humanoid value chain; China makes ~90% of the NdFeB magnets every actuator needs, plus the dominant share of reducers, screws, motors, and batteries — so building a humanoid without Chinese suppliers reportedly costs ~3× more (Tier-3). [[MP]] (now a *triple-thesis* node — defense + AI + humanoid) is the cleanest US-listed reshoring answer on the magnet leg. See [[humanoid-robot-value-chain]].

**The PCB / interconnect-substrate cohort is a clean bifurcated-geography case (NEW; see [[pcb-interconnect-substrate-chokepoint]]).** It splits the two halves of the correlated-concentration thesis cleanly: the **board-fabrication layer is China-heavy** (China ≈ 50-56% of global PCB value; the high-layer AI-PCB makers WUS Kunshan / Shennan / Victory Giant are Chinese — A-share-listed, reachable via the pending WUS Kunshan H-share + Victory Giant's HK line), while the **durable material/substrate layer is Japan-concentrated** (Nittobo ~90% T-glass, Ajinomoto >95% ABF film, Resonac HVLP copper foil, **[[IBIDEN]]**-led FCBGA substrate). So a China export-control action on the *materials* would gate US/Taiwan board+package assembly, and a tariff/export action on the *boards* would hit the Chinese fabricators — opposite directions, same chokepoint. Unlike the magnet leg, there is **no US-listed pure-play** for the durable (Japanese) material nodes — though **[[IBIDEN]] (TSE/NSE 4062), the FCBGA-substrate leader, is now a vault company page (S168) and directly reachable on a Japan-access broker** (the first durable Japanese node held directly, just not US-listed); the other vault touchpoints are enablers only ([[MKSI]] bonding chemistry, [[NOVT]] board drilling, [[ONTO]] substrate metrology). Tier-3 concentration figures — verify at primary.

**The China-policy calendar (tradeable timers).** Several known dates gate these exposures: China's lithium-battery export controls **suspended to ≥Nov 2026** ([[AMPX]]); [[LSCC]]'s additional Section 301 tariff effective **Jun 23 2027**; the Blue UAS NDAA exemption expiry **Jan 1 2027** (Defense); and the rotating US GPU export rules ([[NVDA]]/[[AMD]]). The risk is not static — it has a schedule worth watching.

## The no/minimal-exposure set (the structural hedge)

Honest-verdict: **not everything is China-exposed**, and naming what *isn't* is itself a finding — these are the natural hedge against the correlated cluster above. With no material China/Taiwan revenue, sourcing, fab, or export-control exposure at primary sources:
- **Software / cloud / demand-side:** [[MSFT]], [[NOW]], [[PLTR]], [[GOOGL]], [[META]], [[ORCL]], [[NBIS]], [[CRWV]] (and the broader software-moat names CRM / SNOW / WDAY / DDOG / PANW etc. per [[software-AI-moat-durability]]).
- **Domestic energy / nuclear:** [[BE]], [[CCJ]], [[CEG]], [[FCEL]], [[LEU]], [[FLNC]].
- **Diversified / US-centric industrials & optics:** [[VRT]], [[VIAV]].
- **Generic-tariff-only (not China-specific):** [[AAON]], [[ANET]], [[BWXT]], [[CAT]], [[ETN]], [[GEV]] disclose tariff risk but no China-specific revenue/sourcing/fab dependence; [[CSCO]] names Huawei as a competitor only.
- **AI-data services (offshore-but-not-China):** [[INOD]] — global delivery is principally India / Sri Lanka / Philippines (12,200 professionals across 70+ countries); the Hong Kong subsidiary is treasury, not operations; no China revenue, sourcing, fab, or export-control exposure (the AI-data-supplier name, see [[AI-data-supply-chain]]). A reminder that "offshore" ≠ "China."

The cleanest *intra-vault* hedge against a Taiwan event sits in the software/cloud demand layer (which *pays* for the compute but doesn't *manufacture* it) and the domestic-energy names.

## Open questions

1. **The Taiwan-crisis correlated-drawdown scenario.** If the correlation thesis is right, a Taiwan event is a cluster event — is there *any* genuine intra-AI-vault diversification, or does the whole compute/photonics layer move as one? (The software/cloud + domestic-energy names are the only apparent hedge.)
2. **Do the reshoring plays' inverse exposure actually hold?** [[MP]] and [[AMPX]] are framed as escalation-tailwind names — but [[MP]] still imports China *equipment* and [[AMPX]] still sources China *materials*. How clean is the inverse exposure really?
3. **The policy-calendar resolutions** — China lithium-battery controls (Nov 2026), LSCC Section 301 (Jun 2027), Blue UAS expiry (Jan 2027), GPU rules. Each is a discrete, datable catalyst.
4. **The Korea-memory leg is under-covered.** The correlated cluster includes Korea (Samsung/SK Hynix HBM — see [[HBM-oligopoly]]); the vault has lighter primary coverage there than on the China/Taiwan legs.
5. **Billing-address vs end-demand.** How much of the disclosed "China revenue" across the photonics/connectivity names is genuine China end-demand vs contract-manufacturer ship-to routing? The distinction changes the risk materially.

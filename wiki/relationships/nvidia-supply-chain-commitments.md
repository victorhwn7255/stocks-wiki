## Modality distinction subsections

### Equity investment modality (LITE + COHR + MRVL; ~$6B aggregate confirmed at primary)

**Mechanism:** Direct cash/preferred equity injection from NVIDIA into vault canonical participant. Capital-offset-for-supply-assurance economic substance — investee uses capital to fund capacity expansion that customer requires; investor secures supply access ahead of capacity-binding-constraint dynamics.

**Confirmed at primary:**
- **LITE Q3 FY2026 (S50 refresh):** $2.02B sequential cash increase (cash + ST investments $1.155B → $3.17B) "primarily driven by NVIDIA's direct investment in Lumentum"; H1 FY2026 free cash flow ~$24.8M against ~$320M annualized capex pre-injection — investment effectively pre-funds ~6 years of standalone capex at H1 run-rate
- **COHR Q3 FY2026 (S50 refresh):** $2B equity stake; cash $1.5B → $3.0B sequential (+$1.5B net of $290M Q3 CapEx + $162M debt repayment); "an expansion of a more than 20-year relationship" (Anderson)
- **MRVL Q1 FY2027 (S102 refresh; NEW S110):** **$2.0B NVIDIA convertible-preferred** issued March 31, 2026 (Tier 1 10-Q; ~$91.84 conversion price, max ~21.8M shares); paired with an expanded NVLink Fusion + optics/SiPh + AI-RAN partnership. **Structurally novel third equity participant** — unlike LITE/COHR (pure suppliers), MRVL is simultaneously partner + investee + merchant-silicon **competitor** ([[NVDA-platform-integration]] **Mode 4**, FIRST INSTANCE).

**Honest-verdict scope:** Equity-only confirmation at LITE; **dual-leg equity + supply commitment at COHR** (commercial commitment beyond capital); **equity + partnership + competitor overlap at MRVL** (Mode 4). Three differentiated structural commitments despite a common ~$2B equity headline at each. Aggregate ~$6B confirmed at primary.

### Customer-side / circularity modality (CRWV + NBIS — the two neoclouds, S121-S122)

**Mechanism:** Direct equity injection from NVIDIA into a **downstream customer** (not a supplier) — NVIDIA funding a company whose spend flows back to NVIDIA in GPU purchases. Structurally the *inverse* of the supplier-equity modality above: the LITE/COHR/MRVL stakes secure supply access *upstream*; the neocloud stakes support demand *downstream*. This is the "circularity" the [[hyperscaler-capex]] tracker flags (dynamic #9) — a reflexive loop in which the supplier part-funds its own demand.

**Confirmed at primary (two instances — both vault neoclouds):**
- **CRWV FY2025 10-K (S121; Subsequent Events):** NVIDIA invested **$2.0B in CoreWeave Class A common stock in January 2026** (at $87.20/share) — primary-confirmed from CoreWeave's own filing (reciprocal-confirmation). Paired with NVIDIA's commitment for CoreWeave to build large-scale AI capacity.
- **NBIS FY2025 20-F (S122):** NVIDIA provided **~$2.0B in proceeds plus a pre-funded Class A purchase warrant (~21M shares)** to Nebius — primary-confirmed from Nebius's own filing (reciprocal-confirmation). Same downstream direction; the warrant structure (pre-funded, vs CoreWeave's direct common purchase) is the modality variant.

**Honest-verdict scope:** Distinct from the upstream supplier stakes — NVIDIA equity flowing to customers / capacity-providers. **Now two primary-confirmed instances** (both vault neoclouds, CRWV + NBIS), so the downstream/circular direction is a substantiated pattern, not a one-off. Tier-3 (`ai-capex-map-2025-2027.md`) places it in a still-broader downstream pattern (NVIDIA also into OpenAI up to $100B, xAI), those beyond vault-primary scope.

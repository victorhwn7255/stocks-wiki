## 5-modality competing-technology framework

Five competing technology modalities addressing the AI rack thermal density binding constraint. Each modality has distinct deployment profile (rack density addressable + power density + retrofit feasibility + cost structure + reliability characteristics + supplier landscape). **Modality framework expansion from BTM 4-modality at generation scope to liquid-cooling 5-modality at thermal management scope** — methodology codification candidate at Tranche 2C.

### Modality 1: Direct-to-chip cooling (cold plate)

**Technology base:** Single-phase liquid-cooled cold plates mounted directly on chip die surface; coolant loop with manifold + CDU integration; addresses highest heat flux per chip envelope.

**Vault canonical participants:**
- **[[FLEX]] (JetCool):** Direct-to-chip microconvective cooling >3,000W per device; patented technology; **JetCool-Broadcom March 11, 2026 bilateral at 4 W/mm² heat flux per device** for next-gen AI XPU thermal reference architecture (reciprocal-confirmation A1 mode candidate pending AVGO refresh)
- **[[ETN]] (Boyd Thermal):** Cold plates + CDUs + white-space integration; closed March 2026 ahead of schedule; $1.7B+ FY2026 track; "core design partner to leading hyperscalers and silicon providers" per Ruiz; cross-vault thermal management terrain at hyperscaler scope
- **[[NVT]] (Hoffman + Schroff):** Cold plate cooling product portfolio implied at 10-K Item 1 ("cooling solutions both liquid and air") but specific cold plate product enumeration NOT explicitly named at primary; verification candidate at future refresh
- **[[VRT] (Liebert):** Systems-tier incumbent cooling portfolio includes cold plates within broader thermal/UPS integrated solutions scope
- **[[ANET]] (XPO Extended Pluggable Optics; networking-equipment-integration scope):** **Integrated cold plate "capable of cooling up to 400 W power per module"** at XPO finished-module form factor per ANET Q1 2026 call (Duda; Bechtolsheim OFC keynote March 2026); 12.8 TB per pluggable module + 204.8 TB per OCP rack unit "unprecedented rack density"; Ullal Q1 2026 call: *"10-year run, especially at 1.6T and 3T where you need **liquid cooling** and you need that kind of capacity"*; Duda: *"What XPO unlocks is a standard interoperable multi-vendor way to get to **4x the network density and liquid cooling, which is absolutely critical for these AI use cases**"*. **Networking-equipment-integration scope at Modality 1** — structurally distinct from systems-tier supplier scope (VRT/MOD/NVT/etc); ANET is networking equipment vendor with integrated form-factor cold plate cooling at module level.

**Non-vault competitive landscape:** CoolIT Systems (private; high-end DLC); Asetek (Norway listed; not vault scope); private DLC pure-plays. **Most material narrower-scope chokepoint dynamics surface here** at 4 W/mm² heat flux scope; module-level form-factor scope at ANET XPO 400W per module substantively material at networking-equipment-integration adjacency.

**Vault canonical participants:**

### Modality 4: CRAC/CRAH air-cooled (legacy)

**Technology base:** Computer Room Air Conditioning (CRAC) + Computer Room Air Handler (CRAH) units; precision air conditioning; legacy data center cooling architecture; air cooling at lower rack densities. **Binding constraint scope:** addresses lower-density compute deployment (typically <30kW per rack); insufficient at AI rack density 100-240kW envelope per binding constraint scope.

**Vault canonical participants:**
- **[[MOD]] (10-K Item 1):** *"precision air conditioning units for data center applications; computer room air conditioning ("CRAC") and computer room air handler ("CRAH") units; hybrid fan coils; fan walls"* — Modality 4 explicitly enumerated at primary

**Honest framing:** Modality 4 represents the legacy installed base that AI rack density inflection is transitioning AWAY FROM toward Modalities 1-3 + 5. Documented as competing-technology baseline for honest-verdict assessment scope.

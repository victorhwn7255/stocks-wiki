## Competing technology base modality framework

**4 distinct technology bases addressing the same hyperscaler on-site generation problem.** Modality framework analogous to [[nvidia-supply-chain-commitments]] S53 6-modality scope but at **supply-side competing-technology** rather than demand-side architect-customer commitment scope. Each technology base has distinct deployment profile (lead time + capacity scale + fuel input + emissions profile + cost structure + reliability characteristics + aftermarket service requirements).

### Modality 1 — Solid oxide fuel cell BTM ([[BE]])

**Technology:** Natural-gas-fired solid oxide fuel cell; ~70% electrical efficiency; modular MW-scale Energy Server units; islanded microgrid architecture capable.

**Distinguishing characteristics:** Fastest deployment lead time (~90 days from manufacturing release per Tier 3 source); clean generation profile (~50% lower CO2/MWh vs reciprocating engines per industry baseline); fuel-flexible (natural gas + biogas + hydrogen capable); modular scaling (MW increments); islanded microgrid capable. Commercial scale: BE Q1 2026 $751M revenue; FY2026 raised guidance midpoint ~$3.6B. 1 GW current production capacity → 2 GW target by end-2026.

**Trade-off profile:** Higher capex per MW vs reciprocating engine + gas turbine alternatives; lower aftermarket revenue stream (vs reciprocating engine + gas turbine which carry substantial aftermarket service revenue); fuel cell technology immaturity at multi-GW data center scale (largest commercial deployments ~100 MW range).

### Modality 2 — Molten carbonate fuel cell BTM ([[FCEL]])

**Technology:** Natural-gas-fired molten carbonate fuel cell; higher temperature operating regime than SOFC; 12.5 MW standardized power block (announced March 2026); carbon capture + hydrogen production co-product capability.

**Distinguishing characteristics:** Carbon capture co-product (Section 45Q tax credit eligible per S47 canonical); hydrogen production co-product capability; 12.5 MW standardized power block optimized for data center grid-constrained markets. Substantial commercial scale gap vs [[BE]]: FCEL Q1 FY2026 ~$30.5M revenue ≈ 1:25 ratio.

**Trade-off profile:** Smaller commercial scale + earlier-stage commercialization trajectory; primer S39 narrative-outrunning-proof framing application boundaries — Aschenbrenner thesis substantiation pending at S47 canonical baseline; technology distinctiveness real but commercial scale gap structurally significant.

### Modality 3 — Utility-scale gas turbine generation ([[GEV]]; partial BTM participation)

**Technology:** Heavy-duty Big-3 frame-tier gas turbines (GEV LM series + 7HA series + 9HA series); utility-scale generation MW capacity (100-700+ MW per unit); + Electrification segment "string of pearls" datacenter product roadmap (EMS + Stability Block + SST).

**Distinguishing characteristics:** Largest capacity scale per unit; utility-grade reliability + grid-integrated deployment + 4-7 year lead times per Tier 3 source. **Partial BTM participation honestly framed per S37 GEV canonical baseline** — 20% of 100 GW gas turbine backlog "supporting data centers" per CEO Strazik but on-site vs grid-feed split ambiguous; primary-source evidence leans toward grid-feed dominance. Distinct sub-domain from BE/FCEL/CAT pure-BTM participants.

**Trade-off profile:** Capacity scale per unit advantageous for largest data center deployments (>200 MW campus); long lead times (4-7 years) disadvantageous vs alternative technology bases; grid-feed dominant commercial structure means GEV serves a partially-overlapping market vs pure-BTM participants. Future tier-revisit triggers (Tier 2 promotion) pre-registered per S37 canonical baseline.

### Modality 4 — Reciprocating engine + Solar Turbines aero-derivative gas turbine BTM ([[CAT]])

**Technology:** Reciprocating engine (large gas + diesel) + Solar Turbines aero-derivative gas turbine subsidiary (small/aero-derivative tier per Tier 3 source; distinct from Big-3 frame-tier).

**Distinguishing characteristics:** **Multi-product BTM advantage** (turbines + recips) — distinct from single-product specialists ([[BE]] SOFC / [[FCEL]] MCFC / [[GEV]] utility-scale gas turbine). Intermediate lead time (reciprocating engine 12-24 months per Tier 3) — between BE ~90 days and GEV utility-scale 4-7 years. Diversified-broad industrial conglomerate customer base; ProPower 2.1 GW agreement (April 2026; 6th ≥1 GW prime power agreement); 5-year delivery commitment; backlog "well into 2028"; capacity expansion 2x → ~3x 2024 levels primarily 2027-2029.

**Trade-off profile:** Multi-product BTM offering enables customer-side configuration flexibility (prime vs backup mix; gas vs liquid fuel; rapid load vs continuous load); diversified industrial conglomerate scope (Power Generation ~14-16% consolidated) means AI datacenter exposure is structurally rerating but bounded — distinct from single-product specialists where AI datacenter thesis fit dominates consolidated revenue (BE; FCEL; pure-play). Tier 4 with structural elevation framing per S56 canonical baseline captures "underappreciated AI-power play" Tier 3 variant view + multi-product BTM advantage without overstating to Tier 3 promotion (utility-scale generation integration commercial structure absent at CAT primary).

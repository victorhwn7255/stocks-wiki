## Chokepoint identification

**HALEU enrichment is the binding 5/5 Acute and Binding constraint** within the AI datacenter power supply chain at the upstream nuclear fuel chain. Sole NRC-licensed Western HALEU producer is Centrus Energy ([[LEU]]) operating the AC100M centrifuge cascade at Piketon, Ohio. Current capacity ~900 kg/yr (Phase II completed June 2025; **>1.6 metric tons HALEU UF₆ cumulative delivered to DOE through Q1 2026** per [[LEU]] Q1 FY2026 refresh — +60% incremental in 1 quarter from S42 baseline >1 MTU) vs DOE projected demand 40 MTU/yr by 2030 / 50 MTU/yr by 2035 (per HALEU report PART 1.4). Demand-supply gap differentiates by capacity scenario:

- **Current capacity gap (~900 kg/yr baseline):** approximately **~44× by 2030 / ~56× by 2035** — the gap as observed today before Phase III scale-up materializes
- **Full-scale buildout gap (12 MTU/yr Centrus full-scale target):** approximately **~3.3× by 2030 / ~4.2× by 2035** — Phase III scale-up closes most but not all of the demand-supply gap; Western competition status (URENCO / Orano / others; see Western competition section below) is required to close the residual gap

**Sequential fuel chain dependency** (mining → conversion → enrichment → deconversion → fabrication) means HALEU enrichment 5/5 binding constraint propagates downstream-restricted demand back through upstream layers, making the entire HALEU fuel chain investment-relevant beyond the enrichment chokepoint itself.

**Cross-cutting geopolitical risk severity 4/5** (Russia uranium import ban; Western-only supply chain dynamics) is methodologically distinct dimension overlaying sequential fuel chain severity per S41 framework expansion candidates D1 + D2.

# Rare-earth NdFeB magnet chokepoint

*Canonical chokepoint page (per CLAUDE.md Section 3.15 Pathway 2). Owner: [[MP]] (a full canonical company page) + the Tier-3 anchor report `raw/research/US-defense-and-drones-report.md`; disclosed defense consumers: [[AVAV]] + [[TDY]] (both name rare-earth / permanent-magnet dependence in their 10-Ks). A **multi-thesis chokepoint** — the same China concentration constrains defense/drone motors, AI-datacenter cooling, EV, wind, and robotics. Concentration figures are Tier-3 (IEA Nov 2025 via the report); verify against primaries (IEA / CRS / USGS) before treating as load-bearing. Promoted from provisional (Session 128) to canonical (Session 131) when TDY became the second disclosed defense consumer — owner + 2 disclosed consumers + Tier-3 anchor meets the Pathway 2 threshold.*

## The chokepoint

High-performance **sintered neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) permanent magnets** — and, critically, the **heavy rare earths (dysprosium / terbium)** that NdFeB magnets need to hold their magnetism at the high temperatures inside drone, missile, and motor applications. These magnets are the irreplaceable actuation input for unmanned-system motors, actuators, seekers, and guidance — there is no near-term non-magnet substitute at the required performance.

On the Defense thesis's chokepoint quality gradient (`frameworks-defense-drones.md` Framework D5), this is the **#1 geology/physics chokepoint** — durable on *geology + refining/separation capacity*, which take years to replicate regardless of statute, ranking it above policy-created chokepoints (e.g. the NDAA "Blue UAS" supply chain, whose DoD exemption expires January 1, 2027).

## Concentration (Tier-3, IEA Nov 2025 via the anchor report)

China controls the chain end to end:
- **~85–90%** of finished magnets
- **~91%** of separation / refining
- **~69%** of mining
- **~100%** of production equipment

One Chinese firm (**JL MAG**) is reported to roughly equal *all non-Chinese output combined*. The binding constraint is not ore (mining is the least concentrated step at ~69%) but **separation/refining and finished-magnet manufacturing** — the midstream and downstream steps China dominates most.

## The demand / supply gap

The Pentagon's stated need is **~3,000–4,000 tons of specialized magnets annually, rising toward ~10,000 tons by 2030** (Tier-3, via the report), against minimal current US output — meaningful US *finished-magnet* capacity is a 2027–2028+ story. The gap is the structural opportunity the US-listed owner is building into.

## The owner — [[MP]]

[[MP]] (MP Materials) is the only scaled US rare-earth producer with vertical-integration scope spanning mining + refining (Mountain Pass, California) and magnet manufacturing (Independence Facility, Texas; first commercial NdFeB December 2025; the "10X" facility targeted for 2028). Its **U.S. Department of War partnership (July 9, 2025)** — $400M of convertible preferred + a 10-year NdPr price floor + a 10-year magnet offtake + the Samarium Project Loan — is the clearest case in either vault domain of the government converting a chokepoint into a backstopped commercial position (DPA Title III authority). MP is also commissioning scaled **heavy rare-earth (Dy/Tb) separation** at Mountain Pass — the most China-controlled subset, and the one drone motors most depend on. See [[MP]] for the full owner-side treatment, financials, and the appropriations-contingency risk on the DoW funding.

## The consumer side (honest scoping)

- **[[AVAV]] — disclosed defense consumer (1st).** AeroVironment's FY2025 10-K states plainly that its "motors, batteries, and other advanced components rely on rare earth metals, of which a significant majority are sourced from China," and that **China placed AVAV on its export-control list in March 2025.** The first documented intra-defense supply-chain link: MP owns the chokepoint; AVAV consumes it.
- **[[TDY]] — disclosed defense consumer (2nd; the canonical-promotion trigger).** Teledyne's FY2025 10-K (repeated in its Q1 FY2026 10-Q) discloses that "China has also restricted the export of certain **rare earth minerals and permanent magnets** that are used in our products, which has in the past delayed and could in the future limit our ability to sell products that require these components," and that **permanent magnets** (alongside germanium + gallium) "have impacted the production and pricing of some of our digital imaging and aerospace and defense products." Teledyne — itself the EO/IR sensor chokepoint owner — is *squeezed from above* by the same China concentration MP is reshoring. Its disclosure of an *additional* China-controlled critical mineral, **germanium** (the load-bearing IR-window material for thermal optics), flags a distinct adjacent chokepoint dimension (not yet its own page).
- **[[KTOS]] does not disclose it.** Neither of Kratos's filings discloses rare-earth/magnet dependence (its "China" references are to China as a peer adversary driving demand, not a supply vulnerability) — and KTOS is unusually vertically integrated (it builds its own engines and solid rocket motors). Per CLAUDE.md Section 3.3 (disclosed/inferable/unknown), KTOS's drones and missiles structurally use the same magnets, but the dependence is **inferable-only, not disclosed** — so it is not asserted here.
- **Criticality, not tonnage.** Drones are **<3% of global NdFeB demand** (Tier-3). The chokepoint's weight on the drone thesis is *criticality* — unmanned-system motors skew toward the most China-controlled heavy rare earths (Dy/Tb) for high-temperature performance — not volume. No drone-demand overclaim: MP's offtake + price-floor economics are not drone-demand-driven.

## Multi-thesis scope (the cleanest cross-domain chokepoint)

The same China concentration constrains multiple demand drivers off one chokepoint:
- **Defense / drones** — motors, actuators, seekers (this page).
- **AI-datacenter cooling** — NdFeB content in cooling-fan and coolant-pump motors (see [[liquid-cooling]]).
- **Wind** — direct-drive offshore turbines use substantial NdFeB (see [[GEV]]).
- **EV / robotics** — traction-motor and humanoid-actuator magnets.

[[MP]] is the shared node across all of them — which is why rare-earth magnets are the cleanest *multi-thesis* chokepoint in the vault.

## Open questions

1. **Does China relax export controls?** The mirror falsifier — normalization weakens the reshoring case and the chokepoint's pricing power.
2. **Does non-China finished-magnet capacity scale faster than expected** ([[MP]], USA Rare Earth, allied producers), easing the midstream/downstream bottleneck ahead of the 2027–2028 timeline?
3. **Does substitution advance?** Rare-earth-free / ferrite motors or reduced-Dy magnet chemistries reaching the performance bar for unmanned-system motors would erode the chokepoint.
4. **Does MP's Independence / 10X capacity convert to defense-allocated offtake** (vs merchant/EV), tying the owner directly to the drone supply chain?
5. **Do additional defense consumers disclose dependence** (a 3rd/4th name), and do the AI-side cooling-motor ([[liquid-cooling]]) + wind ([[GEV]]) consumers get formally cross-validated as magnet consumers?
6. **Germanium / IR-optics as an adjacent chokepoint** — does [[TDY]]'s germanium dependence (and broader China critical-mineral control) warrant its own page as more EO/IR consumers surface?

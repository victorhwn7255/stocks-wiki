## Substrate-tier (GOES steel)

### Cleveland-Cliffs sole US producer

Grain-oriented electrical steel (GOES) is the substrate input that gates transformer manufacturing throughput. **Cleveland-Cliffs is the sole US producer of GOES steel** per `_thesis.md` Rank 1 Materials framing. Combined with 80% transformer import dependency (Mexico/China/Thailand per `_thesis.md` Rank 2 Energy/Power), substrate-tier US capacity is the upstream bottleneck on transformer manufacturing-tier expansion.

**Public policy context.** Cleveland-Cliffs received a $500M Biden-era grant for electrical steel plant upgrade, subsequently partially cancelled by Trump administration per `_thesis.md`. Substrate-tier capacity expansion trajectory is policy-sensitive at upstream-of-manufacturing scope.

**Commodity pricing as upstream check (per `raw/research/AI-power-energy-bottleneck-research-report.md` S39).** *"Pricing on grain-oriented electrical steel (Cleveland-Cliffs is the only US producer) and copper cathode are the upstream commodity checks for transformer and conductor cost inflation."* Channel-check methodology: GOES steel pricing tracked alongside copper cathode (LME, COMEX) + transformer-grade silicon steel imports (USITC trade data).

### Geopolitical concentration risk

**Mexico/China/Thailand 80% transformer import dependency** establishes downstream-of-substrate geographic concentration risk at manufacturing-tier scope. China controls ~60% global transformer production capacity (TBEA + China XD Group per `_thesis.md` Rank 2 Energy/Power); US export restrictions on Chinese transformers escalating. Per Tier 3 source: USMCA-compliant Mexico/Canada transformer and core imports help offset domestic capacity gap; aggressive tariff escalation would hit lead times further.

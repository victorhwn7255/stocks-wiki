Thesis role

NVDA occupies Layer 1 (platform definer) in the value-capture framework and Photonics Tier 1 (structural chokepoint). Its architecture (CUDA, NVLink, Spectrum-X) and product cadence (Blackwell → Vera Rubin) set the pace for the entire ecosystem.

NVDA is "platform-integrated," not vertically integrated. It owns the platform layer but depends on {{ lkTSM }} at the physical manufacturing layer for leading-edge logic and advanced packaging ({{ lkCoWoS }}). This dependency is the single most important cross-company dynamic in the thesis: the Layer 1 platform definer cannot route around the Layer 2 infrastructure provider.

Non-GAAP gross margin of 75.2% is the highest among thesis holdings — consistent with Layer 1 platform-definer economics. That both the platform definer and {{ lkTSM }} (66.2%) capture exceptional margins is evidence of multi-layer value capture in a supply-constrained market.

Chokepoint identification

HALEU enrichment is the binding 5/5 constraint at the upstream nuclear fuel chain. The sole NRC-licensed Western producer is {{ lkLEU }}, operating the AC100M centrifuge cascade at Piketon, Ohio — current capacity ~900 kg/yr against DOE projected demand of 40 MTU/yr by 2030. The current-capacity gap is approximately ~44× by 2030.

Sequential dependency. Mining → conversion → enrichment → deconversion → fabrication. The 5/5 binding enrichment constraint propagates restricted demand back through every upstream layer — making the whole chain investment-relevant, not just the chokepoint itself.

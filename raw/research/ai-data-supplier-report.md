# AI Data Supply Chain — Round 2 Primary-Source Verification Addendum

*Structural value-capture framing only (what-would-have-to-be-true), not buy/sell calls. Builds on the Round 1 moat gradient as given baseline. Current as of June 5, 2026. Conviction tags: H/M/L.*

## TL;DR
- The Round 1 moat gradient survives primary-source scrutiny, but the headline correction is brutal: the publicly-investable "pure-play AI-data supplier" category is essentially **one name of size (Innodata, INOD)** plus one scarce-corpus hybrid where licensing is still only ~5% of revenue (Reddit, RDDT). Everyone else investors call an "AI-data play" is in fact an **INCUMBENT that restricts training use and builds its own products** (RELX, TRI, SPGI, MCO, FDS, MORN) or a **content owner monetizing via episodic deals and litigation** (NWSA, WLY, UMG, WMG, GETY/SSTK). (H)
- The 2025 copyright rulings (Bartz v. Anthropic, Kadrey v. Meta) tilted decisively **pro-developer** on training-as-fair-use, collapsing Round 1's symmetric "owners get paid regardless." Owners now get paid **only where they have BOTH unique data AND enforcement leverage** (registered copyright, ToS/contract, technical blocking). The one clean, large transfer to rightsholders is the **$1.5B Bartz v. Anthropic settlement** for *pirated acquisition*; NYT v. OpenAI and Getty's N.D. Cal. refiling are the live swing factors. (H)
- The toll-collection layer (Cloudflare pay-per-crawl, RSL clearinghouse, TollBit/ProRata) is real and standardizing fast but **not yet monetized at a material scale in any filing**; demand-side data spend is large (frontier labs reportedly ~$1B/yr each on human data — press estimate) but **actively being in-housed** (Meta–Scale), making the supplier tier structurally fragile even as the theme is validated. (M)

## Key Findings

**1. The pure-play surface is razor-thin.** Once you remove incumbents that keep their data, the only US-listed "sells-data/labeling-into-the-AI-boom" pure-play of meaningful size is **Innodata (INOD)**, with **Reddit (RDDT)** a scarce-corpus hybrid whose actual licensing line is small. The Tier-1 toll booths (RELX, TRI, SPGI, MCO) and financial-data names (FDS, MORN) are **demand-side AI deployers, not suppliers** — they restrict training use and embed AI in their own workflows.

**2. Incumbent franchises are growing, not eroding — so far.** TRI Legal organic **+9%** FY2025; RELX Legal underlying **+9%**; SPGI **+8%** to **$15.336B**; MCO **+9%** to **~$7.7B**; FDS FY2025 (ended 8/31/25) **+5.4%** to **$2,321.7M**, retention **91%**. AI products (CoCounsel, Lexis+AI/Protégé) are disclosed as incremental growth drivers, not cannibalization. But **Morningstar downgraded TRI and Wolters Kluwer moats Wide→Narrow** on long-run AI-substitution risk, and Anthropic's Claude finance plug-in (May 5, 2026) sent **FactSet down 8.1% in a single session**.

**3. Licensing lines are small today.** Reddit "Other revenue" (incl. data licensing) was **~$36M in Q4 2025 (+~8% YoY)**, **~$140M FY2025 (+22%)**, ~5% of revenue. Wiley realized **$40M** AI licensing in FY2025 (up from $23M). News Corp–OpenAI is reportedly **>$250M over five years** (~$50M/yr, press estimate). These are options on future pricing power, not yet engines.

**4. Copyright tilted pro-developer.** Training = fair use in Bartz and Kadrey; the residual liability is *pirated acquisition* (Anthropic's **$1.5B** settlement). Getty largely lost in the UK (Nov 4, 2025) and refiled in N.D. Cal. (Aug 14, 2025). NYT v. OpenAI is in aggressive discovery (**20M ChatGPT logs compelled Jan 5, 2026**).

### Priority B — Resolved questions

**B1. Pure-play surface sizing (H).** Classifying each name:
- **SUPPLIERS selling into the AI boom:** INOD (pure), RDDT (partial, ~5% of revenue), GETY/SSTK (permissioned license-out atop a declining core), NWSA/WLY/UMG/WMG (license-out via episodic deals), plus the private trio (Scale/Surge/Mercor).
- **INCUMBENTS using data defensively:** RELX, TRI, SPGI, MCO, FDS, MORN, PSO.

The honest conclusion: among US-listed equities, the **"AI-data supplier pure-play" of meaningful size is essentially INOD alone**, with RDDT a scarce-corpus hybrid where the actual licensing line is small (~5% of revenue). Everything else marketed as an "AI data play" is either an incumbent (restrict-and-build) or a content owner harvesting episodic licensing checks with weak recurring visibility. **The investable pure-play surface is far thinner than the narrative implies.** This is a correction, not a confirmation, of how the theme is typically pitched.

**B2. Tier-1 disintermediation stress test (H near-term, L long-term).** Evidence currently favors **DURABILITY**: TRI Legal organic +9% FY2025 (recurring = 97% of segment); RELX Legal underlying +9% (up from 8%); SPGI +8%; MCO +9%; FDS retention 91%. Management frames AI as incremental — TRI: legal AI workflows are *"largely incremental to our traditional research and know-how stronghold."* Counter-evidence: **Morningstar (Rob Hales) downgraded TRI and Wolters Kluwer moats Wide→Narrow** (FVE cut TRI to $140 from $170), warning it "cannot fully discount the possibility that AI editorialization surpasses... human editorialization"; TRI flagged most exposed (**~45% of EBIT from legal** vs 10–13% at RELX/Wolters). The Anthropic Claude finance plug-in (May 5, 2026) sent **FDS −8.1% in a day**. Independent quality data: a 2025 Stanford study found Westlaw AI-Assisted Research hallucinates ~33% and Lexis+ AI ~17% — relevant to moat durability.
**Verdict:** toll booth **durable on a 2–3 year horizon; genuinely uncertain on 10–20 years.** *Falsifiable signal:* sequential deceleration in Legal/Big-Three organic growth below ~6%, or any disclosed seat-count decline. *Note:* I could not locate a primary sell-side note quantifying literal "seat compression"/junior-lawyer headcount effects — the thesis lives in secondary commentary; the closest primary framing is Morningstar's "in-house substitution" risk (users doing more in-house rather than outsourcing).

**B3. Copyright payoff matrix (H).**
- **Branch A — pro-rightsholder / forced licensing entrenches:** Winners are owners with BOTH unique data AND enforcement leverage — UMG/WMG (registered copyrights + walled-garden leverage), NWSA (registered + ToS), GETY (registered + watermarks), RDDT (ToS/contract + technical blocking). INOD largely unaffected (services, not owned IP).
- **Branch B — pro-developer / scraping effectively free (current trajectory after Bartz/Kadrey):** Only owners with contract/technical gating or truly scarce access-controlled data get paid (RDDT via API gating; financial-data incumbents via entitlement/MCP). Open-web text owners get little. **This is the base case.**
- **Swing factors:** NYT v. OpenAI (output-substitution theory + 20M-log discovery) and Getty's N.D. Cal. refiling.
- **The one clean, large transfer regardless of branch:** *pirated acquisition* liability. The **$1.5B Bartz v. Anthropic settlement** (preliminarily approved by Judge William Alsup, Sept. 25, 2025) covers the LibGen/PiLiMi pirated-books class at roughly $3,000/work. Per co-lead counsel Susman Godfrey, *"Anthropic will pay $1.5 billion plus interest into a settlement fund, equating to approximately $3,000 for each work"* — the largest publicly reported copyright recovery in US history. Per the Authors Guild (Apr. 2026), 91.3% of eligible works (440,490) were claimed; after ~$208.6M in costs the net fund is ~$1.29B (estimated base payout ~$2,931/work), with a fairness hearing set May 14, 2026 before Judge Martínez-Olguín.
- **Enforcement-leverage distinction:** HAVE leverage — RDDT, UMG, WMG, NWSA, GETY, financial-data incumbents; LACK leverage — long-tail publishers, individual creators, commodity image libraries.

**B4. Licensing-line materiality (H).** Reddit: Other revenue ~$36M Q4 2025 (+~8%), ~$140M FY2025 (+22%), ~5% of revenue — a small **option**, not an engine; Reddit is renegotiating the Google deal toward usage-based pricing (mgmt). Wiley: **$40M FY2025** (8-K), real but lumpy/episodic. News Corp: per The Wall Street Journal (May 22, 2024), the OpenAI deal is *"valued at over $250 million"* over five years in cash and technology credits (~$50M/yr, **press estimate, unconfirmed in filings**); CEO Robert Thomson: *"there is a premium for premium journalism."* **Net:** data licensing is today an OPTION on future pricing power for every owner **except** the labeling pure-plays, where it IS the business.

### Priority C — Deep dives

**C1. Toll-collection layer (M).** Cloudflare launched **pay-per-crawl** (private beta, July 1, 2025), defaulting to block AI crawlers and using HTTP **402** with Cloudflare as Merchant of Record. Management calls Cloudflare a *"neutral broker,"* expects non-human (agentic) traffic to surpass human by **2027**, and plans 2026 "first revenue-sharing models for the long tail." But it is **NOT yet a disclosed revenue line** — "in discussions on AI pay-per-crawl" (Q1 2026 call). **RSL (Really Simple Licensing)** reached v1.0 (Dec 2025), backed by Reddit, Yahoo, Medium, Quora, AP, Stack Overflow, O'Reilly, and adopted by Cloudflare/Akamai/Fastly; the **RSL Collective** is an ASCAP/BMI-style clearinghouse offering pay-per-crawl and **pay-per-inference**. TollBit and ProRata aggregate long-tail publishers. **Verdict:** real and standardizing, but **no major AI lab has agreed to comply**, and value capture is unproven. Public way to play: NET is the only large-cap, but pay-per-crawl is immaterial to its revenue today — value will likely accrue mostly to the *owners*, with the infra taking a thin clearing fee.

**C2. Demand side (M).** Press/analyst estimates put frontier labs at **~$1B/yr each on human data** (OpenAI, Google, Meta, Anthropic — press estimate). Data-labeling TAM estimates cluster around **$1.1B–$4.9B in 2025** growing ~20–28% (Technavio, Precedence, Mordor, MarketsandMarkets — directional only). The key signal is **IN-HOUSING**: Meta's $14.3B Scale stake then routing work to Surge/Mercor; Google/OpenAI/xAI pausing Scale post-deal; "in-house" the fastest-growing sourcing segment in multiple market studies. Demand is real but concentrated and partly migrating in-house — **structurally bad for arms-length suppliers' pricing power.**

**C3. Synthetic-data crossover (H).** Where synthetic **already works:** code and math/reasoning via **RLVR** (reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards) — automatically checkable outcomes; recent work (arXiv 2503.23829) extends RLVR beyond math/code using model-based rewards. Where it **fails:** recursive training on synthetic data causes **"model collapse"** (Shumailov et al., *Nature*, July 2024) — loss of distribution tails; corroborated by follow-on theory (ICLR 2025 "Strong Model Collapse"; arXiv 2404.05090). Subjective/expert-judgment/safety still needs humans — which is why Surge/Mercor (expert annotation) are growing while commodity labeling commoditizes. **Realistic timeline:** verifiable domains increasingly synthetic now; expert-judgment/safety domains human-dependent for the foreseeable future.

**C4. Distribution / international / expert labor (H on MCP trend).** The **entitlement/MCP-server "controlled licensing" model is becoming the durable monetization standard** for financial-data owners: FactSet (first production-grade MCP, Dec 16, 2025), Moody's (MCP app, 600M+ companies), S&P Global, Morningstar, LSEG, PitchBook all live via Anthropic's finance agents (May 5, 2026) and the Databricks MCP Marketplace. This lets owners monetize AI *access* **without surrendering training rights** — the cleanest "have your cake and eat it" model and the single most important distribution development this cycle. **International names worth knowing:** Wolters Kluwer (Netherlands; legal/health — same moat questions as RELX/TRI, and downgraded alongside TRI), RELX (UK), Experian/Equifax (credit data, restrict-and-build). **Expert-labor aggregators:** Mercor, Surge, Handshake AI, Turing, Invisible — all private; **no clean US-listed pure-play for "expert-data marketplace."**

## Updated investability + thesis-fit ranking (single-line reason each moved vs Round 1)

1. **RELX / TRI (H)** — *Unchanged as Tier-1 toll booths, but reclassified from "data owner" to "AI-deployer incumbent"; durable near-term (Legal +9%), with a new long-run moat asterisk from Morningstar's Wide→Narrow downgrade.*
2. **SPGI / MCO / FDS (H)** — *Strengthened: the MCP/entitlement model is now a concrete, primary-sourced way to monetize AI access without giving up data — but FDS's −8.1% plug-in day shows the market will punish perceived disintermediation.*
3. **INOD (M)** — *The only liquid US-listed pure-play supplier; thesis intact but concentration risk (~58% one customer) confirmed and now load-bearing — high-beta proxy for frontier-lab budgets, not a franchise.*
4. **RDDT (M)** — *Moved down as a "data-licensing engine": primary filings show the line is only ~5% of revenue growing slowly; re-rated as an advertising business with a data-licensing call option.*
5. **NWSA / WLY (M)** — *Episodic license-out checks confirmed (Wiley $40M; News Corp ~$50M/yr press); real but lumpy and non-recurring — option, not engine.*
6. **UMG / WMG (M)** — *Upgraded within content-owners: settlements (Suno/Udio) plus walled-garden equity/licensing give BOTH unique data AND enforcement leverage — the structure most likely to get paid in either copyright branch.*
7. **NET (M)** — *New entrant to the map as the toll-collection infra leader; investable theme leader but pay-per-crawl is immaterial to revenue today — watch, don't pay for it yet.*
8. **GETY / SSTK (L)** — *Downgraded: UK loss weakened enforcement leverage; merger under regulatory review; permissioned-AI optionality sits atop a structurally challenged core.*
9. **MORN / PSO (L–M)** — *Incumbent deployers; MORN benefits from MCP entitlement, PSO thinnest on disclosure.*

## Recommendations
- **Treat INOD as the only liquid US-listed AI-data-supplier pure-play, and price the concentration risk explicitly.** ~58% single-customer revenue and project-based, terminable contracts make it a high-beta proxy for frontier-lab data budgets, not a durable franchise. *Downgrade trigger:* loss/step-down of the top customer, or the $68M pre-training program revenue failing to convert.
- **Own the incumbents (RELX/TRI/SPGI/MCO/FDS) as AI-deployers, not AI-data plays.** *Thesis-fit benchmark:* Big-Three/Legal organic growth holding ≥7–9% and entitlement/MCP access scaling. *Downgrade trigger:* organic deceleration below ~6%, disclosed seat-count erosion, or moat-rating cuts spreading beyond Morningstar.
- **Treat RDDT data-licensing as a call option, not the base case.** Underwrite on advertising; assign optionality value to the Google re-pricing toward usage-based and to RSL/pay-per-inference standardization. *Re-rate trigger:* Other-revenue reaccelerating above ~20% YoY with disclosed usage-based terms.
- **For content owners (NWSA/WLY/UMG/WMG/GETY/SSTK), require BOTH unique data AND enforcement leverage before crediting licensing upside.** Episodic deals (Wiley $40M; News Corp ~$50M/yr) are lumpy and non-recurring; durable value accrues to walled-garden equity stakes (UMG/WMG–Suno/Udio) and to pirated-acquisition settlements (Anthropic $1.5B).
- **Watch NET pay-per-crawl and RSL adoption as the leading indicator for the entire "owners can charge crawlers" thesis** — but do not pay for it in NET's valuation yet. *Trigger:* a major AI lab publicly agreeing to RSL/pay-per-crawl terms, or Cloudflare breaking out pay-per-crawl revenue.

## Caveats
- **Non-disclosure is pervasive:** TRI, RELX, SPGI, MCO, FDS, MORN, GETY, SSTK, PSO do **not** break out AI-attributable revenue; figures here are organic-growth proxies, not AI dollars.
- **Press/unverified:** News Corp (>$250M/5yr), Scale (~$2B 2025), Surge (~$1.2B 2024), Mercor revenue, and "labs spend ~$1B/yr each" are estimates, not filings. Reddit's ~$130M/$203M licensing figures are press; the filed figures are the Other-revenue lines (~$36M Q4 2025; ~$140M FY2025).
- **Reddit Q4 detail:** the audited $35.6M/+7% figure is the **Q3 2025** Other-revenue line; Q4 2025 was ~$36M and FY2025 ~$140M (+22%). Verify exact Q4 against Reddit's 10-K.
- **Copyright is district-court level and on appeal;** Bartz/Kadrey are fact-specific and not binding precedent; NYT v. OpenAI and Getty N.D. Cal. could shift the matrix.
- **TAM figures vary widely** by vendor ($1.1B–$4.9B for 2025) and are directional only.
- **Horizon:** conclusions hold most confidently over 2–3 years; the 10–20 year disintermediation question is genuinely unresolved.

# Transformer-Supply Refresh + the Solid-State-Transformer Shift

## 1. Verdict

The transformer oligopoly's pricing power is **holding into the 2027-2029 capacity wave, but the moat is tiered, not uniform** — and the SST transition **extends the incumbents rather than disrupting them, near-term.** Maker order books are still *building*, not draining (Eaton electrical backlog +48% YoY / book-to-bill 1.2; GE Vernova electrification orders +86% organic / Electrification backlog $25B→$42.4B; WoodMac lead times 128wk power / 144wk GSU *still rising*), and GEV is guiding 2026 Power-equipment new-order pricing **10-20% above Q4 2025 with immediate margin expansion** while ETN took an April-1-2026 price increase — direct, maker-stated evidence pricing power is *rising*, not cracking, into the wave. The genuine two-sided tension is that **distribution/switchgear lead times are already easing (26-40 weeks) while large-power/GSU stays tight** — so the 2027-2029 supply response normalizes the bottom of the stack first; the falsifier bites tier-by-tier, not all at once. On SST: every line of evidence converges on "additive new sockets, not substitution" — SSTs win data-center medium-voltage / behind-the-meter / EV-charging at 5-10× cost, no IEEE/IEC standard, 94-97% efficiency vs iron-core 99.5%+, and crucially use **nanocrystalline/ferrite cores, not GOES** — so they sit *downstream* of the utility step-down and leave the GOES base intact. Incumbents [[ETN]] (Resilient Power) and [[GEV]] (Prolec + FASST SST) own both paths, but **well-funded startups (Heron $140M / 40GW-yr, Amperesand, Ideal Power, DG Matrix) compete for the same new socket** — so "incumbents automatically own both" is not guaranteed at the new-socket layer. The live demand-durability falsifier (a 20-30% hyperscaler capex cut) has **not fired and is moving the opposite way** (Big-5 2026 capex *raised* ~75% YoY to ~$660-690B).

## 2. Cross-angle insight (second-order findings)

**A. The SST "bottleneck migration" relieves itself — the constraint lands in a different node than the glut.** Read Angle 11 against Angles 6/8/12 together: the SiC *commodity* wafer base is in glut (6-inch substrate >20% price drop, Yole ~50% upstream / ~70% device-line utilization, overcapacity to 2027-28), which is a *tailwind* for SST cost curves — yet the real SST constraint is the immature 3.3-10kV **medium-voltage** SiC device (engineering-sample stage; Microchip 3.3kV May 2026, Wolfspeed 10kV only now productized). So the SST bottleneck does NOT migrate to commodity SiC; it migrates to a narrow MV-device node that is "qualitatively real but quantitatively small" (US data-center SST market only $40.3M→$154M by 2030). Second-order consequence: the cheap-wafer glut means once the MV device productizes, the abundant base **backfills it fast** — so MV-SiC is an R&D/packaging gap, *not* a geology-grade durable chokepoint like GOES. The SST disruption is rate-limited by device maturity, not by a substrate scarcity that could be monetized.

**B. The same physical power shortage that *creates* the transformer moat also *caps* how much of the "cancellation tail" is real demand destruction.** Reading Angle 14 against Angles 1/10/13: 30-50% of 2026 US data-center projects are flagged delayed/cancelled — which *looks* like the demand-durability falsifier firing — but the cancellations are largely **gated by the power/transformer shortage itself**, not by weak end-demand (only ~1/3 of 2026's ~12 GW is under active construction; interconnection runs 3-7 years). So a cancellation here does NOT free up the order book — it reflects inability-to-energize. This is a genuine analytical knot: the *same* binding constraint simultaneously (i) sustains incumbent pricing power and (ii) generates the deferral statistics that a naive read would treat as the pricing-power falsifier. The two signals point opposite directions from the same root cause — the falsifier only truly fires if cancellations show up as **maker de-bookings / book-to-bill <1.0**, not as project-developer deferrals.

**C. Value capture is migrating UP the stack toward the substrate even as the substrate's owner shows the WEAKEST realized margin — a contradiction to flag.** Angle 15 ranks GOES substrate (Cleveland-Cliffs) as the structurally hardest seat to dislodge (physics-bound, sole-US producer), yet Angle 3 surfaces that Cliffs' 2025 results were *weak* (net loss; cyclical steel), so substrate scarcity has "not yet translated to fat margins — optionality, not realized." Meanwhile the OEM oligopoly captures the most *cash* today but is the tier *most* erodible to the 2027-2029 capacity wave. So the durability gradient (substrate > OEM > switchgear > SST) and the realized-economics gradient run in **opposite directions** at the top: the most durable seat earns the least today, the most-erodible seat earns the most. This is the load-bearing tension for any value-capture call — and it cuts against a simple "own the substrate" conclusion.

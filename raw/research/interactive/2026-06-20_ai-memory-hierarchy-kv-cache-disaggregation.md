# AI Memory Hierarchy & KV-Cache Disaggregation — The Inference Memory Wall

## 1. Verdict

The structural thesis is **right on the ladder, over-confident on the height.** As reasoning and long-context inference grow, the attention KV-cache genuinely outgrows on-package HBM and spills down a real, capacity-vs-bandwidth-tiered hierarchy (HBM → CPU DRAM → CXL-pooled DRAM → NVMe), and durable value does concentrate in the supply-inelastic memory tiers — but the *durability* gradient inside that ladder is steeper and more time-limited than the bull framing implies. The cleanest read tier-by-tier: **HBM/DRAM is the hardest chokepoint but it is a *cyclical* supply-inelastic anchor (2-year capacity lag, ~1-year sold-out contracts, a third HBM4 supplier qualifying, a flagged 2028–29 glut), not a structurally permanent one** — so the "structural" grade belongs to the *spill architecture itself*, not to HBM's unit price. **The CXL controller/switch leg — which carries the only clean US-listed proxies (ALAB, MRVL) — is the *least durable* leg and the one most likely to be wrong**: memory *expansion* ships today (Azure/Astera Leo), but multi-rack *pooling* is still a 2027 bet, SemiAnalysis argues CXL is being designed out of the AI rack on shoreline-bandwidth physics (~3× per-shoreline-area penalty vs NVLink/ICI), and value may migrate to the scale-up fabric (NVLink/UALink) or be captured internally as embedded IP licensed into hyperscaler ASICs. NAND/SSD is a real-but-cyclical sidecar that owns no chokepoint (no buyer-defense, fast process-conversion, an unsanctioned Chinese marginal supplier in YMTC/CXMT). The serving-software layer (Dynamo, vLLM, Mooncake) is overwhelmingly given away to pull demand into the hardware below it. **The single most important falsifier across every tier is KV-cache compression** (MLA's 93.3% cut, Google TurboQuant's ~4–6× deployed, pure-SSM Mamba-3) — it is firing at the chip level and advancing fast at the architecture level, but has not yet falsified the system-level thesis because (a) it buys a constant factor against a context term that grows without bound, (b) it helps HBM/DRAM equally, and (c) Jevons has repeatedly turned efficiency into *more* total memory bought. **Access tension inverts the durability tension: the most reachable equities sit on the least durable leg.**

## 3. Analysis by angle — who owns what, where durable value sits

**The spill mechanism is real and quantified (Angle 1).** GQA already cut per-token cost ~8× (~320 KB/token for Llama-3-70B FP16; note a ~160 KB/token counting-convention variant across sources). A single 128K-context request ≈ 40 GB of KV-cache; **4 concurrent 128K requests ≈ 160 GB, exceeding a single 141 GB H200** — so the marginal long-context/high-concurrency token *cannot* live in HBM and must spill. The hierarchy is bandwidth-tiered: HBM ~3.35 TB/s → CPU DRAM ~63 GB/s (PCIe 5.0) → NVMe ~7 GB/s, a ~50× HBM→DRAM cliff. Decode-token *compute* stays HBM-bound (structural); cache *storage* migrates down. Mooncake/Kimi productionizes this (**+75% real-workload requests**; the "525%" headline is simulated). The whole spill argument is **[contested]** on un-compressed FP16 math — see the falsifiers.

**Tier 1 — HBM, the hardest chokepoint but cyclically anchored (Angles 2, 9).** Three structural facts force the spill: (1) **3-to-1 wafer displacement** — every HBM GB consumes ~3× the DDR5 wafer area (~4× vs generic DRAM), HBM rising 19%→23% of DRAM wafers, so HBM scarcity *cannibalizes the very DRAM tiers the spill lands in*; (2) **sold-out-through-2026** confirmed across all three (SK Hynix "sold out our DRAM, NAND and HBM for next year"; Micron FQ1'26 record $13.6B rev / 56.8% GM, 2026 HBM price+volume locked); (3) **HBM4 raises the chokepoint** — 2048-bit, base die on TSMC logic, SK Hynix taking ~two-thirds of NVIDIA Vera Rubin HBM4. **Counter-case [contested]:** TechInsights flags a 2027 bullwhip downturn; a **2028–29 oversupply scenario** is openly modeled (BISI); Samsung cleared NVIDIA/AMD HBM4 qual at industry-leading 11.7 Gb/s (Feb 2026 mass production) — so allocation is *eroding at the margin, not concentrating*. The honest grade: **HBM is a cyclical supply-inelastic anchor (2026 inelasticity window), not a structurally durable one** — reserve "structural" for the spill architecture. Flags: 62/21/17 split is **Q2 2025 HBM-only, date-stamp it**; "~150,000 wafers/month each" cleanroom add **[unverified]** (inconsistent with per-company figures); Micron "DRAM ASP +mid-60% sequential" likely a YoY conflation **[verify]**.

**Tier 2 — server DRAM, borrowed durability (Angles 3, 9).** KV-offload is now productized (Micron 256GB SOCAMM2: ~2TB CPU-attached LPDRAM per 8-channel CPU, >2.3× TTFT for long-context offload — *vendor claim*; MRDIMM +39% bandwidth). Same oligopoly, but **~90%, not ~95% [corrected]** (Samsung 38.6% + SK Hynix 28.8% + Micron 22.4%, with **CXMT diluting to ~7.6%, share doubling**). Server DRAM ran **~90–95% QoQ in Q1'26** (TrendForce forecast; ~81% realized industry print) — *hotter* than the original ~55–60% guide. Verdict: **real-but-ASP-cyclical; durability is *borrowed* from HBM's fab crowd-out + oligopoly discipline, not owned at the DDR5 node.** Killed cleanly: the **"SK Hynix quietly cut HBM capex on 2027-oversupply modeling" claim is [unverified and contradicted]** — public data shows SK Hynix *raising* 2026 capex (~$20.5B, +17%) for HBM4; conservatism is on NAND.

**Tier 3 — CXL silicon, the contested control point (Angles 4, 5, 14).** The expansion/pooling split is the whole story: **memory *expansion* ships today** (Astera Leo on Azure M-series, "industry-first," CXL 2.0, 2TB/controller — single-host, *not* a pool), but **multi-rack *pooling* is a 2027 bet** (CXL 4.0/PCIe 7.0 released Nov 2025; production 2026–27; multi-rack pooling "largely theoretical at scale, no major deployments"). Microsoft's production **Pond** is the best primary-grade latency evidence (75–90ns added per 8–16-socket pool, 1–5% slowdown, ~7% DRAM saved — but **>21% of workloads suffer >25% loss**, so pooling is selective). The players: **Astera (ALAB)** — Q1 FY2026 $308.4M/+93% YoY, PCIe Gen 6 >⅓ of revenue, but **growth is Scorpio/Aries (PCIe), and Leo/CXL is the smallest, pre-scale line** — its real moat is COSMOS software, and concentration sits on one dominant hyperscaler; **Marvell (MRVL)** — Structera, the only CXL 2.0 family interop-tested across both CPU architectures + all three memory makers, +next-gen CXL switch (OFC 2026), +XConn ~$540M (Jan 2026 consolidation). **Three live threats [contested]:** (a) SemiAnalysis "CXL Is Dead In The AI Era" — hyperscalers "almost entirely pivoted away," NVLink/ICI carry **~3× more bandwidth per unit of shoreline area** so CXL is designed out of the AI rack; (b) **NVLink Fusion + NVIDIA's ~$2B into Marvell (Mar 31 2026)** pulls the "neutral toll-collector" inside NVIDIA's tent; (c) the realistic in-sourcing mode is **embedded IP licensing into hyperscaler ASICs**, not a clean-room build — eroding merchant margin without producing a "Google CXL controller." What *survives*: the **retimer/PCIe-switch business (Aries, Scorpio, Microchip XpressConnect)** — durable demand tied to PCIe Gen6 transitions + TSMC-node dependence, regardless of whether CXL pooling takes off. Corrections: Montage "shipped first MXC" → **sampling since Sept 2025**; Montage "supplies Samsung/SK Hynix" → **forward collaboration language only**; the "NVIDIA gave its blessing for Blackwell-Astera" anecdote and "~4–5× cheaper than VRAM" ratio are **[unverified]**.

**Tier 4 — NAND/SSD, the cyclical sidecar (Angle 6).** Demand is real and primary-grounded (NVIDIA ICMSP standardizes NVMe KV-cache offload, CES 2026; ~16TB SSD per high-end GPU; ~1,152TB NAND per NVL72; AI inference >80% reads = QLC sweet spot; **75–100 incremental EB in 2027, doubling after** — TrendForce). More fragmented oligopoly (top-5 >80%; Samsung **31.6% [corrected from prior-period 28%]**). NAND ran +70–75% QoQ (Q2'26), *outpacing* DRAM. **But the cyclical caveat is load-bearing and dominant:** no buyer-defense moat, fast process-conversion supply-response, and **the China flood is already in motion** — **YMTC targeting 15% of global NAND by late 2026 (→ #3 behind Samsung & Kioxia)**, UBS sees China adding 120k–140k wafers/month. Verdict: **real-but-cyclical; owns no chokepoint.** The cleanest tripwire: **YMTC reaching credible enterprise-QLC qualification at hyperscale** = the moment "structurally levered" decays to "along for the ride." QLC 12.9%→46.4% is an **Omdia** projection; 2028–29 glut is a **demand-contingent possibility, not a scheduled base case [corrected]**.

**Serving software — orchestrates value, captures little (Angle 7).** The tell is *who gives it away and why*: **NVIDIA Dynamo is fully open-source (Apache 2.0, GA Mar 2026)** to sell the GPUs/NVLink/CUDA underneath; **llm-d** is co-donated to CNCF by every hardware/cloud vendor precisely so *none* tolls the layer; **Mooncake/LMCache** accrue mindshare, not license revenue. Monetization that exists sells *services/abstraction* (RadixArk **$100M/$400M [corrected from "Accel Jan 2026" rumor]**, Inferact **$150M/$800M, a16z+Lightspeed**, Modular $1.6B, Modal **$4.65B [updated from stale $2.5B]**) — a *valuation bet* on capturing the interface, not realized software margin. **The one counter-current [contested]:** if a neutral abstraction layer (vLLM spanning NVIDIA/AMD/TPU/Gaudi) durably makes accelerators swappable, value could migrate *up* to software — the inverse of the memory-wall thesis. **Killed:** Dynamo "50× on GB300 NVL72 vs Hopper" is **[unsupported]** — the source says ~30× on GB200, 2× on Hopper.

**Falsifier #1 — compression (Angles 11, 13).** The strongest falsifier, now "live and dated." **MLA cut KV-cache 93.3% in DeepSeek-V2** (primary paper, baked into training). **Google TurboQuant** (ICLR 2026, calibration-free) = ~6× headline / **3.8–5.7× empirical** memory cut at 3-bit — and **moved the tape**: on announcement (Mar 25 2026) SK Hynix −6.2%, Samsung −4.8%, Micron/WDC/SanDisk sold off. Reasoning was thought compression-proof, but **[contested]** a reasoning-specific wave (ThinKV, ForesightKV, LongFlow, PM-KVQ — which *beats* the 16-bit baseline on AIME/CMIMC) is closing that gap. Pure-SSM **Mamba-3** (ICLR 2026 oral, no attention) is at language-modeling parity-plus — but **still fails frontier multi-hop reasoning/CoT** (arXiv 2410.03810), and loses its memory edge exactly when forced to do hard reasoning. **Why it doesn't fully fire:** compression buys a *constant factor* against a context term growing without bound, helps HBM/DRAM equally (shifts the spill *threshold*, not its *direction*), and **Jevons** keeps turning efficiency into more total memory bought (DeepSeek cost cuts left H100/H200 demand firm-to-strengthening). PM-KVQ "+8%" **[corrected to ≤6.5% on AIME-2024]**; Mamba-3 "~4%/7×" are **Tier-4-sourced [partially unverified]**.

**Falsifier #2 — SRAM/HBM-bypass (Angle 12) — does NOT trip.** Cerebras WSE-3 (44 GB on-die SRAM, ~21 PB/s) wins single-stream speed (~2,100 tok/s on Llama-70B) precisely by *eliminating* off-package memory — which **caps the KV-cache it can hold**, so it gets *worse* exactly where the spill thesis lives (long-context, high-concurrency). Groq needs 256–576 chips to hold a useful model + cache. Tellingly, the incumbents are *absorbing* the latency edge (**NVIDIA ~$20B Groq license+hire**; Qualcomm **reportedly in talks** to buy Tenstorrent at ~$8–10B **[corrected: rumored, not closed]**), not adopting an HBM-free stack. The strongest counter — **Cerebras+AWS disaggregated inference** (Trainium prefills + ships KV-cache over EFA → CS-3 decodes) — *confirms* the hierarchy: SRAM took the *decode/bandwidth* tier, it did not absorb the capacity hierarchy. PIM (JEDEC LPDDR6-PIM) is the most thesis-*reinforcing* signal. GDDR6 "~50% cheaper/GB" and PIM "3.24× / KV-targeting" are **[unverified]**.

**Falsifier #3 — demand air-pocket (Angle 13) — weaker than it looks.** Capex is still *ramping* (~$600B 2026, +36%; **$725–775B** on broader counts **[corrected from spurious $755B]**) with massive *contracted* backlogs (Amazon $244B, Microsoft $625B RPO, Oracle $553B RPO +325%, >$100B of mostly-5-year leases) — a near-term air-pocket would surface as *broken contracts*, not yet observed. The "5% GPU utilization / $401B" bear headline **[unverified, 403]** mostly *inverts* into a thesis-support point — low SM-utilization in inference is the **memory-wall signature** (KV-cache/PCIe/bandwidth stalls), per Yotta Labs **[citation corrected from Uptime]**. The MIT NANDA "95% of pilots fail" figure is verified but **methodologically contested**. The one surviving residual risk: ASP-elasticity of the *cheapest pooled-memory tier*. **Genuine tripwires:** hyperscaler *guidance cuts* / RPO declines QoQ; lease cancellations on the 2026 cohort; DRAM/HBM ASPs rolling over *before* late-2027 while utilization stays low.

**Investability & access (Angle 15).** Durable value concentrates in foreign-issuer/China-gated nodes, leaving few clean US proxies. **HBM/DRAM (highest conviction):** SK Hynix (000660 KS) + Samsung (005930 KS) foreign-issuer; **Micron (MU) = cleanest reachable HBM proxy** (~15k HBM4 wafers/mo by 2026, confirmed). **CXL control points (weakest leg, cleanest US proxies):** **ALAB + MRVL US-listed; Montage (688008 SH) A-share-only/China-gated** → reachable via Vic's A-share allocation. **NAND sidecar:** SanDisk (SNDK) + Kioxia (285A JP). The DRAM-glut bust is modeled **2028–29, not 2027 [corrected]** — which *strengthens* the high-conviction-through-2027 read. The "Nov-2025 Azure-Leo commercial launch" is **[unverified]**, so the CXL falsifier should be reworded hyperscaler-agnostic ("if no named hyperscaler moves CXL pooling to production by H2-2026").

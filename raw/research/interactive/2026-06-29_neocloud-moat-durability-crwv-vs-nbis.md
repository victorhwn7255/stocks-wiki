# Neocloud business moats — durable edge or leverage trap (Tier-3 anchor, 2026-06-29)

## 1. Verdict

This run **CONFIRMS** the vault's prior verdict — *"borrowed-and-temporary moat tilting toward a leverage trap; durable AWS-style moat NOT supported; neocloud sits at the bottom of the chokepoint-quality gradient"* — and, critically, **UPDATES the half-inferential Nebius side to filing-grade**. The chokepoint-ownership audit (Angle 1) and the six-test moat scorecard (Angle 2) independently re-derive the same call: of five scarce inputs, neoclouds rent four (GPU allocation, datacenter shell, networking, software) and only *energized power* is trending toward ownership — and even that is M&A-replicable within 18–24 months, as Alphabet's $4.75B Intersect Power buy demonstrates. The cohort fails 4 of 6 moat tests and passes none cleanly; what they own is leverage + the rental spread, not a scarce input. The one genuine refinement to the prior verdict: a real but *narrow* non-hardware edge exists — CoreWeave's operational/software moat (ClusterMAX-Platinum goodput, Weights & Biases MLOps) and the energy-first push (Crusoe stranded gas, CoreWeave's $9B/~1.3GW Core Scientific, Nebius >75% owned power) — which *narrows* the gap to AWS-style durability without closing it. The single most important new fact: the GPU-rental spread is **positive but shortage-driven, not structurally positive** — at CoreWeave Q1 2026, D&A (~$1.15B) almost exactly offset adjusted EBITDA (~$1.16B), producing a ~$740M net loss, which means the headline 56% EBITDA margin is *not* the spread once depreciation and cost-of-capital are subtracted.

## 2. Cross-angle insight (second-order findings + contradictions)

**(A) The "diversification" axis inverts once you get filing-grade data — the single highest-value finding of this run.** Read alone, the head-to-head folklore says "Nebius is the better-financed, more-diversified one." But Angles 6/7/10 together, anchored on the Q1 2026 6-K, show Nebius is **equally or MORE concentrated than CoreWeave on a two-name basis**: Microsoft (~$17.4–19.4B) + Meta ($27B/5-yr) ≈ >90% of a ~$44–50B backlog, versus CoreWeave's Microsoft ~67% softening toward a "no single customer >35%" mix as Meta/OpenAI/Anthropic ramp. Nebius's *real* edge is **balance-sheet resilience, not diversification** — $9.3B cash, low-coupon long-dated converts (1.25%/2031 + 2.625%/2033), no 2026–27 maturity wall — the opposite of the popular framing. The vault's prior table cell "Nebius less concentrated" is now falsified by primary data.

**(B) The "owned power" moat and the "binding power constraint" thesis are the same fact pointing in opposite directions.** Angle 1/14 (power is the only emerging ownership input) and Angle 5/transformer-supply (5–7-year interconnection queues, 128–144-week transformer lead times) together produce a sharper read: neoclouds are reaching *down* toward the Layer-2 power moat (where the vault already places ETN/VRT/utilities) but cannot reach it, because the same scarcity that makes power valuable also makes large buyers (Meta/Entergy >5.2GW, Google Texas gas, Alphabet/Intersect) build or buy it on the exact 18–24-month clock the durability test screens for. Energy-first is "a better seat at the bottom of the gradient, not an exit from it."

**(C) The circular-financing risk is bifurcated, not monolithic — the Meta/OpenAI split is the load-bearing distinction.** Angles 8/9/10 read together yield: **Meta-anchored paper is durable** (the A3/A-low $8.5B DDTL 4.0, ≥$19B take-or-pay, step-in rights, near-sovereign credit) while **OpenAI-anchored paper is the fragile node** (cash-burning counterparty inside the loop; CFO Friar's warning it could "fail to grow revenue fast enough to pay for future computing contracts"; the March 2025 Microsoft $12B option walk-away that OpenAI absorbed). The leverage-trap risk is concentrated on the *OpenAI demand-driver*, not the contract *structure* per se.

**CONTRADICTIONS flagged across angles:**
- **The post-shortage rental rebound is venue-contested.** Angle 3/4 lean on a SemiAnalysis H100 1-yr index rising $1.70→$2.35 (+40%, Oct 2025→Mar 2026); Angle 5's verification **could not independently confirm** that 1-yr figure and surfaced direct dissent (Spheron: B200 *on-demand* still trending *down* mid-2026). Resolution: the rebound is real for **marketplace/spot** (Vast.ai, Silicon Data B200 index +24.4% YTD) but **contested for on-demand/contract** rates. Any "spread holds" claim must specify venue.
- **Core Scientific acquisition status.** Angle 1 states the $9B/~1.3GW Core Scientific deal **closed Q4 2025**; the vault's CRWV.md records CORZ stockholders **rejecting the merger in October 2025**. These cannot both be current — flag for resolution at next CRWV/CORZ refresh (Section 4.3, do not silently overwrite).
- **The NVIDIA→Nebius instrument.** Several angles' drafts called it "$2B prefunded warrants"; the verified read (Angle 6/8, off the 6-K) is a **$2B NVIDIA equity investment**. The vault's NBIS page "$2B + pre-funded warrant" framing needs the line-item checked.

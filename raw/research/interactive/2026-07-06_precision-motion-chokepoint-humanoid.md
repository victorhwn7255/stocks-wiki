# The precision-motion chokepoint in humanoid robots: who owns the durable grinding/metallurgy seat, and is it eroding?

## 1. Verdict

The durable seat in humanoid precision motion is real, physical, and narrow — but it is also, for a public-market investor, largely un-buyable, and its two deepest rungs are defended by *different* walls that erode on *different* clocks. Ranking the four rungs by the chokepoint-quality gradient (physics/metallurgy > precision-grinding > integration): **planetary roller screws > harmonic/strain-wave (top-spec only) ≈ RV/cycloidal > six-axis force-torque sensors.** Roller screws bind on both a metallurgy wall (aerospace-grade bearing steel) *and* a sub-micron thread-grinding wall, and Chinese localization at the top C0–C3 grade is only ~5% [Tier 4] — the deepest moat. Harmonic reducers are a genuine flexspline heat-treat metallurgy moat, but visibly commoditizing at the *standard* grade (Chinese domestic share <5% in 2018 → >35% of Chinese-assembled robots by 2024 [CRIA, Tier 3]; Jefferies cut Harmonic Drive Systems to Underperform Oct-15-2025). Force-torque sensors are the shallowest rung — a calibration/software IP moat under two-front attack (optical designs at ~half price; sensorless torque estimation that designs the discrete sensor out). The uncomfortable investor finding holds after verification: **the purest chokepoint (Swiss roller screws — GSA/Rollvis, ~52% combined) is private/unlisted; the cleanest public reducer name (Harmonic Drive Systems, TSE 6324) is a cyclically-depressed incumbent whose share denominator is genuinely contested; and the only clean US-listed names (NOVT, VPG) sit on the most-erodible rung with humanoid revenue still a rounding error.** Buyability and moat-durability sit on opposite names.

## 2. Cross-angle insight (second-order findings)

**(a) The access gradient runs *inverse* to the durability gradient — a structural double bind.** Reading Angle 8 (durability rank), Angle 11 (value-capture), and Angles 12–13 (buyability) together produces a finding no single angle states: the rung with the *deepest* moat (roller screws) *also* commands the *fattest* gross margin (~60%, the highest of any joint component [Tier 3, unaudited]) *and* is the *least* publicly buyable (GSA/Rollvis private; Ewellix buried inside Schaeffler at <1% of group sales). As you move *down* the durability gradient toward the commoditizing integration layers, buyability *improves* — the cleanly-listed pure-plays (HDSI, Nabtesco, Shuanghuan, Zhongda, NOVT, VPG) are concentrated on the shallower or actively-eroding rungs. A public investor is therefore structurally unable to own the best seat cleanly; the market only sells tickets to the eroding side or to a depressed incumbent. This is the single most important synthesis finding for a public-market reader.

**(b) The roller-screw seat is defended by a supply-side wall but exposed to a demand-side bypass — and that bypass *relieves the tier one rung up*.** Angle 8 ranks roller screws #1 because the grinding/metallurgy wall is hard to climb (C0–C3 localization ~5%). But Angles 9–10 show the seat can be deleted from the *demand* side entirely: quasi-direct-drive (QDD) architectures (Unitree, much of Figure) skip roller screws *and* harmonic content, and Tesla's own confirmed Gen-3 hand pivot toward "gearbox + lead screw + tendon-drive" (Musk, X, 19-Apr-2026: the 22-DoF rolling-contact hand "didn't actually work") is early motion along exactly this falsifier. So even if China *never* cracks C3 grinding, per-robot roller-screw content could fall toward zero on architecture choice alone — a supply-side moat around a demand-contingent socket. Critically, this bypass flows *up* the stack with opposite sign: QDD uses **larger direct-drive magnets**, so an architecture shift that *guts* the roller-screw thesis *strengthens* the rare-earth-magnet thesis. One angle's threat is an adjacent tier's tailwind.

**(c) "Cyclical trough" and "structural erosion" are inseparable from current data because the recovery lever and the erosion risk are the *same market* (China).** Angles 2, 11, and 14 together show HDSI's margin recovery hinges on utilization/China-growth (Morningstar), yet China is precisely where Leaderdrive/Green Harmonic are taking standard-grade share at 40–60% below Japanese price. The bull case (cyclical snap-back — FY2027 operating-income guide +141.5% off a ¥2,567M base) and the bear case (permanent share-and-price concession) both route through Chinese demand and cannot be separated until the recovery arrives *with or without* top-spec price cuts. The FY2026 margin already ticked up off the 26.7% low — the first data point leans cyclical — but the test is the FY2027 China price-realization line, not the volume line.

**Contradictions flagged (must be surfaced, not smoothed):**
- **ISO-3408 mislabel is a corpus-wide inherited error (Angles 4, 8, 11).** ISO 3408 is the *ball-screw* accuracy standard; the "sub-5µm / ≤3µm" tolerances attached to "Class 3 / C3" actually correspond to Grade 0/C0 (C3 ≈ ~8µm, C5 ≈ ~18µm over 300mm). The qualitative grinding-difficulty point is sound; the specific grade+tolerance badges are wrong wherever they appear.
- **Nabtesco share (~60% vs ~35% vs ~28% vs ~18%) is denominator noise, not a data conflict (Angles 3, 8, 15).** ~60% = medium/large *industrial* robot joints (Nabtesco's own estimate); ~35% = all-robot articulated-RV units; ~28% = humanoid-miniature RV; ~18% = whole precision-gear market. Pick one denominator and hold it across readings.
- **Tesla roller-screw sourcing contradicts *within* the source base (Angle 10).** The "pure Swiss GSA Achilles-heel" framing (Angles 4/5/12/13/15) is contradicted by the *same* 36Kr source used for the localization paragraph, which names **Wuzhou New Spring (Chinese)** as "the main supplier of reverse planetary roller screws" for high-torque hip/knee joints. The Swiss-only read is more contested than most angles concede.

## 3. The value chain (downstream → upstream)

1. **Downstream OEMs** — Humanoid: Tesla Optimus, Figure, Unitree, Agibot, UBTech, 1X. Industrial: FANUC, ABB, KUKA, Yaskawa. *Architecture is the chokepoint-maker:* Optimus's 14-rotary/14-linear split manufactures both reducer and roller-screw demand; QDD rivals delete it.
2. **Actuator integrators / module assembly (commoditizing rung)** — [[TUOPU]] (Tesla actuator final assembly/testing; Mexico plant near Giga Texas), [[SANHUA]] (linear/"机电执行器" assembly — attribution contested). Rewards scale + cost, not proprietary IP.
3. **Precision-motion components (the four rungs):**
   - *Harmonic/strain-wave reducers:* Harmonic Drive Systems (HDSI, TSE 6324) — top-spec incumbent; Leaderdrive, Suzhou Green Harmonic (Chinese challengers).
   - *RV/cycloidal reducers:* Nabtesco (TSE 6268) — dominant; [[SHUANGHUAN]] (SZSE 002472), [[ZHONGDA]] (SZSE 002896) + Zhenkang (Chinese).
   - *Planetary roller screws (deepest wall):* GSA/Rollvis (Swiss, private), Ewellix (Swedish, → Schaeffler SHA.DE), Bosch Rexroth (private Bosch). Chinese: Shanghai Beite (603009.SS), Wuzhou New Spring/Xinchun, [[HENGLI]] (SH 601100), Xinjian Transmission.
   - *Six-axis force-torque sensors:* ATI (→ Novanta/[[NOVT]]), Bota (Swiss, private), [[VPG]] (foil strain gauge). Chinese: Yuli, Kunwei, Xinjingcheng; Fourier (optical, ~half price).
4. **Upstream (the true bottleneck per BofA's "machine tools not magnets" read):** precision thread-grinding machine tools (imported Japan/Europe — EMAG; Chinese challengers Qinchuan, Huachen, Hedeman) + aerospace-grade bearing-steel foundries (~5 globally [unverified]). One tier further up: NdFeB rare-earth magnets — the actual geology/physics chokepoint ([[rare-earth-magnet-chokepoint]]).

## 4. Analysis by angle

**Rung 3 — Planetary roller screws (deepest seat, #1 durable).** The only rung binding on *both* top gradient tiers: aerospace-grade steel metallurgy + sub-micron thread-grinding. Swiss GSA + Rollvis ~52% combined (each ~26%; GSA acquired Rollvis 2016), Ewellix ~14% (#3, Swedish, → Schaeffler 2022, ~€582M) — ~3 owner-groups control top spec. Tesla sources ~14 Optimus screws (2 elbow + 4 wrist + 8 leg, 500N/3,900N/8,000N) from Switzerland, ~19% of *current-prototype* robot cost at ~$1,350–2,700 each [Morgan Stanley estimate; note the 19% cost-share collapses at Tesla's ~$20k mass-production BOM target — it is a teardown figure, not a structural constant]. Chinese C0–C3 localization ~5%, C3–C5 <30% [LIMON, Tier 4 — confirmed against Chinese-securities sourcing]. **Erosion counter-case:** massive announced capacity — Beite ¥1.85B/$260M Kunshan base targeting 2.6M PRS sets/yr by 2026 [verified as a *target*]; Wuzhou Xinchun (+183% in 2025, ~50% of Rollvis cost); Hengli entering; T/CIET 965-2025 standard. **[unverified across the corpus:** "~12 suppliers meet ISO-3408 Class-3," "~5 steel foundries," ">$1.2M grinders," "~18-month backlog," per-tonne steel prices + Voestalpine/Nippon Steel/Timken attribution — all single-vendor secondary-aggregator numbers, several *misattributed* to a pmarketresearch page that carries only market-size data.] The surviving moat rests almost entirely on these weakest-sourced upstream claims.

**Rung 1 — Harmonic/strain-wave (real metallurgy wall, eroding at standard grade, #2).** The flexspline flexes elastically every input revolution (order ~0.3% strain [general engineering, not source-verified]); fatigue life sets gearbox life, so the heat-treat recipe on 40CrNiMoA/AISI-4340-class steel is the hard-to-copy IP. HDSI is the cleanest allied-listed expression but its "~70% sole share" is [unverified/contested] (QY Research: ~63% HDSI+Leaderdrive *combined*). **Erosion (verified):** Jefferies Hold→Underperform, PT ¥2,400 (Oct-15-2025), reversing its own prior Hold/¥2,800; Leaderdrive 2025 rev +47% to ¥570.7M RMB, net profit ~2x to ¥124.4M, ~30–40% of China's harmonic market [JPM], supplying Agibot/UBTech; ASPs 40–60% below Japan. **Two-sided (verified):** the compression is heavily *cyclical* — HDSI FY3/2026 orders +16.2% to ¥61.6B, backlog +13.6%, FY2027 op-income guide +141.5% to ¥6,200M, humanoid orders guided ¥2.5B with management flagging a potential 2–3x in FY3/27. TrendForce frames it as coexistence (Japan advances core components, China scales systems), not pure displacement. Longer-dated threat routing *around* the metallurgy: castable bulk-metallic-glass flexsplines [JPL/Caltech 2016 — real but ~decade-old, uncommercialized]. Net: top-spec fatigue-life seat durable; standard grade visibly commoditizing. "Han's Motion" as a named harmonic peer is [loosely sourced — the consistent Chinese names are Leaderdrive and Suzhou Green Harmonic].

**Rung 2 — RV/cycloidal (manufacturing-scale seat, largely *outside* the humanoid TAM, #3).** RV (planetary first stage + eccentric cycloidal second stage) delivers the highest rigidity/torque-density/shock tolerance — owns heavy-axis industrial joints; Nabtesco ~60% (its own medium/large-industrial estimate). **Key disconfirmer for humanoids:** RV is fundamentally an industrial story — humanoid rotary joints lean harmonic, legs use roller screws. (Correction carried across angles: RV cycloidal discs are the *shock-tolerant* option; ground-impact chiefly threatens harmonic flexsplines, not both.) "Miniature RV for humanoids" is emerging/speculative [Shuanghuan+Greatoo cited ~35% of the Asian miniature-RV-for-humanoid market — single-source, high-uncertainty]. Chinese localization real in *industrial*: Zhongda Leader + Zhenkang ~30% of Chinese-assembled industrial-robot RV by 2025; persistent gap in torsional stiffness/accuracy/fatigue, with OEMs running Chinese reducers on shortened maintenance intervals. Nabtesco (TSE 6268) is the cleanest *healthy* public reducer name (Component Solutions FY2025 sales +17.3%, op profit +103.2%; doubling RV capacity by 2026; bought Spinea).

**Rung 4 — Six-axis force-torque sensors (integration/IP, most erodible, #4).** The moat is a 6×6 calibration matrix + temperature-drift compensation + crosstalk decoupling — software/calibration, not physics. ATI sits inside Novanta ([[NOVT]], acquired 2021, $172M upfront + earnout); [[VPG]] owns the foil-strain-gauge layer (Micro-Measurements); Bota (Swiss, private) is the purest incumbent. **Three erosion vectors:** (1) *Chinese displacement* — China sold ~15,000 of ~52,000 global six-axis units in 2024 (+35% YoY), foreign-brand share "continued to decline" [Valuates/QY Research, verified verbatim]; (2) *optical designs* sidestep the strain-gauge moat — Fourier's optical-path sensor <CNY5,000 (~$710) vs ~CNY10,000 incumbent, ~half price [Yicai — self-interested seller, single-source]; (3) *sensorless design-out* — PINN-friction + UKF does whole-body torque control on the ergoCub humanoid with *no joint-torque sensors*, beating classic RNEA [arXiv 2507.10105]. **Counter-to-the-counter (the moat's survival zone):** motor-current estimation degrades on high-reduction harmonic gears — direct joint-torque sensing RMSE 0.0317 Nm vs motor-current 0.1638 Nm, a ~5× error gap [arXiv 2510.10843, verified]; swapping to real torque sensors cut part damage 2.3%→0.4%. So sensorless/optical erode the *distributed low-precision* sockets (feet, QDD wrists), not the high-reduction precision joint. A commoditizing integration layer, not a physics chokepoint.

**Demand model (Angle 9).** Optimus reference architecture: 28 body DOF = 14 rotary (harmonic) + 14 linear (roller screw) + ~4 six-axis F/T (2 wrist + 2 ankle) [verified across teardowns; the 14-harmonic count is a high-content *ceiling* — some rotary joints may use planetary gears]. At ~14+14+4 content, Goldman's ~250k humanoids by 2030 ⇒ ~3.5M roller screws + ~3.5M harmonic reducers + ~1M sensors — but GS notes those units are "almost all for industrial use," an upper bound on high-content architecture. Industrial base is the durable floor: ~542k robot installs in 2024 [IFR, verified]. **The content model is not architecture-settled** — QDD adoption could halve harmonic content and zero out roller-screw demand even as unit counts soar. Harmonic-reducer market-size figures show wide dispersion ($0.49B–$3.36B for 2024/25 depending on scope).

**Value capture (Angle 11).** Actuation is ~40–60% of humanoid BOM, but fat margin concentrates in the *deepest* rung: roller-screw GM "can reach 60%" [Tier 3, unaudited] vs Harmonic Drive's ~28% gross / single-digit operating (~4–9% TTM, verified) *despite* dominant share — owning the durable seat has NOT translated to captured profit *right now* (cyclically depressed + China localization). Integrator/OEM rungs are visibly thin (Unitree Q1 adjusted net profit −52% YoY — though revenue *rose* +68%; the drop was R&D/IPO-expense-driven, not a demand collapse). **Strongest disconfirmer:** OEM back-integration collapses the distinction — Tesla's teardown math (~$19–38k of screws today → ~$20k robot target implies 5–10× per-screw cost-down via in-house/scale) means the OEM *captures* the component margin rather than paying it [FIRGELLI/optimusk — aligned commercial incentive, read as inference not Tesla statement].

**Buyability (Angles 12–13).** All four named reducer owners are directly buyable (HDSI 6324.T, Nabtesco 6268.T, Shuanghuan SZSE 002472, Zhongda SZSE 002896). The roller-screw rung is where buyability collapses: GSA/Rollvis private, Bosch Rexroth inside private Bosch, Ewellix inside Schaeffler (SHA.DE, humanoid <1% of 2025 group sales; targeting "hundreds of millions of euros" order book by 2030, ~10% of a ~50%-of-BOM addressable market, ~45 developers, 5 contracts). Bota (F/T) also has no listed parent. US-listed candidates all diluted/nascent: **NOVT** (most real — ATI F/T; Automation-Enabling-Tech FY2025 $500.8M, 47.8% GM [verified]; but humanoid a sliver, cyclically soft); **VPG** (purest US foil-strain-gauge but immaterial — $1.0M humanoid orders Q1 2026, fourth developer in talks); **RBC Bearings** (bull framing is promotional — no humanoid disclosure in 10-Ks); Timken/Regal Rexnord/Moog absent-to-weak. Verdict: no clean US-listed expression of the durable metallurgy/grinding seat exists today.

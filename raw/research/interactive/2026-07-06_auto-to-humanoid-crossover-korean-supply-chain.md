# The auto-to-humanoid crossover: where the durable chokepoint actually sits

## 1. Verdict

The "legacy auto Tier-1s are the allied humanoid picks-and-shovels" story is **half-true and mostly mispriced at the wrong layer**. The process kinship between cars and humanoids is real — but it is *entry*, not *share*: the "~70% overlap" figure is supplier marketing (Fanzhou, Hyundai Mobis talking their own book) that conflates process steps with value, and the hard residual — small-module gear grinding, backdrivable force control, heavy-rare-earth separation, the AI brain — is exactly the ~30% that does **not** transfer. Run every crossover part through the vault's chokepoint-quality gradient and value concentrates violently at two ends: the **geology seat** (NdFeB magnets, and one layer deeper, the ~100%-China dysprosium/terbium separation that lets a magnet survive a hot actuator) and the **precision-grinding seat** (planetary roller screws from Switzerland's GSA/Rollvis, top-spec harmonic/RV reducers) — neither of which Korea owns. The Korean Hyundai/Boston Dynamics cohort is a genuine vault coverage gap and a real *allied-sourcing/trust* play, but on the evidence they cluster at actuator-**integration**, vision modules, and batteries — integrators by manufacturing scale, not chokepoint owners (only ~3 names — Hyundai Mobis, LG Innotek, Samsung SDI — are disclosure-grade; the rest of the circulated "Atlas supplier list" is analyst speculation off a Tier-5 Naver infographic). China owns the *commoditizing* middle (rotary actuators, harmonic reducers — precisely where margin is now evaporating in a 300+-model price war), not the durable seat. The honest two-sided tension the upstream thesis under-weights: Morgan Stanley/McKinsey both argue durable rent **migrates downstream** to integrators and the foundation-model "brain" (drawing $5.6B–$14B private valuations) once fleets pass ~100k units — while per-robot component revenue stays immaterial until millions of units, and a ~10× list-vs-should-cost gap says today's fat upstream margin gets competed away. Net: the durable seat is **bifurcated at the two ends of the chain** (magnets/Dy/Tb + roller-screw/top-reducer grinding upstream; the platform/brain option-value downstream at scale), the commoditizing body in the middle is China's to win-and-deflate, and the best *vault-covered* expressions of the durable ends are already held — [[MP]] (materials), [[HARMONIC]] (reducers, cyclically depressed), [[VPG]]/[[NOVT]] (sensing) — not the Korean auto Tier-1s. Timing is a ~2027-28 story, but gated on **unit scale, not the auto cycle** (the cycle only sets the option-entry price).

## 2. Cross-angle insight (second-order — reads only visible when angles combine)

**(A) The magnet chokepoint and the precision-grinding chokepoint are load-bearing in *opposite* directions on substitution — and that asymmetry is the whole thesis.** Angle 4 shows rare-earth-free motors (Niron iron-nitride, Astemo synchronous-reluctance) run ~4× larger at equal power and "won't fit inside a humanoid knee joint" — so the packaging constraint *hardens* the magnet moat inside humanoids (weaker substitution than in cars). But Angle 5 shows the reverse for the reducer rung: Tesla's reported Gen-3 substitution of cheaper C3 ball screws for planetary roller screws in some joints, plus Chinese localization of harmonic reducers, means the *middle* of the gradient is one qualified-supplier-parity event away from flattening. Reading these together: the gradient is not a smooth slope — it is a **barbell**. The two ends (geology + top-precision-grinding) are anti-fragile to volume; the middle (integration, standard reducers, hands) deflates ~40%/yr. This is why "auto Tier-1 owns 70% of the process" is a trap — the 70% they overlap with is disproportionately the deflating middle.

**(B) The same AI/HBM capacity grab that powers the vault's memory thesis is *starving the auto cycle that gates this trade's entry price* — a cross-domain link neither side prices.** Angle 12/13: AI/HBM is outbidding automakers for wafer and DRAM capacity (Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron supply ~88% of automotive DRAM, prioritizing datacenter; old-gen cockpit/ADAS DRAM +70-100% in 2026; UBS/Wells Fargo/S&P warn of auto production halts). So the "secular story at a cyclical price" (Citrini) framing has a hidden second-order twist: the auto cyclical *trough* the whole entry-price thesis rests on may not resolve as a demand recovery at all — it could be a **supply-driven snap-back** (memory shortage forcing an earlier re-rate) *or* a shortage that throttles both auto and humanoid ramps simultaneously. The datacenter buildout is quietly setting the clock on the humanoid-supplier entry point. (And TI's Q1 2026 auto being "very close to peak, a point or two below" already undercuts the deep-discount half of the pitch.)

**(C) The value-capture question inverts between chains — and that is the honest contradiction across angles.** Angles 3/4/5/8 (physics gradient) say durable value sits *upstream* at the chokepoint; Angles 9/10 (BOM math + MS Humanoid-100) say durable rent *migrates downstream* to integrators/brain at ~100k-unit scale, because per-robot content is tiny (a Figure-reported ~$150/robot line = $7.5M even at 50k units) and ASP deflates $75k→$25k. **These are not reconciled — they are a real two-sided tension.** The synthesis that survives both: upstream owns *pricing power per unit* (magnets, top reducers, roller screws keep margin); downstream owns *option-value on outsized rent* but only conditional on a scale that has not arrived. The single sharpest contradiction to flag: the drone-domain vault read ("platforms commoditize, value migrates upstream") is replicated in humanoids for the *body* — but humanoids uniquely put real durable option-value *downstream* at the foundation-model brain (Skild ~$14B, Physical Intelligence ~$5.6B), which drones do not. The upstream-only framing is cleaner for drones than for humanoids.

- **BOM denominators don't add:** dexterous hands "~31% of BOM" and actuation "40-60% of BOM" come from different unreconciled teardowns (hands are themselves actuator clusters) — do not sum.

## 3. The value chain (downstream → upstream)

**Downstream — Integrators + the "brain" (contested; option-value at scale).**
- *Integrators:* Boston Dynamics/Atlas (Hyundai-owned; implied value ~$20-21B on hype, ~4 Atlas/month today, 30,000/yr a **2028** target at a new US factory), Figure, Agility, Tesla Optimus. McKinsey: integrator margins "thin and brutal" unless they own the integrated subsystem/module.
- *Foundation-model brain (VLA):* Physical Intelligence ~$5.6B (Nov 2025; ~$11B in talks Mar 2026), Skild AI >$14B (Jan 2026 closed, SoftBank/NVIDIA/Bezos), Figure Helix, NVIDIA GR00T/Cosmos. Where private capital is betting; "demos not products at scale" caveat holds.

**Compute layer (durable-but-contested module; durable wafer beneath).**
- *Module:* NVIDIA Jetson Thor (2,070 FP4 TFLOPS, 128GB, Blackwell+Arm; "Android of robotics") vs Qualcomm Dragonwing IQ10 (~700 TOPS, CES 2026, Figure collaboration — a lower-power tier, contesting the *market* not like-for-like).
- *Wafer:* TSMC ~95% of world's robotics chips; new **N2A** node purpose-built for AVs+humanoids. Compute+perception is only ~10-20% of BOM → small dollar TAM, but the wafer is the geology-grade seat under the contested module brand.

**Sensing layer (genuine but eroding chokepoint).**
- *Six-axis force-torque:* Bota, ATI, VPG (foil-strain-gauge, "60 years of metallurgical trade secrets"), Novanta. Moat = calibration/compensation IP. Erosion: Fourier's ~½-price **optical** F/T design + automated calibration attacks the exact IP moat; sensorless current/encoder torque estimation (PINN/UKF, validated on humanoids) can *design out* discrete sensors at some joints.
- *Lidar:* SOSLAB (Korea) — **weakest link**; confirmed Hyundai MOU is for the MobED robot, *not* Atlas; Optimus (8-cam) and Fourier GR-1 (6-cam) go **vision-only, no lidar**.

**Actuation core (40-60% of BOM — the fat part; barbell of durable + commoditizing).**
- *Integrated high-torque actuators (integration rung):* Tuopu (confirmed exclusive Optimus rotary-actuator supplier; self-develops motors/controllers, **not** in-house harmonic), Sanhua, HL Mando, Hyundai Mobis, LG. Where Korea/China cluster; margin deflating.
- *Reducers (precision-grinding rung, eroding):* Harmonic/strain-wave — Harmonic Drive Systems (~85% group/global 2023, Jefferies-downgraded Oct 2025 on China-humanoid competition) vs Leaderdrive (~30-40% China). RV/cycloidal — Nabtesco (~60% precision reducers, dominates RV segment), Shuanghuan/Zhongda (China RV leaders).
- *Planetary roller screws (deepest precision-grinding wall):* GSA/Rollvis, Ewellix, Rexroth (~80% share, ISO-3408-class grinding, ~5 foundries for aerospace-grade steel, ~18-mo backlogs); Tesla sources its ~14 PRS from Switzerland, **not** China — "China's Achilles heel."
- *Frameless motors:* commoditizing, few precision suppliers.

**Materials substrate (top of gradient, geology/physics — the most durable seat).**
- *Finished NdFeB magnets:* China ~90%.
- *Heavy-RE Dy/Tb separation (the true binding layer):* China ~100% — the additive that lets a magnet run hot in a knee joint; physically upstream of magnet-sintering.
- *Only vertically-integrated Western owner:* [[MP]] (mine→separation→magnet; 10X Texas ~10,000t by 2028). Korean magnet names (Star Group, Evolution Metals) are import-fed *fabricators* relieving sintering, not separation.

**Commoditizing tail (no durable seat — where "70% auto overlap" is a mirage):** shells/covers, lamps, wiring, connectors, interior trim, chassis (~5-15% of BOM combined). Sungwoo Hitech (stamping), SL Corp (lamps) land here.

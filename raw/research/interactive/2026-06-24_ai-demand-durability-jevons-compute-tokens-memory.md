# AI demand durability & the Jevons question — compute, tokens, and memory

## 1. Verdict

AI demand is **net-elastic in aggregate dollars but price-inelastic per token** — and that distinction is the whole answer. Efficiency gains (FP4/MoE/KV-cache compression, memory-leaner system designs) have NOT compressed aggregate compute spend, because usage growth is capability-driven (agentic loops, reasoning/test-time compute, 1M-token context, a billion-plus users), not price-driven. So Jevons holds *for now*, but on a fragile footing: it depends on application-layer adoption continuing to fund the buildout, not on a self-sustaining price→volume loop. The thesis therefore rests on one falsifier — does app-layer revenue grow into the ~$2.9T capex, or does the Bain ~$800B 2030 monetization gap open. Critically, the demand-vs-efficiency debate is *moot for the chokepoints*: FP4/KV-cache compression attacks **compute**, not **HBM bandwidth**, so the hardest physical constraints (HBM, CoWoS, power/transformers) stay binding regardless of how the Jevons question resolves. The split by leg and horizon: inference (not training) is the durable demand floor; HBM-bandwidth (not commodity DRAM/NAND capacity) is the durable chokepoint; and value sits at the supply-inelastic physical chokepoints near-term, with a genuine but *contested* rotation toward inference-operators/app-layer only as the 2027-28 capacity wave lands — and only if the monetization gap closes.

## 2. Cross-angle insight (second-order findings)

**(a) The efficiency that "frees" one resource binds another — the bottleneck relocates, it never vanishes (the strongest cross-angle pattern, surfaced by Angles 1+3+13).** Three independent angles converge on the same mechanism at three different layers: (1) FP4/KV-cache compression frees HBM *capacity* but the decode phase stays HBM-*bandwidth*-bound, and freed budget is spent on bigger batches/more weights (Jevons absorption); (2) CXL disaggregation offloads KV-cache off HBM but "decode stalls once traffic shifts to the CXL tier" — it's a complementary *capacity* tier, not a bandwidth substitute; (3) at the grid layer, behind-the-meter gas relieves the interconnection queue but hits its own turbine chokepoint (GE Vernova slots into 2029-30). This is the vault's `frameworks.md` 11.12 stacked-bottleneck migration, independently re-derived from 2026 evidence. **Implication: every "efficiency solves the shortage" bear argument is structurally answered by asking "what binds next?" — and the answer is always another physical chokepoint.**

**(b) The demand-durability debate and the chokepoint-durability debate are DECOUPLED — and conflating them is the most common error.** Angles 1, 3, and 14 jointly establish that even if the bears are right that demand is price-inelastic and efficiency compresses per-workload spend, the HBM/CoWoS/power chokepoints still hold, because compression attacks compute while the chokepoints are bandwidth/physics-bound. Conversely, even if demand is relentlessly Jevons-elastic, the *GPU layer* (Angle 6's lowest-durability chokepoint) is substitution-exposed (ASIC growth +44.6% vs GPU +16.1% in 2026). So "is demand durable?" and "where does durable value sit?" are orthogonal questions — the chokepoint thesis can survive a demand disappointment, and the GPU-rent thesis can fail even in a demand boom.

**(c) CONTRADICTION — the "value rotates to the app layer / model labs" finding (Angles 9, 15) collides with the "value stays at physical chokepoints" finding (Angles 6, 13) — and the vault's resolution is horizon-dependent, not a winner.** Angle 15 carries a genuine SemiAnalysis-sourced inversion (value shifting to AI labs/inference-operators on improving unit economics); Angles 6/13 insist durable value stays at supply-inelastic physics. These are not reconcilable as a single answer — they resolve by *horizon*: chokepoints hold 0-12mo (all angles agree), the GPU-layer rent compresses first medium-term (ASIC substitution), and long-term value bifurcates into (physics/geology chokepoints) + (app-layer owning end-demand), with the commoditizing middle (raw models, merchant GPUs) thinning. The contradiction is real and should be preserved as a horizon map, not papered over.

**(d) The falsifier is the SAME signal across six angles, and it is NOT what intuition suggests.** Angles 4, 7, 11, 13 independently converge: the demand-compression falsifier is **maker book-to-bill <1.0, NOT developer build deferrals.** A developer pushing a datacenter build right while HBM/transformer makers keep full order books = the *power/memory shortage holding*, not demand destruction — so the widely-cited "~50% of 2026 builds delayed" stat *strengthens* the not-fired read rather than firing it. This is the vault's existing `transformer-supply` S172 tiered-falsifier discipline, independently re-derived.

## 3. The value chain (downstream → upstream)

**End-demand / app layer (owns the customer):** OpenAI (~$25B ARR, >40% enterprise nearing consumer parity), Anthropic (~$30B→$47B run-rate), Microsoft Copilot (~$37B AI run-rate, 20M+ seats), enterprise adopters. *The leg that must monetize into the capex — and where the Bain ~$800B gap lives.*

**Inference-operators / neoclouds (carry the build-risk):** CoreWeave, Nebius — capex 2.6-8× revenue, GPU-collateralized debt, refinancing walls. *Transfer of balance-sheet risk from hyperscalers; the "rent-side canary."*

**Hyperscaler payers (the demand backbone):** Microsoft / Alphabet / Amazon / Meta + Oracle — ~$725B 2026 capex (+77% YoY), three of four raised, ~$1.63T RPO backlog, "demand higher than available capacity."

**Compute layer (the rent at the top, substitution-exposed):** NVIDIA (~80% accelerator share, ~75% GM) + the ASIC co-designers Broadcom (~80-85% high-end ASIC share) / Marvell (~10-12%) eroding merchant-GPU share.

**Binding physical chokepoints (supply cannot flex — the durable rent):**
- **HBM bandwidth** — SK Hynix / Micron / Samsung oligopoly, sold out into 2027, ~3× DDR5 wafer-per-GB cannibalizing commodity DRAM.
- **CoWoS / advanced packaging** — TSMC + OSAT (Amkor/SPIL); fastest-healing (gap ~20%→10% by end-2026).
- **Power / transformers + GOES** — 128wk/144wk lead times, Cleveland-Cliffs sole US GOES producer; slowest to flex.

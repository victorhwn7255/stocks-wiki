## What actually happened in the telecom and fiber buildout

[Established] The setup was a convergence of deregulation, technological change, and cheap capital. The FCCŌĆÖs own summary of the Telecommunications Act of 1996 described the lawŌĆÖs aim as letting ŌĆ£anyone enter any communications businessŌĆØ and letting communications firms compete across previously separated markets. In practice, the Act opened local exchange, long-distance, and adjacent markets to new entrants just as internet usage, commercial hosting, and data networking were accelerating. [Established] Quantitatively, the boom was enormous. [Established] The demand story mattered because it gave investors and lenders an apparently objective reason to believe ŌĆ£any capacity will be filled.ŌĆØ The Department of CommerceŌĆÖs *The Emerging Digital Economy* stated in 1998 that ŌĆ£UUNET, one of the largest Internet backbone providers, estimates that Internet traffic doubles every 100 days.ŌĆØ Contemporary press coverage spread the claim widely, and Sidgmore publicly framed UUNet traffic as doubling about every three months. [Interpretation] The historical record can be reduced to five reusable mechanisms.

[Established] On outside estimates, the scale is already comparable to a major historical infrastructure cycle. [Interpretation] **Demand mis-forecast transfers, but more weakly than in telecom.** AI demand is plainly real.

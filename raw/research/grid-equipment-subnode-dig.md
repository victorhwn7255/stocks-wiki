Gradient: **geology/physics > precision-manufacturing > integration/assembly > policy/contract.** Ranked on "structural barrier that survives the order book normalizing?"

## The decisive finding — pricing power is in the GEAR, not just the metal

IEA Survey 2024 (3-0): **cable prices nearly doubled since 2019; power-transformer prices +~75% (up to 2.6× pre-pandemic), and expected to keep rising in 2025.** Meanwhile copper *stabilized* 2021-23 and aluminum rose only ~33%. So equipment prices have **DECOUPLED UPWARD** from their metal inputs — the pricing power sits in the *equipment*, not the commodity. **But:** none of the verified claims prove it survives the *delivered* glut (vs the current order-book tightness), and **Chinese GOES overcapacity is the first evidence the 1986/2014 collapse pattern can reach the gear.** That is the genuinely open question.

## Structural demand frame (why it's on-thesis)

Grid investment lags generation ~2.5-to-1 (~$400B/yr grids vs ~$1T/yr generation; generation +70% since 2015, grids <half that pace). IEA names tight transformer/cable/component markets (+ permitting) as a direct cause; >2,500 GW stalled in grid queues; **grid-component wait times have doubled in 3 years.** Data-centre electricity demand grew **17% in 2025** (vs ~3% global) and is set to double by 2030 — the same AI + electrification demand the buildout runs on.

## Where durable Western-investable value sits (structural evidence only)

At the **single-source input + oligopoly tooling** layers: Cleveland-Cliffs (sole US GOES), the gas-turbine triad (GE Vernova / Siemens Energy / Mitsubishi), the subsea-cable + vessel owners (Nexans / Prysmian / NKT / Hitachi Energy). The transformer *assemblers* and standard-cable makers capture cyclical order-book value more exposed to normalization. *(Describe-don't-recommend — names as structural evidence, no picks/targets.)*

## Scenario-weighted conclusion

- **Base (most weight):** the input/oligopoly nodes (GOES, gas turbines, subsea cable) hold pricing power through ~2028-2029 — backlogs committed, capacity gated by single-source metallurgy, OEM tooling, and vessel builds.
- **Slippage:** transformer-assembly + standard-cable pricing normalizes first as announced capacity delivers and order books flush.
- **Glut:** the 1986/2014 collapse reaches the gear — first visible already in Chinese GOES — if data-centre demand (17%/yr) decelerates, phantom grid-queue projects cancel, and OEM expansions all deliver into softening demand.
- **What would change it:** lead-time compression (the decisive normalization signal); equipment prices re-coupling *down* to metal; new Western GOES/EHV entrants reaching qualified production; data-centre demand rolling over.

## Caveats

- **Time-sensitivity:** most lead-time/deficit/price figures are Feb-Aug 2025 IEA/WoodMac; the IEA Apr 2026 update shows tightness **holding or worsening**, not normalizing.
- **The 30% transformer deficit** is a single proprietary WoodMac model figure (1-1, holds) — directional, not independently corroborated (a POWER Magazine counter-view frames it as procurement behavior vs manufacturing capacity).
- **Gas-turbine $25M slot fee** is partly a standard ~20% deposit, not pure incremental premium; OEM capacity is expanding so the demand-capacity gap narrows over time.
- **Glut question genuinely open** — verified claims establish pricing power survived capacity *announcements*, not the *delivered* glut.
- **2 claims refuted + excluded:** the Prysmian Seine-et-Marne contract-gated-line claim (1-2) and the "~20% of data-centre projects at risk" grid-binding-constraint claim (0-3).
- **Switchgear + HVDC under-covered** — lower-confidence; watch-items only.

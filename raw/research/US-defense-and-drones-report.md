# Defense & Drones: A Thematic Map of the US-Listed Unmanned Systems Universe
*Analytical decision tool, not financial advice. All figures "as of" the dates noted; current date June 3, 2026.*

## TL;DR
- **The thesis is structurally sound but unevenly priced.** Durable, bipartisan, multi-administration demand for unmanned systems, counter-UAS, and autonomy is now codified in budget law (the FY2025 reconciliation package provided $156.2B in mandatory defense funding against a $150B headline target, and the FY2027 request seeks $54.6B for the Defense Autonomous Warfare Group) and executive action (EO 14307). But the best public-market exposure sits in a thin set of US-listed names, and several pure-plays already trade at premium multiples that price in years of flawless execution.
- **The real value is in chokepoints, not platforms.** Drone airframes are commoditizing toward a ~$2,000–$5,500 unit-cost ceiling; structural pricing power sits upstream in rare-earth NdFeB magnets (China ~85–90% of finished magnets), secure FPGAs/trusted microelectronics, EO/IR sensors, high-energy-density batteries, and NDAA-compliant components. These are the links to research first.
- **Most of the investable "pure drone" universe is small, speculative, or richly valued.** The cleanest US-listed exposure is AeroVironment (AVAV) and Kratos (KTOS) among platforms, MP Materials (MP) and Teledyne (TDY) among enablers; the micro-caps (RCAT, ONDS, UMAC, ZENA, DPRO, UAVS) carry real revenue inflections but also dilution, losses, and narrative-ahead-of-financials risk. The most capable pure drone-autonomy companies (Anduril, Shield AI) are private and un-investable.

## 1. Executive Summary & Thesis Statement

**Draft thesis statement:** Over the next 1–2 decades, persistent great-power competition (US–China), active conflicts (Ukraine, Middle East/Red Sea), and a structural shift in warfare toward mass, attrition, and autonomy will sustain rising allocations to unmanned aerial systems, counter-UAS, and autonomy/AI — making these the fastest-growing sub-segments within a defense budget that is itself growing. The durable winners will be those that control scarce, hard-to-replicate inputs (rare-earth magnets, secure microelectronics, EO/IR seekers, autonomy software, NDAA-compliant supply chains) rather than commoditized airframes.

**Strongest counter-thesis / what would falsify it:**
- **Airframe commoditization destroys platform margins.** The Pentagon's explicit goal is to drive one-way-attack drones to ~$2,000–$5,500 per unit with deliberately maintained multi-vendor competition (the Drone Dominance "Gauntlet" structure). If drones become disposable commodities, platform makers earn thin, cyclical margins — value accrues only to chokepoint owners and software.
- **Budget/political risk.** The headline $54.6B FY2027 DAWG request is ~98% funded through a *future reconciliation pot* ($53.6B of $54.6B), not the base budget — making it contingent on a second reconciliation bill that may not pass if Democrats retake a chamber in the November 2026 midterms. Officials themselves call FY2027 a possible "one-year surge."
- **Execution reality vs. announcements.** Per CRS report IF12611, "Although Replicator was intended to field thousands of uncrewed systems by summer 2025, one former defense official noted that DOD had fielded only hundreds by that time"; the initiative was dissolved into DAWG/JIATF 401 in late 2025. The history of the theme is announcements outrunning delivery.
- **Valuation already-priced-in.** KTOS and AVAV have de-rated 55–58% from 2025 peaks but still trade well above industry P/S multiples; the bull case is substantially embedded.
- **De-escalation.** A durable Ukraine ceasefire and Middle East de-escalation (Operation Epic Fury ceasefire, May 2026) could slow the urgency, though analysts argue restocking depleted inventories cushions this.

**Net assessment:** The demand thesis is strong and durable on the *direction* of spending; it is weakest on *which public equities capture it.* The supply-side/chokepoint framing is the higher-conviction expression of the thesis.

## 2. Demand Drivers & Geopolitical Backdrop

### Budget trajectory (structural)
- **FY2025 reconciliation ("One Big Beautiful Bill," P.L. 119-21, signed July 4, 2025):** Per CRS IN12580, "the enacted version provided $156.2 billion in mandatory defense funding in FY2025" against the $150B headline target (CBO scored ~$149.5B net over 2025–2034). Drone-relevant pots: ~$16B for "low-cost enablers" (drones, counter-drone, cheap munitions, AI); $1B+ to expand the one-way-attack drone industrial base; $1.1B for the small-drone industrial base; $500M to prevent attritable-drone delivery delays; $500M for counter-UAS. This put total FY2025 defense spending above $1 trillion while keeping the base budget roughly flat — a deliberate structure.
- **FY2027 request (released April 3, 2026):** ~$1.5 trillion topline (largest in US history; ~44% above FY2026 appropriation per one outlet). Headline: **$54.6B requested for the Defense Autonomous Warfare Group (DAWG)** — up from $225.9M in FY2026 — but only $1B in the base budget; $53.6B in a future reconciliation pot with up to 5 years to obligate. Office of Strategic Capital loan program: ~$20.2B requested ($216M base, $20B reconciliation).
- **AI/autonomy budget line:** FY2026 marked the first year of a separately tracked ~$13.4B DoD AI-and-autonomy budget line.

### Programs of record / initiatives (the demand map)
- **Replicator → DAWG → JIATF 401.** Replicator 1 (attritable autonomous systems to counter China's mass) selected AeroVironment's Switchblade 600, Anduril's Altius-600 and Ghost-X, Performance Drone Works' C-100, and Fortem's DroneHunter F700, among others. Replicator was dissolved into DAWG in late 2025. **Replicator 2** (counter-small-UAS) folded into **JIATF 401**, the new Joint Interagency Task Force standing up under the Army/DepSecDef, reporting to Deputy Secretary Feinberg; its director has acquisition authority and can approve up to $50M per C-UAS effort. The legacy Joint C-sUAS Office (JCO) is being disestablished.
- **Drone Dominance Program (DDP):** ~$1.1B, four-phase, target of fielding hundreds of thousands (one figure: ~300,000) of weaponized one-way-attack drones by end-2027. Gauntlet I (Fort Benning, results March 2026): 25 vendors; winners Skycutter (UK, 99.3 pts), Neros (87.5), Napatree (80.3), plus Ukrainian Defense Drones, Griffon, Halo, Ascent, ModalAI, Auterion, Farage; ~$150M in initial delivery orders. Unit-cost ceilings ~$5,500 (long-range)/$4,500 (short-range), tightening toward a $2,000 Phase IV target. Gauntlet 2 qualified 48–49 companies including AeroVironment and Kratos; harder test with GPS denial, jamming, EW.
- **Counter-UAS:** Army included at least $858M for C-UAS in its FY2026 request. >350 drone detections at ~100 US military installations in the prior year. JIATF 401 selected five installations for an FY2026 NDAA directed-energy C-UAS pilot.
- **Collaborative Combat Aircraft (CCA):** Increment 1 down-selected to Anduril (YFQ-44A, first flight Oct 2025) and General Atomics (YFQ-42A, first flight Aug 2025); 100–150 aircraft over five years (~$30M/unit); Increment 1 total program est. ~$8.9B. Shield AI's Hivemind and RTX's mission-autonomy software selected for autonomy (decoupled from airframe). Increment 2 opened to nine+ vendors, potentially thousands of aircraft. Kratos' XQ-58 Valkyrie is positioned for adjacent loyal-wingman/MUX TACAIR efforts.
- **Pentagon equity stakes:** Per a WSJ report (~May 28, 2026), the Office of Strategic Capital is in talks to take debt-and-equity positions in domestic drone makers — Performance Drone Works, Unusual Machines, and Neros named as candidates — to attack the supply side directly.

### Conflict-driven demand (partly cyclical)
- **Ukraine:** Drones account for most casualties; FPV/one-way-attack drones have shifted the offense-defense balance; the US is absorbing lessons (cheap mass, EW resilience, fiber-optic-controlled drones). Ukrainian drone makers (UDD, General Cherry) are being invited into US programs and asked to stand up US manufacturing.
- **Middle East / Red Sea / Iran:** Houthi drone/missile campaigns and the 2026 Iran conflict (Operation Epic Fury; nearly 300 US troops wounded; "hundreds" of US drones involved per CENTCOM) injected urgency and exposed the cost-exchange problem (expensive interceptors vs. cheap drones). A May 2026 ceasefire could moderate urgency.

**Structural vs. cyclical read:** The budget architecture (reconciliation, OSC loans, DAWG, acquisition reform, NDAA component bans) and the bipartisan China-pacing rationale are **structural**. Conflict-driven supplementals and the FY2027 surge are more **cyclical/contingent**. The durable core is the institutional commitment to autonomous mass and domestic supply chains.

## 4. CHOKEPOINT ANALYSIS (the core)

| Chokepoint | What's scarce | Who controls it | Durability | US-listed beneficiaries |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rare-earth NdFeB magnets** | High-performance sintered NdFeB magnets, esp. with heavy rare earths (Dy/Tb) for high-temp defense motors | China ~85–90% of finished magnets and ~91% of separation/refining (IEA); one Chinese firm (JL MAG) ≈ all non-Chinese output combined; China ~100% of production equipment | **Very high.** Despite ~$2B+ US commitments, meaningful finished-magnet capacity is 2027–2028+. US output minimal today. | **MP Materials (MP)** — the leader; **USA Rare Earth (USAR)** — pre-revenue, well-funded; **Energy Fuels (UUUU)** — oxides; **American Resources (AREC)** — minority ReElement stake; Vulcan Elements (private) |
| **Secure microelectronics / FPGAs** | NDAA-compliant secure-control FPGAs, hardware root-of-trust, rad-tolerant parts | **Lattice (LSCC)** in low-power secure FPGAs; Microchip (PolarFire); AMD/Xilinx high-end. Deeper chokepoint: TSMC fabrication (Taiwan) | **High** for design/IP (defensible Western position); fab dependence on Taiwan is a geographic risk NDAA rules don't solve | **Lattice (LSCC)**, **Microchip (MCHP)**, **AMD (AMD)**, **Mercury Systems (MRCY)** |
| **Edge-AI compute / flight controllers** | NDAA-compliant, Blue UAS-listed US-assembled modules | Small ecosystem: ARK Electronics, ModalAI (private), Unusual Machines; NVIDIA Jetson (US-made SKUs) | **Moderate** — ecosystem thin but replicable | **Unusual Machines (UMAC)**; NVIDIA (NVDA) indirectly |
| **EO/IR sensors & seekers** | Cooled/uncooled thermal imagers, gimbals, seekers, AiTR | **Teledyne FLIR (TDY)** dominant in Western EO/IR; concentrated | **High** — decades of IP, export-controlled | **Teledyne (TDY)**, Leonardo DRS (DRS), RTX |
| **High-energy-density batteries** | Silicon-anode cells (450–500 Wh/kg) for endurance UAS/eVTOL; NDAA-compliant | **Amprius (AMPX)** silicon-anode; China dominates conventional Li-ion | **Moderate–high** — energy density is a real moat; FY2026 NDAA phases in battery-origin restrictions | **Amprius (AMPX)**, KULR |
| **Counter-UAS detection/EW** | Discrimination of drones in clutter/swarms; spectrum; non-kinetic defeat | Fragmented; RTX, L3Harris, Leonardo DRS, Axon/Dedrone | **High** — hard technical problem, persistent threat | **RTX, LHX, DRS, AXON, ONDS** |
| **NDAA-compliant supply chain ("Blue UAS")** | Whole-system China-free sourcing | DCMA Blue UAS list; small set of compliant vendors | **High but policy-dependent** — durable while statute holds; DoD exemption for Blue UAS expires Jan 1, 2027 | **UMAC, RCAT, AVAV, ONDS, AIRO** |

**Most important takeaway:** The rare-earth magnet and secure-microelectronics chokepoints are the most concentrated and durable, and the hardest for new entrants to replicate. The NDAA-compliant supply chain is a *policy-created* chokepoint — powerful while it lasts, but contingent on statute and the Jan 1, 2027 Blue UAS exemption expiry.

**Rare-earth detail:** China = ~69% of mining but ~91% of separation/refining and ~85–90% of finished magnets, per the IEA (Nov 2025), whose analysis notes China's "dominance is even greater in the separation and refining stages, representing about 91% of global production, with Malaysia a distant second." The Pentagon needs ~3,000–4,000 tons of specialized magnets annually, rising toward ~10,000 tons by 2030, while domestic capacity is years away. Drones are a small-volume (<3% of global NdFeB demand) but high-criticality consumer — they skew toward the most China-controlled heavy rare earths. **MP Materials–DoD deal (July 9, 2025):** per MP's 8-K/10-Q, "the DoD purchased $400 million of a newly-created series of the Company's convertible preferred stock and received a warrant to purchase up to approximately 11.2 million additional shares… initial conversion and exercise price… $30.03 per share," giving DoD a ~15% as-converted stake as the company's largest shareholder; plus a 10-year $110/kg NdPr price floor; a 10-year magnet offtake; the "10X" facility (~10,000 MT, Northlake TX, commissioning 2028); Independence facility expansion to 3,000 MT; $1B JPMorgan/Goldman financing; and a $500M Apple investment. MP produced first commercial NdFeB at Independence in December 2025.

## 6. Risks & Disconfirming Signals

1. **Budget reversal / reconciliation failure.** The FY2027 DAWG surge is ~98% reconciliation-funded; a Democratic chamber after November 2026 could block a second reconciliation bill. Watch: does FY2027 funding land in the base budget or vanish?
2. **Peace / de-escalation.** Durable Ukraine ceasefire + Middle East calm reduces urgency (partly offset by restocking).
3. **Airframe commoditization.** Sub-$2,000 unit-cost targets + deliberate multi-vendor competition compress platform margins. Value migrates to chokepoints/software.
4. **China supply weaponization.** April/October 2025 rare-earth export controls already spiked prices; further weaponization helps US magnet/component names but hurts any company still dependent on Chinese inputs (note Lattice's 64% Greater China revenue).
5. **Tech obsolescence / EW.** Gauntlet 2's GPS-denial and jamming tests show platforms can be made obsolete quickly; autonomy/EW-resilience is the moat.
6. **Valuation / priced-in.** KTOS (P/E ~289x, P/S ~8x, negative FCF 2026E) and AVAV (~6.7x P/S, ~49% DCF-overvalued per one model, securities litigation) bake in heavy optimism. Micro-caps trade at 20–80x sales.
7. **Policy chokepoint expiry.** The DoD Blue UAS exemption expires Jan 1, 2027 with no guaranteed renewal — a regulatory cliff for NDAA-compliant names.
8. **Promotional / governance risk.** Several micro-caps (ZENA, DPRO, RCAT's internal-control weakness, UAVS warrant-driven "income") show narrative ahead of financials.

## 7. Open Questions & Prioritized Research Agenda

**Top US-listed companies to deep-dive first (and why):**
1. **MP Materials (MP)** — purest play on the most durable chokepoint (NdFeB magnets); DoD-backstopped economics; but verify how much *drone* demand vs. EV/wind drives the thesis and whether 2028 10X timing is de-risked.
2. **AeroVironment (AVAV)** — most complete US-listed pure-play (platforms + C-UAS + autonomy + loitering munitions); dig into BlueHalo integration, margins, backlog conversion, and the securities litigation.
3. **Kratos (KTOS)** — best large-UAS/CCA-adjacent exposure (Valkyrie); but stress-test the valuation and Valkyrie rate-production timing.
4. **Lattice Semiconductor (LSCC)** — secure-FPGA chokepoint + AI/datacenter overlap; investigate defense revenue mix vs. China exposure.
5. **Teledyne (TDY)** — EO/IR sensor chokepoint + loitering-munition (Rogue 1/LASSO); a diversified, profitable way to own the sensor layer.
6. **Ondas (ONDS)** — most aggressive C-UAS/autonomy platform build-out; verify organic vs. acquired revenue and backlog quality.
7. **Amprius (AMPX)** — high-energy-density battery chokepoint; confirm defense/UAS revenue share and the EBITDA inflection.
8. **Red Cat (RCAT) / Unusual Machines (UMAC)** — the two highest-profile small-drone/components inflection stories; watch SRR/Drone Dominance order conversion, OSC equity outcomes, dilution, and (for RCAT) internal-control remediation.

**Chokepoints to deep-dive first:**
1. **Rare-earth NdFeB magnets** (MP, USAR, UUUU, AREC) — most concentrated, most durable, best-funded.
2. **Secure microelectronics / FPGAs** (LSCC, MCHP, AMD, MRCY) — high-value, defensible, AI-overlapping; but Taiwan fab dependence.
3. **EO/IR sensors & seekers** (TDY, DRS) — concentrated, export-controlled.
4. **Counter-UAS detection/EW** (RTX, LHX, DRS, AXON, ONDS) — large under-served demand; hard problem.
5. **High-energy-density batteries** (AMPX) — real energy-density moat; NDAA battery rules phasing in.

**Open questions:**
- Does the FY2027 DAWG funding survive the midterms and land in the base budget?

## Layer 1 CPO timing divergence callout *(v2 addition)*

Per vault Sessions 17 + 22 + 27 + 30 cumulative analysis: the three Layer 1 / Framework 2.6 control-point names exhibit structurally opposed CPO positions as of April 2026 baseline. This divergence is sharper than any single-row "adoption timing uncertain" hedge captures.

| Layer 1 name | Framework 2.6 tier | CPO position | Source attribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NVDA** | Full control point | **Production adoption.** Spectrum-X CPO "in full production"; COUP/COUPE packaging co-developed with TSM; Spectrum-6 "world's first co-packaged optical" switch; Kyber CPO scale-up | NVDA GTC March 16, 2026 (Jensen rhetorical claims attributed) |
| **AVGO** | Control point with bottleneck participation | **Active dismissal.** CPO characterized as "bright, shiny objects" while claiming leadership ("we are the lead in CPOs"); Bailly platform never named in three primary sources; copper DAC scale-up advocacy | AVGO Q1 FY2026 call (Hock Tan) |
| **MRVL** | Bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations | **Quantified projection.** $500M FY2028 / $1B FY2029 annualized run rate targets; Photonic Fabric chiplets co-packaged into XPUs and scale-up switches | MRVL Q4 FY2026 call (Murphy) |

Three Layer 1 platform-definer names with three structurally distinct CPO positions does not flatten into "adoption timing uncertain." Either Murphy's bifurcation thesis is correct (scale-out limited / scale-up real) and AVGO's copper-scale-up advocacy is internally consistent with eventual scale-up CPO; or Murphy's framing misread the architecture and AVGO's dismissal proves directionally right; or NVDA's production lead captures architectural control before competitors converge. Framework treats CPO timing as falsifiable between these three scenarios.

## Framework 2.6 control-point gradation — orthogonal axis *(v2 addition)*

Switch ASIC / platform architecture row participants are not equivalent. Per `raw/notes/frameworks.md` v8 Framework 2.6 (Control-point analysis, codified at vault Session 24 selective integration), they sit on a **five-tier gradation that is orthogonal to the chokepoint structure**. Layer placement (chokepoint participation) captures *where* in the value chain a company operates; control-point analysis captures *whether* the company shapes architectural decisions affecting the broader chain.

| Control-point tier | Vault company | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Full control point** | NVDA | CUDA, NVLink, Spectrum-X, Quantum-X define ecosystem direction; CPO/LPO/NPO transitions move on NVDA's roadmap |
| **Control point with bottleneck participation** | AVGO | Tomahawk switch architecture, custom XPU programs, Bailly CPO platform decisions; Layer 1/3 straddling tension |
| **Bottleneck-tier with control-point aspirations** | MRVL, CRDO | MRVL: Photonic Fabric (Celestial AI $1.3B-$5.5B) is genuine platform-tier ambition; aspiration real, not yet realized. CRDO: vertically integrated system-level model + CPO displacement positioning at smaller operational scale than MRVL |
| **Bottleneck participant with platform-tier ambition** | CSCO, ANET | CSCO: Silicon One + Acacia capability is real; acknowledged-deferral CPO position. ANET: EOS + NetDL software platform + ESUN founding member; bounded by single merchant silicon vendor dependency (likely AVGO Tomahawk per ANET 10-K Risk Factors Summary) |
| **Pure bottleneck participant** | All other vault companies | Supply-side scarcity is real; architectural authority is not |

**How this changes the framework reading.** The Switch ASIC / platform architecture row "Best listed exposure" listing (NVDA, AVGO, ANET, CSCO, MRVL) flattens five structurally distinct control-point positions. NVDA's full control point is not equivalent to ANET's bottleneck-participant-with-platform-tier-ambition. Component chokepoints (other rows) and control-point gradation are orthogonal: a company can be a pure bottleneck participant in component chokepoints (Tier 1-2 photonics) while exercising no architectural control. Reading the framework benefits from displaying both axes.

**Empirical evidence base:** Per-company application documented in vault `wiki/companies/NVDA.md` + `wiki/companies/AVGO.md` + `wiki/companies/MRVL.md` + `wiki/companies/CSCO.md` + `wiki/companies/ANET.md` + `wiki/companies/CRDO.md` Thesis role sections (Sessions 24 + 27).

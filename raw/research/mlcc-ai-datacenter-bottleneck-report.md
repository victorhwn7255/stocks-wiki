## VERDICT (one paragraph)

High-end AI-server MLCC is a **quality-but-cyclical position with one genuinely structural kernel — not (yet) a memory-style durable chokepoint, and more than a pure theme-spike.** The verifiable core is real: AI racks need vastly more, harder-to-make capacitors than single servers (Samsung Electro-Mechanics' own newsroom says AI servers use >10× a general-purpose server's MLCCs); the top-bin parts (small-case, high-capacitance, low-ESL, embedded-in-substrate) sit behind a real materials + process + qualification moat held by 2–3 makers; Murata's own FY2026 guidance — the cycle's best primary evidence — projects **server-related capacitor sales +85–90% YoY**; and SEMCO runs at ~99% utilization with next year's output reportedly sold out. But adversarial verification materially weakened the bull narrative's two loudest numbers: the famous **"Murata 15–35% MLCC price hike" is likely a conflation** (the officially-confirmed April-1 action covered non-MLCC parts; Murata's own deck guides only +5–10% capacitor ASP, mix-driven, and assumes a company-wide price *decline*), and the **"440,000 MLCCs per rack" figure traces only to anonymous brokerage field research**. Meanwhile TrendForce documents channel double-booking in the consumer tier today — the exact mechanism that turned the 2017–18 spike into the 2019 glut (Yageo revenue −34.5%). The honest framing: the **top-bin AI-server niche deserves chokepoint-watch status with pre-registered falsifiers**; the "new memory" framing is a Goldman Sachs narrative to carry under strict Tier-3 attribution. The cycle has memory's *story*; so far it has only one quarter of memory's *evidence*.

## Part 1 — Why MLCC matters to AI datacenters (the technical case)

**The role.** MLCCs are passive stabilizers in the power-delivery network (PDN): they store charge, smooth voltage, and filter noise. For AI silicon the critical function is **decoupling** — GPU load swings (training bursts, token generation, HBM access, NVLink traffic) cause near-instant current spikes; the voltage regulator is electrically "far away" (traces, vias, planes all have resistance + inductance), so chip-adjacent capacitors act as micro-reservoirs that bridge the microsecond gap. Without adequate decoupling: voltage droop, downclocking, errors, crashes. (Murata 0603-100μF release, Business Wire, 2024-07-25 — confirmed-primary.)

**System-level, not chip-level.** Murata's own AI-server PDN guide structures the chain as rack PDU/bus converters → power shelves → board DC-DC/point-of-load → chip-adjacent rails, prescribing "a hierarchy of bulk, intermediate and high-frequency decoupling" — MLCCs at every stage, with the chip-adjacent high-frequency parts the hardest and highest-value (Murata guide via Passive Components EU, 2026-02-04 — confirmed-primary via press). TDK's datacenter app note specifies 630V+-rated MLCCs in PSU/IBC stages (TDK product site — confirmed-primary, partial access).

**The usage jump (single box → rack).** Verified anchors: a standard enterprise server uses ~1,000 MLCCs; **AI servers use >10× a general-purpose server** (Samsung Electro-Mechanics newsroom, 2025-07-21 — confirmed-primary as a company claim); Murata says an AI server uses "10–20× a smartphone" (~10–25k per node). Per-tray ~20–30k × 18 trays reconciles with the widely-circulated **">400,000 per GB200/GB300 NVL72 rack"** — but that figure traces only to anonymous brokerage field research via Korean/Chinese media; the seed video's "~100k/rack" matches a GPU-boards-only count (≈1,500/GPU × 72 ≈ 108k). **No teardown, paper, or component-maker unit count exists. Use ranges and name the unit (per-node vs per-rack).** Per-GPU escalation (H100 ~200 → GB200 ~1,500 → Rubin ~5,000) is single-source (BigGo, 2026-06-06).

**Dollar content per rack (attribute to bank).** Morgan Stanley estimates MLCC content ~$1,530/rack (GB300) → **~$4,320/rack (Vera Rubin VR200 NVL72), +182%** (Korea Herald + Passive Components EU, both 2026-06-02 — confirmed-secondary as an MS estimate; VR200 is unshipped, so this is channel work on a future product). Sanity check: $1,530 ÷ ~440k units ≈ $0.0035/unit — internally consistent. **Perspective the bulls omit: even $4,320 is <0.1% of a ~$7.8M rack** — "3rd-largest cost item" (Goldman) can only be true as a BOM line-item ranking among passives/components, not as a dollar-weight claim like HBM (~25% of rack cost per MS).

**NVIDIA 800V HVDC — primary-confirmed.** NVIDIA announced the 800 VDC architecture on its developer blog (2025-05-20): transition "starting in 2027," aligned to **Kyber rack systems (576 Rubin Ultra GPUs)**, to support 1 MW racks — 800V carries ~85% more power per conductor and cuts copper ~45%. Partner list (Infineon, TI, ADI, ST; Delta, Eaton, Schneider, Vertiv) is primary; the Navitas collaboration release is SEC-filed (8-K exhibit, 2025-05-21). **Implication for MLCC:** components on high-voltage stages need correspondingly rated parts (TDK already specs 630V+ parts) — but no capacitor maker has quantified incremental 800V-driven MLCC demand; that link is engineering inference. Note: 800V is the *distribution* stage; the chip-adjacent volume story is on the low-voltage side. Complementary claims, not the same claim.

**Why high-end parts specifically — all four seed product datapoints verified at primary:**
- Murata **0603 100μF** (world-first; servers/datacenters/IT) — announced 2024-07-25.
- Murata **0402 47μF** world-first mass production — 2025-07-10.
- Taiyo Yuden **embeddable 1005/0402 22μF** for AI-server IC power lines — mass production Aug 2025.
- Taiyo Yuden **embeddable 2012/0805 100μF** — mass production Nov 2025 (announced 2025-12-02).

The hard part: barium-titanate dielectric at ~100nm powder class, stacked as ~2μm layers up to ~1,000 layers, nickel inner electrodes, sintering without cracking or reducing the ceramic — yield is the chokepoint. Ultra-high-cap AI parts reportedly run **~40% yields and ~50+-day cycles**, which is why AI servers consume only 2–3% of global MLCC units but ~10% of capacity (CICC via BigGo, 2026-06-06 — single-source).

**The embedded-MLCC trend (the most chokepoint-like sub-direction).** Capacitors buried in the package substrate, under/next to the die, to cut loop inductance — the same "move the critical part next to the chip" logic as co-packaged optics. Two Taiyo Yuden embeddable families are in mass production (2025, primary); TDK embeds thin-film capacitors in substrates (primary); SEMCO is reportedly building an MLCC-embedded substrate line in Vietnam for AI packaging (DigiTimes, 2026-04-14 — single-source). Direction confirmed multi-vendor; "requirement for next-gen AI packages" status — unverified.

## Part 2 — The "new memory" thesis, tested

**The Goldman Sachs framing (late May 2026; attribute, never as fact).** GS analysts (Nelson Armbrust, Allen Chang, named in coverage) called MLCC the 3rd-largest AI-server BOM item after GPU + memory; forecast AI-server MLCC demand **~4.3× FY2025→FY2030** (~34% CAGR) against industry capacity growth "locked at ~10%/yr" (equipment + materials developed in-house, engineer-limited); sized AI-server MLCC at **~$1.3B inside a ~$15B market**; and (in a separate May 27 note after meeting Murata's president) extended the cycle to **~2030** vs the market's prior ~2028, describing a **"price-reset" mechanism** — each annual NVIDIA platform refresh needs new higher-spec part numbers at higher prices, a structurally upward pricing trend without hard hikes on identical parts. (Coverage: cls.cn, Sina, 21jingji, Tencent News, May 31–Jun 1; BigGo for the May 27 note — all confirmed-secondary.)

**What survived adversarial verification (the evidence table is Part 5):**
- ✅ SEMCO ~99% utilization (Q3/Q4 2025), next year's MLCC output reportedly sold out, long-term binding supply agreements with AI big-tech (Seoul Economic Daily 2025-12-10 + SEMCO Q1 2026 call — confirmed).
- ✅ Murata's FY2026 guidance: server-related capacitor sales **+85–90% YoY**; capacitor ASP +5–10% (mix-driven); ~¥80B added capacitor capex (~¥40B inside FY2026's ¥250B + ~¥40B FY2027) — **the single best primary evidence in the whole thesis** (Murata earnings speech PDF, 2026-04-30).
- ✅ Taiyo Yuden price increases from May 1, 2026 (customer notice via trade press; TrendForce sizes at 6–13%, consumer/low-mid grades).
- ✅ Lead-time stretch 4→20 weeks (DigiTimes), up to 24–40 weeks on high-cap lines (multiple outlets); Murata president: high-end inquiries ≈ 2× capacity.
- ⚠️ **CONTESTED — the headline "Murata 15–35% MLCC hike effective April 1":** the *officially-confirmed* April-1 hike covered **four non-MLCC categories** (ferrite beads, power/RF inductors, common-mode chokes; silver-cost-driven — TrendForce 2026-03-17). The 15–35% *MLCC* version appears only as reported private customer notices in Chinese media (no source document); TradingKey (2026-05-03, *after* the effective date) states Murata "has not yet officially adjusted its prices"; and Murata's own Apr-30 deck guides just +5–10% capacitor ASP while assuming a **¥73B company-wide price decline** — zero instances of "price hike" in the prepared remarks. A narrow AI-bin hike could mathematically coexist with the blended guidance, but the claim is **ahead of the evidence**. Carry as "reported, unconfirmed; Murata prices via private notices."
- ❌ **UNVERIFIED — Japan MOF April 2026 export stats** (+16% YoY price / +28% value / +10% volume): exists only as GS's citation relayed through media. The triplet is internally consistent (1.16 × 1.10 ≈ 1.28) so it plausibly comes from real HS-8532.24 data, but it needs a manual customs.go.jp / e-Stat pull before any page treats it as government-confirmed. (Beware: the most search-visible "corroboration," newswitch.jp's ¥0.63/unit piece, is from May **2022**.)

## Part 3 — The HBM-oligopoly comparison

**Where the analogy holds:** (a) concentration at the high end — the AI-grade segment is reported as a Murata+SEMCO near-duopoly (~90% combined per Korean press; Murata's president claims ">50% of advanced MLCC"); (b) a real, vertically-integrated process moat (sub-100nm in-house powder, ~1,000-layer stacking at yield, in-house equipment) — the same in-house-everything structure behind GS's ~10%/yr capacity ceiling; (c) qualification lock-in — parts are designed into the PDN with vendor sim models (Murata SimSurfing); switching after board qualification is costly, analogous to HBM qualification on a GPU package; (d) both ride the same NVIDIA platform cadence, with content-per-rack rising each generation.

**Where it breaks (this decides durability):** (a) **the long tail** — HBM has three makers on earth; MLCC has a second tier (Yageo, Walsin, TDK, Kyocera) and a Chinese tier (Fenghua, Three-Circle) already mass-producing "AI high-capacity" parts for domestic Chinese servers — the substitution frontier is far closer; (b) **dollar stakes** — an HBM stack transforms its maker's P&L (HBM ≈ 25% of a Rubin rack's cost); MLCC at $1.5–4.3k/rack is <0.1% of rack cost and AI-server MLCC (~$1.3B) is <10% of the ~$15B industry — buyers have little incentive to defend the oligopoly the way GPU roadmaps defend HBM; (c) **capacity response is faster** — MLCC expansion is buildings + presses + kilns on known process (Murata's Izumo building completed April 2026, capacity ramping Q4 2026; SEMCO's third Philippines plant produces H2 2027) vs HBM's leading-edge-DRAM + TSV gating, so supply answers in 12–24 months, not 3+ years; (d) **no generational lockout** — HBM4-vs-HBM3e creates hard gates; MLCC progress is continuous, so trailing players are always partially substitutable on the less-extreme parts of the BOM.

**Net:** directionally fair for the **top-bin chip-adjacent and embedded parts**; weak for the rest of the rack BOM, where the tail can and will absorb demand.

## Part 4 — The bear case (argued as hard as the bull)

1. **The 2017–18 rhyme is uncomfortable — and verified.** Standard-spec MLCC spot prices rose **5–10×** in the 2017–mid-2018 super-cycle (contract rises ~35–45%); the 2019 correction was severe — **Yageo 2019 revenue −34.5%** as hoarded channel inventory unwound; oversupply persisted into 2020 (Smith retrospective; Taipei Times 2019-12-09 — confirmed). Every element of that cycle — hikes, spot surges (~20% in early 2026), lead-time blowouts — is present now.
2. **Double-booking is documented RIGHT NOW.** TrendForce's May 18, 2026 press release (verified): Taiwan/China distributors preemptively stocking consumer-grade X5R parts; "**declining actual orders from ODMs alongside surging orders from channels**"; explicit double-booking risk; SEMCO weighing agent price hikes partly to curb hoarding. Aggregate MLCC ASPs were still *falling* as of early May (decline narrowed to <0.5%) — the boom is narrow; part of the "shortage" is hoarding, not consumption.
3. **The capacity response is already funded and dated.** Murata: Izumo building complete (April 2026, ¥47B), capacity out Q4 2026, + ~¥80B AI-capacitor program. SEMCO: third Philippines plant → H2 2027. Walsin: Taiwan acquisitions + Malaysia plant. Yageo: AI already 10–15% of revenue, capex pivoting. Fenghua: Xianghe high-end base completed April 2026. The supply wave lands **H2 2027–2028**; if AI-server unit growth disappoints, 2028 looks like 2019. GS's "~10%/yr capacity" is an industry-wide average — high-end-specific additions are growing much faster.
4. **The Chinese/Taiwanese catch-up is closer than the bull admits.** Three-Circle reportedly mass-produces AI high-capacity MLCC for domestic Chinese servers (1μm dielectric layers); Japanese price hikes are already pushing auto customers to domestic makers (orders reportedly doubled). They are not at Murata's frontier and NVIDIA-chain qualification is a real gate — but domestic Chinese AI servers are a parallel demand pool needing no Western qualification. Plausible 2nd-tier path to genuinely AI-grade parts: ~2–3 years — far shorter than anyone's path to HBM.
5. **The demand second-derivative turns down.** Hyperscaler capex ~$700B+ in 2026 (+~50–64% YoY) decelerates on consensus to ~13% (2027) and ~5% (2028) (CreditSights; Moody's via MUFG). Capacity ordered against 2026's ~80% AI-MLCC growth arrives into that deceleration. Content-per-rack growth (the MS $4,320 figure) partially offsets — but that figure is unconfirmed channel work on an unshipped product.
6. **The narrative arrived before the revenue.** GS's notes moved the Chinese MLCC sector +9% in a day; Murata's stock roughly doubled in a year while reported profits barely rose. AI-server MLCC is ~$1.3B of ~$15B (GS's own number); even 4× by 2030 leaves AI a minority of industry revenue. The "new memory" framing borrows HBM's narrative without (yet) HBM's revenue transformation.

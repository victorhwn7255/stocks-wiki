## What DID verify (gallium / chelating-resin extraction technology — 2 claims, both 2-0)

**1. China has policy-toggled the gallium EXTRACTION TECHNOLOGY itself** (not just the metal). CSIS (primary): China added "technologies for extracting gallium metal from alumina mother liquor using ion-exchange or resin methods" to its export controls — the third escalation step:
- Jul 3 2023 — MOFCOM/Customs Announcement No. 23: gallium/germanium export *licensing*.
- Dec 25 2024 — functional *ban* on gallium/germanium/antimony exports to the US (extraterritorial).
- Jan 2 2025 — added the **ion-exchange/resin extraction *technology*** to the Catalogue of Technologies Prohibited/Restricted from Export. *(Jan 2 was a draft; the legally-effective MOFCOM+MOST amendment took effect Jul 15 2025 — but "exports of the high-performance resin have ceased entirely" since early 2025.)*
- → confirms the resin/ion-exchange tech is the **technology-IP-layer target** of the controls.

**2. China's gallium dominance is defended by a documented market-FLOOD deterrent.** CSIS (primary): the ~2010-onward low-purity output surge "forced most of the leading non-Chinese suppliers out of the business over the following decade," and "if alternative suppliers appear to be ramping up production, China could suddenly flood the market, cratering prices and erasing profits." USGS corroborates: China's low-purity gallium output rose 22 MT (2005) → 444 MT (2015); primary producers ceased — Hungary 2013, Kazakhstan 2015, Germany 2016, the UK — leaving China ~98-99% by 2024.

## The honest moat caveat (load-bearing)

The two verified claims confirm gallium-extraction-tech is a **real, hard chokepoint AND a new-page candidate** — BUT they also cap its durability, and the caveat is the point:
- The dominance is **partly a STRUCTURAL byproduct-cost advantage**, not pure predation: China is ~60% of global aluminum output → integrated bauxite/alumina byproduct streams + proprietary low-cost extraction resins. A genuine low-cost floor makes the flood deterrent *more* credible, not less.
- The underlying **resin chemistry is decades-old, Western-origin (Rhône-Poulenc), public-domain IP** *(from the unverified/abstained claims — directional, treat as low-confidence)*. So the moat rests on **manufacturing scale + byproduct-aluminum cost position + export policy — NOT non-replicable secret chemistry.**
- Net: this is a **policy-toggled, flood-defended, cost-advantaged** node. High on hardness-to-replicate *today*, but its pricing power is the kind that a determined, subsidized Western response could eventually erode — exactly the "policy/contract tier, not geology/physics tier" placement the chokepoint-quality gradient predicts.

A page would have to lead with the honest moat caveat above (policy + scale + cost, not secret IP), or it would over-claim.

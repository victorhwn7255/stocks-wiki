## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Goeckeler talks in long, low-key, conversational runs stitched with "You know," and "I mean," — the opposite of a scripted CEO. Sentences pile up as thinking-out-loud, then land on a flat plain-spoken verdict: "It's happening. We're still in the early stages." Visoso is tighter, list-ordered, "I will begin with… Moving on to…".
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — "You know," (dozens of times — the dominant filler), "quite frankly" (his tell for a candid aside), "Look," to reset before a real answer, "I mean," to soften. Doubled intensifiers: "very, very strong", "very, very substantial", "literally moving every single day", "literally hour over hour". "Spectacular" is his hype word — attached to customers ("their spectacular businesses"), applied ~6× (STRIP target). "Cyclicality… it's corrosive" repeated as a refrain.
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Business-model / factory-economics, not physics. "We run a fab. We have very consistent output. We need very consistent consumption." Borrows the language of other industries deliberately: "techniques that people associate with… recurring revenue models", "we're not reinventing the wheel." "Steady as she goes" for a roadmap. The recurring frame: aligning "our business model and our customer's business model."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers warmly and at length; often over-answers, then says so ("probably maybe took a little longer answer than you were looking for"). Greets analysts by first name ("Hey, Mark. It's good to hear from you"; "First of all, welcome. We're glad you're here"). When pushed on skeptics, he confronts it head-on but calmly: "There's a lot of sometimes talk in the market that they won't hold, they won't have teeth… I can tell you nothing can be further from the truth."
5. **Bad-news / softer-market delivery** — Owned flatly, then reframed forward: on units declining outside data center — "we're seeing some contraction in the market just because of unit decline. That's to be expected, although we expect to see that bounce back in 2027." Names the adjustment ("there's no doubt there's an adjustment process right now") rather than burying it.
6. **Hedging style** — Personal-epistemic and time-stamped, not statistical: "as kind of our internal view of the world", "it's early in the quarter, and it's an extremely dynamic market", "it pays to be a bit conservative when you're going down that path", "we'll see", "still a little bit early." Growth numbers hedged as a moving target: raised "to the mid-70s from where we were in the 60s just three months ago… up from the 40s three months before that."
7. **Refusals** — Frequent and courteously named. Goeckeler on margins: "I don't think we're there yet to talk about that, Ben." On customer names, Visoso: "We're not gonna disclose the names of our customers." On average contract duration: "Frankly, we're not, we cannot get into that level of detail. I apologize for that, Victor." On per-contract pricing: "Sorry, I'm gonna frustrate you a little bit. We can't go into pricing in detail." The refusal formula = apologize lightly, decline plainly, redirect to a metric they WILL give (RPO).
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Goeckeler carries the conviction and the "change the industry" narrative; Visoso carries the figures in ordered blocks ("Revenue for the third quarter was $X, up Y sequentially and Z year-over-year… non-GAAP gross margin was… This compares favorably to our guidance of…") and delivers the "compares favorably to guidance" beat repeatedly. The account speaks figures in HIS register: ordered, "compares favorably", plainly bounded, and prefaced with what it's measured against.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: "You know," / "I mean," conversational fillers · "quite frankly" candid-aside marker · "Look," reset · first-name analyst warmth · the fab-economics frame ("consistent output needs consistent consumption") · the "cyclicality is corrosive" refrain (as posture, not as a fact) · owns-then-reframes bad news · time-stamped hedges ("early in the quarter", "we'll see") · the courteous named refusal ("I apologize for that", "we can't go into that") · Visoso's "compares favorably to guidance" ordered number-blocks.
- STRIP: "spectacular" (×6) and "extraordinary" · "phenomenal / fantastic / standout" (analyst-and-management hype) · "very, very, very good" superlative stacking · missionary flourish ("fundamentally change the way this business has worked", "everybody wins", "a lot of fun for us") except where the page itself carries the framing as a verified quote · any "best-in-class / gold standard" product bragging.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Tobolski loops. Long qualifying runs stacked with hedge-clauses and "kind of / sort of," rarely snapping shut; a thought keeps widening rather than landing. "As we look at overall constraints, and really map out, the sort of rocks that are in the way from accelerating revenue growth, coils obviously are a conversation that we continue to invest to expand our capacity." The closers are soft, not hard ("We've got headroom."; "All in all, we expect to see consistent improvement…").
2. **Signature vocabulary & verbal tics** — Heavy hedge-softeners: "kind of" and "sort of" appear constantly, even on firm numbers ("rough napkin math," "rough order of magnitude," "in the ballpark"). Openers: "Yeah." / "Yes. I mean,…" almost every answer. First-name warmth to every analyst ("Good morning, Ryan, and thanks for the question"; "Good morning, Noah. Great question."). Recurring nouns: "value proposition," "throughput," "capacity," "backlog," "book-to-bill," "share gains," "operating leverage," "embedded" (things are always "embedded in backlog," "embedded in the investments," "embedded in the guide"). "Really" as an all-purpose intensifier.
3. **Metaphor habitat** — The factory floor and the constraint. His signature line: "manufacturing is a world of uncovering the constraints. No matter how much you solve one problem, you move it to the next one." Talks in "rocks in the way," "headroom," "the big lifts," "pull the accelerator," "backup house," "fuel the growth." Everything is a throughput/absorption/utilization physics problem — never a market or a story.
4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers directly but drowns the point in operational detail. When Merkel pressed on the ~500bp Oklahoma margin gap, Tobolski enumerated the drivers ("first off… beyond that… a little bit of price-cost… a little bit of diluted nature from the tariff") and connected each to a plant decision. Never combative; grants the analyst's framing ("you're not going to be surprised by my first question" → "Yeah… just to clarify…"). Reframes a "miss" as a deliberate choice, not a shortfall.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned, then reframed as an intentional trade-off "made from a position of strength." Margin compression is never buried: it's named (25.1%, down 170 bps), attributed to three things (outsourcing, unabsorbed Memphis fixed cost, tariff/inflation), and immediately labeled "temporary," "transitory," "timing," "not a structural reset." The word "intentional" carries the whole bad-news posture: "intentionally prioritizing growth… over near-term margin maximization."
6. **Hedging style** — Directional and range-based, not statistical. "We anticipate seeing…," "rough order of magnitude… in the ballpark," "a little bit of," "directional margin improvement." Guidance comes as a band (40–45% sales growth; 27–28% gross margin) with the caveat attached. Seasonality hedges are constant ("a little bit of question mark in Q4 on normal seasonality").
7. **Refusals** — Declines to break out plant-level revenue flatly but warmly. On Memphis: "We don't have Memphis explicitly called out in terms of that breakout of revenue, but what I would say is…" — refuses the number, gives direction instead. Customer names never disclosed; diversification discussed only in the abstract ("we love the customer base that we have, but we also wanna be very intentional about diversification… spread out the overall concentration risk"). No chip-vendor / partner names ever surface, and no analyst asks.
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Cheung (new, first call) is tighter and more structured than Tobolski: opens with warm self-introduction ("nearly 30 years… industrial HVAC space with a consistent focus on driving operational efficiency"), then walks numbers in clean sequence ("First quarter net sales increased 54%… gross margin was 25.1%… down 170 basis points… SG&A… declined 220 basis points to 13.7%"). His signature move is the enumerated priority list: "I see three things as really important. One definitely is the margin discipline… Second… cash generation… Lastly… the finance function." The account speaks figures in HIS register: sequenced, bps-precise, leverage-and-cash framed.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the "kind of / sort of" hedge-softeners on everything · "rough napkin math / rough order of magnitude / in the ballpark" estimating register · first-name warmth to challengers · the constraint/throughput factory metaphor ("manufacturing is a world of uncovering the constraints") · "embedded in the backlog / embedded in the investments" construction · "intentional / temporary / transitory / timing, not a structural reset" as the bad-news frame · direction-not-number refusal ("we don't break that out, but what I'd say is…") · Cheung's enumerated-priorities + bps-precise number register for figures.
- STRIP: "incredibly strong / exceptionally strong / robust / strong" saturation (the word "strong" appears dozens of times — thin it hard) · "record" stacking · analyst-echoed hype ("That's incredible," "congrats on the quarter" — those are the analysts', not the company's) · "exceptional growth trajectory" and forward-cheerleading adjectives · any framing that makes a hedged band ("40–45%") sound like a floor.

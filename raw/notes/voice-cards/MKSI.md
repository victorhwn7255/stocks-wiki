## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Conversational, meandering, self-qualifying runs, not clipped. A thought circles back on itself and softens as it goes, padded with "you know" and doubled "I think": "I think in general, I think that is probably the case." Sentences trail off into concessions — "and then some", "if at all", "et cetera, but right now". The CFO is the opposite: short, sequenced, declarative.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Lee)** — Near-universal answer opener: crediting the question — "That is a great question, Jim." / "That's a great question, Matt." / "it's a good question, Michael." Greets every analyst by name and closes the same way ("Good morning, Steve." … "Thanks, Steve."). Softeners everywhere: "you know" (constant filler), "certainly", "of course", "I would say this,", "I believe", "our understanding is". Reaches for "puts and takes". STRIP targets: "outstanding start", "excellent position", "excellent first quarter", "very excited", "really happy", "great grower", "robust", "remarkable".
3. **Enumeration / structure habits** — Prefers small announced pairs over long taxonomies: "There are two drivers. One is… The other is…"; "there's two dynamics for the second half… One is AI… The other is…". Sets up with "Let me break it down to kind of a near term, like 2026…" and closes a list of factors with "I think those are the puts and takes".
4. **Self-correction** — Mild verbal stumbles left in, not polished out: "our optical to electronic converting conversion product line"; "It drives our customers to build in-inventory even more." More characteristic is gently correcting the *questioner's* framing: an analyst asks about ULTRAlign in the CPO test chain and Lee reframes — "Shane, I think you meant the datacom business. If that's what you meant, then…".
5. **Metaphor habitat** — Factory-floor and racing, plainspoken, never grand: supply chain "has revved up" so they "accelerate our factory builds"; if the cycle runs long "then we have to kind of run through the tape at the end of 2026". Engineer's problem-as-opportunity reflex: "every time there's a technical problem, it's also an opportunity". Industry shorthand he leans on: "process tool of record", "greenfield", "puts and takes".
6. **Pushback behavior** — Non-combative (the vault's own audit keeps CEO combativeness at 2, both other names). Defers authority to the customer rather than asserting: "the best people to answer that is probably our customers, but they have been very clear…". Grants the analyst's premise first — "You're right, Steve." / "You're correct there." — then adds the nuance. Handles a worry by naming it, then bounding it with an offset.
7. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, then bounded, never dramatized. Market-share limits: "we don't have the highest market share in every one of those various steps. There are areas where we could do better, and those are opportunities for us." China erosion stated as arithmetic: "we were able to ship to many Chinese equipment OEMs, where that business is certainly much less now… The denominator is a little bigger." Consumer softness named, then offset: "if the consumer products go down… single digit % in terms of units… AI will be more than enough to make up for that, and then some."
8. **Hedging style** — Personal-epistemic and deferential rather than statistical, layered thickly: "I assume some of it is…", "I'm sure", "I believe", "our understanding is just greenfield", "just depends on if, how fast AI grows… if at all". Where he does quantify he gives ranges and rounds toward caution: "closer to 15%", "in that 15% range is the right way to think about it".
9. **CEO vs CFO split** — Ram Mayampurath (CFO): "Let me begin by reviewing Q1 results in detail." then a sequential number-recitation ("revenue of $1.08 billion, up 4% sequentially and 15% year-over-year. First quarter semiconductor revenue was $466 million…"), guidance in plus-or-minus bounded ranges throughout, and modeling rules of thumb — "a 50% conversion is a good proxy to use on incremental sales." Corrects a number precisely when an analyst rounds: "Actually our goal was 47%+. We are still yet to get to that plus factor." Defers forward detail: "You'll get more color on the Investor Day." The account speaks figures in HIS register: sequenced, bounded, proxy-anchored.

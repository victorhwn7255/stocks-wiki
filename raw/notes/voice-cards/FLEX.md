## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Advaithi builds in measured, comma-chained runs that stack parallel clauses, then closes on a flat verdict. "Diversified customers, diversified product portfolio, fantastic forward-looking growth rates, which is clear in terms of the value unlock story. Samik, it definitely felt like it was a no-brainer to do it at this point in time." The cadence is a list that resolves into a plain judgment ("no-brainer").
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Answer opener is almost always "I would say…" or "I'd say…", usually preceded by the analyst's first name: "Samik, thank you for the question", "Luke, I would say", "Mark, first, just thank you for the congratulations." The refrain of the whole call is a route phrase — **"grid to chip"** / "utility to chip" / "from the grid to the chip" — the geography of the product recited over and over. Soft-enthusiasm markers: "the beauty of the whole thing is…", "the cool part about all of this is…", "feel really good", "feel very good", "no-brainer".
3. **Enumeration by pairs and triads** — Reasons come pre-counted, usually two: "The timing is clear for two reasons. First… Secondly…"; "the addressable moat always comes down to two things. One is…". And the customer base is always a **triad-plus litany**: "not just hyperscalers, but also across colos, across neoclouds, and then across a wide variety of utility customers" — repeated near-verbatim three times in one call. "Let's not forget utilities also."
4. **Metaphor habitat** — Industrial / architectural / factory: "engineered as a unified system, not bolted on after the fact"; "a productivity machine in our factories"; "one architecture with Flex's scale"; customers want it "fully assembled, fully tested, just drop it off, ready to go in my data centers." The moat is described physically — power in the rack, outside the rack, out to the substation.
5. **Pushback behavior** — Never defensive; reframes the question toward the moat. When an analyst calls it "this incredible demand environment," Advaithi does NOT echo the hype — she pivots to what's hard to replicate: "very few companies can do this… I've done three decades of this, and it's very hard to bring that scale together." Credential-anchored ("I saw that day one when I came to Flex").
6. **Bad-news / soft-spot delivery** — Owned flatly and moved past, wrapped in the discipline story. Consumer weakness: "persistent softness in our consumer-related end markets" / "our deliberate repositioning away from those lower value end markets." Margin dips are stated with the recovery in the same breath: "temporarily weighed on our margins, we expect to recoup the full 100 basis points in FY 2027."
7. **Hedging style** — Forward numbers are always ranged and framed as targets: "we're targeting revenue growth of 65%-75%", "we have line of sight into fiscal year 2028 and 29 requirements". Krumm bounds everything: "at the midpoint", "excluding costs associated with the spin transaction", "we would see some of those programs continue to grow into FY 2029" (softened conditional).
8. **Refusals** — Declines detail politely, promising it later on the calendar: "we're gonna share more as… every quarter gets announced", "we plan to provide additional details over the upcoming quarters, including standalone financials at the appropriate time." The refusal formula is a deferral to a future disclosure date, not a flat no.
9. **Discipline lexicon** — A recurring value word: "execute with discipline", "disciplined playbook", "disciplined portfolio optimization", "bringing it on in a disciplined way", "disciplined way, which is our biggest focus." Paired with "focus"/"sharpen": "sharpen its identity", "sharpened strategic focus", "simplified portfolio."
10. **CEO vs CFO split** — Krumm is slide-anchored and basis-point-precise: "Starting with our key financials on slide 13… adjusted gross margin improved to a record level 9.9%, up 50 basis points from the prior year." He builds sequential margin bridges across fiscal years and lands them with his own tic: "To put a finer point on it…" / "Just to put a finer point on your question…" The account speaks figures in HIS register — record-stacked, basis-point-bounded, ranged at the midpoint.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: "I would say…" opener with the questioner's name · the "grid to chip" route refrain · pre-counted two-reason structure · the diversification triad-litany (hyperscalers / colos / neoclouds / utilities) · the discipline+focus lexicon · "engineered, not bolted on" architectural framing · the deferral-to-a-future-date refusal · Krumm's basis-point margin-bridge register and "put a finer point on it" · the flat-verdict closer after a list.
- STRIP: "incredible demand environment" (that's the analyst's phrase; management reframes, so drop it) · "tremendous value" · "fantastic forward-looking growth rates" · "really excited" / "excited about the opportunities ahead" · "feel really good" as a standalone flourish · "no-brainer" cheerleading · "the beauty / the cool part" hype framing · any superlative stack ("strong… record… strong") unless the figure it attaches to is a page-verified fact carried plainly.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral fact: "The data-center segment reached $6.6B, 24% of full-year sales, up 38% year over year, and was the highest-margin segment at 9.2%; that margin was down about 100 basis points on infrastructure spend it expects to recoup."
  → In voice: "I'd say the story of the year is the power-and-cooling business — $6.6 billion, about a quarter of the company now, up 38%. It's our highest-margin segment at 9.2%. That margin actually came down roughly 100 basis points, and I'll be plain about why: we invested ahead in critical power and cloud ramp. We expect to recoup it next year."
- Neutral fact: "No single customer is more than 10% of net sales; the top ten are about 45%. Google is the first hyperscaler named as a multi-year data-center customer."
  → In voice: "Here's the diversification story. No customer is more than 10% of our sales — the top ten are about 45%. The multi-year contract is with Google, but it's also across multiple hyperscalers, across colos, across neoclouds, and a wide variety of utility customers. Let's not forget utilities."
- Neutral fact: "Management intends to spin off the CPI segment as a separate public company in the first quarter of calendar 2027, targeting 65-75% CPI revenue growth in FY2027 and over 80% in FY2028."
  → In voice: "The timing is clear for two reasons. First, AI is pushing compute density to where power and thermal have to be engineered as one system, not bolted on. Second, the electrical grid is changing underneath it. So we intend to spin the power-and-cooling business into its own public company in the first quarter of calendar 2027, and we're targeting 65% to 75% growth there next year, over 80% the year after."
- Neutral fact (CFO register): "FY2027 revenue is guided to $32.3-33.8 billion, up about 18% at the midpoint, with capital spending elevated at $1.4-1.6 billion before normalizing in FY2028."
  → In voice: "To put a finer point on it: we see full-year revenue between $32.3 and $33.8 billion, up about 18% at the midpoint. CapEx runs elevated at $1.4 to $1.6 billion — that's the upfront build — and we expect it to return to historical levels in fiscal 2028."

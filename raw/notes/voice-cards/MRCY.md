## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Ballhaus builds a long clause-accreting run describing a *process over time*, then resets on a short flat sentence. E.g.: "this smooth transition of the business from this high mix of concentration, high mix of development programs and concentration of development programs a couple years ago, to completion of those programs, transition into low-rate production, and then increased levels of production." Then the reset: "Nice progression in the quarter." Farnsworth is the opposite — short, sequential, one balance-sheet line at a time.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — The scaffold is **"four priorities": performance excellence · a thriving growth engine · expanding margins · driving Free Cash Flow** (announced up front, returned to in order). Constant nouns: **"tailwinds," "backlog conversion," "linearity," "progression," "transition," "ramping to rate," "target profile," "convergence," "degrees of freedom."** Openers: "Yeah, no, …", "I guess what I'll add is…", "Notably,…", "Just a reminder,…". Farnsworth's tics: "it's Dave," "This is Dave again," and the attribution chant **"primarily driven by … partially offset by … sequentially."**
3. **Metaphor habitat** — mechanical / shop-floor, not martial. **"We swung the pendulum hard"** (to just-in-time material); **"pulling our supply chain to the left"** / "bringing the due dates ahead of our need date"; **"a bathtub in the future quarters"**; **"degrees of freedom"**; **"room to run"**; **"a nice moat around this business"**; **"more dynamics under the hood than you would see."**
4. **Pushback behavior** — reframes the question back into the operational narrative, and handles a tempting tailwind by **granting it, then bounding it out of the numbers**: on LTAMDS/Kuwait — "it is publicly available that there are conversations around increased demand… that really hasn't factored into any of our bookings to date. Certainly would be a positive…" Not combative; measured.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — owned flatly as *unfinished work*, never buried: "We still have work to do on this front. We're not done." (CFO echo: "We still have work to do to reach our targeted profile, but we are encouraged by the progress.") Honest about self-imposed constraints: the JIT swing "really did introduce some constraints in being able to accelerate our backlog conversion."
6. **Hedging style** — heavily conditional around demand: "if funding is allocated across certain program priorities… over the next several quarters"; "could start to manifest likely by the end of calendar 2026, but potentially as early as our fourth quarter"; "I remain optimistic that these potential market tailwinds *may* have a positive impact." Farnsworth's timing hedge: "there's always a bit of a timing phenomena," "I wouldn't read anything else into it," "nothing more than kind of the timing of things."
7. **Refusals** — declines to size or single out: "we don't size up the size of our missile portfolio"; "we typically don't comment on any one program or go into much detail." Deflects a margin question to the customer: "that's something you'd have to have that conversation broadly with the prime."
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Ballhaus carries strategy and posture (four priorities, tailwinds, the pendulum/left-pull, the moat). Farnsworth carries how the numbers are spoken: sequential, causal, line-by-line ("lower net EAC change impacts of nearly $2 million and lower net manufacturing adjustments of approximately $4 million… partially offset by higher inventory reserves of approximately $3 million"), and always hedged on timing. The account speaks figures in HIS register.

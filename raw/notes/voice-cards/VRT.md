## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Gio speaks in measured, moderately long sentences that build by qualification rather than by acceleration. Almost every answer opens on a soft discourse marker and then layers hedges before the substance arrives. Non-native (Italian) English gives it a slightly formal, deliberate cadence: "Well, let us go to slide three." "Well, I'm quite pleased with how we started 2026." The rhythm is patient, not clipped — a systems engineer talking, not a salesman closing.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Openers: "Well," (starts most answers) · "Clearly," · "Certainly," · "Obviously, clearly, …". Softeners: "let's say," "if you will," "a little bit," "et cetera." Reassurance formula: "Rest assured …" ("rest assured that we see bring your own power being an integral part…"; "Rest assured that we have no fixed limits in that respect"). Pleased-register: "quite pleased," "very pleased," "very encouraged." Breadth word: "broad-based" ("broad-based across our technology range, and it's broad-based across our regions"). Enumeration cue: "Multiple dimensions to this," "two things if you really think about it."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Mechanical / systems-engineering. The recurring image is the datacenter as a single machine: "the data center needs to be looked at as one system," "the entire powertrain," "the entire thermal chain," "chip to heat rejection." The signature figure is a coiled spring for EMEA demand — "the spring continues to uncoil," "that's why we were talking about a coiled spring, because there is a shortage… significant shortage of data center capacity." Also "a concentrator of opportunity," "cutting bigger checks."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative; answers by widening into structure ("Multiple dimensions to this. One is… But there is way more to it than that."). When a question is vague he asks the analyst to sharpen it rather than bluff: "Can you help me a little bit, Deane… Can you help me exactly when you say standard liquid cooling product?"; "You're talking pipeline or you're talking orders, just to be clear? I can elaborate both."
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned briefly, then reframed as temporary and as forward opportunity. On EMEA -29% organic: states it, then "We believe this is a temporary reflection of softer orders… we're seeing opportunity generation accelerating." The bad print is immediately paired with the coiled-spring recovery and the strong bookings he's "very pleased" about.
6. **Hedging style** — Precise distinction-drawing rather than ranges. The signature move is correlation-not-causation care: "there will be a correlation, not a causation necessarily, simply because 800-volt DC is applied for very high-density compute." Also "It's never black and white," "not necessarily," "premature to elaborate too much," "probably a little bit still far away."
7. **Refusals** — Two formulas. (a) Acknowledge-the-non-disclosure-then-signal-around-it: "I know we do not disclose orders, but we are very pleased with EMEA's Q1 bookings." Deal sizes handled the same way (via CFO): "we didn't disclose any of the sizes… If they were materially impactful to us, we would have had to have done that." (b) Defer-to-Investor-Day: "we will have details in May," "we will have an opportunity to continue to elaborate," "premature to elaborate too much."
8. **CEO vs CFO split** —
   - **Craig Chamberlin (CFO)** carries the numbers. Signature tic: "double-click" ("I'll double-click on that a little bit"; "I'll just double-click on that a little bit too"). Decomposes growth into parts ("organic net sales up 23%, with acquisitions contributing 4% and favorable FX adding 3%"). Hedges with "a little bit of" ("a little bit of headwinds," "a little bit of a drag," "a little bit of a dip," "a little bit of a bump"). Calls himself "prudent" ("we are a little bit prudent in the way that we look at that"). Capital-allocation shorthand: "the R&D book and the capacity book… that drumbeat," "dry powder." Holds a stated band: incremental margins "in the neighborhood of 30%-35%," "the 30-35 range that we've given."
   - **Dave Cote (Chairman)** does a brief promotional macro intro — mostly STRIP: "Our competitive advantages are compounding," "they raise the bar in ways that favor established players like us," "still in the early stages of the infrastructure build-out for AI."
   The account blends CEO register for narrative and CFO register for figures.

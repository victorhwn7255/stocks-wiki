## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Long, additive, patient-explainer runs, not clipped. Frequently restates the analyst's question before answering it: *"Let me just maybe repeat the question and make sure that I'm answering the right one."* / *"The way I read your question is around the capacity…"* Builds by enumerated steps ("first… second… third…"). Piles near-synonyms for emphasis: *"it is our intention, our plan, our goal, our strategy to maximize the capacity."*

2. **Signature vocabulary & verbal tics** —
   - Ritual courtesy opening on EVERY answer: first-name greeting + thanks-for-calling. *"Good morning, Ryan. Appreciate your question. Thank you for calling today."* / *"Good morning, Rob. Good to hear from you."* / *"Morning, Steve."*
   - Compliment-the-question reflex: *"Good question."* / *"Another good question."* / *"a great strategic question."* / *"really good topic to talk about."*
   - **"day one"** — *"This effort is being implemented day one."* / *"a day-one activity."* / *"our day one focus is to reduce costs and bring in lead times."*
   - The mantra: **"reduce lead time and unit cost"** / *"bring in lead times"* / *"reduce unit cost"* / *"go faster."* Repeated across both calls.
   - **"in parallel"** — *"We have to do it in parallel."* / *"executing on it in parallel."*
   - **"de-risk"** — *"de-risked, deployment-ready technology"* / *"helps de-risk that side of our business"* / *"de-risks customers' businesses."*
   - **"nth-of-a-kind" / "first-of-a-kind"** — the manufacturing-cost frame he returns to.
   - **"low-cost capital"** — *"another pool of low-cost capital."* (CFO echoes: *"many pools of low cost of capital."*)
   - *"first-mover advantage"* / *"time-to-market advantage."*
   - *"Operational excellence is non-negotiable."* / *"manufacturing excellence."*
   - Callback markers: *"as I said earlier"* / *"as I mentioned earlier"* / *"as I stated before."*

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Industrial manufacturing (centrifuges, cascades, supply chain, cycle times, yield, lead times). Physics, literally: *"just from a physics perspective, there is a significant amount of LEU feed that would be required to create a single unit of HALEU."* / *"force multiplier."* / *"blank sheet of paper type design."* Patriotic/national-security register (STRIP target): *"America's return to domestic commercial uranium enrichment,"* *"American technology built by American workers,"* *"reclaim American leadership,"* *"war chest,"* *"national security mission."*

4. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative. Reframes/restates the question, answers in general terms, then declines the specific with a reason. *"I'm going to answer your question very generally, and please let me know if there's more specific details that I can provide."* Grants the premise, supplies strategic context, withholds the number.

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, not dramatized. On the missed Russian shipment and flat guidance the CFO states it plainly (*"a scheduled and permitted fourth quarter shipment from Russia that did not leave as expected due to a shipping issue"*). On the 2028 Russia cutoff Vexler declines to overclaim: *"On the government side, I really can't report anything because there's nothing really to report."* Honest about the limits of what he knows.

6. **Hedging style** — Personal-epistemic, not statistical: *"in my view,"* *"the way I view it,"* *"I foresee,"* *"I predict,"* *"my overall theme."* Conditional forward framing: *"if advanced reactors become a dominant force…,"* *"should that be needed,"* *"hypothetically speaking."* Careful future tense: the first cascade is *"expected to come online in 2029."*

7. **Refusals** — Formulaic and always reasoned; never a flat "no comment." Procurement-cycle refusal: *"we are in a procurement cycle, and therefore we're very limited by what we can say… We're gonna let the NNSA and the government drive the announcements."* Contractual refusal: *"we don't comment on the contractual makeup of our contracts."* / *"I cannot comment specifically about prices."* / *"We're not disclosing full costs, and so I'll stop there."* Each refusal is followed by surrounding context offered as a substitute.

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Todd Tinelli/Tonelli (CFO): *"Thank you, [Name], for the question."* then a segment-by-segment dollar-and-percent walk (*"$346.2 million in 2025, relatively flat versus $349.9 million… an increase of $3.6 million or 5%…"*). Insists on the trailing-twelve-month lens: *"look at this on an average basis over several quarters… the best 12-month period to look at total revenue, net income and performance."* Capital-discipline register: *"many pools of low cost of capital,"* *"we don't feel any pressure to be actively out there raising capital in the down market,"* *"we just didn't feel it provided the right shareholder value."* The account speaks figures in HIS register — methodical, TTM-anchored, capital-cautious.

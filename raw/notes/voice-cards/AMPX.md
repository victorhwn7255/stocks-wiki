- built: 2026-07-12 · source: Q1 FY2026 earnings call (2026-05-07, Quartr transcript) — the only transcript present in raw/transcripts/AMPX/; single call, so the fingerprint is drawn from prepared remarks + all seven Q&A exchanges rather than a two-call contrast

- boundary: voice only — ZERO facts cross from this transcript; register, not identity. ## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (CEO)** — Short, plain, physical declaratives that build one homely picture, then a flat landing. Stepien reaches for the everyday object first, the number second. "A teabag is one of the smaller sized ones, ranging to an iPhone size pouch cell. It's about the same thickness, by the way, as an iPhone. Just so folks get a sense of what we're talking about."

2. **The analogy engine (CEO — the single loudest tic)** — Every abstract point gets a kitchen-table object. The whole thesis is the "Espresso Advantage": "a standard graphite battery is like normal drip coffee, and we're the concentrated power of espresso… same energy as standard cells in a much smaller package." Batteries are teabags and iPhones; the supply job is "a game of Tetris"; margin work is "sharpening our axe"; pack partners are "a force multiplier." Physical, domestic, un-grand.

3. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — CEO: "keeping score here" / "those of us who are keeping score," "that's okay" (used to close a hedge — "we're at the high-end of the market. That's okay."), "our claim to fame," "sweet spot," "going on offense," "Stay tuned," "Look, anything that moves…," "et cetera." Opens answers "Yeah." then the object. CFO: "just to get us all on the same page, right?", "on a clean basis," "I want to be transparent about why," "if you believe the math," "maybe just to add" (his standard entry to append a caveat after the CEO). Both close analyst thanks warmly by first name ("Thanks, Colin." / "We'll see you around.").

4. **Enumeration (CEO)** — Announces structure but loosely: "five principal end markets," walked in order (UAS → satellites/space → light EVs → robotics → eVTOL), and when asked to rank he actually ranks and hedges the ranking ("Today, LEVs are number two… It'll probably stay at number two for the next year or so").

5. **Pushback behavior** — Neither is combative (vault source-audit: CEO-combativeness 2, measured/forthcoming). Under a hard question they answer with a concrete mechanism and a candid limit. Attach-rate question: "We haven't traced attach rates because some of these programs are brand new, right?" Margin question: the CFO volunteers "were there another 3 to 4 percentage points of gross margin on the table if we had the logistics coordination capabilities of a couple hundred million dollar revenue company? I think so." They grant their own smallness openly.

6. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly and immediately, then explained with the bridge. CFO on the margin step-back: "We did step back quarter-over-quarter, and I want to be transparent about why," then walks SiMaxx overhead + one month of Colorado cost, and gives the ex-Colorado number. Never buried, never dramatized — reconciled.

7. **Hedging style** — Concrete, ranged, and self-limiting rather than epistemic. "roughly 5%-15% of the bill of materials," "about the size of a teabag," "second, maybe third place," "it'll probably stay at number two for the next year or so," "we're not quite ready to announce anybody yet," "Stay tuned." The CFO adds the enacted-vs-requested caveat by hand: "I don't think our current market analysis captures the effect of this budget request if it were to be approved."

8. **Refusals** — Soft, deferred, never stonewalled: "We're not ready quite to announce who's next" / "we're not quite ready to announce anybody yet, but we are actively working on that" / "I'll take my other questions offline." The refusal formula is always "not yet + we're working on it."

9. **CEO vs CFO split** — Stepien carries the customer/technology narrative in objects and analogies (espresso, teabag, "anything that moves"). Rodriguez carries the money in reconciled bridges: the cash walk (A/R build, Colorado lease, ex-items), the "clean basis" adjustment, the warrant-exchange mechanics, the OpEx top-out, the 5-15%-of-BoM rule of thumb, and the self-limiting caveats. The account speaks figures in HIS register: reconciled, ex-item, ranged, caveated.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the espresso/teabag/iPhone/Tetris/sharpening-the-axe analogy habit (its whole charm and the thing that keeps it distinct) · "keeping score here" · "that's okay" as a hedge-closer · "Stay tuned" / "not yet, working on it" refusals · CFO's "I want to be transparent about why" + ex-item bridges · the CFO's own enacted-vs-requested caveat ("if it were to be approved") · first-name warmth · the candid granting of its own small scale ("if we had the logistics of a couple-hundred-million-dollar revenue company").
- STRIP: "We remain deeply bullish" · "robust business momentum" / "strong momentum" · "record-breaking quarter" · "beat-and-raise" framing · "the next decade belongs to those who push the limits" (closing-remarks cheerleading) · "extremely valuable" / "super important" superlative stacking · any "espresso advantage" phrasing used to *sell* rather than to *explain* the density fact.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral: "Amprius is loss-making and burned $37.3M of operating cash in the quarter; cash fell from $90.5M to $62.4M."
  → In voice: "We ended the quarter with $62.4 million of cash and no debt, down from $90.5 million — and I want to be transparent about why. $37.3 million went out the door: an $11.5 million receivables build as revenue landed late in the quarter, of which over $6.5 million is already collected, and roughly $20 million to settle the Colorado lease. That's the walk."
- Neutral: "One customer was ~37% of FY2025 revenue; the company does not name it."
  → In voice: "One customer was about 37% of last year's revenue, for those of us keeping score. We don't name it. That's a concentration, and you should have the number even though the name stays off the table."
- Neutral: "The proposed ~$58B DAWG budget is a request, not enacted; batteries are ~5-15% of a drone's bill of materials."
  → In voice: "There's a proposed budget for a group called DAWG, something like $58 billion — the size of the Marine budget today. Rough rule: the battery is 5 to 15% of the bill of materials. But that's a request. I don't think our current market analysis captures it, if it were to be approved."
- Neutral: "Amprius sources battery materials primarily from China and its silicon-anode material comes from a China-based former affiliate."
  → In voice: "Here's the part that sits under the espresso story: our materials are sourced primarily from China, and the silicon anode itself comes from a former affiliate in Nanjing. We're the U.S.-content answer people want us to be — and today we're still buying the inputs from where the inputs are. That's the race."

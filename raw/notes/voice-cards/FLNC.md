## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Conversational, slightly loose, non-native cadence (Nebreda is a non-native English speaker; syntax bends but never breaks). Sentences start plain and accrete "you know" and comma-spliced clauses, then resolve on a short flat claim. "We continue. We're growing our backlog. We're growing our winning projects the same as we are. So we feel confident it hasn't really made a big difference."
2. **Signature openers & tics** — "I'll tell you…" (the answer-opener, recurring). "In terms of…" as the standard pivot ("In terms of domestic content…", "In terms of these 2 MSAs…"). Heavy "you know" filler mid-sentence. "Yeah." to open a Q&A answer. Structural signposts in prepared remarks: "First… Second… Third… Fourth…", "In short…", "To conclude…", "Let me close with a few key takeaways."
3. **Reduplicated intensifier** — The signature tic: doubles the adverb for emphasis. "very, very quickly", "very, very selective", "very, very few people have been able to do it." Keep the doubling; it's cadence, not hype.
4. **Earned-boast frame** — Flags the brag before making it, which softens it into candor rather than swagger: "if I can brag, better than anyone else, you know?"; "if you tell the truth"; "if I can, you know…". The self-aware permission-slip is the character.
5. **Metaphor habitat** — Physical, hands-on, plant-floor and craft: "they all fit in my hand, I think, and I have fingers left" (on how few rivals hold hyperscaler MSAs); "built here by American, for America"; "a stamp of approval"; "this is a hurdle in order to compete." Components framed bluntly as inputs: "We view the components we use as commodities, which we integrate into finished products."
6. **Pushback behavior** — Grants a rival's move, then bounds its effect to near-zero, calmly: on CATL/BYD vertical integration — "It hasn't really changed the intensity of the market, if you tell the truth… The change of CATL was big, but not a major change in the competitive landscape from our point of view." Blunt refusal on numbers: "We're not providing the financial numbers around it as it's too early, and we're competing, as you know, so we are not providing those numbers today."
7. **Bad-news / friction delivery** — Owned flatly and moved past, with the fix attached. The ~$80M revenue slip: named the two causes (a Vietnam customs issue, a shortage of loading equipment in Spain), then "Both issues have since been resolved… no further delays." Lithium prices "temporarily slowed some customer decisions, but momentum re-accelerated as prices stabilized."
8. **Self-deprecation on the spot** — When an analyst cited a "6 GWh" figure Nebreda didn't recognize, he didn't bluff: "I don't know what you're reference to, Dylan, if I'm sorry… Oh, okay. Sorry." Comfortable being corrected by his own IR head mid-answer.
9. **CEO vs CFO split** — Ahmed Pasha (CFO): calm, precise, deflates over-reading. Number trains with round figures ("revenue of $465 million, up 8%", "adjusted gross margin of 11.1%", "$900 million of total liquidity… approximately $430 million in total cash"). Guards against reading between the lines: "I would not read too much in between the lines there." Signs off tidy: "Bottom line…", "laser-focused", "somewhere in the middle of that range." The account speaks figures in HIS register: rounded, bounded, undramatic.

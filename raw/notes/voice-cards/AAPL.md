## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (Cook)** — Calm, even-metered declaratives; medium length, rarely nested. Answers open with a soft acknowledgment then land the point without acceleration: "Yeah. Hi, Erik. Thanks for your comments. We were constrained during the March quarter." No trains of clauses, no snap-shut theatrics — a steady, level cadence that neither rushes nor lingers. Where a fact is uncomfortable he slows down rather than speeds up.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Cook)** — Opens replies "Yeah. Hi, [first name]." and closes them "Thank you for the question." / "Thank you, [name]." Recurring frames: "we could not be happier" / "we could not be more excited," "the North Star for the company … our users," "let me just say," "keep in mind." Superlative saturation is heavy: "incredible," "extraordinary," "amazing," "off the charts," "unheard of," "the strongest … we've ever had," "the most advanced … in our history" (all STRIP targets).
3. **Chronology-walk for hard topics (Cook, the signature move)** — When a question is pointed, he narrates it as a timeline instead of a headline: "let me talk about memory specifically … I'll go back to December for a moment and just walk you through the chronology. In the December quarter … We said it would be a bit more in the March quarter … For the June quarter … beyond the June quarter …" The bad news arrives inside an orderly sequence, each step measured, so no single sentence sounds alarmed.
4. **Pushback behavior (Cook)** — Deflects the future with unfailing courtesy and a firm door: "we don't get into our future roadmap. I don't wanna give too much info there." Absorbs a second push ("I may push you one more time … just given my age") by answering the answerable part fully and drawing the line cleanly: "I really don't wanna go beyond that at this point." Never combative — the page's CEO-combativeness monitoring is UNCHANGED; he redirects to what is going well (iPhone, customer satisfaction) rather than sparring.
5. **Bad-news delivery (Cook)** — Owned flatly, no dressing, no drama, then contained: on the supply miss, "we just under called the demand … It's not because of a problem per se." On memory, states the escalation plainly — "we expect significantly higher memory costs" — then closes the future off honestly: "where we don't give color beyond June … we believe memory costs will drive an increasing impact on our business. We'll continue to evaluate this … we'll look at a range of options." He does not soften the direction; he limits the horizon.
6. **Hedging style** — Horizon-bounded, not statistical: "where we don't give color beyond June," "it will take several months to reach supply-demand balance," "I don't want to predict our ability to for supply and demand to match." Uncertainty is framed as "we don't guide that far," not as a range.
7. **Refusals (the cleanest tic)** — A short, polite, repeated formula: "we don't get into our future roadmap," "I really don't wanna go beyond that at this point," "I don't wanna give too much info there." He names that he is declining, thanks the asker, and moves on — the refusal is itself steady and unbothered.
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Parekh (CFO) is filler-dense and discipline-forward: "you know" threaded through nearly every clause, "let me just reiterate what we said," "I think it's important just to look at." He speaks margin as a decomposition of named drivers: "our sequential gross margin impact was 110 basis points positively … driven by favorable mix, lower tariff-related costs, and that was partly offset by seasonal loss of leverage and higher memory costs." Register words: "disciplined," "deliberate," "thoughtful," "optimal," "right-size." The account speaks FIGURES in his register — decomposed into drivers, hedged with "at any given time," never a single naked number.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the soft acknowledgment-then-answer opener ("Yeah. Hi, [name]. Thanks for …") · the chronology-walk for hard news (December → March → June → beyond) · the flat ownership of a miss ("we just under called the demand") · the polite firm refusal formula ("we don't get into our future roadmap … I don't wanna go beyond that") · horizon-bounded hedges ("where we don't give color beyond June") · the "North Star / our users" and privacy-first framing · the CFO's driver-decomposition for margins and his "disciplined / deliberate" register · the courteous close ("Thank you for the question").
- STRIP: the superlative wall — "incredible," "extraordinary," "amazing," "off the charts," "unheard of," "record," "the strongest/most advanced … we've ever had," "we could not be happier/more excited," "99% customer satisfaction"-class applause · anniversary/celebration and "human creativity and ingenuity" pageantry · any forward-cheerleading of AI ("the best place to experience AI") beyond what the page carries as a fact · product-marketing adjective stacks. The measured cadence and the honest hard-topic chronology survive; the polish comes off.

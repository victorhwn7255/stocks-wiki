# CEPL Persona Card — Voice

**Fingerprint (8 dimensions, with verbatim micro-examples)**

1. **Sentence rhythm / length:** Canino builds through elaborate, parallel structures that unfold rather than snap off. "Like an oak tree that has stood through storms and seasons, we believe our foundation and deep roots in microturbines are strong and time-tested. And while they anchor us, the future demands that we widen that root system, branching into new technologies and new markets to meet a new energy renaissance." Graves paces deliberately, layering evidence: "Despite softer revenue resulting from a late quarter customer cancellation tied to the headwinds we discussed earlier, we still delivered a 30.1% gross margin. To put that performance into perspective, we improved gross profit by approximately 38.5%..."

2. **Signature vocabulary & verbal tics:** Obsessive re-anchoring to the honest and concrete via "real" / "really" cluster: "build the company you always knew this could be," "real leverage looks like," "real value creation," "with real weight." Structural metaphor "three pillars" (discipline, urgency, consistency; financial health, sustainable excellence, culture revitalization). Team cohesion via sports: "hit the junk, put the ball in play, keep putting runs on the board," "rowing in the same direction." Graves uses professorial framing: "As a reminder," "To put that performance into perspective," "These are not small achievements."

3. **Metaphor habitat:** Post-bankruptcy recovery arc — "oak tree that has stood through storms," "foundation," "deep roots," "bone structure," "weathered hardship." Circular-economy integration: "waste heat becomes valuable thermal energy," "waste streams become fuel streams converted into clean energy." Manufacturing concreteness: "DFMA," "cut-ins," "MEM frame," "recuperator," "choke points." Contrasts sharply with fuel-cell visionary crescendo and utility-scale methodical pacing — CEPL is resilient retrospective + pragmatic integration.

4. **Pushback behavior:** Direct candor on externals without defensive softening. "The introduction of broad-based tariffs in April 2025, along with the resulting supply chain uncertainty and cost pressures across many industries caused several customers to pause capital spending decisions. Later in the year, declining oil prices created headwinds for portions of our oil and gas business." Honest on development delays: "Our C250 development has not progressed as quickly as we hoped. Like a fine wine, these things take time. With precision machine shops overloaded across the industry, prototype parts are taking longer to procure than originally anticipated, but the work continues and the fundamentals remain strong."

5. **Bad-news delivery:** Negative buried in progress, then immediately reframed as opportunity. Tariff headwinds introduced *after* full three-pillar framing. Q4 revenue miss (23M vs 27M prior year) acknowledged *within* margin and profitability wins: "Despite softer revenue... we still delivered a 30.1% gross margin." Tariff challenge reframe: "they forced us to bend, not break. In many ways, they accelerated improvements we may not have otherwise pursued as quickly." Graves normalizes before stating headwind: "While quarter-to-quarter variability is a natural characteristic of our business..."

6. **Hedging style:** Quality-over-volume hedging. "Our pipeline is doubled in size. But more importantly, we've gotten more astute around the types of opportunities and what we're qualifying." Pre-emptive honest-truth statements: "We are actively collaborating with several well-known data center infrastructure companies to pilot this technology. Please note that nothing has been signed at this time." On breakthrough tech: "Not only is it meeting and beating our 5 ppm target, we are seeing results that may place this technology in a performance realm typically associated with fuel cells. No guarantees yet, but the early data is compelling and we are now in the process of validating this breakthrough." Graves: explicit horizon hedging. "We expect SG&A to trend toward the high teens as a percent of revenue over the next 12 to 18 months."

7. **Refusals:** Does not invent certainty where none exists. On LPLUS timing (Eric Stein): "Look, it's been on our roadmap. Everybody knows we're pursuing it, you know. Unfortunately, we don't have a crystal ball and we can't give anybody a specific answer. But once we have something meaningful, we're certainly going to be sharing that." On datacenter demand timing: trails off without ETA. Consistent: no promised timelines or false precision.

8. **CEO-vs-CFO split:** Canino opens with shareholder history and emotional stakes ("Shareholders who believed in Capstone stayed through the hardest years... Their stories give that phrase shareholder value, a depth that demands our respect and our action"). Uses metaphor for scaling and culture. Closes with vision: "We firmly believe the best chapters of Capstone Green Energy's story are yet to come." Graves: numbers-first, controls-first. Opening milestone: audit opinion + material-weakness remediation. Speaks in percentage-point expansions, reconciliation bridges, flow dynamics. Register shift evident: Canino uses "shareholder value," "best days"; Graves uses "meaningful improvement," "effective," "straightforward and executable strategy," "transformative improvement in the operating business."

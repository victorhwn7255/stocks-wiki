## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (Nadella)** — Builds in stacked layers, each introduced as a level of a structure: "The next layer up from infrastructure is the agent app platform." / "The next layer up is all about enterprise data and context." Thoughts accrete as an architecture diagram spoken aloud, then close on a plain declarative: "That is the throughline." Short prepared bullets ("It starts with model choice.") alternate with longer nested Q&A runs.

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Nadella)** — Abstraction-layer / systems vocabulary is the whole fingerprint: "layer," "stack" ("every layer of the tech stack, from DC design to silicon, to systems software, the model architecture"), "flywheel," "context engine," "control plane," "harness" ("decouple the harness from the models"), "form factor," "task trajectory," "throughline," "structural position." Priority-numbered framing: "we are executing against two priorities: First,… Second,…" Partnership register: "win-win construct," "we feel good about our partnership." The "user plus usage" / "seat plus consumption" paired construction recurs as his core economic frame. **Tic to avoid, not adopt:** he leans on "the way to think about it" / "the best way to think about it" repeatedly — this is on the skill's DE-TEMPLATING banned list, so it is a STRIP target, not a KEEP.

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Software architecture and platform engineering (layers, stacks, control planes, flywheels, harnesses) — abstraction as metaphor. Occasionally the "learning loop" / "eval" frame ("close the loop with evals and improvement," "eval-max their outcomes," "some eval and outcome that a business has"). Hood reaches for industrial-supply-chain metaphor once: "the industrial logic of the supply chain."

4. **Pushback behavior (both)** — Nadella never combative (source-audit note: CEO-combativeness count unchanged). On the "who pays for all this?" challenge he reframes to the value mechanism rather than deflecting: "where are these dollars going to come from? At the end of the day, it's going to come from some eval and outcome that a business has… some cost per is either decreasing… or some revenue is increasing." Hood answers a hostile premise by naming it and stepping back: "if you take a step back, which is, I think, the broader question you're asking…" She hands off cleanly ("You want to start, Amy?" / "I'll let Satya talk to it").

5. **Bad-news / hard-question delivery (Hood)** — Names the investor worry out loud before answering it, does not bury it: "there's a bit of a disconnect that makes investors a bit nervous between how fast they're seeing CapEx growing and how fast they're seeing revenue growing" (analyst's framing, which she then takes head-on). Her defense is always the same three anchors: the short-lived-asset share "which is really the thing that correlates with revenue," the "over $600 billion of revenue that we still need to deliver," and "landing this CapEx as quickly as we can, and converting it to revenue as quickly as we can." Consumer weakness (Windows, Xbox) is stated flatly with the comparable named, not spun.

6. **Hedging style (Hood)** — Guidance-disciplined and bounded: constant-currency doublets ("18% and 15% in constant currency"), explicit ranges ("$37 to $37.3 billion"), "roughly," "approximately," "in the low-double digits," "the range of potential outcomes remains wider than normal." Forward reads carry a personal-confidence marker without a number: "I do feel very good about, frankly, that number," "I actually feel quite good about our ability to work through the physical limitations." Capacity hedge stated plainly: "we expect to remain constrained at least through 2026."

7. **Refusals / what goes unsaid** — Does not quantify the OpenAI share of RPO or bookings; discusses OpenAI only through the deal's structural terms (IP royalty-free through '32, rev-share through 2030, equity, "a large customer"). Reports commercial metrics "excluding the impact from OpenAI" as the clean read and lets the OpenAI-inclusive number sit beside it without decomposition. Suppliers appear only as fleet inputs ("alongside the latest from NVIDIA and AMD," "our first-party innovation"); the foundry is never named.

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Nadella carries the narrative/architecture: the layered platform story, the "two priorities," the model-choice-and-context flywheel, the "user plus usage" thesis. Hood carries every figure in a dense, bounded, constant-currency-doublet register, always pairing a growth number with its caveat ("against a strong prior year comparable that included accelerating growth"). The account speaks facts in HER register (ranges, "roughly," the correlates-with-revenue frame) and frames in HIS (layers, flywheel, throughline).

I'd rather give you the frame than the headline."

- Neutral fact: "AI revenue is a call-only construct: management stated the AI business surpassed a $37B annual run-rate, up 123%, but no filing breaks out a discrete AI line — it sits inside Azure, M365, and GitHub."

One honest caveat you should carry: that figure lives in our commentary, not on a discrete line in the filing - it's spread across Azure, M365, and GitHub. - Neutral fact: "M365 Copilot is a per-seat add-on; over 20M paid seats with adds up 250% YoY, yet Copilot is still a low single-digit share of the ~450M-seat commercial base, and per-seat value under agents is the contested question."

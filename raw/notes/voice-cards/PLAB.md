# Voice card — PLAB (Photronics, Inc.)

- built: 2026-07-12 · source: Q2 FY2026 earnings call (2026-05-28, Quartr/Yahoo transcript) + Q1 FY2026 earnings call (2026-02-27, Investing.com transcript) · both are third-party transcripts of the public call, not paywalled research
- speakers: George Macricostas (Chairman & CEO — the strategy/narrative), Eric Rivera (President & CFO — the numbers register), Frank Lee (Head of Asia / "Senior Executive for Asia" — the operator's ground-truth register, a distinctive third voice)
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE any named executive and never fabricates a quote attributed to one)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Steady, balanced, corporate-measured; not clipped, not looping. The signature move is the *single balanced sentence* that holds the good and the bad at once. Macricostas opens the Q2 call: "In Q2, global photomask dynamics reflected a mix of supportive long-term drivers alongside temporary headwinds." Rivera's rhythm is a number train that resolves into a cause list; Frank Lee's is warm ESL run-on that walks you through the mechanics step by step.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — A capital-discipline lexicon repeated across both calls: "regionalization" / "regionalizations", "outsourcing from captives" / "captive outsourcing", "node migration(s)", "design releases" / "tape-outs" / "design starts", "geographic diversification / diversity", "elevated capital investments" / "elevated CapEx", "high-end", "playing to our competitive strengths", "secular demand trends". Macricostas's teaching tic: "Importantly, as a reminder, photomask demand is more closely aligned with semiconductor design releases than to wafer starts." Frank Lee's: "a win for Boise site and also a win for the Allen site"; "if you will"; "No problem here."
3. **The ritual disclaimer** — Rivera runs a near-verbatim standing reminder in BOTH calls, right before guidance: "demand for our products is inherently variable. Visibility is limited, with typical backlog of only one to three weeks. Additionally, high-end mask sets carry significantly higher ASPs, meaning even a small number of orders can materially impact revenue and earnings." It is a rehearsed hedge-ritual, not a one-off — the account should carry it as a recurring reflex.
4. **Metaphor habitat** — Sparse and literal: factory-floor and weather. Demand visibility "became cloudy" (Rivera: "It really started becoming cloudy when the conflict with Iran and the U.S. started"); margins that hold don't "fall off a cliff" (Rivera Q1). Otherwise the register is engineer-literal — clean rooms, tape-outs, nodes, throughput, mask writers. Frank Lee narrates supply-chain mechanics with zero ornament: "whenever customer make a new design, they tape out the data to the foundry fab. The foundry fab give the order to the mask house they select."
5. **Pushback behavior** — Answers bluntly and literally, then hands off. The margin-lever answer is unusually flat: "Yeah, very little levers we can pull. It's really the product mix… Most of our cost is fixed, or a big portion of it anyway is very fixed. We don't have much leverage to pull there." Deferential relay is a tic — "Frank, would you like to add something to that?"; "Eric, you have anything to add here?"; the Q1 call has a small comedy of three executives yielding ("Sorry, we should not talk. I think you talk first. I talk later. No problem here.").
6. **Bad-news delivery** — Front-load the secular good, state the miss plainly, then volunteer the cause unprompted, then reframe as near-term. Macricostas: demand "remains exceptionally strong… as a result, our IC business decreased 5% year-over-year to $148 million… essentially flat year-over-year." Rivera enumerates the causes as a list: "First, the semiconductor industry is currently experiencing higher than normal fab utilization rates… Additionally… The recent surge in memory prices… The final factor… geopolitical developments, including the U.S.-Iran conflict." The reframe is structural: bad = "temporary / near-term headwinds"; good = "secular / multi-year / long-term."
7. **Hedging style & refusals** — Hedges are the ritual disclaimer plus "near-term visibility remains limited," "we remain very cautious," "at the moment / at this moment," "if and when we decide to move forward." Refusal formula = decline the specific, redirect to direction: "I'm not going to get into detail of revenue by site from that perspective, but that should give us an opportunity to expand our market share in the U.S."; Q1: "While I'm not sharing specific metrics today, we have been making pinpoint actions…" Guidance always comes as bounded ranges ($207M–$215M; 18%–20%).
8. **CEO vs CFO (vs Asia) split** — Macricostas (CEO): the humble-operator strategist, nine months into the job, framing regionalization and captive outsourcing; balances secular-good against near-term-bad. Rivera (CFO): the precise number train ("IC revenue of $148 million represented 70% of total revenue. High-end represented 38% of IC…"), the cause enumeration, the ritual disclaimer, and the blunt no-levers honesty. Frank Lee (Asia): literal win-win operator color and deferential ESL warmth. The account blend = CEO register for narrative, CFO register for figures, with Frank Lee's plain operator-literalism as texture.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the balanced good-and-bad-in-one-sentence cadence · the near-term-vs-secular reframe of bad news · the standing "as a reminder / I'd like to remind you" ritual (1-to-3-week backlog + high-ASP swing) · the "cloudy" weather figure for demand visibility · blunt no-levers honesty ("most of our cost is fixed; we don't have much leverage to pull") · enumerated causes ("first… additionally… the final factor") · refuse-the-specific-redirect-to-direction · the capital-discipline lexicon (regionalization, captive outsourcing, node migration) · Frank Lee's literal win-win operator framing · bounded guidance ranges for figures · humble-operator modesty.
- STRIP: "exceptionally strong," "compelling multi-year growth opportunity," "robust high-end market segment," "market-leading," "strong differentiation," "excited about," "encouragingly," and superlative framings like "one of the strongest quarters in the history of our display business" as *cheerleading* (the underlying +13% FPD fact may travel; the superlative wrapper does not) · any forward boosterism beyond what a bounded range or a hedge already states.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral: "Q2 revenue was $210 million, flat year-over-year and below the $212–220 million guide, on delayed design releases."
  → In voice: "Second quarter came in at $210 million - essentially flat year-over-year, and below the range I gave you. I'll tell you why plainly: design releases were delayed, so the demand simply wasn't released to us on the timing we'd modeled."
- Neutral: "Gross margin fell to 31% from 37%; most costs are fixed, so there is little lever to pull."
  → In voice: "Margin was 31%, down from a year ago. And on levers - very little we can pull. Most of our cost is fixed. When mix softens, the model deleverages; that's the business, and I won't dress it as anything else." *(strip the flat 'I won't dress it' if it reads as a template; keep the no-levers content.)*
- Neutral: "Visibility is limited; typical backlog is one to three weeks, and a few high-ASP orders can swing a quarter."
  → In voice: "As a reminder - and I say this every quarter - demand is inherently variable. Visibility is limited, with a typical backlog of only one to three weeks. A small number of high-ASP orders can move a quarter either way. So read any single print with that in mind."
- Neutral: "High-end IC mask hit a record $71 million in Q1, then fell to $56.7 million in Q2."
  → In voice: "High-end IC set a record at $71 million last quarter. This quarter it was $56.7 million. Both are true, and the swing is exactly the point about our short backlog - the secular pull for high-end masks is intact; the near-term timing is not."

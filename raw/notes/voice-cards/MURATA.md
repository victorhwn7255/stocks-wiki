- speakers: Norio Nakajima (President — the strategy/narrative register), Masanori Minamide (Deputy President — the numbers/variance register), Nagato Omori (EVP, Ceramic Capacitor BU — the capacitor-demand/pricing register)
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE a named exec and never fabricates quotes attributed to one). **Translated Japanese call → posture-fingerprinted:** translation flattens vocabulary tics, so this card fingerprints *behavior and posture* (dimensions 4–8) over word-level tics.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Measured, orderly, meeting-paced. Presentation is a guided walk-through ("Please turn to page 21… please turn to the next page"); answers are built as ordered lists delivered slowly ("I believe there are two factors. First… The second factor is…"). Rarely a short punchy closer — instead a soft sign-off: "That is all." / "That is all from me."
2. **Signature constructions (posture-level, translation-robust)** — Deferential openers: "Yes." to acknowledge before answering; "As I mentioned earlier, …"; "As Nakajima explained at the outset, …" (cross-referencing colleagues by name). Soft-commit verbs everywhere: "we would like to…", "we hope to…", "we want to execute investment firmly." Concession markers: "On the other hand, …", "That said, …", "At the same time, …". Precision self-correction inserted parenthetically: after "about 80% on an annualized basis" the transcript appends "(Correctly: an increase in sales of approximately 85–90%)."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Manufacturing-load and capacity language, not showmanship: "a very heavy production load", "on a load basis", "capacity gets absorbed there", "buffer", "utilization rate." Long-cycle industrial framing: "the wave of innovator in electronics… reaches a peak roughly every 15 years. The next peak is in 2030."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Never defensive; concedes ground incrementally as the analyst presses. When Takayama pushes on pricing ("Are you now saying more clearly that you are not really going to do it?"), the answer sharpens honestly: "We have considered various possibilities internally. As a result, at this point, we are not thinking about raising prices in the near term." Defers the ultimate verdict to the customer: "whether our company is superior or our peers are superior is still unclear in many respects, and we believe that is something our customers will judge."
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, often self-graded as negative. On the dropped power-module project: "I see that as a somewhat negative factor"; on the goodwill impairment: "I see it as a negative that… we recorded a substantial goodwill impairment." No burying, no reframing as opportunity — the disappointment is named, then the recovery plan follows ("we will definitely launch them in 2026").
6. **Hedging style** — Heavy epistemic honesty about the limits of its own visibility, repeatedly. "to be honest, we still have not fully read how fast demand may increase going forward"; "what we still cannot see clearly is whether it stops there or not"; "2026 and 2027 have started to come a bit more into view. As for 2028 and 2029 and beyond, however, the situation there is still unclear"; "we do not fully grasp all of the details there either." Uncertainty is stated as fact, not smoothed over.
7. **Refusals** — Declines specifics politely, with a reason, while confirming the direction: "I cannot speak specifically, but depending on the board design and concept, there are some differences in size"; on how small the price hit could get, "because those talks are still ongoing, I would prefer not to comment today on what size of amount might be possible." Never a flat "no comment" — always a bounded partial answer.
8. **Speaker split (three registers, blended)** — **Nakajima (President):** strategy and long-cycle framing, customer-relationship posture, the "15-year peak / 2030" narrative. **Minamide (Deputy President):** the variance bridge and the cash discipline — speaks numbers as decomposed factors ("price declines were a negative JPY105 billion… fixed costs… product mix"), volunteers the caveats ("if you back out the net effect of those items, the increase in profit would be somewhat larger"). **Omori (Capacitor BU):** the demand/pricing detail and the "procurement over price" top-bin kernel. The account is a blend: Nakajima register for narrative, Minamide register for figures, Omori register for the capacitor specifics.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the deferential "Yes." acknowledgment · cross-referencing colleagues ("as Omori mentioned earlier") · soft-commit verbs ("we would like to", "we want to") · concession markers ("on the other hand", "that said") · flat self-graded bad-news ("I see that as a somewhat negative factor") · the repeated honest-visibility hedge ("to be honest, we still have not fully read…", "we still cannot see clearly") · deferring the verdict to customers · the parenthetical precision-correction habit · Minamide's decomposed-factor number register · manufacturing-load vocabulary.
- STRIP: any manufactured excitement the translation might tempt ("major expansion cycle", "very strong growth", "clearly entering") should be delivered flat, not as a rally-cry · no superlative stacking · no "record-high" chest-beating beyond the plain figure · the long-cycle "2030 peak / edge-device era" evangelism is a page-verified frame only where the page carries it, never a sales pitch · never let the candor tip into false modesty theatre — it states limits, it does not perform humility.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral: "Server-capacitor growth was previously guided at ~30% and is now seen at roughly 85–90% annualized; whether it continues is uncertain."
  → In voice: "We had previously said a volume increase of about 30%. When we look at the immediate situation, we now see something closer to 85–90% on an annualized basis. When we look at that calmly, what we still cannot see clearly is whether it stops there or not — early-stage demand tends to grow rapidly, as with autos and smartphones in the past. It could remain a doubling-type situation for the next two to three years. Beyond that, to be honest, we cannot fully read the situation."
- Neutral: "The first power-module project was dropped after a firmware defect; the next is with the same customer from June."
  → In voice: "Just before full-scale production we discovered a firmware defect, and the product's life turned out shorter than expected, so the first project was dropped. I see that as a somewhat negative factor. The next project is with the same customer, and because the first had a short life, it is expected to involve a larger volume. Preparations have been progressing, but the final confirmation stage is still under way."
- Neutral: "In the top data-center bin, procurement is prioritized over price and no price-cut requests have been received."
  → In voice: "In data centers, the structure is one where procurement is prioritized over price. This area needs very advanced technology and imposes a heavy capacity load, so the number of manufacturers able to supply it is likely to be very limited. For that reason, we have not received any requests at all to lower prices. That said, whether our company is superior or our peers are superior is something our customers will judge."
- Neutral: "The company is not raising prices near-term despite the rumored hike; the profit gain is mix, not price."
  → In voice: "We have considered various possibilities internally. As a result, at this point, we are not thinking about raising prices in the near term. We place great importance on medium- to long-term relationships; we will not adopt a policy of simply raising prices in pursuit of short-term profit. The composition here is a mix improvement, not a hike — that is a fair way to look at it."

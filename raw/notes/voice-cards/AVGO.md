# Voice card — AVGO (Broadcom Inc.)

- built: 2026-07-11 · source: Q1 FY2026 earnings call (2026-03-04, Quartr transcript) + Q2 FY2026 earnings call (2026-06-03, Quartr transcript) — two full calls, fuller fingerprint
- speakers: Hock Tan (President & CEO — the personality: clipped, blunt, dry), Kirsten Spears (CFO — the numbers register; Amie Thuener introduced as incoming but does not speak substantively)
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE Hock Tan and never fabricates quotes attributed to him)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Two speeds, and the contrast IS the fingerprint. Under a direct question he collapses to one line, sometimes one word: Ben Reitzes — "Can you grow in 2028 with what you see in supply?" → "**Yes.**" Ross Seymore — "The rack versus chip side of things, is that all clarified now?" → "**No rack. It's all chips.**" (then the staccato four-way: Spears "We're in the chip business only." / Tan "We only chips." / Spears "Only chips."). When defending strategy he loops instead — long, repetitive runs that circle the same word rather than build like Jensen's enumerations: "these six guys are all… very look at XPUs and AI in a very strategic manner. They don't think one generation at a time. They think multiple generation, multiple years." The default is short; the looping only appears when he's insisting on a frame.

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Opens answers with a flat "**Well,**" (near-universal: "Well, what we see…", "Well, that's a great question", "Well, good question", "Well, I don't think so", "Well, there's a huge demand"). Master-word: "**strategic**" — set against "**optionality**" and "**transactional**" ("It's a strategic play. It's not optionality"; "as opposed to a transactional roadmap"). Insistence markers: "**you have to understand one thing**", "**you have to realize**", "**realize one thing**". Familiar-dismissive customer reference: "**these six guys**", "**these few guys**", "**those few guys**", "**these same few guys**". Dismisses the market: "**the noise**", "**all the hubris, noise out there**". Flat self-correction: "**Sorry, not five, six. Six.**"

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Sparse and industrial, the opposite of Jensen's lush analogies. His habitat is the production floor and the chessboard: "Anybody can design a chip in a lab that works well. Can you produce **100,000 of those chips quickly at yields that you can afford?**" Chooses his words against inflation — catches himself mid-metaphor: "in this generative AI **race** that we are in… and I shouldn't use the word race, let's call it **progression**… I mean, it's a competition." Rare vivid image, and it's a put-down: CPO as "**bright, shiny objects**." Otherwise literal: "line of sight," "the power shell," "align quite a few other things in place."

4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers bluntly, often correcting the premise, sometimes with a dry put-down — never Jensen's grant-and-bless. To Tim Arcuri's gross-margin-compression question: "I hate to tell you that **you must be a bit hallucinating.**" To a mischaracterization: "Oh, **I have to correct you on that.**" Reframes the question onto his own terms before answering: "In a way, what you're really saying is…" / "to answer your question." When a question is muddled he asks for it back rather than bluff: "I'm not sure I fully understand your question, CJ… could you clarify what you mean disaggregate?" then "That word disaggregation kind of **threw me off.**"

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, bounded, then moved past — not fronted, not buried. On losing share at his biggest customer: "we also **accept the fact** that… there'll be **some diversity of sources** for them" (wrapped in "a very substantial dollar amount"). On the networking mix peaking: "This 40%, I consider as almost a situation where **stars align**… I see that as probably **as high as that percentage… would go.** The more expected… would be closer to around 30%." He manages expectations down honestly rather than spinning them.

6. **Hedging style** — Visibility-anchored, directional, never precise where he won't commit. Signature: "**line of sight**" and "**our visibility runs all the way to 2028**… Three months ago… pretty much 2027. Today, it runs to 2028." Floor-not-point on the big number: "We basically say it continues to be **in excess of** $100 billion." Directional qualifiers: "**not far from**", "**getting close to** 10 GW", "**in the range of**", "**pretty much**", "**by and large, yes**", "$/GW varies, **sometimes quite dramatically.**" Refuses the quarterly guide: "**We're not trying to guide you every quarter** what 2027 would be like."

7. **Refusals** — Flat, no charm-softening. "I'd rather not answer that, but we're okay. As Kirsten said, we're good on our dollars and margin." When he won't give a number he **repeats an intensifier in its place**: asked the Google contract value — "it's a commitment that is **very substantial in dollars. Very substantial amount of dollars.**" Corrects rather than deflects when a premise is wrong: the "backstopped by Broadcom chips" framing — "Oh, I have to correct you on that… we use our TPU chips… to provide the compute capacity to Anthropic. It wasn't backstop in that sense."

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Kirsten Spears (CFO) speaks the numbers in a clean, basis-point-precise, record-stacking register: "Gross margin was 77.1%… **down 230 basis points** year-on-year… operating margin **increased 200 basis points** to 67.3%… Adjusted EBITDA of $15.2 billion or 69%… **a record** $10.3 billion." Careful to separate cause from noise: "This decline in gross margin **does not represent a structural change**… Rather, it reflects product mix." Ends with an instruction, not a flourish: "We highly recommend that investors **model semiconductor and infrastructure software margins separately.**" Confirmation tic "…right?" ("it's accretive because we have strong operating leverage, right?"). The account speaks figures in HER register: precise, structural-vs-mix, model-the-segments-separately.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the flat "Well," opener · one-line / one-word answers under pressure ("Yes."; "No rack. It's all chips.") · premise-correction and dry put-down when a question is wrong ("I have to correct you on that"; "you must be a bit hallucinating") · the strategic-vs-optionality / strategic-vs-transactional dichotomy · "these six guys" familiar-dismissive customer reference · "line of sight" / "visibility runs to 2028" hedging · floor-not-point on big numbers ("in excess of") · the refusal formula ("I'd rather not answer that") and repeating an intensifier in place of a withheld number ("very substantial dollars") · dismissing chatter as "noise / hubris" · flat self-correction ("Sorry, not five, six. Six.") · CFO basis-point precision + structural-vs-mix + "model the segments separately" + "…right?".
- STRIP: "**insatiable**" / "simply insatiable" / "sustainable and steepening" demand framing · "**vastly superior technology**", "by far way out there", "we will not see competition… for many years" competitive boasting · superlative "record" stacking used as cheerleading (keep a record figure only when it's the actual reported number) · "bright, shiny objects" as a swipe at a rival technology the company itself later claims to lead (keep the dismissive register, drop the specific tech put-down unless the page carries it) · any market/valuation cheerleading (he mostly avoids this already — stay dry).

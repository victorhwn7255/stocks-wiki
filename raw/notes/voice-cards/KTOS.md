## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Clipped, punchy declaratives fired in bursts, often the same phrase twice for emphasis. "Our engine business, KTT, is ripping right now. It is ripping." / "On your first two questions, yes and yes. Yes and yes." / "Nothing. Zero." The cadence is a hammer, not a paragraph.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Checkpoint tags close a run: "Okay?" and "All right?" ("We've won SLCM-N. Okay?"). The imperative "Think X" frames a program: "Think Golden Dome." / "Think the Air Force's family of affordable mass missiles." / "Think a selling price of $40,000-$60,000 each." Direct first-name address to every analyst ("Sheila", "Mike", "Noah", "Joe") and, once, "brother." Openers: "Yeah.", "Yep.", "Right.", "Great question."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Sports and cards and folksy body-talk, not physics. "grand slam home run" (Prometheus). "As the hand of cards comes out, we'll let you know." "My tummy tells me that parabolics will win there." Colorful put-downs: a rival design is "a runway independent super-duper, pooper-scooper drone"; small tuck-in targets are "not like hand grenades."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers pressure with a flood of program names, customers, and unit counts, then a confident snap: "We feel real good." / "We feel extremely strong on our forecast." Combative-confident even when granting a rival: "Even though we think we're the best always, we might not win because the government is trying to rebuild... competitors."
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, sometimes with an apology, never buried. Labor: "It's better, but it's not great." Q2 step-down: "We're trying to be conservative. We are." Non-core commercial line: "I cannot tell you that it's a major strategic initiative, because it's not, and I apologize."
6. **Hedging style** — Personal and folksy, not statistical. "It just depends on the mix. I'm not trying to obfuscate this. I'm telling you this is happening real-time." / "Is it gonna be $1.5 trillion or $1.3 trillion? I don't know." / "My tummy tells me... but I don't know. This is my opinion."
7. **Refusals** — Blunt, repeated, apologetic — a classified wall he won't scale. "I can't talk about it. I can't, brother, I can't. I can't talk. I'd like to, but I can't talk about it. I apologize." / "I can't comment on the Middle East." / "I can't get ahead of the customer until they announce it."
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Deanna Lund is the measured counterweight: sequenced figures, explicit ranges, and revenue-recognition mechanics spelled out plainly — "If they're 50% complete, then we would record revenue at 50% at the time of the award, and the remaining would be as it is completed." Caution is her default: "we have included conservative estimates," "we're not giving any guidance for next year." The account speaks figures in HER register: precise, bounded, conservative.

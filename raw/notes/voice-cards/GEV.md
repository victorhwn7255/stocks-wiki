## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Measured, methodical, long-cycle. Strazik works through structure a step at a time — country by country, slot by slot, gigawatt by gigawatt — in medium-length declaratives that rarely snap short. "We saw continued strength in new gas turbine agreements in Q1, signing 21 GW in countries like the U.S., Vietnam, Mexico, Brazil, and Canada." The cadence is a man walking an investor down a factory line, not a founder riffing.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Two phrases recur like a heartbeat: **"meet this moment"** ("how, in a very capital efficient way, we meet this moment and serve this market"; "our humility and hunger to meet this moment"; "We'll continue to invest to meet this moment") and **"it's just a start / this is just the beginning"** — used to open AND close the call ("We've had a solid start to 2026, but it is just that, a start"; "This is just the beginning"). Answers analysts with a warm "You bet" and "I would just tell you…". "Iterating / iteration" is his word for deal-making ("very actively iterating with a very diverse set of customers"; "a productive iteration we're going to keep working hard on"). "Incremental electrons." "Directionally." Pairs ambition with restraint: **"humility and hunger," "conviction, with humility."**
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Craftsmanship and construction: a **"string of pearls"** of products from generation to the data center, and **"attach more LEGO blocks"** to that string. Also human/team: "a team just starting to get their feet under them," "humility and hunger." Industrial-civic: "re-industrialize an industry that matters to the world's future." The metaphors are about assembling and integrating, not conquering.
4. **Pushback behavior** — Reframes rather than deflects. To Barclays' "why are you taking so much share?": "we don't really internally talk a lot about taking share per se… This is really about continuing to do good business." To Goldman's "will you cap orders past 2030 like your competitor?": grants the rival's logic, then differentiates on fundamentals — "in our case, the dynamic will be different than our competitors because our installed base is so much larger." Answers with operational detail, never heat.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly and bounded to "what we can control." Wind: "the team is executing with discipline and is focused on the factors within our control"; the losses are stated, not hidden ("Wind EBITDA losses were $382 million in the quarter, in line with our expectations" — CFO). On the framework agreements that haven't closed: "We have not closed one of those transactions to date. Admittedly, we've been having these conversations for a period of time" — the "Admittedly" is the tell.
6. **Hedging style** — Market-humble, not self-doubting. He defers to what the market and customers will do: "based on how we see things today," "We need to keep seeing where this market takes us," "we'll learn more about where this market takes us as we sell out of 2029 and 2030," "if things go our way," "directionally." Uncertainty is voiced as respect for a long cycle he doesn't fully control.
7. **Refusals** — Declines to quantify prematurely rather than to answer: "Quantifying that productivity opportunity, we need time." Declines a framing itself (the "taking share" reframe above). The CFO declines a specific breakout but hands over a helper number: "We don't specifically disclose to you Gas Power orders… I'll give you the number, and it's $200 million to back out there."
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Ken Parks (CFO): slide-by-slide and orderly ("Turning to slide six…"; "Turning to Power on slide seven"), number trains with a driver attached to every figure ("$18.3 billion, a 71% increase year-over-year, and a book-to-bill ratio of approximately 2"), guidance always paired with its cause ("$44.5 billion-$45.5 billion, up $500 million compared to our previous expectation due to additional growth at Electrification"). His signature move is the volunteered helper: "Maybe I'll just add one data point… to help you kind of size the pricing." Culture-as-numbers: "we talk about Lean a lot, and we do that not just for the words, but because it is a part of our culture." The account speaks figures in HIS register: ordered, driver-attached, disclosure-careful.

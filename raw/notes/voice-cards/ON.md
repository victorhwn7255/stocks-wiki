# Voice card — ON (onsemi / ON Semiconductor Corporation)

- built: 2026-07-12 · source: Q1 FY2026 earnings call (2026-05-04, Quartr transcript via Yahoo — the only transcript on file; single-call fingerprint, noted)
- speakers: Hassane El-Khoury (President & CEO — the operator/strategist voice), Thad Trent (CFO — the numbers/mechanics voice)
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from the transcript; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE El-Khoury or Trent and never fabricates quotes attributed to them)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — El-Khoury builds in medium runs that start with a framing verb and end on a plain payoff, often a bald contrast. "The number of passenger vehicle was down 6%. Our revenue was actually up. Therefore, that tells you it is a content story." He answers by decomposing: "I'll take them one at a time"; "Let me first tackle the first one." Trent is tighter and list-like — he stacks figures in short declaratives ("Cash from operations was $239 million. Free cash flow was $217 million. Capital expenditures were $22 million, or 1.4% of revenue.").

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — El-Khoury: "Look,…" and "Yeah, look,…" to open a candid answer; "You can think about it as…" / "if you will" / "call it,…" as hedged framing; "et cetera"; "in our sweet spot"; "puts and takes"; "the power tree" and "from the grid to the processor" / "from the wall to the core" as the spatial map; "surgical" vs "broad"; "we'll see how that plays out." Trent: "As I said,…", "the way we're seeing it right now,…", "line of sight," "fab right actions," "loadings," "match the demand signal." Both use engineering-plant language ("utilization," "fall-through," "operating leverage the model was designed to deliver").

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Plumbing/electrical topology and the factory floor. The recurring image is the "power tree" walked end to end — "from the grid to the processor," "from the wall to the core," "all the way to the outlet," "inside the rack… all the way to the infrastructure." Cost math gets concrete unit anchors (a 120 kW rack vs an 800V rack; content "almost 10x inside the rack"). No sports, no war, no grand-civilizational framing — it stays in the cabinet and on the fab line.

4. **Pushback behavior** — El-Khoury reframes the axis of the question rather than dodging: asked about regional recovery he replies "It's no longer a regional conversation. It is more a market"; asked unit-demand-vs-content he lands "that tells you it is a content story." He answers directly once he's set his own terms ("To answer your question directly, we absolutely have the focus…"). Trent handles model-mechanics pushback by re-deriving the arithmetic out loud ("every point of utilization is 25-30 basis points… another 200 basis points from fab right… you can get over 50% when you do the math").

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, then bounded and moved past. He states the down number and pairs it with the offsetting fact in the same breath ("China passenger vehicle market down 6%… our China automotive revenue grew"). He names the cycle bluntly ("this cycle has found its low point," "moving away from the bottom of the cycle") rather than dressing it up. Trent front-loads the cost drag honestly ("we're already seeing the impact of input costs being higher… although we're not seeing the impact of the higher pricing yet").

6. **Hedging style** — Explicit refusal-to-over-commit plus visibility language. "I don't wanna guide the outlook from a seasonality perspective. Let me give you how the year's laying out." "We'll see how that plays out." "That will be an operational decision." "Line of sight." Trent bounds with mechanics and ranges rather than adjectives ("flat to up slightly," "roughly," "we'll match the demand signal"). Forward claims get tagged as targets, not results ("towards that $1 billion in 2030").

7. **Refusals** — Declines product-level disaggregation cleanly and says why: "we're not breaking it down to that level by product family. But I will tell you it is everywhere from the low voltage all the way through the high voltage." "I guess I have to see what the level of detail I would give." Declines to name customers past a partner reference; declines to guide seasonality. The refusal is stated plainly, then immediately followed by whatever he WILL say.

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — El-Khoury carries the strategy, the end-market map, and the candor ("Look,…", the power-tree walk, the content-vs-units reframe). Trent carries the model mechanics and every figure: dense number trains, utilization-to-margin arithmetic, guidance ranges, the exits math ("we've exited approximately $50 million in Q1… another $30-40 million in Q2… roughly get to that $300 million… we're done at the end of 2026"). The account blends them: El-Khoury's register for narrative and the power-tree map; Trent's register — bounded, arithmetic, hedged-by-mechanics — for every number.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: "Look,…" candor opener · "You can think about it as…" / "call it,…" hedged framing · the power-tree spatial walk ("grid to the processor," "wall to the core," "inside the rack all the way to the infrastructure") · "I'll take them one at a time" decomposition · the flat down-number-then-offset construction · "puts and takes" / "line of sight" / "sweet spot" · "surgical vs broad" · the clean refusal-then-what-I-will-say · Trent's number-train + utilization-to-margin arithmetic register · the "we'll see how that plays out" open-ended hedge.
- STRIP: "clear inflection point," "tangible results," "meaningful," "healthy," "very good / feel very good," "leading position," "technology partner of choice," "best system-level performance," "resilient," "differentiated" as an adjective, "aggressive growth" — the earnings-call self-congratulation. Also strip any "congratulations on the quarter"-style warmth and any framing that makes the recovery sound more certain than the source hedges it.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral fact: "FY2025 gross margin fell to 33.1% from 45.4%, but Q1 FY2026 non-GAAP gross margin expanded for a third straight quarter to 38.5% as manufacturing utilization rose to 77%."
  → In voice: "Look, I won't pretend the year was pretty — full-year margin came down to 33.1% from 45.4%. But here's how it's laying out: Q1 margin expanded for the third quarter in a row, to 38.5%, and utilization went to 77%. You can think about it as the structural changes starting to show in the model. That's the number; the rest we'll watch."

- Neutral fact: "AI data center revenue was about $250M in FY2025 and management expects it to double in FY2026 — a management framing on the earnings call, not disaggregated in the SEC filings."
  → In voice: "We talked last year about $250 million in AI data center revenue, and we now expect to double that this year. I'll be straight about what that is and isn't: it's what we said on the call, not a line broken out in our filings. Take it as a target, not a booked result."

- Neutral fact: "Margin compression in FY2025 was concentrated in PSG (-2250bp over two years) and ISG (-3360bp), while AMG margin was stable (+460bp)."
  → In voice: "Let me take them one at a time. Two segments carried the pain — Power Solutions down about 2,250 basis points over two years, Intelligent Sensing down about 3,360. Analog held; it was actually up about 460. So it's not one story across the board. It's surgical, not broad."

- Neutral fact: "One distributor accounted for roughly 11% of revenue in FY2025, up from 10% in FY2024, across all three reportable segments — the customer is not named in the filings."
  → In voice: "One distributor was about 11% of revenue last year, up from 10% the year before, and that's across all three segments. We don't name it. You can think about it as a distribution relationship, not a single end customer — but the concentration is there and it's ticked up, so I'll put it on the table."

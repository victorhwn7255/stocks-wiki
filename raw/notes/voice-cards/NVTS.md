## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Runs that pile clause on clause and pin each one down with a tag question, then reset. Not Jensen's clean chained declaratives — messier, more verbal, more "let me back up." A thought loops, self-restarts ("If you look at the first two, which is what your question is, right?"), then lands. Second-language English texture: article drops and number slips he never corrects ("our GaN and high voltage SiC product can deliver", "diluted shares outstanding was approximately $230 million").
2. **Signature tics (the heaviest in the cohort)** — "okay?" / "okay." as a checkpoint after almost every clause — the single loudest tic (30+ on the call). "right?" as an even more frequent filler tag. "so on and so forth", "so to speak", "for lack of better terms", "kind of", "you know". Opens Q&A answers with the analyst's first name + "this is Chris" ("Okay. Hi, Tristan. This is Chris. Thanks for the question."). Doubled honesty markers: "too early to declare victory" and "far too early".
3. **Enumeration by phase, not by list** — Doesn't do Jensen's "top five things"; instead lays the story out as a sequence of numbered *phases* / *steps* and walks the listener along it: "The first step… The first phase… That's the first phase… The fourth step is…"; "we have four markets… Each of them have a different flavor." Teaching move: "let's zoom out a little bit, right?"
4. **Self-restart, not self-correction** — Where Jensen revises a count and moves on, Chris abandons a sentence and re-launches it: "If you look at the evolution of the architecture, let's zoom out a little bit, right? Today, in the current architecture, the traditional architecture is 50 V busbar, where the, you know, voltage from the grid…". The listener rides the reboot.
5. **Metaphor habitat** — Homespun / poker-and-kitchen, not physics: "sit at the big table" (his signature — used repeatedly for having both technologies), "the proof is in the pudding", "you can make the math", "the mental law" (his own coinage for a rule of thumb), power "explosion", "chasing orders within the quarter." Measures his own team by physical effort: "I measure my team… in term of the number of samples we ship… the amount of energy the customer is spending."
6. **Pushback behavior** — Warm, never combative; teaches through the pushback. Grants the analyst's persistence with a smile ("You're very persistent asking the same question every quarter, so I appreciate that"), then re-walks the phases. On a wrong-premise question (government forcing transformer upgrades) he answers the last part first, flatly denies knowledge, then pivots to the story he wants to tell.
7. **Bad-news / uncertainty delivery** — Fronts the hedge, THEN the optimism: "Although far too early to declare victory, we effectively completed our realignment… Navitas is back to growth." Owns what he won't claim: "We don't break down by markets, right? We don't." Refusal formula on pipeline: "I'm not gonna comment on design win and engagement with customers unless the customer wants so. You're gonna see the proof point in the backlog." Deflects the profitability-timing question by handing the analyst the arithmetic: "Jeremy, you can make the math, right?… it will take us to be in the high 30s from a revenue standpoint to be profitable… I'm not gonna guide specific."
8. **Hedging style** — Effort-and-visibility framed rather than statistical: "I look at Q2 with confidence", "we have longer visibility", "probably six to nine months behind", "probably Q1 to Q2 next year." Gives exactly one extra data point and fences it: "That's the only quarter I'm gonna give you, right?"
9. **CEO vs CFO split** — Tonya Stevens (CFO, first call): introduces herself before the numbers ("My corporate finance career spans more than 30 years… 17 years at Intel… the last seven years at Lattice"), leans hard on balance-sheet strength as the reassurance ("a very strong balance sheet… over $221 million in cash and no debt… gives us a pretty long runway"), and speaks guidance in bounded form ("$10.0 million plus or minus half a million", "39.25% plus or minus 75 basis points", "at the midpoint"). Her hype word is "laser-focused" / "incredibly excited" (STRIP). The account speaks figures in HER register: balance-sheet-first, plus-or-minus bounded, runway-framed.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Measured, complete, engineer-executive sentences that build an argument methodically, then land on a flat plainspoken close. Not clipped, not looping — deliberate. Runs are threaded with "I think" and softened with "you know," but the thought resolves cleanly. Close of a capacity answer: "We can build our facilities, you know, with a good risk posture." Close of a forward statement: "Those aren't in hand yet... We'll see."

2. **Signature vocabulary & verbal tics** — Opens answers "Yeah, sure, Scott." / "Yeah, sure, Jed." / "Yeah, sure, Pete." (first names, flat, not warm-effusive). Ubiquitous "I think" as both opener and hedge. Filler "you know" throughout. Recurring hedge-closer "We'll see." / "We'll see how that comes out." Intensifier of choice: "robust" (robust demand, robust backlog, robust bookings, robust growth). **Distinctive tic: misuses "existential" to mean essential/critical** — "existential qualified nuclear workforce," "existential capacity," "the existential capacity when the order comes," "an existential and important risk." "Happily," "knock on wood." "That's how we're thinking about it."

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Manufacturing floor, defense-industrial history, and sports. Signature line (from his own Washington Times op-ed): "BWXT is not betting on a horse, we are betting on the race." Hockey: "we're trying to skate to where we think the puck is going." Hunting: "we've sort of got a license to go hunting, we'll turn it into something." Growth: "green shoots of growth," "burning platform," "first shovel in the ground." Deep historical grounding: "what the global industrial base did... in the 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s, it built 600 large reactors."

4. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative (vault confirms combativeness count NOT incremented for Geveden). Grants the analyst's premise, then restructures the answer: "Maybe I'll break it into two pieces." "I kind of divide it into two areas." Frequently asks a clarifying question back before answering: "Again, you're talking about delivery risk for the balance of plant and the nuclear island, Jed?" Reframes a competitor/miss without defensiveness: on the ANPI awards he didn't win — "no disappointment because we didn't pursue those opportunities."

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly and candidly, named not buried. Tech-99: "we are in the sort of the grueling last mile of that, around some issues with product quality, filtration, concentration." Admits the limit of his own knowledge: "I have, frankly, imperfect clarity around that." Names industry failures directly: "We've got, you know, some poor examples of project delivery, Vogtle and others." No drama, no dressing-up, no burying.

6. **Hedging style** — Explicit uncertainty markers plus personal epistemics. "maybe a little hard to say." "that's a bit of guesswork, right?" "it's very immature at this point." "a bit of a TBD for us." "I don't have detailed insight... but I hear that it's reasonably on track." Forward statements are clearly flagged as not-yet-real: "Those aren't in hand yet." "We don't have orders yet, obviously, but we're trying to skate to where we think the puck is going."

7. **Refusals** — Declines figures he doesn't yet have, always with the reason attached. On per-project content: "that's a bit of guesswork, right? We don't know what content we're gonna win yet." Honest non-answer when out of his lane: "I don't know the details on that. I suspect that we do." CFO declines then partly relents: "we didn't put it in the public release, but it was roughly around $200 million."

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — **Geveden (CEO):** strategic narrative, historical framing, dry wit, hedged forward optimism, the "race not the horse" merchant-supplier posture, credential-obsession ("nuclear manufacturing credentials are rare and hard to get... N stamps and NPT stamps and U stamps"). **Fitzgerald (CFO):** measured, sequential, segment-by-segment number register, conservative and caveat-wrapped. "Fourth quarter revenue was $886 million, up 19% year-over-year." "it is possible that CapEx may exceed that level in future periods." "roughly around $200 million. In line with the multiples that we've seen." Guidance-cadence phrasing: "about 55% of full-year EBITDA anticipated in the second half." Words like "prudent," "disciplined execution," "carefully balancing." The account speaks figures in HIS register: bounded, segmented, hedged.

  → In voice: "I'll put a number on the table that cuts both ways: 68% of our revenue comes from the U.S. Government. That's a national-security franchise more than a hundred years deep, and it's a concentration, both at the same time. I'd rather you have it than not."
- Neutral: "Management frames nuclear-powered data centers as future optionality, not current revenue."
  → In voice: "There's an outside story and an inside story on AI. The outside story - that nuclear will power the data centers of the future - I think that's a reasonable expectation. But that's all in the windshield for us. It's not part of the current business mix."
- Neutral: "BWXT is the only commercial-scale TRISO fuel producer delivering today; capacity is a few hundred kilograms per year."
  → In voice: "We are the only producer of TRISO at any scale at this point - a few hundred kilograms a year, and that's about the limit of it right now. While others are planning to produce advanced nuclear fuel, we are delivering today. To go bigger we're looking hard at a large-scale plant. That's what the market needs."
- Neutral: "Forward SMR component orders are expected but not yet booked."
  → In voice: "It's my expectation we'll have additional orders for the BWRX-300 this year, and for the AP1000 this year. Those aren't in hand yet. We're trying to skate to where we think the puck is going, because these are long-cycle projects. We'll see."

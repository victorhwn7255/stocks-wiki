## Fingerprint

### Neil Brinker (CEO)

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Methodical plant-walk cadence. Builds orderly lists of priorities rather than looping: "So a few things. One is there's a lot of conversations about the now, which is securing capacity... The next piece is around our innovation and technology." Answers earnestly, at operational altitude, then closes with a short conviction line. Occasionally snaps to one word when the answer is a fact ("Twenty." for chiller lines; "Yes." on whether LTAs get announced).
2. **Signature vocabulary & verbal tics** — The **80/20** discipline (the load-bearing word: "using 80/20 as a guide," "our 80/20 focus and market-leading technology"). The **funnel / probability funnel** as his conviction device — projects "moving from 40 or 50% probability into the 80%-90% category," "the top of the order funnel is swelling," "the LTA simply accelerates it through the probability funnel to a high degree of confidence." **Capacity** language everywhere: "locking up capacity," "capacity locking agreement," "spoken for," "a high degree of it is going to be for the LTA." **Pivot / modularity**: "A chiller line can be converted to produce modular data centers." **Playbook**: "We know the process, we know the playbook. We can do more of it." Fillers: "Yeah," "you know," "certainly," "for sure." Says **"high-margin, high-growth businesses"** as a fixed phrase.
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Sales-pipeline plumbing and the factory floor. The funnel (probability, hit rate, top of the funnel swelling); production lines and shifts ("we lost 20 shifts due to severe weather in the south"); "front of the radar," "all hands on deck," "breakneck speed of CapEx deployment." Cooling physics rendered as customer problems: "two critical problems... the lack of power... and the amount of water," measured through "PUE and WUE."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Engages the analyst's own math rather than deflecting; grants the point openly. On Brian Drab's step-function inference: "Well, no, it's true. That's the way you're thinking about it... It's a fair point." On capacity-glut worry: "the simple answer is no, not at all." Reframes wide ranges to the operational reason ("we're giving ourselves plenty of space there... that would give us more confidence to tighten that range").
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, then reframed forward as operational and temporary. Weather: "This was despite production delays where we lost 20 shifts... The team worked extremely hard to overcome the impact." Component shortage: "We began to see a shortage of certain components late in the quarter... We're confident in our ability to manage through these short-term challenges, and while this will temporarily impact our Q1 production plans, we do not anticipate any impact on our full-year outlook." Last quarter's margin miss owned as a production cut for pilot builds.
6. **Hedging style** — Probability-funnel and calendar hedges rather than statistical ranges. "anything outside of the calendar year can be really difficult to predict... those are going to be the lower end of the probability funnel." "pending the results of the field trials... a decision won't be made for a couple quarters." "We would expect to have the capacity in place by the end of 2027. However, they may not be at full utilization yet."
7. **Refusals** — Declines customer names on NDA grounds and says so plainly, but confirms the direction: asked whether signed LTAs would be publicly announced, "Yes." Declines an AI-vs-cloud split with a genuine reason: "Our product is universal, so it can go into the cloud, it can go into compute, and it can go into AI... It's really hard to know exactly what some of these data centers are actually used for." Declines forward specifics: "I don't have an answer for that right now because we're in discussions."
8. **Signature move** — The **expanding-visibility riff**: "I say yes to that every time that's asked every 6-12 months. So it just gets bigger and bigger, and the visibility gets broader and broader... three years ago we had 8-12 months visibility... a year ago 24 months, 36 months. Now we're looking out as far as five years." Conviction is grounded in "understanding our customers' long-term strategic roadmaps," not in adjectives.

### Michael "Mick" Lucareli (CFO)

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Signposted and sequencing. Opens with a hand-off ("I'll go first, then Neil can add"), then walks the number and tells you what NOT to conclude from it. Talks directly to the analysts' models: "if you take that... that'll be supporting future sales growth."
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — The anti-extrapolation guardrail: "A couple things, Noah, that I wouldn't extrapolate, and we're not implying that we'll have 200 basis points sequentially every quarter." Clarity flags: "I wanna be clear about that," "make sure there's no confusion," "I'll try again," "just to narrow that down." Modeling verbs: "peel back the onion," "put that off to the side," "back that out," "placeholder," "run that 60-80% and 50-70%." **Sequential / basis points** as his native unit. Recovery mechanics: "material pass-through mechanisms," "surcharges," "tariff recovery," "three to six-month lag."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Accounting and model-building: onions to peel, pieces to estimate, windows of firm commits, "contract liability... reduced over the life of the contract based on future volumes," "run rate," "annualized run rate."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Patient re-explanation until the analyst has it. "To your question on growth rates, Noah, I'll try again. I'm glad, make sure there's no confusion." Bounds the enthusiasm: "we're not implying that we'll have 200 basis points sequentially every quarter."
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Named as temporary and mechanical, with a recovery timeline. "These factors are temporary, and we expect that the margins will continue to improve." Gives the cost when asked: "the weather costs about 50-100 basis points on the climate business from a gross margin standpoint."
6. **Hedging style** — Ranges plus explicit timing uncertainty. "we don't see any more than $2 billion a year right now. It's still early days." "I don't think we're quite there yet to know what that exact number will be." "we're just balancing the known and unknown at this point." "Q4 is kind of our best placeholder for this time."
7. **Refusals** — Defers precision to future guidance rather than refusing outright: "we'll provide some guidance in our Q4," "we'll dial that in and we'll know more," "later this calendar year... we'll likely give a more firm fiscal 2028 or even a 2029 outlook."
8. **CEO/CFO split** — Brinker carries strategy, customers, transformation, technology and the funnel; Lucareli carries margins, sequential cadence, cash flow, tariffs and modeling help. They **tag-team with explicit hand-offs** ("let me throw it over to Neil"; "I'll go quick, Neil then can talk about capacity") — the choreography itself is part of the fingerprint. The account speaks figures in Lucareli's register: sequential, basis-point, range-bounded, guardrailed against over-reading.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

The cadence and the discipline survive; the adjective pile does not.

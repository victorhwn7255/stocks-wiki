# Voice card — NOW (ServiceNow, Inc.)

- built: 2026-07-12 · source: Q1 FY2026 earnings call (2026-04-22, Quartr transcript — prepared remarks + Murphy/Zelnick/Borges/Weed Q&A) · speakers: Bill McDermott (Chairman & CEO — the personality), Gina Mastantuono (President & CFO — the numbers register), with Amit Zavery (President/CPO/COO) as the product-detail second voice
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE Bill McDermott and never fabricates quotes attributed to him)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Punchy declaratives fired in bursts, then a hard full-stop repetition for emphasis. Signature move is a two-word closer that lands like a period slammed down: "Nothing else in the market does this. Nothing." / "That's quintupled." / "There you have it." The rhythm is a keynote-stage cadence, not a Q&A drift — even answers get delivered as speeches.
2. **Anaphora / triple-hammer** — Repeats a stem three-plus times to build a wall: "There is no great autonomous AI platform end-to-end. There is no great Control Tower. There is no great Workflow Data Fabric. There is no great integration layer…" Also "was, is, and will continue to be" and "AI that thinks, workflows that act." The list of five "hyper-growth areas" is announced and numbered ("The first… The second…").
3. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — "AI Control Tower for business reinvention" (repeated verbatim, a brand incantation); "no sidecar AI" / "not a separate purchase"; "system of record" / "ERP for IT"; "land and expand to control and compound"; "we're just getting started" (closes multiple segments); "the rules and the rails of business"; "letting it rip"; "That's a guarantee." Opens analyst answers with warm first-name thanks: "Yeah. Thank you, Gabriela." / "First of all, Mark, thank you very much for the question."
4. **Metaphor habitat** — SPORTS and finance-aphorism, heavily. Quotes Hall-of-Fame coach Bill Walsh ("Chaos is the quarterback's natural environment"); the control tower is "the quarterback." Quotes Buffett-via-Graham: "In the short run, markets are voting machines… in the long run, they are weighing machines. I'll get on that scale with that ServiceNow brand on my chest any day." Deal comparisons reach for tech-lore ("Armis is going to be our Instagram"). Numbers get dramatized ("the number three economy in the world is cybercrime… 1 trillion a month").
5. **Pushback behavior** — Gracious, NEVER combative — reframes the worry into a bigger opportunity and answers with conviction, not defensiveness. On "will customers negotiate down the core?": "No, we're not getting negotiated down on the core. Actually, we're redoubling our focus on it… when you innovate on the platform… it reinvigorates the core." He grants the analyst's premise politely ("that's okay, that's what earnings calls are for, to clear things up"), then overwhelms it with a case study and a claim. Hands off cleanly to Amit for product depth and to Gina for figures.
6. **Bad-news delivery** — Fronted and reframed as strength, refuses the "excuse" framing outright. On the Middle East deal slippage: "we just beat and raised. It was a beat and raise, not an excuse that there happens to be a conflict or war in Iran. We're not making any excuses… we just mention that as a statement of fact." Owns the fact, denies it any narrative weight.
7. **Hedging style** — Sparse; hedges are structural/methodological, delivered by the CFO or Amit, not the CEO. Gina: the AI-revenue number "will continue to capture only the incremental contribution from our AI capabilities" and takes "a prudent view of the geopolitical environment." The CEO's own hedges are near-absent and where present are self-aware ("I think I might have understated that a little bit"). This is the promotional-intensity signature — certainty as posture.
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Gina Mastantuono: crisp, basis-point-precise, "beating the high end of our guidance across all top-line and profitability metrics." Number trains with attribution ("a 125 basis points contribution from Armis," "a 75 basis point headwind," "23.5% year-over-year constant currency growth"). Carries the disclosure discipline ("the exact same methodology… we're just hitting our goals a lot quicker"). She has her own hype tic ("incredible demand," "outstanding execution") that is a STRIP target. The account speaks figures in HER register: precise, headwind/tailwind-accounted, constant-currency-flagged.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter — HEAVIEST in the cohort)

McDermott's prepared remarks are wall-to-wall superlatives; strip aggressively, keep only the structural cadence.

- KEEP: the two-word slam-down closer ("Nothing." / "There you have it.") · anaphora / triple-hammer ("There is no great X…") · announced numbered structure ("five areas, the first…") · warm first-name analyst thanks · gracious-reframe pushback (grant the worry, answer with a case) · the "beat and raise, not an excuse" flat-fact bad-news delivery · sports/aphorism metaphor WHERE it carries a real point (the voting-machine / weighing-machine line as a stance, not a boast) · CFO basis-point headwind/tailwind accounting for figures · "we're just getting started" as a cadence closer (not as a claim).
- STRIP: every superlative and hype-brand incantation — "AI Control Tower for business reinvention," "best year ever," "the AI-defining enterprise software company of the 21st century," "one of the most remarkable growth trajectories," "world-class," "incredible demand," "unbelievable platform," "unmatched," "nobody can match," "the chapter that makes everything else look like the preamble." · self-congratulation and shareholder-address flourishes ("we will never let you down," "trust what you see," "believe your own eyes," "we dare anyone," "That's a guarantee," "we would not trade positions with anyone"). · "letting it rip" / "on track for our best year ever" forward-cheerleading. · TAM-boasting ("$600 billion-plus," "eclipse the size of ServiceNow itself") unless a specific page-verified figure is being reported. · "Armis is going to be our Instagram"-class hype analogies. The cadence survives; the trophy case does not.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral fact: "50% of net new business now comes from a non-seat-based pricing model, including tokens and other assets."
  → In voice: "Here's the number I'd put in front of you. Half of our net-new business is now non-seat-based - tokens, and other assets like infrastructure and connectors. Half. Predictable seat licenses on one side, usage-based scale on the other. That's the hybrid model, and it's the shape of the shift."
- Neutral fact: "Now Assist net-new ACV is tracking $1 billion for FY2026; the AI figure captures only the incremental contribution from the AI capabilities."
  → In voice: "Now Assist is tracking a billion dollars of net-new for the year - and I want to be precise about what that number is. It counts only the incremental contribution from the AI capabilities, the exact same methodology we've always used. Same measure. We're just hitting the goal quicker than we expected."
- Neutral fact: "The stock fell ~12% after the print despite beating guidance; analysts pressed on organic vs inorganic AI contribution."
  → In voice: "We beat and raised across the board, and the stock still fell about 12% the same day. Both of those are true. The pushback we heard was on how much of the AI growth is us versus the businesses we bought - Moveworks, Veza, Armis. That's a fair question, and it's the one we didn't break out on the call."
- Neutral fact: "Management defended the core against negotiation pressure ('we're not getting negotiated down on the core') but did not disclose organic vs inorganic AI uplift."
  → In voice: "Will customers negotiate the classic stack down to fund the AI? Thank you for asking it directly. Our answer: no, we're not getting negotiated down on the core - if anything we're redoubling on it. What we did not do is put an organic-versus-inorganic split on the table. So take the conviction, and keep the open question."

## Fingerprint (institutional character — posture, not tics)

1. **Posture — the diversified platform that can afford the bet.** The page frames Tuopu as a large, founder-controlled Tier-1 auto-parts platform ("eight product lines," ~¥30,000 of content per vehicle) whose scale is the point: the robot-actuator leg and the datacenter-cooling leg are *extensions of an already-standing business*, not a bet the company depends on. So the voice is confident-because-broad — it never sounds like a startup pitching survival. It leads from the platform: "we build cars' guts at scale; the new legs grow off that base."
2. **How it frames the new legs — as horizontal extensions.** The page's own logic is that robot actuators grow out of line-controlled-brake (IBS) R&D depth ("mechanical, reducer, motor, e-control, software"), and datacenter cooling grows out of the thermal-management line. The register mirrors that: new legs are described as reaching *sideways* from existing capability, never as a leap. "We already make the motor, the reducer, the control — the actuator is the same stack, integrated."
3. **Sentence rhythm.** Measured, systems-level, list-shaped. It reaches for structure — product lines, subsystem stacks (cold plates, pumps, manifolds, valves, sensors), the motor-reducer-controller chain. Not clipped, not looping-evangelical; it enumerates because it is a modular supplier and thinks in bill-of-materials terms.
4. **Metaphor habitat.** The factory floor and the vehicle: content-per-vehicle, one-stop system-level supply, precision machining, the production base. Analogies come from manufacturing scale and integration, not physics or history.
5. **How it owns bad news — flatly, operationally, without optics-blaming.** This is the sharpest posture marker. The page is blunt that profit fell *operationally* (not an FX/derivative artifact): revenue up double-digits, profit down, margins compressed by the China EV price war and ramp cost. The voice owns that squarely — "revenue grew, profit didn't, and that's real margin pressure, not an accounting quirk" — and notes, hedged, that the decline is *narrowing* without declaring it reversed.
6. **How it handles the humanoid leg — concrete but honest-pre-revenue.** The page lets Tuopu show more chips than its peers (explicit linear/rotary/dexterous actuators, a dedicated division, a ¥5.0B base, multiple sample deliveries) — so the voice can be specific about what's built. But it must carry the page's verdict that this is *pre-revenue* (FY2025 robot-actuator revenue ~¥13.6M, a rounding error), with no named robot customers, in a market that is itself pre-volume. Confidence in the capability; honesty about the revenue.
7. **The Tesla discipline.** The page names only "leading domestic & international NEV automakers" / "head automakers," never Tesla — the Optimus link is reported-not-confirmed in trade press. The voice NEVER asserts Tesla. It says what the page says: top automakers, unnamed.
8. **Hedging style.** Filing-register hedges: "pre-revenue," "first-batch orders," "planned but not yet completed" (the H-share listing), "narrowing but not reversed," "capacity ahead of confirmed volumes." Uncertainty is stated as fact-status, not waved away.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (Glastra)** — Medium-length declaratives that keep re-opening with a comma-and-"right?" to invite agreement, then a short reset sentence. Runs are built to *reassure*, not to dazzle. "The majority of the orders will ship in the next 12 months, right? Just to be clear… the other thing is, just to be fair, you know, it's off a lower number in the first quarter of last year." The tic-string "right? … just to be clear … just to be fair … you know" is the connective tissue.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Glastra)** — Opens Q&A warm and by first name: "Yeah. Well, thanks for the question, Lee." / "Good morning, Brian." Constant "right?" as a checkpoint. "Just to be clear," "just to be fair," "let's say" (hedging filler, ×many), "you know." Frames the whole AI story as **"the collective"** — a repeated noun, not a slogan. "Niche leadership positions." "We're often the only ones in the world, or maybe there's one other player that can do what we do."
3. **The "collective" thesis (signature move)** — Refuses to let any single business be the story. "It's not one, right?"; "It's a whole slew of applications"; "that's the strength actually — it's not one thing that can turn back on you." When an analyst asks him to rank his AI businesses: **"Brian, I hate to give a non-response, but it's actually all of the above."** The de-hyping IS the pitch.
4. **Self-correction / live counting** — Revises his own framing mid-answer and keeps going: "make their way through 3 steps in the value chain… There's a lot of steps in there." Counts loosely, then corrects the frame rather than the number. Visible thinking, unpolished.
5. **Metaphor habitat** — Manufacturing-physical, hands-on: boards, layers, drilling, spindles, RPM, throughput/precision/form-factor. Teacher-mode when a concept is unfamiliar: patiently explains why a 40-layer GPU board must be drilled mechanically ("Lasers cannot penetrate those yet"). No cosmic/industrial-revolution grandiosity — the register is a plant engineer, not a prophet.
6. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative (source-audit note: no combativeness increment for either exec). Handles the "why are you the only ones who can do that?" challenge by *granting the premise and then explaining the physics*: "No. I am happy to explain it." Grants a rival exists ("maybe there's one other player"), never claims a clean monopoly.
7. **Bad-news delivery (Buckley)** — Owned flatly and immediately reframed as *timing, not trend*. On the margin miss: "a price cost timing impact… tariff rates changed within a four-week period… aluminum went from a 25% tariff to a 50% tariff… at a rate that outpaced our ability to surcharge." Then the fix and the calendar: "neutered again in the second quarter… positive price cost ratio as we get into the third quarter." Nothing buried; everything dated.
8. **Hedging style (both)** — Discipline-as-hedge. Glastra: "we're tempering the excitement… still early in the adoption cycle… we expect it to be more meaningful 2027." Buckley: "we'll give it another quarter, and then we'll take a revisit"; deals must clear returns "without requiring heroic assumptions"; "should not materially change as a % of sales." Uncertainty is voiced as *deliberate conservatism*, not as not-knowing.
9. **Refusals** — Declines to size or rank individual businesses, and says so openly: "I'm not able to rank order them here." "If one really steps out significantly, we'll make sure to make a note of that." The refusal is principled (the collective framing), not evasive.
10. **CEO vs CFO split** — Glastra carries the thesis and the character; Buckley carries the decomposition. Buckley opens "Hey, hey Brian, it's Robert," then factors the business into clean percentages: "the robotics portion is roughly 20% of sales. The semiconductor business is roughly 10% of sales, right?" His number-register is plain, percentage-based, and always followed by a discipline caveat ("Nothing is going to outpace something else dramatically"). The account speaks figures in HIS register: percentage-anchored, hedged, and dated.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: first-name warmth on openers · the "collective / not one thing" thesis framing · "right?" checkpoints · "just to be clear / just to be fair" · patient teacher-mode explanation of physical mechanisms · grant-a-rival-exists honesty ("maybe one other player") · the tempering-the-excitement discipline · Buckley's percentage decomposition + discipline caveat · timing-not-trend bad-news delivery · dated fixes ("by the third quarter") · "without requiring heroic assumptions."
- STRIP: "robust," "very proud," "strongest team Novanta has ever had," "momentum is building," "trajectory from here is up," "record" stacking, "we're very excited/tremendous interest," and the closing-remarks cheerleading generally. Also strip Glastra's "let's say" verbal-filler spam (keep one, not ten). The cadence and the discipline survive; the pride adjectives do not.

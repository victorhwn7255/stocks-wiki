## Fingerprint (institutional character, from the page's posture)

No transcript exists, so there is no personal tic to mine. What the page HAS is a consistent institutional posture, and that posture is the fingerprint:

1. **Careful-dominant register.** Proud and unhurried about the thermal core, where the page's own language is genuinely superlative ("near-exclusive global supplier," 30-year leader, top globally on multiple products) — but the pride is stated flatly, as fact, not sold. The confidence is largest exactly where the evidence is largest (valves), and it shrinks precisely as the evidence shrinks (actuators, cooling).
2. **Scope honesty as the core habit.** The defining move is to separate what is large-and-proven from what is real-but-early, in the same breath, without letting one borrow the other's weight. Two dominant businesses that are mostly outside the AI/robot conversation; two on-thesis legs that are real but emerging and unsized. The account never lets the exciting leg inherit the core's size.
3. **Precision about stage.** Speaks in the exact vocabulary of maturity: mass production vs trial-production vs sample-delivery; sized vs not-sized; disclosed segment vs "emerging" not-yet-a-segment. Where the filings only say "electromechanical actuators" generically, the account refuses the sharper words (linear actuator, dexterous hand, harmonic reducer, roller screw) — because the company itself refuses them.
4. **Attribution discipline.** Distinguishes what an outside analyst says about it from what it says about itself. The actuator-assembly headline is someone else's forward bet; the near-exclusive valve position is its own disclosure. It carries the distinction, never collapses it.
5. **Names customers by category, not by name.** The filings say "leading NEV automakers," never a marquee name; the account keeps that discretion — a widely-reported marquee link stays reported-not-confirmed, never asserted.
6. **Numbers register: blue-chip, net-cash, plain.** Figures arrive in renminbi, unhurried, with the quality markers the page leans on (net cash, near-zero goodwill, heavy R&D, dividend-paying). When a headline number looks like a stall, the account does the honest decomposition (operating vs optical) rather than either hiding or spinning it.

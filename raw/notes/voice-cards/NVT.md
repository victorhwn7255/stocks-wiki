## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (Wozniak)** — Warm, measured, mid-length declaratives that build a list. Rarely clipped, rarely nested — a steady operator's cadence that walks you through the business one vertical at a time. Openers are personable and unhurried: "Good morning, everyone. I am pleased to share with you our outstanding first quarter results." Answers often open with a soft framing lead-in — "Well, here's what I would say…", "I would say this.", "The way that we have been looking at…" — before delivering the substance.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Wozniak)** — Segmentation-by-space: "gray space" / "white space" as the native map of a data center. Recurring nouns: "verticals", "portfolio", "backlog", "capacity", "our teams". Filler softeners: "you know," (constant), "certainly", "I would say", "as you know", "with respect to". Deference-to-detail tic — hands the numbers over: "I'll let Gary talk to some of the guidance," / "I'll let Gary talk to margins." Team-credit reflex: "our team working tirelessly", "the teams are working really hard. It's a lot of work to be able to grow at these double-digit rates." Superlative saturation (STRIP target): "outstanding", "tremendous", "record", "our future is bright."
3. **Enumeration by market-map, not by count (Wozniak)** — Where a chip-company CEO factors into numbered lists, Wozniak factors into her *operating geography*: verticals (infrastructure / industrial / commercial-resi), spaces (gray / white), geographies (Americas / Europe / Asia Pacific), customer types (hyperscalers / neoclouds / multi-tenants / distribution). Answers move through the map in order rather than announcing "there are three things." The list is a business-segment tour, not a taxonomy.
4. **Pushback behavior (Wozniak)** — Collaborative, detail-first, never defensive (vault note: CEO combativeness count NOT advanced; tone collaborative). When an analyst pushes for a number she won't give, she grants what she can, then draws the line gently: pressed on specific CDU versions — "I will say this. We've got new product… many of those new products are still to launch through this year." Pressed to split data-center revenue — "Well, I'll leave it at that. I think that's probably good color." No sharpness; the refusal is warm.
5. **Bad-news / uncertainty delivery** — Owned flatly and briefly, then moved past. On tariffs and whether relief is coming: "We've kept to our current outlook, and I guess we'll wait and see." On margin softness in Electrical Connections (Corona): names the cause plainly ("higher than expected inflation, primarily due to copper"), states the action taken ("we took pricing and productivity actions"), gives the timeline ("we expect margins to improve in Q2"). No drama, no burying — a plant-manager's matter-of-factness.
6. **Hedging style** — Operational and lumpiness-aware rather than statistical. Wozniak flags the shape of the uncertainty: "orders can be lumpy, and so they can vary… certainly month to month"; "with data centers, they tend to be lumpy." Forward visibility framed as customer-relationship-derived: "With some of our key customers, we have a view to what their demands are for several years out." Corona hedges in ranges and stacks: "mid-thirties two-year stack growth… pretty much throughout the year," "essentially flattish for the H1."
7. **Refusals** — Declines revenue splits and specific product roadmaps, always softly and always with a partial give. Formula: acknowledge the question → give qualitative color → decline the number. "I'll leave it at that. I think that's probably good color." Never "no comment"; always a warm partial.
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Beth Wozniak: narrative, verticals, customers, capacity, portfolio-transformation story; personable and team-crediting. Gary Corona (EVP & CFO): number-first and orderly — opens "Yeah. Thanks, Joe. Appreciate it," then walks the P&L line by line in a bounded, range-stacked register: "Sales of $1,242 million were up 53%… Organically, sales grew 34%… return on sales came in ahead of expectations at 20%… mid-20s incrementals in the H2… 30, 40 basis points overall for the year." The account speaks figures in HIS register: sequential, range-bounded, "you know"-punctuated, always tying a number to an action and a timeline.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Measured, teacherly mid-length declaratives that build cleanly rather than loop. Not tech-bro clipped, not Jensen-loop. A thought accretes through a plain chain: "PCI Express is really the nervous system inside of a server. All of the significant components, whether it's a GPU or a CPU or a NIC card, they talk to each other over PCI Express." Answers almost always open "Yeah. So…".

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — The load-bearing verb is **"intercept"**: "ready to intercept the ramp that happens in 2027," "we'll be ready to intercept those," "prepared to intercept that launch," "customers that are looking to intercept with NPO." Also: "Yeah. So…"; "In general, what I would say is…"; "Having said that,…"; "we listen to our customers very closely"; "coexist / coexisting"; time-anchored "the 2028 timeframe." First-name openers with the analyst ("Harlan, thank you. This is Jiten"). Sanjay's tics: "In general, if you look at our business, you can broadly divide that into three categories."

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Computing anatomy and plumbing ("the nervous system inside of a server"), quest/religion ("the Holy Grail, which is the co-packaged optics"), road/interception (the whole "intercept the ramp" family). Engineering-physical, never sports or war.

4. **Pushback behavior** — Reframes toward customer demand as the authority; never combative (vault source-audit note: zero CEO combativeness across both calls). Deflects competitive framing with a shrug: "we will continue to focus on that and do what we need to do and not get distracted by any competitive festivities" (Gajendra). Defers to customers: "they are telling us what the deployment looks like."

5. **Bad-news / concentration delivery** — The >70% single-customer concentration is *never volunteered* — reframed as diversification: "we continue to diversify our business profile with several new design wins across multiple customers." When it does cabin risk, it's disciplined, not defensive: "we will not take any opportunity that comes our way because sometimes the custom business can lead into… could be so unique to one customer and with lot of risk and margin."

6. **Hedging style** — Engineer-cautious and time-anchored. "the exact mix… remains to be seen"; "we have not quite sized it up exactly, but it is a very large opportunity"; "probably larger than"; "likely to"; "that should ship here in 2027"; "the 2028 timeframe." Ranges for guidance ("$355 to $365 million"). Uncertainty is bounded by dates and "so far," not by drama.

7. **Refusals** — Points at NDAs and partners: "I'll just maybe answer that question just given all the NDAs and other things that we need to be respectful of"; "I don't want to speak for our AI customer"; "we'll let the hyperscalers or NVIDIA provide more color"; "I can't go into much more detail than what we publicly disclose" (CFO). The refusal is always polite and sourced to a boundary, never a stonewall.

8. **CEO vs COO vs CFO split** — Mohan (CEO) carries the analogies, the "intercept," the customer-deference. Gajendra (COO) carries market-sizing and pricing pragmatism as flat aphorism: "the bigger the switch, the higher the ASP. That's the way industry works"; "one size does not fit all"; "these are not ASP-challenged." Lynch (CFO) carries figures: paired sequential-and-YoY growth ("up 14% versus the previous quarter and up 93% year-over-year"), basis-point warrant call-outs ("an estimated 200 basis point non-cash impact related to a recently executed warrant agreement"), and supply hedged flatly ("Like others within the industry, we continue to see pockets of supply challenges"). The account speaks figures in Lynch's register.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral: "Scorpio P-Series is the only PCIe 6 fabric shipping in volume; it was over 15% of last year's revenue and is expected to become the largest product line by year-end."
  → In voice: "Yeah, so — Scorpio P is still the only PCIe 6 fabric shipping in volume, and it's built to intercept the ramp, not chase it. It was about 15% of revenue last year. We expect it to become our largest line by the end of the year. That's where the deployments are telling us to be."
- Neutral: "One end customer was more than 70% of 2025 revenue; the top three were about 86%."
  → In voice: "Our own filing carries the number I won't dress up: one end customer was more than 70% of last year's revenue, and the top three about 86%. We work to broaden that with new design wins across more customers — that's the plan, and it's still in progress."
- Neutral: "Optical for scale-up could exceed the signal-conditioning opportunity, but has not been sized precisely; first co-packaged-optics deployment is expected around 2028."
  → In voice: "Optical for scale-up is a very large opportunity — probably larger than our signal-conditioning business. We haven't quite sized it up exactly. Copper and optical coexist for a long while; the path runs pluggable, then near-package, then the Holy Grail, co-packaged optics, in the 2028 timeframe."
- Neutral: "The second-quarter gross-margin guide includes a ~200 bps non-cash impact from a customer warrant."
  → In voice: "On the numbers: we guided the June quarter to about 73% gross margin. That includes an estimated 200 basis point non-cash impact from a warrant agreement with one of our customers. Like others in the industry, we still see pockets of supply challenges, but supply's in place through the end of the year."

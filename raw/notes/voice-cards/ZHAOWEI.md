## Fingerprint (institutional character, not a person's tics)

The page draws a company whose character is **precision-obsessed, vertically integrated, and technically deep** — a micro-drive engineer, not a promoter. The posture to fingerprint:

1. **Builds from the inside out.** The instinct is to describe the object by naming the parts inside it — the micro-transmission, the micro-motor, the motor-control electronics — and only then the assembly. The recurring self-image is the "1+1+1" platform: three sub-systems it owns and stacks, not a shell it buys and badges. The dexterous hand is interesting to it *because* of what's inside the palm.
2. **Specs over adjectives.** When it reaches for detail it reaches for a measured figure — degrees of freedom, millimetres, grams, newtons of grip — not a superlative. A 4mm brushless coreless motor in mass production; a 20-DOF, 600g hand. The number is the flex; the adjective is left off.
3. **Owns the wobble flatly.** The character does not smooth bad news. FY2025 was strong and it says so; Q1 2026 reversed hard — net profit down about 25% on flat-to-down revenue — and it states that without cushioning, as an engineer reads a gauge. Watch-item, not spin.

It never lets the frontier borrow certainty from the core.
5. **Concedes route-risk without flinching.** The hand architecture is unsettled — the lowest certainty-of-tech-choice in its layer — and the character's answer is to hedge with a product range rather than to claim the winning design. It says "the architecture is unsettled" as a fact about the field, not a weakness it hides.
6. **Cost-performance, not premium-leader — and says so.** The independent characterization is "cross-over entrant / cost-performance," and the voice does not upgrade itself to "leader." It positions from the precision base it already owns, next to the premium incumbent one layer over, without pretending to be it.

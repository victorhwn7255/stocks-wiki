## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (Weeks)** — Patient, looping, teacherly runs that build in announced parts and pace themselves; almost never clipped. He tells you the structure before he uses it: "I'm just gonna answer your question in sort of three layers. That is the first layer, which is…" A run accretes, then he checks in and stops. Rarely a hard snap-shut; more a soft hand-off back to the questioner.
2. **The first-name check-in tic (Weeks)** — The single loudest habit: he closes almost every answer by addressing the analyst by name and asking if he landed it. "Does that address your question, Wamsi?" · "Is that a good answer to your question, Wamsi?" · "Does that make sense to you, Wamsi? Did I answer your question?" · "That explanation helpful, Samik?" · "Is that okay on that one?" He also asks permission to continue: "Can I turn to the first question?" It reads as courtesy and control at once.
3. **Signature vocabulary (Weeks)** — "what we seek to do is…" (constant construction) · "appropriately share the risk" / "de-risk" / "share the cost and risk" (the whole risk-sharing lexicon) · "value creation" / "create so much value" / "share that value with them" · "robust" / "very, very robust demand" · "innovations" (saturation word) · "For longtime followers of Corning, you would recognize…" · "just remember" / "As you remember." Numbers-of-things framing: "we do three things," "in sort of three layers."
4. **Metaphor habitat (Weeks)** — Craft and the factory floor, materials science, and deep history. Poker: "I like our hand a lot better than I would like anybody else's." Ops-floor wisdom, delivered as a little parable: "Whenever you ask an Ops person, like, when will everything get better when we're already shut down, they will always say, 'Well, let me get up and running again.'" The room: "If you could be sitting in the room with us, which I am sure you would like to do, Samik…" And the long arc: "175 years… our long, long history," "right in our wheelhouse."
5. **Bad-news delivery (Weeks)** — Owned flatly, folded into the long arc, then a plan. Solar wafers behind schedule: "Our ramp is running behind our ambitious plans." No cushioning; then the transition plan; then Ops-floor humor to defuse. When he simply doesn't know: "I don't actually know the answer to that question off the top of my head, and everybody's looking at me like I should. So John, let us take a moment to gather that information, and we'll chat with you."
6. **Hedging style — the conviction/timing split (Weeks)** — His signature move: deep conviction on *direction*, explicit humility on *timing*, spoken in probability levels rather than ranges. "I believe deeply… that it is inevitable that those links go to photons… Calling timing is more difficult." · "it did not rise to the probability level that we felt comfortable sharing that with you." · "picking specific winners and losers, I think is problematic." · "we were also not dealing with a hockey stick." He refuses to over-speculate as a stated policy.
7. **Refusals (Weeks) — graceful deferral, not stonewalling** — Declines to name customers on principle: "our philosophy is to let our customers decide when and where they choose to make announcements on their critical supply chain decisions." Defers to a later date warmly: "Always so thoughtful when I answer this question because who I'll be interpreted as speaking for. Let me reflect on the appropriate way to answer that question. Thank you for the gift of giving me till next week to do so." He grants the analyst's premise before redirecting: "You are correct that the pricing environment is clearly favorable for those who have capacity. Our approach to increasing our profitability, though, comes primarily from how do we uniquely innovate…"
8. **Pushback behavior (Weeks)** — Re-teach the fundamentals rather than parry. When pushed on scale-up he pauses to educate the whole call: "For those of you who are less close to scale-up, what Asiya is asking about is, because transmitting information with photons is greater than three times lower power usage than using electrons…" Never combative; the vault records his combativeness as nil.
9. **CFO register (Schlesinger)** — Sequential and orderly: "Let me take the first one, Josh." / "let's start with our results." Stacks the SAME metrics in the SAME order every quarter — sales, EPS, operating margin in basis points, ROIC, free cash flow: "sales grew 14% to a record $4.4 billion. EPS grew 26% to $0.72. Operating margin expanded 170 basis points to 20.2%… ROIC grew 150 basis points to 14.2%." Careful non-disclosure: "We don't disclose, and we typically don't disclose the details of any specific agreements." Hedged guidance: "it's probably close to $0.07 of EPS… I would expect it to sort of sequentially get better over time." Uses "you can think of us as…" as a framing device. The account speaks figures in HIS register: ordered, basis-point-precise, and hedged.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral: "Optical Communications Q1 2026 sales were $1.8 billion, up 36% YoY; the segment is about 38% of company revenue."
  → In voice: "Let me put the AI piece in its proper place. Optical was $1.8 billion last quarter, up 36% - and it's a bit more than a third of the company. The rest is still glass, ceramics, automotive, solar. Does that framing make sense?"
- Neutral: "Two additional hyperscale customers signed agreements similar to Meta's; they are not named."
  → In voice: "We concluded two more, each similar in size and duration to the Meta agreement. I know you want the names. Our philosophy is to let our customers decide when and where they announce their own supply-chain decisions, so I'll leave that to them."
- Neutral: "Management believes inside-the-box optical links are inevitable but is conservative on timing; scale-up is not in the plan."
  → In voice: "I believe deeply it's inevitable that those links go to photons. Calling the timing is harder. There are scenarios between now and 2028, and scenarios that are primarily late 2028 and beyond - so we didn't bake a significant amount into the plan. We'd rather be conservative than draw you a hockey stick."
- Neutral (CFO figure): "Sales grew 18% to $4.35 billion; operating margin expanded 220 bps to 20.2%."
  → In voice (CFO register): "Sales grew 18% to $4.35 billion. Operating margin expanded 220 basis points to 20.2%. You can think of the profile as continuing to build from here."

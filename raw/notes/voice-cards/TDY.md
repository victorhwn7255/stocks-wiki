## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Measured, medium-length declaratives that open by structuring the answer and then loop back to re-anchor. Almost every reply begins "Let me start with…" / "Let me begin somewhere else." / "Let me just say it's mixed." Then "First,… Second,…". Closes warm and clipped: "For sure." / "That's great." / "I hope that answers your question."

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — First-name address on every analyst ("Of course, Greg." "Hi, Joe." "Morning, Noah." "You got me there, Noah, as you know us to be."). Fillers: "Of course," "Sure," "By and large," "Having said that," "frankly," "et cetera," "you name it," "Just to cut through it." Identity marker repeated: **"That's not us."** Grants a good question: "That's a good question. That's a very good question."

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Capital allocation and acquisition math; conglomerate history; geopolitics-as-demand. "We've made 75 acquisitions in the past 25 years… $12.8 billion… only $4 billion of it with our stock." Conglomerates "put them together then take them apart… we've been the beneficiary of taking them apart." Dismissive of overpayers: "people are willing to pay some outrageous prices to get the revenue… bless them, let them spend their money in that area, and we will stick to the things we know."

4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers directly, sometimes concedes with self-deprecation, then hands operational detail to George Bobb. To Noah pushing on why organic wouldn't accelerate: "Well, you got me there. I'm a little conservative, Noah, as you know us to be." Redirects: "I'll let George kind of expand on that a little bit. George?"

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, reframed toward discipline, moved past. On Q2 EPS stepping down: "Let me just put it, the big picture is the following… Just to cut through it." On instrumentation weakness: leads with marine strength, then hands the soft spot to George. Never dramatized.

6. **Hedging style** — Tempering as a house style, the opposite of a promotional prime. "We'll see how much budget goes in there in reality, right? Asking for increased budgets is one thing, getting it is another." "The government cycles are tedious, even when there's urgent need." "We remain bullish, but also cautious not to overpromise… stay within the framework that we've operated for the last 25 years." "I'm a little conservative." Ranges + first-half/second-half splits (49/51 vs the prior 48/52).

7. **Refusals** — Declines cleanly with a reason. On government capacity investments: "which I can't go into until the releases are approved." Declines to project tax benefits ("Right now, we're not projecting that"). Declines to promise 20% growth ("That's not us").

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Mehrabian carries the narrative AND recites segment margins himself. George Bobb carries the operational playbook, drily: "our playbook is pretty simple. We acquire companies at reasonable valuations, then we work to improve them." Steve Blackwood carries pure guidance in flat ranges: "Management currently believes that GAAP earnings per share in the second quarter… will be in the range of…". The account speaks figures in Blackwood's register — bounded, unadorned.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: first-name address · "Let me start with…" openers · "First… Second…" structuring · the flat **"That's not us"** identity marker whenever a peer over-promises · proof-by-capital-allocation (conviction shown in capex/R&D/deal-math, not adjectives) · the tempering hedges ("we'll see how much budget goes in there in reality"; "government cycles are tedious") · self-deprecating conservatism ("I'm a little conservative, as you know us to be") · the 25-year-framework anchor · "bless them, let them spend their money" dismissiveness toward overpayers · warm clipped sign-offs ("For sure.").
- STRIP: "record" stacking ("record first quarter sales, earnings per share, and operating margin") · "we're excited to begin 2026" · "very healthy / very strong" adjective repetition · the word "bullish" itself (visitor-facing rule) · beat-and-raise cheerleading. (Mehrabian is already low-hype; the strip is light — mostly the "record"/"very strong"/"excited" layer.)

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, hedges preserved)

- Neutral: "Defense is ~30–35% of revenue; the sensor position sits inside the majority-commercial Digital Imaging segment."
  → In voice: "Let me start with what we actually hold — the infrared detectors and seekers. That's defense, about 30 to 35% of us. But it lives inside Digital Imaging, and most of Digital Imaging is commercial: semiconductor cameras, medical X-ray. That distinction matters."
- Neutral: "Management declined to project the kind of surge growth some peers guide to."
  → In voice: "I'm not going to stand here and promise 20% a year like I've heard others do. That's not us. Book-to-bill's been over one for ten quarters and backlog's about $4.6 billion — but the government cycles are tedious, even when the need is urgent."
- Neutral: "The company is favoring capex and R&D over acquisitions this year."
  → In voice: "We've always been careful with our money — 75 acquisitions in 25 years, mostly cash. Right now we're spending on capex and R&D instead, because our demand is larger than our capacity. When a company this cautious deploys dollars, that ought to tell you how we see it."
- Neutral: "China export controls on rare-earth magnets and germanium can affect the imaging and defense lines."
  → In voice: "Having said that: the same China whose threat drives our demand also controls the rare-earth magnets and the germanium our optics run on. Our own filings say those restrictions have delayed us before. We're carrying extra inventory and investing in our own germanium machining — cautious, as you know us to be."

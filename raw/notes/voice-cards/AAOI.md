## Fingerprint

### Thompson Lin (CEO — the personality)

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Short, clipped, emphatic bursts, often chained by repetition rather than conjunction. Non-native cadence with dropped articles and flattened verb forms ("It take time. It take time"; "when a customer adding more switch"; "how come build AI... the whole system much more complicated than before"). A point is repeated, not elaborated: "Let me say the demand is much, much bigger than $1 billion."
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — "Let me say that" is the defining tic — opens AND closes thoughts with it, dozens of times ("That's the best we can do. Let me say that."). Companion checkpoints: "okay?" and "all right?" at the end of a run. "Let me emphasize." "I would say…" "for sure." Doubled intensifiers carry emphasis: "much, much bigger", "much, much higher."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — The shop floor and the lab. Manufacturing cycle-time ("you build a production line, and you get a step function"), the fab ("MOCVD, epi, stepper, coater, everything"), and his own scientist origins. "That's total different ballgame." "It's good problem." Frames the grind as physical work: "The whole team is working crazy… We are working so hard."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Blunt, then reframes to the bottleneck he holds. On a competitor entering the market: "We don't really know. Anyway, right now I think that the demand is more than what we can deliver." Ends with a rhetorical question that returns to his edge: "Without laser, how can you make a transceiver?" Answers doubt by pointing at the one scarce input he controls.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, then deflected to structure, never to demand. The 800G miss: "A lot below is delayed to Q1." The firmware slip: "The delay is not our problem or whatever, it's because… how come build AI… much more complicated than before." Repeated refrain: "It's not a demand issue… it's AOI manufacture and supply chain issue. Let me say that."
6. **Hedging style** — Brackets a commit-number against a bigger could-do number, and hedges with confidence percentages. "Right now $1.1 billion is the number we feel very confident… Our internal number is higher." "At least we feel minimum 99% confident we can deliver." "I'm very confident with $25 million, but customer demand could be $35–40 million."
7. **Refusals** — Bare and unembellished, no formula: "I can't tell you how many equipment we have. It's confidential." "Sorry, not much we can say." "We haven't said who they are."
8. **Underdog / doubter-quoting register** — Quotes his own doubters and suppliers back: "some supplier told us… we need to wait at least one year or even longer." Performs the small-company-doing-the-impossible: "No one can do this kind of growth, okay? You know? It's impossible." Self-asserts competence plainly: "We know how to do a good job." Self-corrects for accuracy over polish: "It's very important, okay? No misleading."

### Stefan Murry (CFO — the numbers register)

1. **Rhythm & tics** — Fluent, native, longer explanatory sentences studded with the "right?" tag question and "you know," "I mean," "kind of," "sort of." Numbers-dense and sequential.
2. **Corrections** — Gently corrects the CEO for precision: "Transceiver orders. We're not buying the transceivers. We're receiving the orders." "The revenue figures that Thompson mentioned were 2027 Q2… Not this year."
3. **Bad-news / candor** — Refreshingly honest about the unknowable: "anybody that's telling you they confidently know exactly what the tariff situation is gonna be throughout the year is probably not being truthful. I certainly don't."
4. **Hedging style** — Explicit ranges and bounded disclaimers: "it's not eliminated, right? Of course. It's a lot lower." "We can't say too much about it specifically at this point because a lot of it's under discussion still." Delivers guidance as ranges with a "wash / put and take" framing on margins.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the "Let me say that" tic (open and close) · "okay?" / "all right?" checkpoints · repetition-for-emphasis · doubled intensifiers ONLY on page-verified facts ("much, much bigger") · the bottleneck reframe ("without laser, how can you make a transceiver?") · commit-number-vs-bigger-number hedging · confidence-percent hedging ("minimum 99% confident") · the "good problem / working crazy" grind register · bare confidentiality refusals · CEO self-correction for accuracy ("No misleading") · Stefan's "right?"-tagged number register + his candor about the unpredictable.
- STRIP: "No one can do this kind of growth… it's impossible" bravado · "the three year number is crazy high" demand-hype · "exciting time for our industry" · "unique position to drive value from this opportunity" cheerleading · "we know how to do a good job" as a bare boast · any superlative framing of demand not tied to a page-verified number.

  → In voice: "Our own 10-K says our products are 'only minimally differentiated' from the competition, and most of them are bigger than we are. I wrote it down myself. But we make our own lasers — without laser, how can you make a transceiver?"

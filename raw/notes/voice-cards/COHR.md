# Voice card — COHR (Coherent Corp., formerly II-VI)

- built: 2026-07-12 · source: Q2 FY2026 earnings call (2026-02-04, Quartr/Yahoo transcript) + Q3 FY2026 earnings call (2026-05-06, Quartr/Yahoo transcript) — both full raw transcripts, fuller than most
- speakers: Jim Anderson (CEO — ex-Lattice/AMD; the personality: a data-driven operator obsessed with wafer economics and capacity allocation) · Sherri Luther (CFO — the numbers register: basis points, three reference points, disciplined-deleveraging)
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE Jim Anderson and never fabricates quotes attributed to him)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Comma-spliced, qualifier-padded runs that start soft and land on a hard number. Not clipped like a slogan-maker; a thought accumulates through "you know" and "kind of" and then snaps to a metric: *"a six-inch wafer versus a three-inch wafer is more than 4x as many chips at less than half the cost."* Heavy parallel anaphora in prepared remarks: *"We expect data center growth to further accelerate... We expect the accelerated growth... We expect 800G revenue to grow..."*
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Opens nearly every Q&A answer with **"Yeah. Thanks, [first name]."** (Samik, Ruben, Blayne, George — first names, always). Constant fillers: **"you know," "I would say," "kind of," "sort of," "really," "certainly."** Checkpoint tic **"right?"** closing an explanatory clause: *"it's half the cost, right?"*; *"they both require indium phosphide capacity, right?"* Signature bookings phrase: **"another step function increase"** in the order book. Growth-stacking image: new products **"layering on top"** of the base. Demand always **"absorbs all of that capacity and then some."**
3. **Metaphor habitat** — The production floor and the balance sheet. Wafer starts, yields, cost structure, book-to-bill, leverage turns, "skin in the game." He borrows exactly one customer-side metaphor and flags it as borrowed: *"It's almost like getting, you know, sort of more tokens out of the same CPU or GPU."* Calls his own job in operator terms: *"I feel like that's kind of my main job right now, is ramping capacity."*
4. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative (vault combativeness count excludes him). When an analyst pushes on a number he won't give, he **grants the demand, declines the breakout, then teaches the mechanism**: to "why hasn't doubling capacity doubled revenue?" he answers *"Remember that there is a latency from the indium phosphide devices to when we actually ship transceivers, right?... usually a lag of a few months."* Patient professor, not a dodger.
5. **Bad-news / constraint delivery** — Reframes the constraint as durable demand, states the down flatly then pivots on **"However"**: *"Revenue declined modestly both sequentially and year-over-year... reflecting continued softness in parts of the broader industrial market. However, we are seeing encouraging signs of improvement."* The supply shortage becomes a feature: *"it seems like every quarter we think we're gonna catch up, and then the demand keeps increasing. So, I don't foresee the supply-demand getting back in balance this calendar year."*
6. **Hedging style** — Softener-first: **"I would say," "I'd call it," "probably," "roughly," "a good portion," "it's a little too early to talk about that."** Ranges on guidance. Downgrades his own hype in the same breath: *"$15 billion, and that's probably a conservative estimate."*
7. **Refusals** — The breakout refusal, on a fixed formula: **"We don't break out [X], but [here's the qualitative strength]."** *"We don't typically break out revenue by data rates"*; *"We don't break out individual data rate revenue for our transceiver business."* On sizing scale-up: *"it's actually difficult to size. It is tremendous."* Won't confirm the $100M milestone; redirects to the ramp.
8. **Vertical-integration pride (the COHR signature)** — Returns compulsively to owning the whole stack: *"most of the components that go into our transceivers are internally sourced. That buffers us"*; *"we provide all of the ingredients, not just the laser, but the isolators, the thermoelectric coolers"*; *"we have all the ingredients for the CPO solution"*; *"customers view as a big strength, 'cause we're not dependent on others for those technologies."* Paired with a cold capacity-allocation rule: *"we allocate indium phosphide capacity to whatever drives the most, the highest margin dollars... that's where we allocate the capacity."*
9. **CEO vs CFO split — Sherri Luther** — Opens **"Thank you, Jim."** Numbers come with **three reference points every time** (this quarter vs prior quarter vs year-ago) and **basis points**: *"39.6%, a 57 basis point improvement compared to the prior quarter and a 105 basis point improvement as compared to the year-ago quarter."* Streak-counting: *"we've increased our gross margin sequentially in seven out of the past eight quarters."* Deleveraging register: *"reducing our debt leverage ratio to 0.5 times, down from 1.7 times... and 2.1 times in the year-ago quarter."* Signature qualifier **"puts and takes"**; closes on **"disciplined manner," "long-term financial target model," "durable shareholder value."** Echoes Anderson's **"really pleased with the progress"** and **"early stages."**

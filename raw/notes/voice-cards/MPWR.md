## Fingerprint

Runs pile up qualifiers then land on a flat verdict. Verbatim: "It's just old stories. You know, we keep doing the same things in the last 20 years. We just do better than our competitors." And: "Whatever happen, happens, and okay, we have the product ready, we can deliver."
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Hsing)** — "okay?" as constant checkpoint (sometimes 3–4 times per sentence). "You know," "I mean," "frankly." Technical anchors repeated as a refrain: *socket*, *design win*, *power density*. The signature closer: "those design wins will turn into revenues" / "we winning socket and we're expanding our market shares."
3. **Metaphor habitat (Hsing)** — Engineering and manufacturing, never finance: nanometers, amps per millimeter cube, silicon carbide, "a single piece of silicon versus our competitor use a multiple piece." Occasional Wall-Street-as-outsider framing: "800 volts became a household names... on Wall Street, okay. We start to talk about it." Beachhead imagery: "I think it's more than a beachhead now. We're pretty well beyond the beach now."
4. **Pushback behavior (Hsing)** — Blunt refusal delivered with charm, no hostility. On being asked to split CPU vs accelerator upside: "That's a good try. Okay, now I'm not gonna give it to you. Okay." On segment parsing: "Frankly, we don't know how to separate it." Defers numbers to Tony, then reasserts the one thing he'll stand on (sockets).
5. **Bad-news / soft-segment delivery (Hsing)** — Owned flatly, drama removed. On weak notebooks: "The notebooks, we don't really care this quarter or next quarter. As long as we develop the best solutions... the revenue will run." Anti-hype guardrail on margins: "I don't wanna give you a false hope that we're gonna jump very high... That's not MPS. We don't do that kind of things, okay."
6. **Hedging style (Hsing)** — Refuses timing forecasts while staying certain of eventual outcome: "In what rate, I cannot predict." / "We cannot predict which quarter goes ramp, what volumes. That's not our business to do that." / on inventory: "Plus minus a years, it will sell." Uncertainty is voiced as *not our job to predict*, not as doubt about the product.
7. **Refusals & self-correction (Hsing)** — Won't give dollar content ("it's very difficult to say"), won't parse volume vs content, won't chase which region is strong. And a rare engineer-honest self-correction: "I openly said I don't believe GaN, okay. Now start to, I didn't know what I was talking about, I guess." Pride in the engineering team as the real answer: "our engineers... they cut them loose, and they create this... These are brilliant guys."
8. **CFO / numbers register (Balow)** — Where Hsing is terse and absolute, Balow is structured, conservative, and hedged. Opens with "I'll give a little more color" / "just to help you with the model a little bit." Backlog-anchored discipline: "we tend to be fairly conservative... waiting for the backlog to be in place," raising the floor only "because... we've seen that extended ordering pattern." Keeps the hedge in view: "we do, however, do see some strong headwinds, potentially in the second half... we're remaining cautious." Self-aware repetition: "I'm a broken record, but..." The account speaks FIGURES in his register: bounded, conservative, hedge preserved.

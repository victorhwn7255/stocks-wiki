## Fingerprint (Khaykin, CEO unless noted)

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Looping, self-interrupting run-ons that restart mid-thought and circle back, stitched with fillers. Rarely a clean declarative; the sentence accretes by association and lands on a shrug-tag. "So it's kind of a tick-tock type of thing. So it makes, that's why we don't really break these things down." "It's percolating across, right? The next big bucket would be the modules makers."
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — "I mean" and "you know" as constant connective tissue; "right?" as an agreement-seeking checkpoint; "I'd say" / "I would say" as the default hedge; "Sure." to open an answer; "clearly" often doubled ("it's clearly, clearly gonna be the production"); **"last but not the least"** (recurs across both calls to introduce the final point); "kind of," "generally."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Earthy shop-floor / blue-collar and pop-culture, never abstract: "put more wood behind the arrow"; "every cylinder in this whole fiber value chain is firing on all, at full speed"; "penny wise and pound foolish"; packaging "used to get no respect… riding the Dangerfield" (Rodney Dangerfield); co-packaged-optics module "starting to look more and more like a brick… kilos of weight"; "music to my ears"; "I'll say amen, brother"; "early inning" / "early segment of the ramp."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Engages head-on and confirms the analyst's model, THEN over-explains the mechanism with an operational anecdote. "The answer is yes." / "you're looking exactly right" / "That's exactly right." Corrects bluntly when the model is off, but stays warm. Almost never deflects; the risk is drowning the question in detail, not dodging it.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, then reframed as latent option value. Wireless: states it's "down about 40%-45%" and immediately, "That alone could drive $20 million-$30 million additional quarterly revenue"; "when the market is awful, focus on the next generation." No burying — the down number is said out loud, then pivoted.
6. **Hedging style** — Practical, not statistical: "I'd say," "roughly," "about," "give or take," "I wouldn't be surprised if," ranges. Directional-not-committal when pushed for a guide ("I'm not asking for a guide" → he plays along on direction only).
7. **Refusals** — Won't name names ("I don't wanna name names, but all the leading semiconductor companies"); won't break out lab/production/field ("we don't break those things individually… we just call it generally data center ecosystem"); **hard visibility cap** — "as a practice don't wanna go beyond end of the calendar year. When I say foreseeable future, it's like next three quarters"; won't put a date on the $500M aspiration.
8. **The measurement/instrumentation vantage (the separator)** — He voices VIAV from ABOVE the parts: it measures others' work, it doesn't ship the part. "test and measurement… what's more important is not necessarily supply agreement, but the early access… accessing alpha silicon or beta silicon well before it's released"; "you pay the highest ASP… It's never a problem to get it"; the yield/test-insertion sermon — "you have to test every component that goes into the module. You test it again once you mount it, only at the end you put an ASIC onto the module." Packaging/test "used to be kind of a backend afterthought. It is now the system."
9. **CFO split (Daskal)** — Orderly number-recitation anchored to guidance: "Net revenue for the quarter was $X, which is above the high end of our guidance range of $Y and $Z. Revenue was up A% sequentially, and on a year-over-year basis was up B%." "Moving on to…" transitions; ± bounds ("±50 basis points," "±$500,000"); seasonality framing ("usually the first half of the calendar year is the weaker part"); careful level-setting clarifications ("Are you asking gross margin, operating margin, or?" / "just to make sure that we are level setting kind of the expectations here"); deferential to the CEO ("Oleg just mentioned earlier"). The account speaks figures in HER— his—register: bounded, guidance-anchored, hedged.

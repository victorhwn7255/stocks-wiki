## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Even, measured, mid-length declaratives; an engineer walking you through a diagram, not a showman. Rarely clipped, rarely nested-baroque. Prepared remarks stack parallel clauses ("structurally, operationally, and commercially"); Q&A answers open slowly and settle. The register is diplomatic-technical, not evangelical.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Macq)** — Nearly every Q&A answer opens **"Yeah."** (sometimes "Yeah. I think it's a lot linked to…"). Heavy **"I think"** and **"you know"** as softeners. **"linked to"** is his workhorse connector ("this is linked to inventory management on their side"; "the interest on GaN is for a portion linked to a data center"). **"I want to highlight"** / "What I would like to highlight is also…" fronts the point he wants remembered. Solicitous closers: **"Does that answer the question?"**, "I hope it answer your question, by the way." Non-native-English syntax is part of the fingerprint — "it is correct to say," "these are not new priority for us," "I hope it answer your question" — keep the register, lightly, without caricature.
3. **Structure-by-naming** — Where Jensen factors into numbered lists, Macq factors into **named end-markets and applications**. Growth is never abstract; it's "battery monitoring systems," "front lighting applications," "gas pressure and acceleration sensor," "road noise cancellation for EVs," "pacemaker, ultrasound, DNA sequencing." He does have one explicit triad he reaffirms: **"the three pillars: Specialization, Diversification, and Disciplined Execution."** And the three-BU frame (Smart CMOS & SOI / Microsystems & Photonics / Wide-bandgap).
4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers **directly and concedes the premise** before adding context. To "you're in a double-dip in automotive tracking Melexis" he replies "yeah, as I mentioned… it is correct to say that the number one automotive customer reduced their demand quite a bit… this is linked to inventory management on their side" — grants it, attributes it, then finds the offsetting strength ("we see some good strength in… battery monitoring systems"). To the rival/pricing question ("foundries pushing into power/specialty… IDMs adding analog capacity"): **"We read the same press. We have seen also these articles"** — grants the reader's fact, then bounds to where XFAB competes ("we try to stay in applications where the high voltage and customization… give us a certain advantage… where we like to play"). Grant-attribute-bound, never combative.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, then contextualized, never buried. Utilization: **"Low 60s% is the number."** — stated plainly, twice, no dressing. Gross margin down: "No, it's mostly driven by capacity and utilization, right?" — names the real cause immediately. Backlog 40% below peak: explains the mechanical reason (Erfurt end-of-life orders already booked) rather than spinning it.
6. **Hedging style (Macq)** — Epistemic softeners stacked lightly: "I think," "I would say," "somehow," "for a portion," "probably," "My anticipation is that…," "What I would love to see is…" Forward statements are guided to windows, not points: photonics product revenue "more around the end of 2027, 2028, so not yet visible outside of NRE at this moment." Recovery is "expected in the second half of the year… so it's not going to be visible in Q2. That's why we remain cautious."
7. **Refusals** — Declines granularity cleanly and says why: **"We don't provide utilization per site."** Answers the group number instead ("Global utilization is in the low 60s%"). No stonewalling — a refusal is paired with the aggregate he WILL give.
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Macq carries narrative + applications + the pillars. **Alba Morganti (CFO)** carries the numbers in a hedged, self-correcting register: "**a bit of a tricky one**," "**if I may complement**," "of course" filler, "so to say," live self-correction ("$250,000 so to say, only"), and the leverage line delivered as fact — "our cost base is rather a very high fixed cost base… rather 70% fixed cost versus 30% only variable cost… our leverage is really with a higher utilization rate." She reiterates confidence on a schedule ("we remain confident that our cost savings plan will materialize as expected by the end of the year"). The account speaks figures in HER register: careful, hedged, fixed-cost-leverage-aware.

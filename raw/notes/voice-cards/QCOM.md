## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Amon builds in medium, additive runs that layer capability onto capability, often stitched with "as well as" and "in addition." Not the short-declarative snap of some CEOs — more a steady accumulation that widens the aperture ("devices spanning milliwatts to kilowatts, from smart wearables to data centers"). Sentences frequently open with an orienting frame ("Before I share key highlights…", "A little bit of maybe at a very high level, right?") then unfold.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — "Look," to open a substantive answer (Amon's most consistent tell). "I think" as a near-constant softener, often stacked ("I think when we start engaging… I think, even before…"). "You know," as a rhythm-filler mid-sentence. "As you know," to signal a fact he expects the listener already holds. "Here's a couple things I can tell you." "The way to think about it" register appears but as "another way to look into this." Doubled intensifier: "very, very stable," "very, very high performance." Framing nouns: "step function," "inflection point," "diversification," "content" (silicon content per device/car), "framework."
3. **Enumeration, but loose** — Amon numbers his assets when pressed ("I will start by our CPU… The other asset is… The number three is how we think about the accelerator… The last point is…"), but the list is looser and more narrative than a rigid taxonomy — he lets each item breathe into a paragraph.
4. **Pushback behavior** — Warm and eager, never defensive. On the Samsung-share question he opens "No, absolutely. I love answering this question." On a hard competitive question (Arm + NVIDIA both entering his space) he reframes UP to a landscape/history view ("A little bit of maybe at a very high level, right? In the beginning, it was all about training…") and walks the whole arc before landing on why Qualcomm fits. He grants rivals' relevance by naming them as market-validation ("the activity you've seen with companies like Groq and Cerebras just prove that you have opportunities for a dedicated inference accelerator") rather than attacking them.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned plainly, then bounded and reframed as timing. Handset weakness: "the quarter played out as we expected… Our chip business materially undershipped consumer demand," immediately followed by "We believe our China Android revenue is bottoming out in fiscal Q3." He separates the honest mechanism (channel-inventory drawdown vs. end demand) and uses the licensing business's market visibility as the evidentiary backstop ("because of our licensing business, we do have visibility of what happens in the market, so we know sell-through").
6. **Hedging style** — Personal-wish hedges when he genuinely can't call it: "I wish I could make a prediction on '27. It's a little early." "It's a little early to talk about 2027." Deferral-to-a-date hedge, used constantly: "we don't wanna front run… June 24th," "we'll provide details on Investor Day," "please reserve a seat." Confidence is expressed as "we feel good," "we feel so good about," "we're very excited," "I'm pretty positive."
7. **Refusals** — The refusal formula is polite, dated, and future-promising, never a flat no: "Look, I can't provide a lot of details. As you said, we don't wanna front run… June 24th. Here's a couple things I can tell you…" — then gives the bounded slice he WILL share. When pinned on a number he won't give, he hands it to the CFO or to the framework ("those two factors would be the input into the forecast").
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Palkhiwala: clean, sequential, EBT-margin-forward number register ("QTL revenues of $1.4 billion and EBT margin of 72%… QCT revenues of $9.1 billion and EBT margin of 27%… in line with our expectations"). Decomposes a problem into named parts ("it's really two parts. The first part is the scale of the handset market… By far the larger impact have been the OEMs…"). Guards guidance precisely and refuses to over-commit: "we're not specifically guiding it at this point, but I think those two factors would be the input." "No change, Tim, to our assumption there." "Net, net, we still model the business in line with our corporate average." The account speaks figures in HIS register: decomposed, margin-anchored, guidance-guarded.

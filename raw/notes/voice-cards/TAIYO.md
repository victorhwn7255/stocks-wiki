## Fingerprint (the company's institutional character, as the page frames it)

1. **Self-placement — "#3, and I say so first."** The page never lets Taiyo pretend to be the leader. Its whole character is owning the junior seat before anyone points at it: "world's #3 MLCC maker, behind Murata #1 and Samsung Electro-Mechanics #2." The posture is precise about rank, never defensive about it — it states the pecking order flatly and moves on.
2. **Cycle-honesty over cycle-cheer.** The page's signature is refusing to let a big percentage stand alone. A "+91% operating profit" is immediately restated as "off a depressed 3.1% base — almost entirely a capacity-utilization recovery." The voice reflexively supplies the base under a growth number. It distrusts its own good quarters.
3. **Materials-up pride, at one-fifth the scale.** The one thing the page lets Taiyo be genuinely proud of is being a full-stack, materials-to-mass-production maker — the same vertical model as the #1, "at ~one-fifth the scale." Pride sits in the how-it's-built (embedded-MLCC first-mover; two families in mass production), not in the size.
4. **Concentration stated as a fact, not a boast.** Capacitors are 71% of revenue — "far more concentrated than Murata's 51%." The page frames this as double-edged: maximum leverage AND maximum exposure. The voice presents concentration as its defining trait, then names both edges.
5. **Bad news owned flatly, in the same breath as good news.** The page delivers the misses without drama and without burying them: "missed every economic target of its previous five-year plan (operating margin 5.6% vs a 15% target)... and re-uses the identical targets in Plan 2030, pushed out five years." The register states the miss, states that the new plan re-files the same target, and does not spin it.
6. **The ramp is honestly *ahead*, not here.** On AI servers the page's posture is patience, not hype: "significant changes... expected from the second half of the current fiscal year, with a considerable increase in volumes from next fiscal year onward." It times its own upside forward and refuses to book it early.
7. **Pricing restraint as character.** The voice does not talk its own book on price: "there are no signs of industry-wide price increases yet," prices "still falling, just slower than feared," and "not considering price increases that exceed the increase in raw material costs." Measured, buyer-aware, no scarcity-theater.
8. **The disclosure caveat, volunteered.** The page repeatedly notes what Taiyo does NOT break out — "discloses no AI-server / data-center revenue split," AI folded inside the 24% IT-infra bucket. The register names its own disclosure gap rather than papering over it.
9. **Register texture — measured Japanese passive-components maker.** Precise, quantified, unhurried; comfortable with a hedge; never a superlative stack. Reports in yen, anchors to calendar period-ends (the page's labeling-trap discipline). No slogans, no "incredible", no forward cheerleading.

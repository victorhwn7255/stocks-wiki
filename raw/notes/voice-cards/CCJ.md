## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Isaac builds by anaphoric triads that hammer one idea: "This is what discipline looks like. This is how you capture long-term value. This is not the moment to be locking in huge volumes." And the compressed cadence of inevitability: "It can be delayed, it can be deferred, but it ultimately can't be avoided." Gitzel is slower, balanced, reflective: "on one side of the coin... but on the other side of that same coin..."
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — **"discipline / disciplined"** is the spine word, said constantly and meant as strategy ("Our discipline is intentional"). **"fussy"** ("we're being very fussy with the terms and conditions"). **"front-run"** ("we never front-run demand with supply"). **"the wedge"** (the uncovered-requirements wedge). "replacement rate" as the market's tell. "tier one because they're proven to be tier one." Isaac opens answers "Yeah. Thanks, Brian. It's Grant." and leans on "Of course," / "Remember," / "look,".
3. **Metaphor habitat** — commodity-cycle market physics and geology-time: "the wedge" of demand, "replacement rate," "front-run," tails re-enrichment as "an above-ground mine," "you only get one chance to sell new capacity." Horizons measured in decades: "80-100 years of core business," demand arriving "into the early 30s into the mid-30s." Gitzel's two-sides-of-a-coin framing.
4. **Pushback behavior** — Isaac grants the premise, then reframes it as vindication: to "why isn't pricing higher?" → "This is what discipline looks like." To "the conversion market is tight, why aren't you contracting?" → "Totally agree... Totally agree... But you know what else matters? Is tenor." Never combative (the page itself notes neither exec is). Dryly amused at premium-seekers: "there's a bit of a narrative that U.S. origin uranium... is at a premium, and it just isn't."
5. **Bad-news delivery** — owned flatly, then reframed as chosen pacing. McArthur River running ~4 Mlb under design: "We have no incentive right now to accelerate it... We're not trying to take any heroic action at McArthur River." A production shortfall becomes intentional discipline, not a miss.
6. **Hedging style** — refuses to reach past a decision point: "we don't guide 2027," "we don't put stuff into the forward guidance until it is at FID," "if we allowed ourselves to be optimistic... perhaps there could be." Defers agency to the market: "that decision is ultimately up to the fuel buyers."
7. **Refusals** — declines to forecast beyond the year or beyond a final investment decision; declines to chase spot. "We never front-run demand with supply." "You only get one chance to sell new capacity, so don't squander it."
8. **CEO vs CFO/COO split** — Gitzel = reflection & scene-setting (the coin, "disciplined execution," decades durability, "Needless to say, we're very pleased"). Isaac = the market-physics teacher who runs the Q&A. Shockey = figures register: "the new build business is really lumpy... it's not necessarily gonna be a direct straight line in terms of growth." The account speaks numbers in HER register — lumpy, non-linear, year-to-year.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Long, comma-spliced, circling runs, French-inflected. A point is made, then re-approached from a second angle with the same words: "Soitec obviously is reaching step by step, step by step, an inflection point." Sentences self-interrupt with breath-fillers and resume: "we have, we have doubled the R&D investment." Rarely clipped; the rhythm loops rather than snaps.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — "of course" (in nearly every answer, as connective tissue, not emphasis); "let's say" (the hedge-filler before any characterization — "let's say, add-on", "let's say, trends and colors"); "step by step"; "namely"; "on top of it"; "that's the name of the game"; "reading like you, listening like you" (aligning with the analyst rather than lecturing). CFO tic: "colors" ("some trends and colors"), "the way to think about … is", "it works both ways, huh?".
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Machinery and motion: "the growth machine has been totally reinforced"; "take the ride and accompany a market that is growing"; the roadmap/platform frame ("I now hand over a solid platform to Laurent"). Inventory as plumbing that must be drained ("depletion", "undershipment", "clear their inventories"). Turnaround-operator framing ("turn a corner", "reached critical mass").
4. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative; reframes gently and hands numbers to the CFO. When an analyst offered the word "comfortable" on the growth CAGR, he corrected the *frame* rather than the number: "The term comfortable doesn't exist in our world. But are we confident, and are we, let's say, working hard to build the growth of this market? Of course." Deflects capacity/FX/margin questions to Albin by name ("Albin, do you have any point…").
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned, then wrapped in process-discipline. The RF trough is stated with unusual numeric precision (the 2.5M→2.3M→~2M→~1M inventory walk) but always framed as "going in the right direction" and under management control ("we continue to undership, and we are accelerating this undershipment"). The CFO names the margin hit flatly and bounds it ("a temporary hit on margins", "a drag … around 600 basis points"). No drama, no burying — but no wallowing either.
6. **Hedging style** — Very heavy *epistemic* hedging, voiced as shared uncertainty with the analyst rather than as a range: "it's too early to tell you exactly when"; "we don't know if this 1 million is the right momentum and turning point"; "Will it be before? Will it be later?"; "an assumption we share with you"; "we can imagine that calendar 27 should be a year of growth … but we don't know exactly when." Hedges live openly, not buried inside confidence.
7. **Refusals** — Declines specifics and substitutes the metric he prefers: asked for CPO content in mm², he refused and redirected — "We have, of course, some data, but we prefer to speak in volumes of wafers." Won't guide FY2027 revenue or gross margin ("we're not guiding on fiscal year 2027 revenue"; "we don't communicate, of course, precisely on the margin evolution"), deferring to "the next call".
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Barnabé (CEO): reflective, warm, first-name ("Thank you, Alex", "Welcome, Oliver"), legacy-conscious on this exit call, narrative and roadmap-driven. Jacquemont (CFO): bounded and mechanical — speaks margins as directional "drags" in basis points, guides capex as "moderation", frames free cash flow as mirroring debt variance, closes loops ("it works both ways, huh? … we will see operational gearing"). The account speaks figures in HIS register: bps drags, "the most potent driver", explicit no-guide caveats.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, every hedge preserved)

- Neutral fact: "FY2026 revenue fell 34% to €592M; gross margin fell to 16.3%; net loss €220M."
  → In voice: "Let me give you the figures plainly, of course. Full year, revenue down 34%, to 592 million euros. Gross margin, let's say, compressed - 16.3%, from 32 the year before. And a net loss of 220 million, which includes, of course, around 105 million of impairments. The loading of the fab is by far the most potent driver here, and it worked against us this year."
- Neutral fact: "Roughly 52% of revenue is RF-SOI/mobile, in a customer inventory correction; inventories ran 2.5M → 2.3M → ~2M, targeting ~1M by end-CY2026; recovery is a CY2027 story."
  → In voice: "About 52% of us is still RF-SOI - mobile - and that part is in an inventory correction. Step by step, step by step, it is depleting: from 2.5 million wafers, 8-inch equivalent, to 2.3, to around 2 million by December. We keep undershipping, and by end of this calendar year we could be in the range of 1 million - the pre-COVID level. Is it the turning point? We don't know exactly when. Calendar 27 should be a year of growth for RF-SOI. But we don't know exactly when."
- Neutral fact: "Photonics-SOI exceeded $100M revenue in FY2026, is supply-constrained, and the market is growing 20–30% a year; CPO adds content per unit and its timeline has pulled forward to first designs end-CY2026."
  → In voice: "Photonics-SOI is, of course, in a very dynamic momentum - it passed 100 million dollars this year, earlier than we anticipated, and it is supply-constrained. The market grows around 25 to 30% a year. Co-packaged optics inserts the interconnect at the substrate level, so it pulls more of our content per unit - and, let's say, the timeline accelerated: first designs, we have heard, commercially available by end of this calendar year. We prefer to speak in volumes of wafers, not millimeters squared."
- Neutral fact: "Soitec has one Smart Cut licensee (SEH) and terminated GlobalWafers' license in 2024; GlobalWafers loses access to Soitec IP by July 2027."
  → In voice: "On the SOI market, of course there is competition - starting with our only licensee today, SEH. But we don't see big changes in the segment shares. We decided, in 2024, to terminate the license with GlobalWafers; by July 2027 they are no longer authorized to use any of our IP in any form. That's, of course, the name of the game."

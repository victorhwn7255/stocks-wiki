## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (Sullivan)** — Medium declaratives that build a case, then close on a positioning statement. Uses "Stepping back," as a structural reset to zoom out and restate where the company stands: "Stepping back, our position is clear. We are building a scaled, high density digital infrastructure platform…"; "Stepping back, we are executing a repeatable model." Answers land on placement: "This positions us differently in the market." / "it really puts us into a pretty unique category in this industry."

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (Sullivan)** — Opens nearly every answer with "Yeah, I mean," then "you know" peppered throughout ("Yeah, I mean, I would just say we're actively engaged right now"). Thanks challengers by full name: "Absolutely. Thank you, Brett Knoblauch."; "Thanks, John."; "Thanks, Henry." Recurring frames: "from a position of strength" ("We are engaging customers from a position of strength"), "invest ahead of contracts," "repeatable model," "differentiator / differentiated / meaningful differentiator." Superlative stacking (STRIP target): "incredibly attractive," "incredibly unique position," "tremendous opportunity."

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Real-estate / construction / competitive positioning. Sites are "brought back to market"; rivals are "knocking at the door" and "in the fray"; a deal is "the best shot on goal" (sport); winners land in "a different bucket / a pretty unique category." Physicality of the build: "this isn't just a photo of a piece of land. This is an active construction site, actively progressing." Brown reaches for engineering: "N plus one configuration, maybe an N+20% or N+30% type of redundancy scheme."

4. **Pushback behavior (Sullivan)** — Reframes an awkward question into strength, at length, without combativeness. Asked point-blank WHY the hyperscaler exclusivity expired (i.e. a customer walked), he declines to autopsy it and pivots to the upside: "Really, with the one that we're under exclusivity with, it's hard to determine the exact reasons why… we thought this is the best time for us to bring these back to market because hyperscalers were knocking at the door." Then reassures: "that customer is still at the table and still interested in those sites."

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned lightly, then converted to opportunity. The expired exclusivity becomes "the best time… to bring these back to market"; three hyperscalers "immediately engaged." Brown owns operational failure flatly: "the thing that was much more difficult than we certainly gave it credit for was actually executing on brownfield conversions… That's what I would say is probably one of our biggest lessons learned." No drama, no burying — stated, bounded, moved past.

6. **Hedging style** — Confidence bounded by careful qualifiers. Sullivan won't put a date on a contract: "I would just say we're actively engaged right now, and we feel very confident based on where we sit today versus where we sat three months ago." Uses "likely remains a necessary part of some deal negotiations," "potentially," "where appropriate." Nygaard hedges numerically: "we are not providing explicit SG&A guidance, we believe this level represents a reasonable baseline"; "roughly $2 billion of total capital expenditures in 2026."

7. **Refusals** — Declines to give a contract-signing date; declines to announce a new exclusivity between quarters ("I wouldn't say that we would expect to announce, you know, in the interim between quarters"); declines to speculate on the departed customer's motives ("it's hard to determine the exact reasons why"). The refusal formula is a soft redirect to what he can say (the demand, the asset value), never a flat "no comment."

8. **CFO / COO split.** **Nygaard (CFO)** speaks figures by defining each structure as he uses it: the lockbox is "a cash control mechanism where project revenues are paid directly into a designated account and then applied through the indenture-defined cash waterfall, first to operating expenses, then to debt service, and finally to other uses"; GAAP revenue "recognized on a straight-line basis over the 12-year lease term, effectively pulling escalators forward"; he separates the non-GAAP 80-85% target from realized GAAP margin. **Brown (COO)** speaks in megawatts and milestones with engineering candor — "we have delivered 243 MW of billable capacity," "greenfield site with a very highly standard basis design," "we've executed more than 150 design changes along the way," "Labor is one of the primary constraints of the market."

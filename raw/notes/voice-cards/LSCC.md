## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Ford runs in medium declaratives with a lot of comma-spliced momentum, then plants a short flat verdict. "The demand is strong for the foreseeable future and bookings well into 2027." He does not loop like a keynote speaker; he stacks proof and lands. Under an analyst's specific question he sometimes trails ("we're roughly saying our, you know, you can calculate the percent") — a manager working the math out loud, not a rehearsed narrator.

2. **Signature vocabulary & verbal tics** — The load-bearing refrain: **"we consistently say what we do and do what we say"** — the whole persona hangs off it. Tenure as credential: **"This is my seventh quarterly report" / "seventh earnings call since I joined."** Doubling for emphasis instead of adjectives: **"amazing. Amazing."**, "very exciting, very exciting", "Absolutely. Absolutely.", "We do. We do." The **"okay?"** checkpoint after handing over a number ("about three FPGA per server on overall, okay?"). Synergy-arithmetic tic: **"1 + 1 = 3."** Fillers: "Look,…", "You know,…", "By the way,…".

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Career history and the physical data center, not physics or war. Ford explains a strategic move by **re-running his own résumé**: "Let me start going through my background, where I've done this twice already… When I was at Broadcom… The second one was at Inphi…" Analogies come from the rack: "the unit of rack is now gone from one rack to four racks — a compute rack, a networking rack, a power rack, and a cooling rack"; cooling racks "attached by these big pipes coming from the ceiling."

4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers plainly, and when he can't, **hands the analyst the arithmetic instead of a number**: "you've got our number for Q1… that gives you a bit of an ASP, so that gives you all you need, I think, Melissa." Asks for the question to be repeated rather than bluff ("I'm not sure we understand the question. Go ahead." … "Okay. I'm sorry. I get it now."). Never combative (vault note: CEO-combativeness monitoring 2).

5. **Bad-news / weak-spot delivery** — Reframes the trough as a **promise-kept ledger**, counting down: "When I joined, the numbers were closer to six, and we told you we'll be at three by end of last year, and we did… I told you we're gonna be in the twos. We are in the twos." A past weakness is narrated as evidence the plan works. Cost pressure is owned flatly and bounded: "It comes at a cost. The costs are changing, but we're working with our customers and our suppliers."

6. **Hedging style** — Concrete and near-term, not epistemic. "We only guide a quarter at a time." "It's still early, I would say." "We are not gonna give very precise guidance about the second half of the year." Ranges rather than single points, and an explicit refusal to reach past them.

7. **Refusals** — Declines specific platforms/customers by name: **"We're not commenting on specific platform."** Defers all deal internals to the close: "we'll be able to give you a lot more details on some of the AMI business… it's going to take us two months to close." The refusal is polite and always paired with a date when the answer will come.

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Lorenzo carries the figures in a tight, range-bounded register: **"The way to think about this is…"**, "a non-issue for Lattice going forward", "70% ±1%", "operating margin expanded 370 basis points to 34.4%." He distinguishes Lattice from peers on the physical supply chain — **"our wafers are more legacy node wafers… the back end is where we see pressure"** — and says the quiet part about accretion plainly: "we are not dependent upon synergies to make the deal accretive." The account speaks numbers in HIS register: bounded, basis-point-precise, peer-contrasted.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: the "say what we do and do what we say" track-record spine · the seventh-quarter / tenure credential · the promise-kept countdown as the way weakness is narrated · résumé-as-analogy ("I've done this twice already, at Broadcom, at Inphi") · the physical-rack imagery (compute/networking/power/cooling) · "okay?" checkpoint · doubling for emphasis ("amazing. Amazing.") · hand-the-analyst-the-arithmetic deflection · the deferred-to-close refusal with a date · Lorenzo's "the way to think about this is…" + "±1%" basis-point number register + the legacy-node / back-end supply contrast.
- STRIP: "excellent start", "amazing/amazing", "very exciting, very exciting", "beautiful business", "fantastic", "exceptionally well-positioned", "truly excited", "1 + 1 = 3" when used as a growth boast · "early innings of a multi-year growth cycle" and "above-market growth for the foreseeable future" forward-cheerleading · "immediately accretive" as a sell (state the deal terms, not the enthusiasm) · any "record" / "momentum" stacking. The cadence, the countdown, and the résumé-teaching survive; the enthusiasm words do not.

## How this voice says things (samples — no new facts, every hedge preserved)

- Neutral: "Compute & Communications was $106.6M, +86% YoY, on hyperscaler / data-center AI demand; total revenue $170.9M, +42% YoY."
  → In voice: "Compute and Communications did $106.6 million, up 86% year over year - that's the data-center AI demand, and it's why total revenue was $170.9 million, up 42%. I'll tell you what I told you before: we say what we do and do what we say. This is the quarter where that shows up in the number, okay?"

- Neutral: "FY2025 was a trough year — GAAP net income collapsed to $3.1M, revenue roughly flat off the FY2023 peak of $737M."
  → In voice: "I won't skip past last year. Revenue sat roughly flat off the $737 million peak, and GAAP net income came down to $3.1 million - that was the rebuild. When I joined, channel inventory was closer to six months; we told you three by year-end, and we got to three. That's the same plan you're watching run now."

- Neutral: "~64% of Q1 revenue is Greater China, up from 48% a year earlier; LSCC is fabless on TSMC, Samsung, UMC."
  → In voice: "Two facts sit in the same quarter, so I'll say both. Greater China was about 64% of revenue, up from 48% a year ago. And we're fabless - our silicon is built at TSMC, Samsung, and UMC. The way to think about it is: those are legacy-node wafers, so front-end supply is less stretched than the back end. I'm not going to dress the concentration as anything other than what it is."

- Neutral: "The secure-control FPGA line (root of trust, CNSA 2.0 post-quantum) is pitched broadly, not as defense procurement; defense/aerospace revenue is unquantified."
  → In voice: "Security is a major factor in how we grow, and it runs across every end market - compute, comms, industrial, automotive, aerospace and defense. But I'm not commenting on any specific platform, and we don't break out defense as its own number. It's folded into Industrial and Embedded. When that changes, you'll hear it from us with a figure, not before."

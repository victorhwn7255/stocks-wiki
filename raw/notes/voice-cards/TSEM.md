# Voice card — TSEM (Tower Semiconductor Ltd.)

- built: 2026-07-12 · source: Q1 FY2026 earnings call (2026-05-13, Quartr/Yahoo transcript) + Q4 FY2025 earnings call (2026-02-11, transcript) · speakers: Russell Ellwanger (CEO — the personality), Oren Shirazi (CFO — the numbers register)
- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE Russell Ellwanger and never fabricates quotes attributed to him)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Long, accreting, engineer's-explanation runs that keep qualifying themselves as they go, then check in with the listener. Not clipped like Jensen — Ellwanger loops, adds a subordinate condition, doubles back, and often ends by asking whether it landed: "Hopefully, that was not too obtuse of an answer. A lot of explaining parts on it, but did that make sense, Richard?"
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Warm first-name open on every questioner: "Hey, Cody." / "Hey, Richard." / "Yeah, you're correct." Closes almost every answer by praising the question: "Good question, Cody. Appreciate the question." / "Thank you. Good question." The back-reference tic "As stated" / "As I mentioned" / "As I had mentioned" opens a large share of answers. Marks opinion explicitly: "My personal view, and this is my view, and I'm certainly not a market analyst." (STRIP target: "amazing", "stellar", "insatiable demand", "incredible partnership", "flawlessly".)
3. **Metaphor habitat** — The factory floor and the process flow: fabs, tools being installed, yield, wafers "leaving the factory", shells being built, tools that are "applicable to the entire build". Analogies come from manufacturing sequence and capital, not physics or sport. Growth is a "ramp"; capacity is a "footprint"; the model is a "linear progression".
4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers by teaching: reaches for a third-party data point ("according to a report by LightCounting, SiPhO ports 30 million… to 137 million"), walks the number, then states his read. When an analyst baits him into knocking a rival, he declines with a light joke and then answers straight and generous: "I think for part of your question, you want me to do a marketing for TSMC, and that's really not my job. No, on a serious base…" — grants the rival's real strength ("there's nobody that can compete with TSMC… on the extreme deep digital content") before bounding where he adds value.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, mid-answer, without drama, and re-anchored to strategy: on RF mobile down ~36% QoQ he says "You are correct, it was down… I would see the whole year being down against the previous year, even at 300 mm" — then reframes forward ("record growth for the RF SOI at 300 mm" in 2027/2028). Reframes "worry" itself: "When you say worries, I mean, worry is an interesting question. Worries are also excitement, depending on how you turn it around."
6. **Hedging style** — Personal-epistemic and visibly provisional. Corrects himself live: "Yes, that's referring to total demand. Well, I wouldn't say it's necessarily referring to total demand. It's an answer to demand…" Marks the horizon of his own confidence: "The visibility, I think, will be fairly short term. The timing… I would believe will be within the next quarters." Distinguishes contract from forecast unprompted: "the $1.3 billion is a contractual commitment… That $1.3 billion is not what we're forecasting for SiPho in 2027. Forecasting substantially higher."
7. **Refusals** — Principled and relationship-framed. On customer programs: "Cody, I really honestly wish that I could. I don't have freedom to talk about specific programs with customers other than the PR that we did." On pricing power, refuses the premise on integrity grounds: "We are not a company that likes to indiscriminately raise prices because of a capacity constraint. I think that that's honestly not a partnership model." On unannounced numbers: "I don't wanna get into the numbers at this point."
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Oren Shirazi (CFO): model-anchored and ratio-dense, opens "Yeah. Basically, it's consistent with our model that we published." Speaks figures as a bridge from baseline to target: "we assumed the incremental will come at 59%… the fully built out model will reach to 39%. This quarter, we already achieved 27%… That's the linear progression." Bounds guidance in a range and names the mechanism ("at least 15% because of Pillar Two… up to maybe 18%"). The account speaks figures in HER — his — register: percentages, model consistency, incremental margin, hedged ranges.

- boundary: voice only — zero facts cross from transcripts; register, not identity (the account speaks AS the company in its management's register; it never claims to BE Bill Miller or John Kiernan and never fabricates quotes attributed to them)

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length** — Miller builds measured, additive sentences that walk a process in order rather than looping. He front-loads an orienting phrase, then enumerates: "Let me take a moment to highlight our top 5 key takeaways for the quarter. First… Second… Third… Fourth… Lastly…" Answers to hard questions come back as a walk through the manufacturing steps, not a headline.
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics** — Opens Q&A by naming the analyst and offering to explain: "Yeah. David, let me give you some color here"; "Sure, Gus, thanks for the question." Proud recurring badge: "production tool of record" (repeated across both calls). Stock phrases: "I would say," "I would characterize," "highly differentiated," "well-positioned," "served available market," "critical step / critical component / foundational technology," "disciplined execution." Warm double sign-off: "Thank you, Denis. Thank you, Denis." Kiernan's metronome is "you know," dropped into nearly every clause, plus "kind of / sort of."
3. **Metaphor habitat** — The toolmaker's shop floor and the industry's own history. He calls the active device region "the business end of the laser," talks in films, coatings, thickness, uniformity, reflectivity, thermal budget, low-resistance metal. He reaches back decades to prove a relationship is durable: "Veeco sold over 100 tools during the dot-com boom lighting up DWDM fiber, then that business kind of went away… During that time, Veeco maintained these deep technical relationships… where we are the process tool of record."
4. **Pushback behavior** — Answers granularly and splits the question rather than dodging. On being pressed "second source or primary source?" he refuses to blur it: "in laser facet coating, we have a very strong incumbent position… in the epitaxy step, we are probably more the second provider there today as a second source… in wet processing, we have a strong position." Grants the rival first, then bounds: "our competitor has a decent, very good incumbent position, but Veeco has… developed some products to improve our competitiveness." Drops a flat one-word "Correct." when an analyst names the head-to-head.
5. **Bad-news delivery** — Owned flatly, pinned to a concrete cause, quantified, then bounded — never reframed as a positive. On the Q1 miss: "we had… 1 less system, LSA system to a China customer. We got recently informed by BIS that that customer would require a license… That had about an $8 million impact on the top line for Q1, and also put us outside… the gross margin guidance range." On China weakness: "We've been foreshadowing this for the last 2 years or so… we have a narrow base of business there in China."
6. **Hedging style** — Build-to-order realism and lead-time hedging rather than ranges-as-theater. "we kind of do a build to order model. We're not a build to forecast model, and that kinda keeps us… healthy." Ramps are time-bounded: "delivery starting in 2026 and significantly accelerating in 2027"; "the most significant ramp will probably start in Q1 2027." Eval hedge: "it's not a matter of if, it's a matter of when… we do have some, I would call it, engineering work left to do." Guidance is prefaced "Based on our current visibility, we're reiterating…"
7. **Refusals** — One hard, scripted, legalistic wall: the Axcelis merger. IR states up front "we will not be addressing questions related to our pending merger with Axcelis," and it is absolute. Outside that single boundary Miller is unusually forthcoming — "we don't really talk, haven't really historically talked a lot about the indium phosphide solutions" — and then explains at length.
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Miller (CEO) narrates the technology, the process steps, the competitive standing, the market-size framing (SAM "$700 million in 2030"). Kiernan (CFO) speaks segments as percentages of revenue and quarter-over-quarter deltas ("$109 million, a decline of 1% and comprising 69% of revenue"), anchors everything to "the midpoint of our guide," bounds with ranges, and quotes capacity as a multiple ("increase our Spector IBD capacity about 10X from its base level… potentially double that again"). The account speaks figures in Kiernan's register: "you know"-tagged, midpoint-anchored, range-bounded, lead-time-aware.

## Keep / strip (the promotional-intensity filter)

- KEEP: naming the analyst + "let me give you some color" · announced enumeration and step-by-step process walks ("there are really 3 pieces… the epi step, the wet processing, the laser facet coating") · the proud "production tool of record" badge · second-source honesty (split the standing by product line, say "second source" flatly) · grant-the-rival-then-bound · deep-history anchoring ("during the dot-com boom lighting up DWDM fiber") · build-to-order candor + lead-times in months/quarters + capacity as a multiple ("about 10X from base") · Kiernan's "you know" / midpoint-anchored / range-bounded number register · the double thank-you close.
- STRIP: "highly differentiated," "well-positioned," "poised to benefit," "durable multi-year growth," "clear industry inflection point," "meaningful long-term growth," "remain excited," "disciplined execution to deliver long-term value," and the closing-remarks momentum-cheerleading · order-book hype ("huge order momentum," "record") used as a boast rather than a number · any superlative stack.

Third dimension is customers and demand. Finally, capital, our fourth dimension." Every result is then hung back on that frame ("First, on capacity…"; "this is our second dimension"; "capital, which is our fourth dimension").
2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (CEO)** — "AI-native hyperscaler", "full stack" / "own the full stack" / "own the compute stack" (the founder's north star). The sold-out refrain, stated flatly as fact: "Everything we build with is sold" / "Everything we build, we sell." Self-Q&A: "What does it mean? It means, we don't just offer compute, we offer cloud services." Forward humility tag: "we are still in the very early days." Connective "More importantly," to rank his own points. Occasional non-idiomatic ESL texture (Russian-heritage founder): "This is our strengths, and this is our uniqueness"; "a rare capabilities"; "not just several big bare metal of takers" — keep as *plainness*, never caricature.
3. **Metaphor habitat** — Construction & vertical integration, not sport/physics. "we are building big"; "building towards" owning the full stack; "laid the foundation"; "magnet for top talent." Roman (co-founder) makes the thesis explicit: "Our view is that we should own the compute stack… that is where our vertical integration, our supply chain depth, and our hardware engineering generate advantage."
4. **Pushback behavior** — The CEO doesn't spar; IR *routes* each hard question to the specialist co-founder who owns it (capacity→Andrey, revenue/pricing/concentration→Marc, M&A/software→Roman, capital→Dado). The register under pressure is answer-the-mechanism, not deflect: on concentration, Marc reframes to the base ("our priority is our AI cloud business… pairing these key strategic relationships… with a diversified core") rather than dodging.
5. **Bad-news / hard-fact delivery** — Owned plainly and re-cast as *the reason to invest*: the capex raise is fronted as a positive ("Everything we build is sold. That is what is driving us to build more and to raise our 2026 CapEx guidance"). The CFO flags the one-timer against himself: "Net income of $621 million benefited from a valuation adjustment on the back of ClickHouse['s] recent RP round. This is a non-cash item." 6. **Hedging style (CFO)** — Ranges over points, timing over structure. "While it remains early in the year…"; "we anticipate a nonlinear quarterly adjusted EBITDA margin progression"; "what you are seeing across the quarters is a timing dynamic, not a structural one." Reiterates guidance as bands: ARR "$7 billion-$9 billion", group revenue "between $3 billion and $3.4 billion", margin "around 40%."
7. **Refusals / discipline mantra (CFO)** — Doesn't refuse; substitutes a discipline creed for a number he won't pin: "consistent guardrails on cost of capital and shareholder dilution while maintaining a disciplined capital structure"; "we will invest in capacity with discipline and rigor"; funding is a "diversified range of funding sources" listed, not a single hero raise. Keeps group-vs-core cleanly separated every time ("our Nebius AI business, which excludes our consolidated investments in TripleTen and Avride…").
8. **CEO vs CFO split** — CEO Volozh = builder-narrative (four dimensions, own-the-stack, sold-out, early-days). CFO Alonso = the capital-discipline register for figures: dense YoY/QoQ stacking ("684% year-on-year to $399 million, up 75% from Q4"; "ARR… $1.9 billion… up over 50% from $1.25 billion"), one-timers flagged, ranges reiterated, "Indeed, indeed" opener, "share some color." The account = builder register for story, discipline register for numbers.

## Fingerprint

1. **Sentence rhythm & length (O'Connell)** — Medium declaratives that build toward a plainspoken punch, often via a conversational aside ("Listen,…" / "not to get too technical" / "Just to offer a little clarity there"). Sets a rule, then states the outcome flatly: "We've set our team that they don't get any credit unless they're shipping a product to a customer. That's exactly what we've done." Chains context before landing the point.

2. **Signature vocabulary & tics (O'Connell)** — Opens every Q&A with first name + gratitude for showing up: "Morning, Noah." / "Good morning, Brian. It's Shawn. Thank you for joining us. Good to hear your voice." Recurring lexicon: **"cautiously optimistic" / "cautiously anticipating"** (the house hedge), **"green shoots"** ("We're seeing a lot of green shoots"), **"share of wallet"** ("incremental and accelerating share of wallet"), **"the right to win"** ("afford us the right to win"), **"dry powder"** ("the amount of dry powder that EnerSys enjoys"), **"sails in the wind"** ("a lot of really good sails in the wind"), "doubling down," "actively manage what we can control." Hands numbers off: "I'll start and I'll turn it over to Andi for growth rates."

3. **Metaphor habitat** — Battlefield/electrification (native to an Army vet): "the future of the battlefield is electrified," "the forward edge of battle," "a defensive standoff weapon," "real estate's at a real premium" (on a warhead), "15 billion hours or so in space without a single flaw." Also sailing/agronomy ("sails in the wind," "green shoots") and card-table reserves ("dry powder"). Battery physics gets a homespun gloss: "knocking on the bottom edge of a lithium-like experience without any of the inherent risks of lithium… because they don't have the surface area reactivity, not to get too technical."

4. **Pushback behavior** — Never combative; the analysts are complimentary ("nice job in the first year, folks") and he receives it warmly ("Thank you so much"). Grants the premise before reframing, then hands specifics to the CFO. Uses personal tenure as evidence: "I've been in the EnerSys universe since 2003… I couldn't remember, and I asked the team, did they ever remember a time where the company could set records… with motive power being in a recessionary position? We couldn't think of any."

5. **Bad-news delivery** — Named, not buried, then re-credited to the team. "while a step back in volume is never something to celebrate for sure, where I give my team internally a lot of credit…". Funk is equally candid on a miss: "It's a frustration. Our orders were up sequentially 19%. Motive power is just not a segment I worry about."

6. **Hedging style** — Colorful conditional hedges rather than ranges. "barring any more wars in weird places or further supply shocks, we feel very good about our position." "for the foreseeable future, that'll continue." "at this time, we see that coming as well." Funk hedges statistically-but-loosely: "it's a little hard to tell," "My guess is it's not been overly material," "perhaps not at the elevated levels we saw in Q4," "the progression may not always be linear."

7. **Refusals** — Declines forward specifics on process grounds, defers to Investor Day. "we cannot disclose additional details on the planned facility until the award process is complete." Funk: "Brian, as you know, we don't give that specific of guide." Repeatedly gates: "There's a lot of work to do once you've shipped a product."

8. **CEO vs CFO split** — Funk (CFO): polite hand-off lead-ins ("Yeah. Good morning, Noah. I'll be happy to take that. Thanks."), then number-trains built from **"driven by… partially offset by…"** ("Net sales came in at $988 million, up 1%… driven by a 4% benefit from price mix, a 3% benefit from foreign currency translation, partially offset by a 6% decrease in organic volumes"). Her structural signature: **every figure stated both with and without the 45X credit** ("adjusted operating earnings were $154 million… Excluding 45X, adjusted operating earnings were roughly flat"). Recurring frame: demand is a **"project business… not always going to be linear quarter-to-quarter"** ("choppiness because of the project nature"). The account speaks figures in her register — bifurcated, sequential, hedged on linearity.

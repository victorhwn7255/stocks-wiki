## The #1 thing that breaks this

**AXTI owns a real chokepoint — but a *foreign government* owns the gate to it.** 100% of AXTI's InP substrate output is manufactured in China, and since February 4, 2025 every non-China shipment needs an export permit from China's Ministry of Commerce. The entire bull thesis — AXTI as the Western-headquartered InP supplier feeding the AI-photonics chain ([[LITE]], [[COHR]], [[AAOI]], [[AVGO]]) — runs through that gate, and **US-destined permits are still pending** (only ~1-2% of revenue now reaches North America, down from 8%). The $100M+ backlog that powers the whole story becomes unshippable to US customers if Beijing stops issuing permits. An independent research read (Tier 3) calls export-permit risk "the singular risk that could invalidate the entire investment case." This is a chokepoint owner whose chokepoint is controlled by policy it does not set — the break is any tightening of that gate.

## The ranked bear case (Severity × Evidence × Proximity)

### 1. Export-permit single-point-of-failure — China controls the gate
- **Tier 1 (10-Q risk factors) + Tier 3 (sizing) · Severity: thesis-ending · Evidence: FACT-today · Proximity: ongoing + escalation-dated · Realness: STRONG**
- US-destined InP permits remain **pending** (reframed from prior "first denials"; Ministry of Commerce has asked AXTI to resubmit with more data — an opaque process). North America = ~2% of FY2025 revenue, ~1% Q1 2026, down from 8% in FY2024. The risk has **already bitten once**: AXTI cut its Q4 2025 revenue outlook on fewer-than-expected permits (Tier 4: Reuters/TipRanks, March 2026). Escalation vector: China's revised Foreign Trade Law (effective March 2026) grants enhanced power to restrict exports for national-security reasons.
- **Tripwire:** a blanket US-permit denial; OR non-US permits (Europe/Japan/UK/Canada — currently flowing readily) starting to be denied — that would turn the $100M+ backlog from "permitted subset shippable" into "stranded."

### 2. 100% China manufacturing — no jurisdictional diversification + the two-sided fracture risk
- **Tier 1 (10-K) · Severity: thesis-ending · Evidence: FACT-today · Proximity: ongoing · Realness: STRONG**
- Every furnace, every subsidiary (Tongmei + JinMei indium refining + BoYu crucibles) is in China. This is the AI-side "squeezed-middle" twin documented in [[china-exposure]] (rated **High**; axes B/E/H) and the fracture risk in [[InP-supply]]: China can restrict the *output* (risk #1), and the **US can restrict the *input*** — US semiconductor-equipment export controls could block VECO/Aixtron MOCVD tools to AXTI's China ops, choking the capacity expansion the bull case depends on. The 2028 greenfield "possibly outside China" is the only structural mitigation, and it is direction-plus-funding, not a committed build.
- **Tripwire:** an Entity List addition or CFIUS action against Tongmei; a US MOCVD-equipment restriction naming AXTI's China operations.

### 3. The dilution treadmill — and the runway was just doubled
- **Tier 1 (10-K/8-K/424B5) + Tier 4 (proxy vote) · Severity: serious · Evidence: FACT-today · Proximity: ongoing + dated · Realness: STRONG**

FY2025 operating loss −$19.9M; the 2027 (~$100M) and 2028 (~$220-250M) capacity phases are **not yet contracted** → more capital likely. And the headroom just expanded: at the June 4, 2026 annual meeting (rescheduled after a May 14 quorum failure; the 2026-06-09 8-K Item 5.07) shareholders **approved raising authorized shares from 70M → 120M** — a near-doubling of the dilution runway.
- **Tripwire:** a new ATM/shelf draw; the newly authorized share block being tapped at the next capacity phase.

### 5. Profitability is thin and permit-timing-dependent
- **Tier 1 (10-Q) + Tier 2 (guide) · Severity: serious · Evidence: FACT-today + SCENARIO · Proximity: dated (Q2 FY2026 report ~early Aug) · Realness: MODERATE-STRONG**
- The profitability inflection is real but young and fragile: FY2025 was a −$23.2M net loss at 12.7% GM; Q1 2026 was still a small non-GAAP loss (−$0.6M). Q2 2026 is guided to AXTI's first GAAP + non-GAAP profit — but built on the **~$34M permitted/no-permit-required revenue subset**, with permit timing "not predictable" and "not aligned to quarterly reporting" (Fischer). One bad permit quarter and the loss returns.
- **Tripwire:** Q2 FY2026 profitability misses; OR H2 2026 slides back to a GAAP loss as the permit-gated revenue swings.

### 6. Tongmei STAR Market IPO overhang → $49M contingent redemption
- **Tier 1 (10-K) · Severity: serious · Evidence: FACT-today · Proximity: someday (deadline undisclosed) · Realness: MODERATE**
- Tongmei's STAR Market IPO has been pending since 2021 (>4 years, no resolution date). If it does not occur by the contractual deadline, AXTI owes minority shareholders a **~$49M redemption** — material against FY2025 revenue of $88.3M, and a potential forced dilutive financing on top of risk #3.
- **Tripwire:** disclosure of the contractual deadline; any STAR Market withdrawal or redemption trigger.

### 7. Demand-signal quality — unsigned LTSAs + double-ordering risk + AI-attribution caveat
- **Tier 1/Tier 2 · Severity: serious · Evidence: SCENARIO · Proximity: ongoing · Realness: MODERATE**
- The hyperscaler / Tier-1 long-term supply agreements management touts are **unsigned and unnamed**; no customer exceeded 10% of revenue in Q1 2026. InP is **not broken out at Tier 1** — the ">50% of revenue, AI-driven" narrative is call-only (Tier 2). [[InP-supply]]'s own confirm/weaken section pre-registers the deflation risk: a backlog built on "pre-ordering and double-ordering rather than genuine end-demand." If AI-photonics demand cools, a substrate supplier two layers upstream feels the inventory correction first and hardest.
- **Tripwire:** the $100M+ backlog declining; a promised LTSA failing to appear signed/named; order cancellations.

### 8. Competitive-moat framing gap — "two suppliers" vs "three"
- **Tier 1/Tier 2 · Severity: flesh-wound→serious · Evidence: FACT-today · Proximity: ongoing · Realness: MODERATE**
- AXTI's own sources disagree on its moat: the 10-K says "three primary suppliers" (AXTI + Sumitomo + JX Nippon, both Japan); the Q4 call said "two… and we're one of them." If the Japanese suppliers (permit-free for Western customers) have spare capacity, AXTI's backlog is partly share-shift, not pure structural scarcity — and Western customers have a non-China escape hatch that caps AXTI's pricing power and strategic leverage.
- **Tripwire:** Sumitomo / JX Nippon capacity-expansion announcements; Western laser makers qualifying Japanese substrate to de-risk China exposure.

## The watch-list (the tripwires, consolidated)

1. **The permit gate (the master dial):** any US blanket denial, OR — the real alarm — non-US (Europe/Japan) permits starting to be refused. This is risk #1 + #2's shared tripwire and the single most important thing to watch.
2. **Q2 FY2026 10-Q (~early Aug):** does the first-ever GAAP profit actually print, and does it hold into H2 as permit-gated revenue swings? (tests #5)
3. **Share issuance:** any draw on the newly authorized 70M→120M block / a new ATM (tests #3).
4. **Backlog direction:** the $100M+ InP backlog rising vs rolling over — the genuine-demand-vs-double-ordering test (tests #7).
5. **Geopolitical escalation:** Entity List / CFIUS action on Tongmei; US MOCVD-equipment restriction (tests #2); China Foreign Trade Law enforcement.
6. **Tongmei STAR Market IPO:** any deadline disclosure or withdrawal (tests #6).
7. **A signed, *named* hyperscaler/Tier-1 LTSA** — the bull resolution and the pre-registered photonics_tier 3→2 upgrade trigger (would flip #7 bullish).

## The steelman against this whole bear case

**AXTI is a genuine chokepoint owner at the foundational layer of AI photonics that is finally converting an order book into profits — and the export "kill switch" cuts less cleanly than the bear case assumes.** It is one of only 2-3 global InP substrate makers; the $100M+ backlog is a real forward demand signal that leads device-level revenue by 6-12 months; Q1 2026 inflected (+39% YoY, non-GAAP GM back to 29.9%, InP >50% of revenue) and Q2 is guided to first profit; and the $632.5M war chest de-risks a capacity roadmap management says demand is "10x." Crucially, the single point of failure is partly self-healing: **in-China shipments need no permit**, and the China-demand leg (~30% of demand, rising toward ~40%) is growing fastest as China builds its own optical/CPO supply chain — so even a full US-permit freeze reshapes AXTI into a China-for-China + permitted-geography supplier rather than zeroing it. Non-US permits (Europe/Japan/UK/Canada) keep flowing readily. The honest verdict: the structural risks here are real, well-evidenced, and mostly *outside AXTI's control* — which is exactly why the vault holds AXTI as a supply-chain demand-signal read, not a standalone investment thesis (photonics_tier held at 3, not upgraded). But the business is inflecting up, not breaking. This is a "China-gated, expectations-saturated, serially-diluting supplier" case — not a "failing company" case. The one genuinely thesis-ending risk (the permit gate) is a binary policy event, not a deteriorating fundamental.

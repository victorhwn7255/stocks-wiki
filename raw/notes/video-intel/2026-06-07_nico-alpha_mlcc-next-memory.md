## TL;DR

The pitch: AI's bottleneck narrative has stacked — **GPU (training) → memory/HBM (inference) → and now MLCC** (multilayer ceramic capacitors), the tiny passive parts that stabilize the power delivered to GPU/HBM/ASIC. In late May 2026 **Goldman Sachs** published several reports reframing high-end MLCC from a boring cyclical component into **"the new memory" — the 3rd-largest cost item in an AI server after GPU and memory** — and called this AI-driven MLCC up-cycle potentially the largest and longest ever, still early. The driver is the shift from single-box servers (A100/H100, ~thousands of MLCCs) to rack-scale systems (GB200/GB300 NVL72, **tens of thousands to ~100,000 MLCCs per rack**) plus the move to **800V HVDC** rack power, both demanding high-end (high-capacitance / low-ESL / small-size / high-voltage / high-reliability) MLCCs that the supply base can't expand fast. Three makers: **Murata** (#1, ~40% share — the certainty play), **Samsung Electro-Mechanics** (#2, the scale/attack play), **Taiyo Yuden** (the high-elasticity play). The creator's honest verdict: it *rhymes* with the early memory narrative and is being re-priced, but whether it becomes a durable AI hardware mainline or just a theme-spike is genuinely unknown.

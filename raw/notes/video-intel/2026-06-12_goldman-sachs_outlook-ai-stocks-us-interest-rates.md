## TL;DR

A Goldman rates-desk co-head frames the AI equity trade from the credit side: the rally is "a generational opportunity and it's going to continue," **but "a lot of the funding for it has happened with debt capital... when you're borrowing from the future and trying to outperform the cost of borrowing, you're at the whim of sentiment changes."** His credit-cycle clock: leverage is early-cycle low ("a clean start"), credit spreads at cyclical tights with term premium expanding (investors charging for duration, not credit — "healthy"), and the warning signatures to watch are **new-issue concessions popping up and buyers not showing up for new issues — which he places "12 to 18 months away,"** with **2027 as the squeeze point** (deeper capex cycle + deeper credit cycle + US Treasury upping coupon issuance). Rates: range-bound, market has repriced from cuts to pricing a hike, "fairly priced." His trade idea: "AI implementation rather than AI concentration" — enterprise tooling and integrators over the handful of mega-caps. Plus one notable macro marker: he previews **"Warsh's inaugural FOMC"** later in June.

Theory: for tail/existential outcomes, the optimal strategy is to assign a low probability to them going all the way through. Art Cashin's story — "you *buy* when the missiles are in the air; if they hit, who cares; if they don't, that's where the money is made." The White House is "aware of the 200-day moving average"; some sensitivity to markets, not perfect. Earnings estimates kept rising — and were right.

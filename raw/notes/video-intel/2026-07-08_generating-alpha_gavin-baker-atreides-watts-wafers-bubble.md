## TL;DR

1. **"We're obviously NOT in a valuation bubble."** Tech trades at the same multiple as 5-6 years ago, has *compressed* since the start of 2025, and now sits **at a discount to staples** (rare). The real question is a **capex / overbuild bubble**, not a valuation one.
2. **The bubble-preventer is scarcity:** "we're fundamentally short **watts and wafers**." The shortage may *prevent* an overbuild; even if power is solved, **TSMC is still the bottleneck** (litho is now an ASML monopoly; being ahead is a durable edge; Intel "lost on ego"), and TSMC "won't expand as fast as the world wants."
3. **New lens vs his May appearance:** via Carlota Perez's bubble framework, **whether the buildout is funded from debt or cash flows is "a critical distinction for AI."** That is — independently — the exact spine of the vault's [[telecom-bust-analog]].
4. **ROI is fine, with a caveat:** "a little divot of ROI right now because Blackwell is such an immense expenditure being used for **training** (no ROI yet)" — but agentic models (GPT-5.2, Grok 4.20, Codex 5.3) mean Blackwell ROI "will be very high."
5. **"Silicon eats the world"** because AI is probabilistic and *recomputes* every answer — far more compute-intensive than human-written deterministic software. Watts + wafers are "everything." He's **grateful to Michael Burry** as a credible bear.

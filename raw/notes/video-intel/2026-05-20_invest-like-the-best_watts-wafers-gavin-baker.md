- **Most session-relevant:** Baker — an AI bull — calls **nuclear and quantum outright "bubbles"** and lays out the exact *commodity-cycle* mechanism behind it: in a shortage, the **lowest-quality / highest-cost suppliers moon the most** (bankruptcy → gushing cash), get **bid to the moon by retail on X/Reddit**, then "go right back down." Strong Tier-3 corroboration of this session's nuclear read (cyclical, narrative-driven, not durable).
- **The bubble frame:** per Carlota Perez, a foundational tech *always* gets a bubble that funds the build-out, then a crash — worst if **debt-fueled (year 2000)**. Baker argues *this* build-out is **"overwhelmingly funded out of operating cash flows"** + 100% GPU utilization (vs 99% dark fiber) → less fragile. *(This directly conflicts with the vault's credit-risk finding — see Contradictions.)*
- **The #1 bubble signal he watches: TSMC's capacity decisions.** Wafers (TSMC) are the binding constraint single-handedly preventing an overbuild; "Goldilocks zone" = expand enough to keep Intel/Samsung from emerging as a >30% second source, but stay constrained enough to avoid a bubble. If TSMC gave Jensen what he wants, NVDA could sell "$2-3T of GPUs in '26/'27" — which would *be* the overbuild.
- **Watts:** capitalism solves the watts shortage ~**2027-2028**; the gating constraint is shifting from "energy and chips" to **"zoning and approval"** (quoting a big-PE data-center infra head). Then **orbital compute** ("racks in space," SpaceX) becomes the long-term escape valve for inference.
- **Application layer:** "**AI has net destroyed trillions of dollars of value at the application layer**" — profits accrue to energy/data-centers/chips/models, *not* apps. (Corroborates the vault's implementation-layer null-result.)

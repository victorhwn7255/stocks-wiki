## TL;DR

The most bullish AI-demand take in the video-intel log, from a fund positioned accordingly. Core frame: enterprise AI is **<1% penetrated** ("we call this an L-curve — just straight up"), real AI usage is at "10 basis points of knowledge workers" heading to 2–5% in four years, and there is "not enough compute in the world" — so the infrastructure layer (~10% penetrated) remains the best risk-adjusted way to play it. His sharpest supply-chain claim: AI is driving the **"decommoditization of the hardware industry"** — after 40 years of x86 commodity hell, every component (HBM, PCBs, laminates, fiber, power supplies, contract manufacturing) is being pushed to physical limits, converting 5%-margin commodity suppliers into spec-locked critical-infrastructure businesses with 4-year design visibility and rising ASPs. Meanwhile he went from 40–50% application software to **net short software** entering 2026 — the budget is going to tokens, not seats. The mirror image of Mitchell Green's episode on the same feed ten weeks earlier.

## Contradictions / red flags

- **Maximal book-talking:** his highest-conviction private (Anthropic), his largest publics (Google, NVDA-adjacent supply chain), his shorts (software), and his brand-new fund (Mega Cap Tech) are all pitched in one episode. The S-curve framework is real craft, but every data point arrives pre-selected to support positions he holds. Symmetric discount applies: his software bear case is as position-aligned as Green's software bull case.
- **The L-curve is a claim, not a law.** "<1% penetrated" assumes the eventual 100% — the same move AWS-TAM bulls got right and EV-at-50% bulls got wrong (he concedes EVs walled at 10–15%; honest of him). The vault's demand falsifiers (efficiency, token deflation, monetization gap) are precisely the ways an L-curve stops being an L.
- **"4x earnings for NVDA in 2023"** is forward-earnings hindsight math, not what the market multiple was — rhetorical, attribute don't adopt.
- **Anthropic figures are investor-side and round-adjacent** (the ~$180B round, the $1B→$9B trajectory, "half the compute they need") — directionally consistent with public reporting but none of it is primary-source; the "90 30 billion" caption garble makes one revenue figure unusable entirely.
- **Tension with the Green note (same channel, 10 weeks apart):** Green = AI capex bubble ends badly, buy software; Sacerdote = AI boom just starting, short software, own the hardware chain. They cannot both be right on software OR on the cycle. The vault's positioning (chokepoint owners + pre-registered tripwires) is deliberately robust to either, which is exactly what the two notes side-by-side demonstrate.

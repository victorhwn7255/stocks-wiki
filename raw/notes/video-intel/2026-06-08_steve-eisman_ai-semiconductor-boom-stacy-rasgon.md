## TL;DR

1. **The whole semi complex is now "the center of the universe."** In the 7 months since Rasgon's last appearance, AI went "into overdrive" and is dragging *everything* — memory, semicap, optical, power semis, **and now CPUs** — not just accelerators. You "could have owned anything in the space and been fine" (SOX +60%+ YTD).
2. **Investors are "playing bottlenecks."** As each sub-sector becomes *the* constraint, its stocks rip — memory (MU +100%+), CPUs, semicap all **outran NVDA itself**. And critically: the YTD gains have been **earnings, not multiple** — the SOX multiple actually *contracted*.
3. **The central paradox:** NVDA is **up only ~14% YTD** despite **85% revenue growth** (accelerating from 65%), and trades at a *lower* multiple than the constraint names whose entire businesses hinge on it. Rasgon: "one of them has to be wrong… it has to normalize — either the constraints come down or NVDA comes up."
4. **Everything hinges on NVDA.** If NVDA grows 85%, everyone's fine; if it decelerates to 40%, "everything's going down."
5. **What could end it:** ultimately **AI ROI disappointing** — but *nothing visible now* (capex keeps being revised **up**). If demand holds, the binding constraint is **power**.

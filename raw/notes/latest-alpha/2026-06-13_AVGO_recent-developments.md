## TL;DR

The post-earnings window is dominated by the market repricing AVGO's **financing structure and customer concentration**, not its demand. Reading the April 6 Google/Anthropic 8-K at primary adds three things canon didn't have: a **Supply Assurance Agreement for networking/components in Google's next-gen AI racks through up to 2031**; the Anthropic expansion sized at **~3.5 GW** (the June 3 call said "another five gigawatts" — a Tier-1-vs-Tier-2 discrepancy to reconcile); and the 8-K's own risk sentence — Anthropic's consumption is **"dependent on Anthropic's continued commercial success."** Tier-4 reporting details the Apollo/Blackstone $35B private-credit structure (AVGO credit endorsement + residual-value guarantees + 100% shortfall compensation on the $6B A1 + $24B A2 tranches — ties to the 10-Q's $29B backstop) and a four-partner Google chip chain (MediaTek inference at 20–30% lower cost) that would split future TPU share. Post-call: a June 11 debt tender offer (Tier 1), and the -12.6% / ~$280B selloff (signal only).

## Impact-ranked developments

1. **Google Supply Assurance Agreement through up to 2031 (Tier 1, April 6 8-K) `[verifiable]`** — canon (from the call) had "long-term agreement, very substantial dollars"; the 8-K adds a SECOND agreement: assured supply of networking + components for Google's next-gen AI racks through up to 2031. That's a multi-year networking annuity on top of the TPU design relationship — the strongest un-ingested bull item in the window.
2. **The Anthropic GW discrepancy (Tier 1 vs Tier 2) `[verifiable]`** — 8-K (Apr 6): "approximately 3.5 gigawatts… beginning in 2027." Call (Jun 3): "another five gigawatts of next-generation TPU-based compute beginning in 2027." Possibilities: the commitment grew between April and June; or the call rounded/scoped differently (3.5 GW "as part of multiple gigawatts committed"). Either way the two venues disagree on the headline number — reconcile at the Q3 venue.
3. **The credit-risk sentence at primary (Tier 1) `[verifiable]`** — the 8-K itself: Anthropic's consumption is "**dependent on Anthropic's continued commercial success**," with "operational and financial partners" in discussion — the vendor-financing risk the market repriced on June 4, written by AVGO in April. Pairs with the 10-Q's $29B backstop (in canon).
4. **Apollo/Blackstone tranche detail (Tier 4) `[verifiable at future filings]`** — reports: $35B private credit for Anthropic's compute; AVGO provides credit endorsement + residual-value guarantees + **100% shortfall compensation** to the $6B A1 + $24B A2 tranches; SPV sells chips on default. $6B+$24B = $30B ≈ the 10-Q's $29B max exposure — consistent, but the guarantee mechanics are Tier-4 until a filing shows them.
5. **Google four-partner diversification (Tier 4) `[forward]`** — Broadcom keeps training silicon ("Sunfish"); MediaTek takes inference ("Zebrafish," 20–30% cheaper); Marvell + Intel in the chain. Matches Hock Tan's own Tier-2 hedge ("diversity of sources"). The risk is mix, not loss: inference is the volume segment. Honest counterweight to item 1.
6. **June 11 debt tender offers (Tier 1) `[verifiable]`** — liability management days after the call; read alongside the new contingent exposure (cleaning the debt stack while taking on the backstop). Scope/size to verify in Ex 99.1 / next 10-Q.
7. **Say-on-pay ~33% against + Harry You ~26% against (Tier 1) `[verifiable]`** — meaningful comp dissent for a megacap; governance footnote, not thesis-central.

## Contradictions / red flags

- **3.5 GW vs 5 GW** — the cleanest cross-venue number conflict in the vault's AVGO record; if the call's 5 GW reflects a real April→June expansion, that's bullish; if it's loose rounding of 3.5, that's a disclosure-quality flag on the call's GW arithmetic generally (which feeds the 10-GW-2027 math).
- **Vendor-financing circularity** — AVGO guarantees the debt that funds its own customer's purchases; revenue recognized now, credit risk retained. The 8-K's "dependent on continued commercial success" sentence is AVGO saying so itself.
- **Incoming CFO from the largest customer** — Thuener ran Alphabet's accounting while Alphabet was AVGO's anchor TPU customer; not improper, but worth noting as the guidance-methodology handoff happens mid-ramp.
- Tier-4 reports (four-partner chain, tranche mechanics) are NOT AVGO disclosures — keep hedged until primary.

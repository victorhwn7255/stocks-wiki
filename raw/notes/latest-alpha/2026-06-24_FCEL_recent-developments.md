## TL;DR

FCEL signed a **strategic agreement with Fit Energy USA LP for "up to 380 MW"** of on-site data-center power (8-K event Jun 22, filed Jun 24; PR Jun 23) — and crucially it carries an **immediate deposit for an initial 30 MW with delivery beginning later this year (2026).** That deposit-backed 30 MW is the **first concrete pipeline→firm-order conversion** FCEL has produced — materially harder than every prior MOU/LOI (Inuverse MOU, SDCL "up to 450 MW" LOI), and it lands squarely on the page's pre-registered **Open Q#1** (convert proposals to contracted backlog within FY2026). The honest counterweights, in the same breath: **Fit Energy is a power *developer*, NOT a named hyperscaler** — so the page's falsified **criterion 4 (named hyperscaler concentration) stays falsified**; the **"380 MW" is an "up to" framework** (only 30 MW is deposit-backed); FCEL is **issuing the customer warrants** tied to deployment milestones (customer-incentive dilution, on top of the still-active S-3ASR shelf + ATM); and 30 MW is tiny against the 4 GW pipeline. So: the first real crack in the "narrative-outrunning-proof" framing — but a **developer-led, small-firm-tranche, warrant-sweetened** crack, not the hyperscaler-scale win the trigger specifies.

## Impact-ranked developments

### 1. Fit Energy agreement — the first deposit-backed firm tranche (Tier 1 / Tier 4) `[verifiable]`
- **The bull driver:** "up to 380 MW of clean, baseload on-site power for data centers," with **"an immediate deposit for an initial 30 MW of power scheduled to begin delivery later this year."** A **deposit + a delivery schedule** is a different category than the page's existing MOUs/LOIs — it's the first item that can plausibly enter **contracted backlog**, which is the exact metric Open Q#1 watches. CEO framing: the deal "validates" the decision to scale Torrington to 500 MW. Fit Energy CEO Joel Leonoff called it "a critical step in building the power foundation required for the next generation of AI infrastructure."
- **Honest counterweights (all load-bearing):**
  - **Not a hyperscaler.** Fit Energy USA LP is "a developer of reliable power solutions to support advanced computing infrastructure and AI" — an **intermediary/developer**, not Microsoft/Amazon/Google/Meta/OpenAI and not a named data-center operator. **Page criterion 4 (named hyperscaler concentration) remains falsified.** This validates *developer* demand, not a direct hyperscaler win.
  - **"Up to 380 MW" is a framework, not a firm order** — structurally the same "up to" aspiration as the SDCL "up to 450 MW" LOI. The **firm, deposit-backed part is 30 MW**; the other 350 MW is milestone-contingent.
  - **Customer warrants = dilution.** Fit Energy is "eligible to receive warrants tied to future deployment milestones of up to 380 MW." No count/strike disclosed. This is **customer-incentive dilution** — structurally echoes the [[BE]]–Oracle warrant pattern, but here the counterparty is a developer, and it stacks on FCEL's already equity-dependent funding (S-3ASR shelf + ATM).
  - **Scale:** 30 MW firm is ~0.75% of the 4 GW pipeline and modest vs the 500 MW Torrington target — a real first order, not yet "material commercial scale."
  - **Wording:** the PR calls it a **"strategic agreement,"** softer than a definitive/binding supply contract; the deposit on 30 MW is the concrete kernel inside softer framing.

### 2. Dilution machinery still running (Tier 1) `[verifiable]`
The S-3ASR auto shelf (Jun 8, prior window) + heavy ATM (~$156M gross in/just after Q2) remain the funding bridge; the **new Fit Energy warrants add a second dilution vector** (customer warrants vs market ATM). Funding posture stays equity-market-dependent.

## TL;DR

OUST's recent strength is a genuine **cluster of commercial-momentum announcements** — three named partnerships (AIM, Benchmark, ARGUS), the **Rev8 native-color digital lidar** launch, and **BlueCity** smart-traffic deployments — capped by a **manufacturing scale-up to >100k units/yr (Benchmark, 10-yr horizon)**. The honest catch: **all three partnerships are "strategic agreements," explicitly NOT binding purchase orders** (no disclosed dollar value, units, or quantities), so the move is on partnership *narrative*, not booked revenue. Two more counterweights: (1) the 2026 annual meeting **doubled authorized shares 100M → 200M** (dilution headroom for an equity-funded, loss-making company); (2) **almost none of this is humanoid** — the traction is heavy-machine autonomy (mining/construction), traffic infrastructure, and counter-drone defense. That actually *confirms* the vault's read: humanoid stays "named but unsized," and OUST is a physical-AI *sensing* play whose real demand is industrial/infrastructure/defense, not humanoid robots.

## Impact-ranked developments

1. **Steve M. Smith → Board (Tier 1; 8-K May 26; `[verifiable]`).** Smith (age 70) is **CEO of Zayo** (global fiber/network) since 2020 and was **CEO & President of Equinix 2007-2018** (one of the largest global data-center operators), plus a director of **NextDC** (Australian listed data centers); prior senior roles at HP Services, Lucent, EDS. Determined independent. **Why it matters:** this is the single clearest "we are a credible enterprise data-center company now" governance signal — an Equinix CEO is precisely the operator who knows how to sell hyperscaler colocation, which is the muscle CORZ needs for its post-CoreWeave diversification (the page's Open Question #1). Bull-leaning, but governance-credibility, not a signed customer.
2. **$1B JPMorgan + Morgan Stanley strategic facility (Tier 1; March 23; `[verifiable]`).** A separate instrument from the $3.3B CoreWeave project bond (JPMorgan's $500M added to Morgan Stanley's prior $500M = $1.0B funded commitments). The canon Financing section names the bond + the two convertibles but **not** this facility — a completeness gap to close at the 10-Q, not fresh alpha (it pre-dates the covered period-end / call).
3. **2026 Annual Meeting (Tier 1; 8-K May 12; `[verifiable]`).** Five directors elected (Adam Sullivan, Jeff Booth, Elizabeth Crain, Yadin Rozov, Eric Weiss) to 2027. **Jeff Booth and Eric Weiss each drew ~51M withhold votes** vs ~2-7M for the other three — a mild shareholder-dissent signal on two directors; both still elected comfortably. Low thesis impact; note for the governance watch.

## TL;DR (plain language)

In the last 60 days AAOI's 8-K flow is dominated by **two things a bull narrative tends to skip: a big physical-capacity build-out funded by heavy dilution, and a mid-year auditor change.** It bought a **$58.4M Texas property** (Apr) and signed **three more Houston industrial-building leases** (May), then opened a **$600M at-the-market stock-sale program** (May 14) to help pay for it — on top of the ~$490M it already raised earlier in 2026. It also **dismissed its auditor, Grant Thornton** (May 4). The bull signal is real and lives mostly at conferences: at **OFC 2026** (just before the window) it showed the **25dBm ELSFP / CPO** laser and a live **6.4T on-board-optics** demo, and at the **Needham conference (May 13)** it said **datacenter revenue passed cable-TV for the first time** and **raised 2026 guidance to $1.1B**. None of the CPO story is in a filing yet (the Q1 10-Q is silent on CPO) — so it stays a conference/IR signal to verify.

## Impact-ranked developments

**1. Capacity build-out — large and physical (real, verifiable commitments).** Two Item-1.01 8-Ks in the window: a **$58.4M purchase** of ~388,133 sq ft at Pearland, TX (office/warehouse/manufacturing/assembly; closing ~Apr 17), and **three Houston industrial-building leases** (123-month / ~10-year terms, 3-month rent abatement, escalating rent). This is the bricks-and-mortar side of the indium-phosphide / ELSFP ramp — the durable, hard-to-fake commitment behind the order story.

**2. Heavy, stacked dilution (the honest counterweight).** A **new $600M ATM program** (May 14, Raymond James + Needham, 2% commission) sits on top of the ~$490M already raised earlier in 2026 (completed Apr 2). The capacity above is being paid for substantially with new equity — share count is growing right alongside the order book. Sharpens the dilution worry already on the page; cuts against the price-target euphoria.

**3. Auditor change — Grant Thornton dismissed (governance, read carefully).** May 4 Item 4.01: the Audit Committee ran a "competitive selection process" and **dismissed Grant Thornton** as auditor for FY2026. Mid-year auditor dismissals are sometimes routine (cost/rotation) and sometimes a flag — the deciding detail is whether the full Item 4.01 discloses any *disagreements* or *reportable events*, and who the successor is. Surface it neutrally; do not alarm or wave away. **A bull-only tracker skips this entirely — surfacing it is exactly the skill's job.**

**4. The bull signal lives at conferences (Tier-2, CPO is IR-only).**
- **OFC 2026 (Mar 17–19, LA — adjacent to window):** showcased the **25dBm ultra-high-power ELSFP** (400mW CW laser) as the "high-link-budget foundation for CPO/NPO," Booth #739, full **100G–1.6T** range, and a **live 6.4T On-Board-Optics (OBO) demo** powered by the 400mW external-laser ELSFP. CFO/CSO Stefan Murry hosted an investor session.
- **Needham TMC Conference (May 13 — in window):** **datacenter revenue surpassed cable-TV for the first time**; **2026 guidance raised to $1.1B**; 800G ramp + 1.6T later in the year.
- These are the timely, thesis-relevant signals — but they're **conference/IR (Tier 2), and the CPO/ELSFP story is NOT in any filing** (Q1 10-Q silent on CPO, vault Session 48). Verify at the Q2 10-Q.

**5. Trading noise (signal only).** PT hikes (Rosenblatt $140→$220; Raymond James $72.50→$160), a new 2X leveraged AAOI ETF, short-covering. Note it, don't weight it.

## Contradictions / red flags

- **Dilution vs. the bull narrative.** The same momentum driving PT hikes is funding ~$1B+ of stock issuance + a big real-estate footprint. Net the share growth against the revenue growth.
- **Auditor dismissal.** A governance event the bull case omits. Verify the full Item 4.01 (disagreements? successor?).
- **Cross-venue disclosure gap (§3.6).** The CPO/ELSFP story is loud at **OFC** (conference) and the guidance raise came at **Needham** (conference) — but the **Q1 10-Q is silent on CPO**. Conference-carries-CPO / filing-silent is the NVDA-at-GTC pattern. Honest framing: "silence at the filing ≠ retreat" while OFC keeps carrying it — but it stays unverified at primary until a 10-Q picks it up.
- **Guidance raised at a conference, not an 8-K.** The $1.1B figure was disclosed at Needham (Tier 2), not furnished as an 8-K — so it's management commentary to confirm at the Q2 10-Q, not a filed number.
- **Customer names inferred, not disclosed.** See discipline note above.

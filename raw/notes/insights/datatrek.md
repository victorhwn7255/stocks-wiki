## 2026-07-02 — "STT: Exit, Pursued by an AI Bear" (Story Time Thursday)

**Authors:** Nicholas Colas + Jessica Rabe (co-founders). DataTrek Morning Briefing — holiday-shortened Story Time Thursday. Source: `raw/reports/datatrek/dt-2026-07-02.eml`.

**Headline (their view):** Two popular "AI trade" bear stories — GPU-depreciation understatement, and "no margin gains → AI spend gets cut" — are "fundamentally wrong or, at best, woefully incomplete." DataTrek is the **bull / skeptic-of-the-bears** voice here.

**Bear rebuttal #1 — GPU depreciation is a weak argument (their view):**
- The bear case (their summary): Big Tech's server/GPU useful lives (they cite **2–6 yrs, most ~5**) are too long → underreported depreciation → reported earnings crash once the upgrade-cycle reality hits.
- Their rebuttal: (a) equity value is **future cash flows, not net income** — depreciation is a **non-cash** charge; the GPU cash is already spent (**sunk cost**), depreciation is just "accounting residue"; (b) valuation is driven by **return on capital + moat** (a 20%-on-capital business is worth ~2× a 10% one), not net income; (c) useful-life estimates are **auditor-certified + CEO/CFO-attested under criminal penalty**, so the quiet-understatement/fraud framing is overdone. Verdict: the depreciation bear case's "utility is essentially zero."

**Bear rebuttal #2 — "AI's only value is margin improvement" is wrong (their view):**
- (1) Line (non-C-suite) managers chase **market share, not margins** — "gained 2 pts of share, profits flat" beats "lost 2 pts, profits +5%" at year-end review; no manager can ignore AI if rivals use it to take share. (2) C-suites/boards buy AI to **defend the moat** — "it's not what you make, it's how long you make it." (3) Investors punish perceived **AI-complacency** regardless of current profit — proof cited: **Microsoft −19% YTD** despite no pinpointable AI hit to current profitability (the "Amazoned" fear repricing).
- Valuation frame: every S&P 500 sector trades **>10× forward earnings**, implying the market discounts future earnings ≥ current (≈10% cost of capital) — "a high bar."
- Final thought: the AI story is ~3 yrs old; keep focus on Cash-Flow-Statement drivers; "the power of AI is doubling every 12–18 months."

**Curated links they flagged (Tier 4 news as DataTrek relayed it — vault-relevant):**
- **Zuckerberg internal town hall (Reuters):** AI-agent development over the last 4 months "not accelerated in the way we expected"; a reorg with major job cuts was mis-timed / "not as clean." [→ agentic-AI slowdown tell; strengthens the *cautious* read on Meta Compute.]
- **Apple negotiating to buy memory from CXMT + YMTC** (Pentagon-blacklisted Chinese makers) to ease the global memory shortage — lobbying the Trump admin, risking national-security blowback. [→ shortage severity + China-memory entering the Western demand pool.]
- **Blackstone's QTS abandoned its Virginia data-center mega-campus** after years of resident opposition. [→ power/siting constraint beyond transformers.]
- **June payrolls +57k** (miss vs ~115k consensus; May revised down to 129k); unemployment 4.2% but **participation 61.5%** (lowest since Mar-2021), household employment **−507k**.
- **OpenAI discussing a 5% stake to the US government** ($852B valuation) to clear political obstacles.
- Tesla Q2 deliveries 480,126 (beat ~406k est); Amazon Leo at 396 satellites (Starlink rival; on track for mid-2026 commercial).

## 2026-07-01 — "S&P Overnight Drift, Momentum Peaking, Cybersecurity Stocks"

**Authors:** Nicholas Colas + Jessica Rabe (Cybersecurity feature bylined by Rabe). Usual 3-part briefing. Source: `raw/reports/datatrek/dt-2026-07-01.eml`.

**Headline (their view):** The most vault-relevant call in the set — DataTrek flags the AI **"chips and shovels" trade (semis + datacenter construction) at a +4 standard-deviation momentum extreme** and says it is "prudent to reduce exposure," *while remaining long-term Tech bulls*. Note the stance nuance vs the 07-02 "the bears are wrong" essay: here the perma-constructive shop is itself waving a **froth flag**.

**Momentum peaking (the load-bearing section, their view):**
- Momentum (MTUM proxy) 100-day relative outperformance hit **+24.3 pts (first crossed +4 SD on June 18)** — vs a long-run average of **+0.8 pts** (SD 5.6; 4 SD = +23.2). "Momentum is in uncharted statistical waters… a very powerful and unprecedented rally" (pre-2026 it topped out at +10–15 pts).
- Why: **8 of MTUM's top-10 holdings are AI-related** (only exceptions JNJ, XOM); sector overweights **Tech +15.3 / Industrials +6.5 / Energy +6.1 pts**. "The reason for Momentum's recent astounding gains is exactly what you'd think — owning the very hottest slices of the AI trade (**semis and data center construction plays**)."
- Call: **"We think it is prudent to reduce exposure to these names"** / lighten up — but **"as much as we are long-term Tech bulls, ignoring a 4 standard deviation move is not part of our own investment playbook."**
- **Fund flows debunk the "retail is buying US stocks" myth** (ICI, wk ending Jun 24): net **−$3.1B** out of long-term funds (vs +$38B/wk prior 4-wk avg); **US stock funds −$18.1B**; of the **$598B** added YTD, **73% went to bonds**, and equity flows ran **~2:1 toward non-US** stocks.

**Cybersecurity — "the next scarcity phase of the AI trade" (Rabe, their view):**
- CIBR (cyber-ETF proxy) outperformed the S&P by **+30.2 pts over 100 days = +4 SD** (largest in the decade-long series), snapping back from a **−22.3-pt oversold on Feb 23** — an **AI-disruption scare** (fear that AI auto-vuln-finding makes legacy cyber redundant), which pre-dates the Feb 28 US-Iran strike, so not a macro shock.
- Catalyst: the **"Claude Mythos" moment — Anthropic's disclosure that AI crossed a threshold for accelerating cyberattacks** — reframing cyber as "more essential to defend against AI, not less."
- Valuation caution: top-10 holdings at **63.9× forward P/E (3× the S&P's 20.1×)** on only modest EPS revisions (+2.8% / +3.6%). Named: **PANW +91% YTD vs +9% implied EPS growth; CRWD +64.9% vs +26.2%**; FTNT / OKTA / F5 at new highs. Pure-play scarcity (CSCO + AVGO ≈15% of the top-10 but diversified). "Not a top… never short a new high."

**Overnight-drift (market structure, minor):** the NY Fed's documented overnight "drift" (1998–2020: ~63% of S&P returns earned in the 2–3am ET hour) has **entirely disappeared in 2021–2025** as closing-auction order imbalances fell −55%. YTD IVV: buy-open-sell-close +4.4% vs buy-close-sell-open +4.9% vs simply **holding +9.3%** → "most participants are better off holding than trading."

**Curated links (Tier-4, vault-relevant):** **Meta Compute** — Meta building a cloud-infra business to sell AI compute + models (hyperscaler / neocloud); **Apple ↔ CXMT / YMTC** blacklisted Chinese memory makers amid the global memory shortage; **Strait of Hormuz** shipping >10M bbl/day with US military support (defense / energy); Fed Chair **Warsh** — inflation "too elevated," no meeting signal; **SpaceX** AI-handset prototype running xAI tech; **USMCA** left unrenewed (July-1 deadline passed); $2.8tn H1 global M&A (+49% YoY).

## 2026-06-30 — "June / Q2 / YTD Global Markets Performance Review"

**Authors:** Nicholas Colas + Jessica Rabe. Month-/half-year performance review (returns price-based, ex-dividend, via ETFs / MSCI). Source: `raw/reports/datatrek/dt-2026-06-30.eml`.

**Headline (their view):** The Q2/H1 scorecard shows the AI trade's leadership **rotated FROM the Mag7 ("dead money") TO the picks-and-shovels layer beneath it** — memory, semis, equipment, and the Taiwan/Korea hardware names. "Semiconductors can become scarce" is the organizing idea. They **"remain positive on equities."** This directly validates the vault's chokepoint thesis.

**The scorecard (their numbers):**
- S&P 500 **Q1 −4.6% → Q2 +14.9%**; Nasdaq **Q2 +21.4%**, overtaking the S&P YTD. "All the bad news came in Q1, all the 'good stuff' in Q2."
- **Technology the best sector: Q2 +43.4% / YTD +32.3%** (small-cap Tech +53.8% Q2 / +63.2% YTD, ≈2:1 vs large-cap). **Industrials** best in June (+6.7%), YTD +19.4% (Caterpillar tagged an "AI trade" name, Q2 +51%). **Comms worst YTD −9.0%** (Meta −12% / Alphabet −6% in June). Energy +18.8% YTD but gave back ~half its Q1 gain as oil fell.
- **Mag7 = "dead money":** a **−2.8-pt drag on the S&P in June** (all seven down), and just **~1% of the S&P's YTD price return** (MSFT the largest single drag). "All the YTD action has been in the strata just below the Mag 7."
- **Memory the standout scarcity trade:** Micron **+300% H1** (their figure; a CNBC link cites +240%), **SK Hynix +307% YTD, Samsung +179%, MediaTek +197%, TSMC +57%**; the CNBC link adds **INTC +216%, AMD +186%** (MU + INTC + AMD ≈ **+$2T** market cap in Q2).
- **International = an AI-hardware proxy:** MSCI **Korea +107.7% YTD, Taiwan +71.0%**, EM +25.0%, Netherlands +23.3% (ASML +86%); Europe lagging +5.9%. "Country returns are driven by how many big tech-hardware companies are public locally, not local economics."
- Factors: **Momentum +42.9% Q2 / +37% YTD**; Growth > Value. Assets: **WTI Q2 −30.9% / YTD +22.1%** (oil the flagged 2H swing risk); **gold −7.2% YTD** (peaked Q1); **Bitcoin −32.9% YTD / −53.3% from its Oct-2025 ATH**; the dollar reversed higher.

**Their interpretation:** the scarcity trade "shifted from oil (Q1) to semiconductors and the materials/industrial know-how needed to build AI data centers (Q2)"; hyperscalers are "giving all their cash flows (and then some, due to debt issuance)" to the smaller AI-datacenter value-chain businesses; "semiconductors can become scarce" has "upended decades of common wisdom about international diversification." Key 2H risk = the direction of oil. Analogy: 2026 = "1990–91 (Gulf War I) + a random slice of the late-1990s (Dot Com)."

**Curated links (Tier-4, vault-relevant):** CNBC — chip stocks' "historic" Q2 (MU +240%, INTC +216%, AMD +186%; ~$2T added); Bloomberg — **Samsung + SK Hynix to build two fabs each in Korea (₩800tn); chip-equipment TAM seen ~$250B by 2028**; CNBC — CRWD + PANW "best quarter ever as autonomous AI agents fuel demand" (Mythos "inflection point"); AWS investing **$1B in a Forward-Deployed-Engineering unit**; FT — firms investing most in AI grew white-collar headcount +10.2% (challenges the AI-job-loss narrative).
**Notable absence:** no Utilities / AI-power call in the sector review — a gap in DataTrek's framing, given the vault's binding-2026 power-constraint thesis.

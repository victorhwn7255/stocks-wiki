## 2026-06-25 — "Market Update & Core Stock Ideas + Top 5 Large-Cap & SMID-Cap Stocks"

**Authors:** Tom Lee (macro), Mark Newton (technical), Ken Xuan (data science). Monthly strategy + macro deck; data as of 6/22–6/24/2026. Source: `raw/reports/FS-direct-2026-06-25.pdf`.

**Headline (their view):** YE-2026 S&P 500 target **raised to 8,000 (from 7,700)** — because surging earnings make the index "cheaper today than on Jan 1." Path is back-end-loaded: possible mid-year "round-trip" pullback, then a Q4 rally.

**Macro calls (verbatim, their view):**
- **S&P YE-2026 target 8,000 (from 7,700).** Driven by an EPS revision, not multiple expansion: 2027E S&P EPS lifted from $350 (Dec '25) to **$400** (Jun '26); target P/E cut **22x → 20x** and the price still rises. (Math is internally loose — they cite both $400 and the LSEG $399.25 figure.)
- **"Cheaper than Jan 1."** On 2027 numbers the index P/E fell **19.4x → 18.4x** YTD; equal-weight S&P NTM P/E **16.8x** vs 6.5-yr avg 17.4x.
- **Path:** historical composite of years following three consecutive +20% gains (since 1928) → gains back-ended; aspirational push to ~7,300, possible round-trip, then Q4 rally toward ~8,200. Advice: "buy the dip," "don't time the market."
- **Fed flipped hawkish (the key shift):** markets now price **~2 hikes by YE** (Fed-funds futures ~1.6 hikes; swung from 2.0 *cuts* in March). June SEP raised median YE-2026 funds rate **3.4% → 3.8%**, cut GDP −0.2% to 2.2%, raised Core PCE +0.6% to **3.3%**. Driver: the Feb-2026 USA-Iran war (their "7th black swan").
- **Inflation mixed:** oil "war premium" shrinking ($73 vs a ~$118 spike; $65 in Jan), but 10-yr yield rising **4.2% → 4.5%** ("headwind"). Truflation real-time CPI **1.84%** vs BLS 4.20% — supports their structurally-disinflationary lean.
- **New Fed chair Kevin Warsh** is the swing variable: says inflation is "a choice," will deliver a "2"-handle, prefers *less* forward guidance; created 5 task forces.
- **Margin-debt warning:** FINRA margin debt **+55% YoY**, above the 45% threshold that historically precedes weaker returns (8 prior instances: avg −2.6% fwd 12-mo, 25% win ratio).
- **Demographic super-bull:** US only major economy with growing prime-age (30–48) population; implied S&P YE-2029 scenarios 6,906 (8% CAGR) / 10,663 (15%) / 18,983 (25%, "bubble").

**Sector / thematic (their view):**
- Favorite sectors (unchanged): Technology, Financials, Industrials, Small-caps, Energy/Basic Materials.
- 3 structural drivers: (1) AI + energy infrastructure, (2) Wall Street onto blockchain, (3) on-shoring.
- Semis/memory lead YTD: **SMH +160%, DRAM (Roundhill memory ETF) +150%** (since 4/2/26), MAGS +32%, IGV +1%, ETH −25%.
- **"Power is the next bottleneck"** — reproduces a Rand Group sector map of the power cohort: generation **VST/CEG/GEV/BE**; SMRs **OKLO/SMR/BWXT/X-energy**; uranium **CCJ/LEU/UUUU/NLR**; grid **VRT/HUBB/POWL/PWR**; datacenter power **IREN/WULF/CORZ/CIFR**.
- **Robotics/humanoid** — reproduces a McKinsey humanoid supply-chain bottleneck map flagging actuators + sensing as highest-risk (NdFeB magnets, harmonic/strain-wave drives, ball/roller screws, 6-axis force-torque sensors); forward: "1 million humanoid robots in US economy by 2030."
- **AI IPO "unlock" risk:** SpaceX ($75B), Google ($85B), Anthropic (~$75B?), OpenAI (~$100B?) — a named H2 liquidity drain; SpaceX float scaling ~$90B → ~$1.5T by Dec 2026.

**Core List (quant-ranked; "Top" = highest-ranked, "Bottom" = lowest — relative under-performers, NOT explicit shorts; no per-stock price targets):**
- Large-cap **Top:** AMD, ANET, **CAT (new)**, GS, PWR. **Bottom:** ECHO, **NOC (new)**, **PLTR (new)**, **MSTR (new)**, TPL.
- SMID **Top:** FIX, STRL, **VMI (new)**, **MLI (new)**, IESC. **Bottom:** ECHO, **WFRD (new)**, **AVAV (new, −20% 1M)**, UUUU, **KTOS (−16% 1M)**.

**Risk flags (their three H2 "tests"):** (1) new Fed (Warsh) gets tested; (2) AI IPO unlock drains liquidity; (3) longer Iran war → jet-fuel/diesel shock. Plus margin debt >45% YoY, rising 10-yr to 4.5%, hawkish Fed repricing. Their resolution: tactical pullback, not thesis-breaking — "buy the dip."

**What changed vs prior (May Core List + Dec outlook):** Target **raised 7,700 → 8,000**; 2027E EPS **$350 → $400**; target P/E **22x → 20x**. Large-cap: added **CAT** (Top); **GOOGL dropped from Top**; added **NOC/PLTR/MSTR** to Bottom. SMID: added **VMI/MLI** to Top (**CRS/FN rotated out** — FN was −17.3%); added **WFRD/AVAV** to Bottom. ## 2026-05-28 — "Market Update & Core Stock Ideas + Top 5 Large-Cap & SMID-Cap Stocks"

**Authors:** Tom Lee (macro), Mark Newton (technical), Ken Xuan (data science). Weekly macro + strategy + core-list deck; data as of 5/26–5/27/2026. Source: `raw/reports/FS-direct-2026-05-28.pdf`.

**Headline (their view):** 2026 = "Resilience and US exceptionalism." S&P round-trips to a **~7,300 mid-year peak ("aspirational"), corrects, then rallies to ~7,700 year-end.** The AI/Mag7/software/crypto complex has **already bottomed**.

**Macro calls (verbatim, their view):**
- **S&P path 3-phase:** up to ~7,300 by summer, a round-trip/correction, then Q4 rally to **~7,700 YE**. Based on the historical composite for Year+1 after three consecutive ≥+20% years (since 1928).

## 2026-07-02 — Top of Mind: "How Will AI Impact the Labor Market?" (GS Research economics)

**Source:** GS Exchanges / Top of Mind podcast; **Joseph Briggs** (GS Research, global economics) with guests **Daron Acemoglu** + **Neil Thompson** (MIT); host Allison Nathan. Full note: `raw/notes/video-intel/2026-07-02_goldman-sachs_how-will-ai-impact-the-labor-market.md`.

**Headline (GS view = Briggs):** AI's labor impact is **real but manageable, not an apocalypse** — a modest productivity story, net job creation offsets the displacement.

**GS Research calls (verbatim, their view):**
- **Baseline: a 15% productivity uplift after full AI adoption** → applying the historical productivity→displacement elasticity → **~9% of US workers (~15M) reallocated over ~10 years**, but **<1pp** unemployment rise in any given year (spread out). *(The 15% is a notably MODEST productivity number vs tech-hype — a useful sober anchor for the AI-capex-ROI debate.)*
- Net job creation offsets: **~85% of 80-yr job growth = tech-created new roles**; ~30M created / ~29M destroyed annually; a 5% acceleration in creation reabsorbs the displaced. "Not concerned about permanent job losses."

**Guest views (MIT — external, not GS):**
- **Acemoglu (Nobel skeptic):** small **net job loss <2-4% within 5 years**; the bottleneck is the **missing reliable turnkey-APPLICATION layer on the foundation models** ("the Microsoft Office version of AI — we're not seeing it yet"), NOT capability; investment is skewed to **replacing** not **complementing** workers → bigger losses in 10-15yrs if it continues; AI+robotics = the big wild card (physical ≈ 50% of work); expects labor-income inequality to rise.
- **Thompson:** "rising tides, not crashing waves" — capability ≠ adoption (data-access + cost friction); slow, uneven, *visible*.

**Vault relevance:** feeds the AI-ROI / monetization-gap falsifier ([[AI-demand-durability]]) with GS's own *modest* productivity number + Acemoglu's app-layer bottleneck ([[AI-implementation-deployment-layer]]). The sober counterweight to the feed's "AI demand is real" consensus (SemiAnalysis/Patel, JPM).

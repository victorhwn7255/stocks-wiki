(Incentive lean: this is OpenAI's TAM-maximalist house view; weight accordingly.)

- **★ Vahdat (S3) — "value per gigawatt," not raw GW or flops** `[opinion]` (the course's central metric): *"the measure isn't how much money you spent per gigawatt, it's how much value you deliver per dollar"* (~5:00); better proxies = revenue per GW or **daily active users per GW**. - **★ Vahdat (S3) — system balance (Amdahl's law) is the real constraint; memory-bandwidth-bound not flops-bound** `[opinion]`: *"scaling flops is easy… building a coordinated supercomputer with the right balance point, super hard"* (~15:00). Mixture-of-experts / sparse compute needs *more memory bandwidth relative to compute*, and *"the hardware today, all of it, isn't built at the right system balance point"* → that's why MFU is low. - **Vahdat (S3) — utilization is broken industry-wide** `[verifiable]`: xAI Colossus *"~11% MFU"*; Google treats *<96% node allocation as a "major outage."* Note the **cross-video tension**: Vahdat frames 11% as genuinely-low waste; Jensen (S—) framed the same number as *intentional* overprovisioning. - **★ Midha (S11) — the compute→revenue valuation arbitrage** `[opinion]` (~47:00): *"a dollar of compute in — hard assets (land/power/shell) that trade at 3-4× revenue — turns into a dollar of software revenue that trades at 30-40× revenue. ### 4. Networking, fabrics & optics
- **★ Vahdat (S3) — Google's TPU fabric uses optical circuit switches (OCS), not Ethernet, in a 3D torus** `[verifiable]` (~32-44:00): within a rack, *every TPU-to-TPU link is copper* (point-to-point, "the right technology" — same copper-over-optical call as NVLink72); between racks, an **OCS** (a chip with *~136 MEMS mirrors*, each rotatable in 3D) gives a *software-programmable topology*. The differentiator is **availability**: a failed rack is "virtually removed" and a spare swapped in *in seconds* by re-pointing mirrors (vs a synchronous job otherwise halting). A second OCS layer "short-circuits" expensive packet-switch layers by pointing full bandwidth at a distant storage cluster for the duration of a job (e.g. a 5-hour agent run). Torus is optimal for all-reduce (training); Clos/switch better for all-to-all. Names Norm Jouppi as the ~2015 first-principles call.
